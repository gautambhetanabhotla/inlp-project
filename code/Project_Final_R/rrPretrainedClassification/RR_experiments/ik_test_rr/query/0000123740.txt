RatioOfTheDecision	As a lease is a transfer of an estate of inheritance, it does not terminate with the death of the original lessee, but survives during the: remainder of the term of his heirs and representatives and if the heirs and representatives are acknowledged as tenants and they continue in occupation of the premises as tenants after the efflux of  the time limited by the original lease, there is holding over.

RatioOfTheDecision	The question which we now propose to dispose of is whether the permission granted by the District Magistrate under Section 3 of the U. P. Control of Rent and Eviction Act No.
RatioOfTheDecision	III of 1947 was a valid and good permission.
RatioOfTheDecision	The permission of the District Magistrate for a suit for ejectment postulated under Section 3 of the U. P. Rent Control and Eviction Act relates to cases which are not covered by Clauses (a) to (g) of that section.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause (b) covers a case where the tenant has wilfully caused or per-mitted to be caused substantial damage to the accommodation.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause (c) covers a case where the tenant has, without the permission in writing of the landlord, made or permitted to be made any such construction as in the opinion of the court has materially altered the accommodation or is likely substantially to diminish its value.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the suit is regarded as one falling under Clauses (b) and (c) the permission of the District Magistrate would not be  necessary.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case there is concurrent finding of fact of the two courts below that the tenants wilfully caused or permitted to be caused substantial damage to the accommodation and they without the permission in writing of the landlord, made constructions which materially altered the accommodation.
RatioOfTheDecision	The present case was therefore covered by Clauses (b) and (c) of Section 3 and the permission of the District Magistrate would not therefore be necessary.

RatioOfTheDecision	Leases for a definite period, such as a lease for a year, or for a term of years, expire on the last day of the term and the lessor may enter without notice or other formality.
RatioOfTheDecision	Under Clause (g) of Section 111 a breach of condition only makes the lease voidable, the forfeiture is not complete unless and until the lessor gives notice that he has exercised his option to determine the lease.
RatioOfTheDecision	In <NAME> v. <NAME>, (1947) 1 All E. R. 116 (B) it has been explained that when a forfeiture of a lease is incurred the lease is voidable and not void and in those circumstances the giving of a notice to quit may recognise the subsistence of the lease and may amount to a waiver of forfeiture, but when the tenancy is determined by a notice to quit the position is entirely different.
RatioOfTheDecision	When a valid notice to quit is given, the lease is determined and a new tenancy can be created by an agreement, express or implied, and no such agreement can be infer red from the fact of service of a second notice.
RatioOfTheDecision	14.
RatioOfTheDecision	Under Section 112 of the Transfer of Property Act a forfeiture under Section 111 , Clause (g) is waived by acceptance of rent which has become due since the forfeiture, or by any other act on the part of the lessor showing an intention to treat the lease as subsisting, provided that the lessor is aware that the forfeiture has been incurred; provided also that where rent is accepted after the institution of a suit to eject the lessee on the ground of forfeiture such acceptance is not waiver.
RatioOfTheDecision	The election not to avoid the lease may be manifested in other ways besides acceptance of rent.
RatioOfTheDecision	Instances of such acts showing an intention to treat the lease as subsisting are a demand of rent accruing due since the breach, a suit for such rent, or acceptance of a sum paid into court as damages for breach of covenant to repair alleged to have been committed during the term.
