RatioOfTheDecision	Considered from that perspective, the question arises: whether the view taken by the <ORG> is justified in law?
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that filling up of the posts are for clear or anticipated vacancies arising in the year.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is settled law that mere inclusion of one's name in the list does not confer any right in him/her to appointment.

RulingByLowerCourt	<CITATION_982107> [(1991) 2 SCR 567], <ORG> had held  that inclusion of the name of a candidate in a merit list does not confer any right to be selected unless the relevant recruitment rules so indicate.

Precedent	[] it was held that the mere fact that a candidate's name finds a place in the select list as a selected candidate for appointment to a post, does not confer on him/her an indefeasible right to be appointed in such post in the absence of any specific rule entitling him to such appointment.
