Fact	CASE NO.
Fact	: Appeal (civil) 14152 of 1996 PETITIONER: STATE OF U.P.
Fact	RESPONDENT: ALLIED CONSTRUCTIONS DATE OF JUDGMENT: 31/07/2003 BENCH: <NAME> CJ & <NAME> & <NAME> JUDGMENT:  JUDGMENT 2003 Supp(2) SCR 55 The following Order of the Court was delivered :  Under a contract entered into by and between the appellant and the respondent, the respondent undertook construction of bridge-cum-fall at Munda Khera Scape at the estimated cost of Rs.
Fact	37.2 lakhs.
Fact	While the work was in progress, the work area was flooded in the night of August 25 and 26, 1991.
Fact	The respondent-contractor herein filed a claim on account of loss sustained by him due to flooding of the work area.
Fact	Ultimately, the matter was referred to an arbitrator.
Fact	The arbitrator gave an award for payment of a sum of Rs.
Fact	12,55,365 together with interest at the rate of 18 per cent from 1.11.1991 till the date of the award and 6 per cent thereafter.
Fact	The respondent filed the award for being made rule of the Court.
RulingByLowerCourt	The appellant herein filed a petit ion, inter alia, on the ground that the arbitrator has misconducted the proceedings, inasmuch as the force majeure contained in Clause 47 disentitled the respondent from making any claim which was on account of unprecedented rain.
RulingByLowerCourt	The said objection was rejected and the award was made rule of the Court.
RulingByLowerCourt	The appellant thereafter filed a first appeal from order before <ORG> and the same was dismissed.
Fact	It is against the said judgment, the appellant is in appeal before us.
Argument	Learned counsel appearing for the appellant reiterated his argument raised before <ORG>.
Argument	In fact, his argument based on force majeure is that because of unprecendented rain the liability of loss cannot be thrust upon the appellant.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not find any merit in this contention.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause 47 of the Agreement runs as under:  "Neither party shall be liable to the other for any loss or damage occasioned by or arising out of act of God, such as unprecedented flood, volcanic eruption, earthquake or other convulsion of nature and other a cts such as but not restricted to general strikes, invasion, the act of foreign countries; hostilities or warlike operations before or after declaration of war; rebellion, military or usurped power which prevent performance of the contract and which could not have been foreseen or avoided by a prudent person."
RatioOfTheDecision	A perusal of Clause 47 reproduced above shows that it protected the <ORG> from liability and damage occasioned by unprecedented flood which could not have been foreseen or avoided as a prudent person.
RatioOfTheDecision	The appellant herein did not lead any evidence before the arbitrator that the rain as a result of which the loss was sustained by the respondent was unprecedented and in fact it was an act of God.
RatioOfTheDecision	In absence of such an evidence, the arbitrator as well as <ORG> has recorded a finding of fact that the flood which has caused loss to the respondent was not due to the unprecedented rain and, therefore, Clause 47 of the Agreement was not attracted.
RatioOfTheDecision	Any award made by an arbitrator can be set aside only  if one or the other term specified in Sections 30 and 33 of the Arbitration Act, 1940 is attracted.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not a case where it can be said that the arbitrator has misconducted the proceedings.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was within his jurisdiction to interpret Clause 47 of the Agreement having regard to the fact-situation obtaining therein.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is submitted that an award made by an arbitrator may be wrong either on law or on fact and error of law on the face of it could not nullify an award.
RatioOfTheDecision	The award is a speaking one.
RatioOfTheDecision	The arbitrator has assigned sufficient and cogent reasons in support thereof.
RatioOfTheDecision	Interpretation of a contract, it is trite, is a matter for arbitrator to determine (see <CITATION_588099> , AIR (1989) SC 890).
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 30 of the Arbitration Act, 1940 providing for setting aside an award is restrictive in its operation.
RatioOfTheDecision	Unless one or the other condition contained in Section 30 is satisfied, an award cannot be set aside.
RatioOfTheDecision	The arbitrator is a Judge chosen by the parties and his decision is final.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Court is precluded from re appraising the evidence.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even in a case where the award contains reasons, the interference therewith would still be not available within the jurisdiction of the <ORG> unless, of course, the reasons are totally perverse or the judgment is based on a wrong proposition of law.
RatioOfTheDecision	An error apparent on the face of the records would not imply closer scrutiny of the merits of documents and materials on record.
RatioOfTheDecision	Once it is found that the view of the arbitrator is a plausible one, the <ORG> will refrain itself from interfering [see <CITATION_1341010> .,  3 SCC 397 and <CITATION_1483262> ,  6 SCC 347].
RulingByPresentCourt	For that reason, we are of the view that the appeal has no merit and must fail.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, the parties are agreed that from the date of filing of the claim till the date of award the interest chargeable would be at the rate of 1?
RatioOfTheDecision	per cent in place of 18 per cent.
RulingByPresentCourt	For that reason, the award and judgment under challenge stand modified to that extent.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeal is disposed of in the aforesaid terms.
RulingByPresentCourt	There shall be no order as  to costs.
