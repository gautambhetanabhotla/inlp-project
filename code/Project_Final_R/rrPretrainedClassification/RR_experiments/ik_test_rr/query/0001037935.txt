Fact	If a criminal court is to view the testimony of the aforesaid three witnesses as unnatural it would be easy to brush it aside with the stereotype d reasoning that those persons had no cause to remember having seen the man with the girl accompanying him.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such a reasoning overlooks the broad aspect that a human mind, on hearing about any shocking incident, would have the tendency to recollect any previous event which could have had a connection with that incident If as a matter of fact those witnesses had occasion to see a crying girl of that age on the very day of the gruesome episode as happened in this case, there is nothing improbable in those witnesses remembering the person who was seen in the company of that girl.
RatioOfTheDecision	If they had immediately informed the police that they noticed a similarly aged girl crying in the company of an utter stranger of that locality that cannot be brushed aside as a doubtful conduct.
RatioOfTheDecision	Either the three witnesses concocted the story falsely or what they said must be true.
RatioOfTheDecision	Why should they concoct it falsely.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are not told of any reason whatsoever for those three witnesses to bother themselves to concoct such a canard.
RatioOfTheDecision	It seem s that a minor discrepancy in their evidence had affected their credibility before <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	They said that they went to the police station on 24.12.1995, whereas <ORG> said that they visited me police station only on 25.12.1995.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not attach any significance to the aforesaid discrepancy as PW-26 should have been more correct because he was speaking with the help of investigation records while the witnesses would have spoken from their memory only.
RatioOfTheDecision	Another reason advanced by <ORG> is that when PW-3 <NAME> went to the bereaved house he did not inform anyone in that family as to what he saw earlier.
RatioOfTheDecision	But PW-3 himself gave an explanation for it that as members of that family were then in a shock he did not venture to tell them about it at that occasion.
RatioOfTheDecision	Here also the question is not whether PW-3 should have told them despite his hesitation but whether the witness had chosen to adopt such a reticence in a situation like that.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not for the <ORG> to suggest that h e should have divulged it to the members of the bereaved family despite his own thinking about it At any rate we are not impressed by the aforesaid reasoning for rejecting the testimony of an important witness like PW-3.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> then advanced a theory that there is "an inherent incredibility in the evidence" on the premise that a culprit kidnapping a minor girl with sinister design would normally take the precaution not to be seen by any other person on the way, but in this case the culprit along with the girl had moved from place to place in the town.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are unable to appreciate such a reasoning as proposition of human conduct.
RatioOfTheDecision	For considering that reasoning it must be remembered that <NAME> would certainly have been abducted by some body (even assuming that it was not this respondent) and that person had taken the abducted girl from her house up to the farm.
RatioOfTheDecision	Unless it is suggested that there was another alternative and safer route for the culprit to take the girl unnoticed by any shopkeeper or ev en a pedestrian there in no rationale in the reasoning that there is "inherent incredibility" in the version that respondent would have taken the girl through this route.
RatioOfTheDecision	The last reasoning of <ORG> is based on a criticism of the modes adopted by the Executive Magistrate who held the test identification parade.
RatioOfTheDecision	The aforesaid criticism was based on the evidence of two witnesses who said that the accused were taken on foot from police station to the place where the parade was conducted and that their faces were not covered during such transit.
RatioOfTheDecision	Ext 17 is the minutes of the test identification parade conducted by the Magistrate who himself was examined as PW-2.
RatioOfTheDecision	It contains the details of the steps adopted by him.
RatioOfTheDecision	Seven other persons were kept ready in the room and the witnesses were kept in another room from where they could not see the suspect Thereupon the suspect was brought from the lock up with the help of two respectable persons and all precautions were taken that the witnesses could not see  the suspect during such transit.
RatioOfTheDecision	Then me suspect was permitted to stand anywhere among the 7 persons.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was thereafter that the witnesses were brought with the help of the same respectable persons and the witnesses were then asked to identify the person whom they saw on the crucial day.
RatioOfTheDecision	If potholes were to be ferreted out from the proceedings of the magistrates holding such parades possibly no test identification parade can escape from one or two lapses.
RatioOfTheDecision	If a scrutiny is made from that angle alone and the result of the parade is treated as vitiated every test identification parade would become unusable.
RatioOfTheDecision	We remind ourselves that identification parades are not primarily meant for the court.
RatioOfTheDecision	They are meant for investigation purposes; The object of conducting test identification parade is two fold.
RatioOfTheDecision	First is to enable the witnesses to satisfy themselves that the prisoner whom they suspect is really the one who was seen by them in connection with the commission of the crime.
RatioOfTheDecision	Second is to satisfy the investigating  authorities that the suspect is the real person whom the witnesses had seen in connection with the said occurrence.
RatioOfTheDecision	So the officer conducting the test identification parade should ensure that the said object of the parade is achieved.
Precedent	If he permits dilution of the modality to be followed in a parade, he should see to it that such relaxation would not impair the purpose for which the parade is held, vide <CITATION_312114> ,  2 SCO 128 and <CITATION_87528> ,  3 SCC 86.
Precedent	When we scanned through.
Precedent	Ex.
RatioOfTheDecision	17 minutes of the test identification parade, we feel that the safeguards adopted by PW-2 Executive Magistrate were quite sufficient for ensuring that the parade was conducted in a reasonably foolproof manner.
RatioOfTheDecision	We feel that <ORG> niggled on unimportant details and came to the wrong conclusion that the test identification parade was irretrievably vitiated.
RatioOfTheDecision	The reasons by which the testimony of those three witnesses had been jettisoned by <ORG> were fatuous and we cannot support them.
RatioOfTheDecision	One of t he formidably incriminating circumstances against the accused was that the dead body was recovered as pointed out by the respondent.

RatioOfTheDecision	But unfortunately <ORG> of <ORG> did not rely on the above circumstance on a very fragile reasoning.
RatioOfTheDecision	The first limb of that reasoning was based on a mistake committed by PW-3 <NAME> in his evidence when he said that he saw the dead body of the child on 23,12.1995.
RatioOfTheDecision	Much strain is not required in holding that what PW-3 said should have been understood as 24.12.1995.
RatioOfTheDecision	The second limb of the reasoning is that two other possibilities could not have been ruled out.
RatioOfTheDecision	Of which one is that respondent would have seen someone else placing the dead body at that spot, and the second is that respondent would ha ve been told by somebody else that the dead body was placed there:  We too countenance three possibilities when an accused points out the place where a dead body or an incriminating material was concealed without stating that it was conceded by himself.
RatioOfTheDecision	One is that he himself would have concealed it Second is that he would have seen somebody else concealing it.
RatioOfTheDecision	And the third is that he would have been told by another person that it was concealed there.
RatioOfTheDecision	But if the accused declines to tell the criminal court that his knowledge about the concealment was on account of one of the last two possibilities me criminal court can presume that it was concealed by the accused himself.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is because accused is the only person who can Offer the explanation as to how else he came to know of such concealment and if he chooses to refrain from telling the court as to how else he came to know of it, the presumption is a well justified course to be adopted by the criminal court mat the concealment was made by himself.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such an i nterpretation is not inconsistent with the principle embodied in Section 27 of the Evidence Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is regrettable that <ORG> had practically nullified the most formidable incriminating circumstance against the accused spoken to by PW-22 Dr. <NAME>.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have pointed out earlier the injuries which the doctor had noted on the person of the accused when he was examined on 25.12.1995.
RatioOfTheDecision	The significant impact of the said incriminating circumstance is that the accused could not give any explanation whatsoever for those injuries and therefore he had chosen to say that he did not sustain any such injury at all.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have no reason to disbelieve the testimony of PW-22 Dr. <NAME>, False answer Offered by the accused when his attention was drawn to the aforesaid circumstance renders that circumstance capable of inculpating him.
RatioOfTheDecision	In a situation like this such a false answer can also be counted as providing "a missing link" for completing the chain.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is disconcerting that a case like his in which the p rosecution has presented such reliable and formidable circumstances forming into a completed chain and pointing unerringly to me irresistible conclusion mat the little girl <NAME> was raped and killed by none other than the respondent himself, ended in unmerited acquittal from <ORG> of <ORG> Criminal justice unfortunately became a casualty in this case when <ORG> side-stepped all such circumstances and exonerated the culprit of such a grotesque crime.
