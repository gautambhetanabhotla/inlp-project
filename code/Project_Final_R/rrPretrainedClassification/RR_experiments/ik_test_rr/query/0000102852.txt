Fact	STANDARDS OF JUDICIAL REVIEW OF  CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS:  Constitution is not an ephermal legal document embodying a set of legal rules for the passing hour.
Fact	It sets out principles for an expanding future and is intended to endure for ages to come and consequently to be adapted to the various crisis of human affairs.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, a purposive rather than a strict literal approach to the interpretation should be adopted.
RatioOfTheDecision	A Constitutional provision must be construed not in a narrow and constricted sense but in a wide and liberal manner so as to anticipate and take account of changing conditions and purposes so that constitutional provision does not get fossilized but remains flexible enough to meet the newly emerging problems and challenges.
RatioOfTheDecision	This principle of interpretation is particularly apposite to the interpretation of fundamental rights.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is a fallacy to regard fundamental rights as a gift from the <ORG> to its citizens.
RatioOfTheDecision	Individuals possess basic human rights independently of any constitution by reason of basic fact that they are memb ers of the human race.
RatioOfTheDecision	These fundamental rights are important as they possess intrinsic value.
RatioOfTheDecision	Part-III of the Constitution does not confer fundamental rights.
RatioOfTheDecision	It confirms their existence and gives them protection.
RatioOfTheDecision	Its purpose is to withdraw certain subjects from the area of political controversy to place them beyond the reach of majorities and officials and to establish them as legal principles to be applied by the courts.
RatioOfTheDecision	Every right has a content.
RatioOfTheDecision	Every foundational value is put in Part-III as fundamental right as it has intrinsic value.
RatioOfTheDecision	The converse does not apply.
RatioOfTheDecision	A right becomes a fundamental right because it has foundational value.
RatioOfTheDecision	Apart from the principles, one has also to see the structure of the Article in which the fundamental value is incorporated.
RatioOfTheDecision	Fundamental right is a limitation on the power of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	A Constitution, and in particular that of it which protects and which entrenches fundamental rights and freedoms to which all persons in the <ORG> are to be entitled is to be given a generous  and purposive construction.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the case of <CITATION_243002> this <ORG> has held that while considering the nature and content of fundamental rights, the <ORG> must not be too astute to interpret the language in a literal sense so as to whittle them down.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> must interpret the Constitution in a manner which would enable the citizens to enjoy the rights guaranteed by it in the fullest measure.
Precedent	An instance of literal and narrow interpretation of a vital fundamental right in the Indian Constitution is the early decision of the Supreme <ORG> in the case of <CITATION_1857950> .
Precedent	Article 21 of the Constitution provides that no person shall be deprived of his life and personal liberty except according to procedure established by law.
Precedent	The Supreme <ORG> by a majority held that 'procedure established by law' means any procedure established by law made by the <ORG> or the legislatures of the State.
Precedent	The Supreme <ORG> refused to infuse the procedure with principles of natural justice.
Precedent	It concentrated solely upo n the existence of enacted law.
Precedent	After three decades, <ORG> overruled its previous decision in A.K.
Precedent	Gopalan10 and held in its landmark judgment in <CITATION_1766147> that the procedure contemplated by Article 21 must answer the test of reasonableness.
Precedent	The <ORG> further held that the procedure should also be in conformity with the principles of natural justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	This example is given to demonstrate an instance of expansive interpretation of a fundamental right.
RatioOfTheDecision	The expression 'life' in Article 21 does not connote merely physical or animal existence.
RatioOfTheDecision	The right to life includes right to live with human dignity.
RatioOfTheDecision	This <ORG> has in numerous cases deduced fundamental features which are not specifically mentioned in Part-III on the principle that certain unarticulated rights are implicit in the enumerated guarantees.
RatioOfTheDecision	For example, freedom of information has been held to be implicit in the guarantee of freedom of speech and expression.
RatioOfTheDecision	In India, till recently, there is no legislation securing freedom of infor mation.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, this <ORG> by a liberal interpretation deduced the right to know and right to access information on the reasoning that the concept of an open government is the direct result from the right to know which is implicit in the right of free speech and expression guaranteed under Article 19(1)(a).
RatioOfTheDecision	The important point to be noted is that the content of a right is defined by the <ORG>s.
Fact	The final word on the content of the right is of this <ORG>.
Fact	Therefore, constitutional adjudication plays a very important role in this exercise.
Fact	The nature of constitutional adjudication has been a subject matter of several debates.
Fact	At one extreme, it is argued that judicial review of legislation should be confined to the language of the constitution and its original intent.
Fact	At the other end, non- interpretivism asserts that the way and indeterminate nature of the constitutional text permits a variety of standards and values.
Fact	Others claim that the purpose of a Bill of Rights is to protect the process of decision maki ng.
Fact	The question which arises before us is regarding nature of the standards of judicial review required to be applied in judging the validity of the constitutional amendments in the context of the doctrine of basic structure.
RatioOfTheDecision	The concept of a basic structure giving coherence and durability to a Constitution has a certain intrinsic force.
RatioOfTheDecision	This doctrine has essentially developed from the German Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	This development is the emergence of the constitutional principles in their own right.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not based on literal wordings.
Precedent	In <NAME> & Others etc.
Precedent	v. <ORG> & Others etc.
Precedent	, the basic structure concept was resorted to although no question of constitutional amendment was involved in that case.
Precedent	But this Court held that policies of a State Government directed against an element of the basic structure of the Constitution would be a valid ground for the exercise of the central power under Article 356 , that is, imposition of the President's rule.
Precedent	In that case, secularism was held to be an essenti al feature of the Constitution and part of its basic structure.
Precedent	<ORG> may be dismissed not because it violates any particular provision of the Constitution but because it acts against a vital principle enacting and giving coherence to a number of particular provisions, example: Articles 14, 15 and 25.
Precedent	In S.R.
RatioOfTheDecision	Bommai12, the <ORG> clearly based its conclusion not so much on violation of particular constitutional provision but on this generalized ground i.e.
RatioOfTheDecision	evidence of a pattern of action directed against the principle of secularism.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, it is important to note that the recognition of a basic structure in the context of amendment provides an insight that there are, beyond the words of particular provisions, systematic principles underlying and connecting the provisions of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	These principles give coherence to the Constitution and make it an organic whole.
RatioOfTheDecision	These principles are part of Constitutional law even if they are not expressly stated in the form of rules.
RatioOfTheDecision	An instance is  the principle of reasonableness which connects Articles 14, 19 and 21.
RatioOfTheDecision	Some of these principles may be so important and fundamental, as to qualify as 'essential features' or part of the 'basic structure' of the Constitution, that is to say, they are not open to amendment.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, it is only by linking provisions to such overarching principles that one would be able to distinguish essential from less essential features of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	The point which is important to be noted is that principles of federalism, secularism, reasonableness and socialism etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	are beyond the words of a particular provision.
RatioOfTheDecision	They are systematic and structural principles underlying and connecting various provisions of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	They give coherence to the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	They make the Constitution an organic whole.
RatioOfTheDecision	They are part of constitutional law even if they are not expressly stated in the form of rules.
RatioOfTheDecision	For a constitutional principle to qualify as an essential feature, it must be established that the said principle i s a part of the constitutional law binding on the legislature.
RatioOfTheDecision	Only thereafter, the second step is to be taken, namely, whether the principle is so fundamental as to bind even the amending power of the <ORG>, i.e.
RatioOfTheDecision	to form a part of the basic structure.
RatioOfTheDecision	The basic structure concept accordingly limits the amending power of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	To sum up: in order to qualify as an essential feature, a principle is to be first established as part of the constitutional law and as such binding on the legislature.
RatioOfTheDecision	Only then, it can be examined whether it is so fundamental as to bind even the amending power of the <ORG> i.e.
RatioOfTheDecision	to form part of the basic structure of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is the standard of judicial review of constitutional amendments in the context of the doctrine of basic structure.
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, the doctrine of basic structure has essentially emanated from the German Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, we may have a look at common constitutional provisions under <ORG> which deal with rights, such a s, freedom of press or religion which are not mere values, they are justiciable and capable of interpretation.
RatioOfTheDecision	The values impose a positive duty on the <ORG> to ensure their attainment as far as practicable.
RatioOfTheDecision	The rights, liberties and freedoms of the individual are not only to be protected against the <ORG>, they should be facilitated by it.
RatioOfTheDecision	They are to be informed.
RatioOfTheDecision	Overarching and informing of these rights and values is the principle of human dignity under the German basic law.
RatioOfTheDecision	Similarly, secularism is the principle which is the overarching principle of several rights and values under the Indian Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, axioms like secularism, democracy, reasonableness, social justice etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	are overarching principles which provide linking factor for principle of fundamental rights like Articles 14, 19 and  21.
RatioOfTheDecision	These principles are beyond the amending power of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	They pervade all enacted laws and they stand at the pinnacle of the hierarchy of constitutional values.
RatioOfTheDecision	For example, under the German Con stitutional Law, human dignity under Article 1 is inviolable.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is the duty of the <ORG> not only to protect the human dignity but to facilitate it by taking positive steps in that direction.
RatioOfTheDecision	No exact definition of human dignity exists.
RatioOfTheDecision	It refers to the intrinsic value of every human being, which is to be respected.
RatioOfTheDecision	It cannot be taken away.
RatioOfTheDecision	It cannot give.
RatioOfTheDecision	It simply is.

RatioOfTheDecision	(emphasis supplied) Therefore, while judging the width and the ambit of Article 16(4A) we must ascertain whether such sub- classification is permissible under the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	The sub-classification between "OBC" on one hand and "SC and ST" on the other hand is held to be constitutionally permissible in Indra Sawhney5.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the said judgment it has been held that the <ORG> could make such sub- classification between SCs and STs vis-`-vis OBC.
RatioOfTheDecision	It refers to sub-classification within the egalitarian equality (vide paras 802 and 803).
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, Article 16(4A) follows the line suggested by this <ORG> in Indra Sawhney5 .
RatioOfTheDecision	In Indra Sawhney5 on the other hand vide para 829 this <ORG> has struck a balance between formal equality and eg alitarian equality by laying down the rule of 50% (ceiling-limit) for the entire BC as "a class apart" vis-`-vis GC.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, in our view, equality as a concept is retained even under Article 16(4A) which is carved out of Article 16(4).
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, Article 14 enables classification.
RatioOfTheDecision	A classification must be founded on intelligible differential which distinguishes those that are grouped together from others.
RatioOfTheDecision	The differential must have a rational relation to the object sought to be achieved by the law under challenge.
RatioOfTheDecision	In Indra Sawhney5 an opinion was expressed by this <ORG> vide para 802 that there is no constitutional or legal bar to making of classification.
RatioOfTheDecision	Article 16(4B) is also an enabling provision.
RatioOfTheDecision	It seeks to make classification on the basis of the differential between current vacancies and carry-forward vacancies.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the case of Article 16(4B) we must keep in mind that following the judgment in <NAME> the concept of post-based roster is introduced.
RatioOfTheDecision	Consequently, specific slots for OBC , SC and ST as well as GC have to be maintained in the roster.
RatioOfTheDecision	For want of candidate in a particular category the post may remain unfilled.
RatioOfTheDecision	Nonetheless, that slot has to be filled only by the specified category.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, by Article 16(4B) a classification is made between current vacancies on one hand and carry-forward/backlog vacancies on the other hand.
RatioOfTheDecision	Article 16(4B) is a direct consequence of the judgment of this court in <NAME> by which the concept of post-based roster is introduced.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, in our view Articles 16(4A) and 16(4B) form a composite part of the scheme envisaged.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, in our view Articles 16(4), 16(4A) and 16(4B) together form part of the same scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, Articles 16(4A) and 16(4B) are both inspired by observations of <ORG> in <NAME> and <NAME>.
RatioOfTheDecision	They have nexus with Articles 17 and 46 of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, we uphold the classification envisaged by Articles 16(4A) and 16(4B).
RatioOfTheDecision	The impugned constitutional amendments, therefo re, do not obliterate equality.
RatioOfTheDecision	The test for judging the width of the power and the test for adjudicating the exercise of power by the concerned <ORG> are two different tests which warrant two different judicial approaches.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case, as stated above, we are required to test the width of the power under the impugned amendments.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, we have to apply "the width test".
RatioOfTheDecision	In applying "the width test" we have to see whether the impugned amendments obliterate the constitutional limitations mentioned in Article 16(4 ), namely, backwardness and inadequacy of representation.
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, these limitations are not obliterated by the impugned amendments.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, the question still remains whether the concerned <ORG> has identified and valued the circumstances justifying it to make reservation.
RatioOfTheDecision	This question has to be decided case- wise.
RatioOfTheDecision	There are numerous petitions pending in this Court in which reservations made under <ORG> enactments have been challenged as excessive.
RatioOfTheDecision	The extent of reservation has  to be decided on facts of each case.
RatioOfTheDecision	The judgment in <NAME> does not deal with constitutional amendments.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our present judgment, we are upholding the validity of the constitutional amendments subject to the limitations.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, in each case the <ORG> has got to be satisfied that the <ORG> has exercised its opinion in making reservations in promotions for SCs and STs and for which the concerned <ORG> will have to place before the <ORG> the requisite quantifiable data in each case and satisfy the <ORG> that such reservations became necessary on account of inadequacy of representation of SCs/ STs in a particular class or classes of posts without affecting general efficiency of service as mandated under Article 335 of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	The constitutional principle of equality is inherent in the Rule of Law.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, its reach is limited because its primary concern is not with the content of the law but with its enforcement and application.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Rule of Law is satisfied when laws are applied or enfor ced equally, that is, evenhandedly, free of bias and without irrational distinction.
RatioOfTheDecision	The concept of equality allows differential treatment but it prevents distinctions that are not properly justified.
RatioOfTheDecision	Justification needs each case to be decided on case to case basis.
RatioOfTheDecision	Existence of power cannot be denied on the ground that it is likely to be abused.
RatioOfTheDecision	As against this, it has been held vide para 650 of Kesavananda Bharati13 that where the nature of the power granted by the Constitution is in doubt then the <ORG> has to take into account the consequences that might ensue by interpreting the same as an unlimited power.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, in the present case there is neither any dispute about the existence of the power nor is there any dispute about the nature of the power of amendment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The issue involved in the present case is concerning the width of the power.
RatioOfTheDecision	The power to amend is an enumerated power in the Constitution and, therefore, its limitations, if any, must be found in the Constitution itself.
RatioOfTheDecision	The concept of reserv ation in Article 16(4) is hedged by three constitutional requirements, namely, backwardness of a class, inadequacy of representation in public employment of that class and overall efficiency of the administration.
RatioOfTheDecision	These requirements are not obliterated by the impugned constitutional amendments.
RatioOfTheDecision	Reservation is not in issue.
RatioOfTheDecision	What is in issue is the extent of reservation.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the extent of reservation is excessive then it makes an inroad into the principle of equality in Article 16(1).
RatioOfTheDecision	Extent of reservation, as stated above, will depend on the facts of each case.
RatioOfTheDecision	Backwardness and inadequacy of representation are compelling reasons for the <ORG> Governments to provide representation in public employment.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, if in a given case the court finds excessive reservation under the <ORG> enactment then such an enactment would be liable to be struck down since it would amount to derogation of the above constitutional requirements.
RatioOfTheDecision	At this stage, one aspect needs to be mentioned.
RatioOfTheDecision	Social justice is concerned with the  distribution of benefits and burdens.
RatioOfTheDecision	The basis of distribution is the area of conflict between rights, needs and means.
RatioOfTheDecision	These three criteria can be put under two concepts of equality, namely, "formal equality" and "proportional equality".
RatioOfTheDecision	Formal equality means that law treats everyone equal.
RatioOfTheDecision	Concept of egalitarian equality is the concept of proportional equality and it expects the States to take affirmative action in favour of disadvantaged sections of society within the framework of democratic polity.
RatioOfTheDecision	In <NAME> all the judges except <NAME>, J. held that the "means test" should be adopted to exclude the creamy layer from the protected group earmarked for reservation.
RatioOfTheDecision	In <NAME> this <ORG> has, therefore, accepted caste as determinant of backwardness and yet it has struck a balance with the principle of secularism which is the basic feature of the Constitution by bringing in the concept of creamy layer.
RatioOfTheDecision	Views have often been expressed in this <ORG> that caste should not be the determinant of  backwardness and that the economic criteria alone should be the determinant of backwardness.
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, we are bound by the decision in Indra Sawhney5.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question as to the "determinant" of backwardness cannot be gone into by us in view of the binding decision.
RatioOfTheDecision	In addition to the above requirements this <ORG> in Indra Sawhney5 has evolved numerical benckmarks like ceiling-limit of 50% based on post-specific roster coupled with the concept of replacement to provide immunity against the charge of discrimination.
RatioOfTheDecision	CONCLUSION:  The impugned constitutional amendments by which Articles 16(4A) and 16(4B) have been inserted flow from Article 16(4).
RatioOfTheDecision	They do not alter the structure of Article 16(4).
RatioOfTheDecision	They retain the controlling factors or the compelling reasons, namely, backwardness and inadequacy of representation which enables the States to provide for reservation keeping in mind the overall efficiency of the State administration under Article 335.
RatioOfTheDecision	These impugned amendments are confined only to SCs and STs.
RatioOfTheDecision	Th ey do not obliterate any of the constitutional requirements, namely, ceiling-limit of 50% (quantitative limitation), the concept of creamy layer (qualitative exclusion), the sub-classification between OBC on one hand and SCs and STs on the other hand as held in <NAME> , the concept of post-based Roster with in-built concept of replacement as held in <NAME>.
RatioOfTheDecision	We reiterate that the ceiling-limit of 50%, the concept of creamy layer and the compelling reasons, namely, backwardness, inadequacy of representation and overall administrative efficiency are all constitutional requirements without which the structure of equality of opportunity in Article 16 would collapse.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, in this case, as stated, the main issue concerns the "extent of reservation".
RatioOfTheDecision	In this regard the concerned <ORG> will have to show in each case the existence of the compelling reasons, namely, backwardness, inadequacy of representation and overall administrative efficiency before making provision for reservation.
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated a bove, the impugned provision is an enabling provision.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> is not bound to make reservation for <ORG> in matter of promotions.
RatioOfTheDecision	However if they wish to exercise their discretion and make such provision, the <ORG> has to collect quantifiable data showing backwardness of the class and inadequacy of representation of that class in public employment in addition to compliance of Article 335.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is made clear that even if the <ORG> has compelling reasons, as stated above, the <ORG> will have to see that its reservation provision does not lead to excessiveness so as to breach the ceiling-limit of 50% or obliterate the creamy layer or extend the reservation indefinitely.
RatioOfTheDecision	Subject to above, we uphold the constitutional validity of the Constitution (Seventy-Seventh Amendment) Act, 1995, the Constitution (Eighty-First Amendment) Act, 2000, the Constitution (Eighty-Second Amendment) Act, 2000 and the Constitution (Eighty-Fifth Amendment) Act, 2001.
RulingByPresentCourt	We have not examined the validity of individual enactments of appro priate States and that question will be gone into in individual writ petition by the appropriate bench in accordance with law laid down by us in the present case.
