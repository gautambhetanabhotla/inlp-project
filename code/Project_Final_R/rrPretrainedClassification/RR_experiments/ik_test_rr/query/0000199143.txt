Fact	PETITIONER: <NAME> Vs. RESPONDENT: C C JACOB & <NAME>.
Fact	(WITH C.A NO.10909/96) DATE OF JUDGMENT: 18/03/1999 BENCH: <NAME>, <NAME> JUDGMENT:  <NAME>, J.  C.A.
Fact	Nos.10658-59/96 :  These appeals arise out of the order dated 22.9.1995 made by <ORG>, Ernakulam Bench, (for short `the tribunal') in O.A.
Fact	Nos.186/94, 961/95 and 1192/94.
Fact	The applicants therein questioned the selection made by <ORG> and consequential promotion of the appellant to the post of Superintendent of Customs in a vacancy reserved for Scheduled Castes.
RulingByLowerCourt	The contention before the tribunal was that the percentage of reservation has always to be worked out in relation to the number of posts which formed the cadre strength and the said reservation is not to be made with reference to a vacancy.
RulingByLowerCourt	Since the law in regard to the above stated position was nebulous, <ORG> of this Court in the case of <CITATION_1871744> .
RulingByLowerCourt	(1995 2 SCC 745) settled the said issue  holding that such reservation is in relation to the number of posts comprising in the cadre and not in relation to vacancies.
RulingByLowerCourt	The judgment of <ORG> was delivered on 10.2.1995.
RulingByLowerCourt	This <ORG> in the said judgment after taking into consideration the fact that the law was not clear till that date, observed thus : "We, however, direct that the interpretation given by us to the working of the roster and our findings on this point shall be operative prospectively."
Fact	The question that arises for our consideration in this case is: was it open to the tribunal to apply the law laid down in <NAME> case (supra) to the facts of the case in hand.
RulingByLowerCourt	The brief facts necessary for the purpose of deciding this question are that in June, 1993, <ORG> (for short `the <ORG>') considered the suitability of candidates eligible for promotion to four vacancies which arose during 1993-94 in the cadre of Superintendent of Customs (Preventive) from the post of Preventive Officers in which p roceedings of the <ORG> the appellant was chosen to be promoted against a reserved vacancy earmarked for the Scheduled Castes.
RulingByLowerCourt	The said decision of the <ORG> was challenged before the tribunal on 27.1.1994 wherein the applicants contended that they are entitled to be considered for promotion to the category of Superintendent of <ORG> in the concerned vacancy, treating these vacancies as unreserved.
RulingByLowerCourt	Consequently, they prayed that their case be considered for promotion on merits.
RulingByLowerCourt	During the pendency of the applications before the tribunal, the appellants herein came to be promoted on 26.6.1994 as against a reserved vacancy which arose on 1.6.1994.
RulingByLowerCourt	The tribunal on 22.9.95 following the judgment of this <ORG> in the <NAME>'s case (supra), allowed the applications and held that there had been an erroneous application of the principle of reservation resulting in appointment of Scheduled Caste candidates in excess of the quota earmarked for them.
RulingByLowerCourt	It directed the concerned respondents to recalculate the entitlements  of different categories and take further action applying the quota rule to the cadre and not to the vacancies as they arose.
RulingByLowerCourt	It further directed that till the quota is correctly maintained, no appointment will be made from the groups which have exceeded the quota reserved for them.
RulingByLowerCourt	As noted above, the finding of the tribunal is based on the ratio of the judgment laid down by this <ORG> in <NAME>'s case (supra).
RulingByLowerCourt	The contention of the appellant in these appeals is that the judgment in <NAME>'s case was made effective prospectively, hence, the same could not have been applied to the promotion of the appellant.
RulingByLowerCourt	This contention was negatived by the tribunal on the ground that the decision of the <ORG> in selecting the appellant does not amount to an appointment and in view of the fact that the appellant's promotion was made subsequent to filing of the petition, the appellant cannot claim the benefit of the prospectivity given to the <NAME>'s judgment by this <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	We are unable to agree with this view of  the tribunal.
RulingByLowerCourt	It is to be noted that the prospectivity given to <NAME>'s case was obviously on the ground that there was a doubt in regard to the position of law until the same was clarified by this <ORG> in <NAME>'s case.
Fact	The decision of the <ORG> was taken in June, 1993; much prior to the judgment in <NAME>'s case.
Fact	It is only pursuant to the decision of the <ORG>, the appellant came to be promoted on 27.6.1994 which is also a date prior to the delivery of the judgment in <NAME>'s case.
Fact	In our opinion, the prospectivity was given to <NAME>'s case only to see that status prevailing prior to the judgment in <NAME>'s case should not be disturbed.
Fact	The prospective declaration of law is a devise innovated by the apex court to avoid reopening of settled issues and to prevent multiplicity of proceedings.
Fact	It is also a devise adopted to avoid uncertainty and avoidable litigation.
Fact	By the very object of prospective declaration of law, it is deemed that all actions taken contrary to the declaration of law p rior to its date of declaration are validated.
Fact	This is done in the larger public interest.
RulingByLowerCourt	Therefore, the subordinate forums which are legally bound to apply the declaration of law made by this <ORG> are also duty-bound to apply such dictum to cases which would arise in future only.
RulingByLowerCourt	In matters where decisions opposed to the said principle have been taken prior to such declaration of law cannot be interfered with on the basis of such declaration of law.
RulingByLowerCourt	In the instant case, both decisions of the <ORG> as well as the appointing authority being prior to the judgment in <NAME>'s case, we are of the opinion that the tribunal was in error in applying this decision.
RulingByLowerCourt	For this reason, these appeals succeed and are hereby allowed; setting aside the orders and directions made by the tribunal in OA Nos.186/94, and 961/95.
