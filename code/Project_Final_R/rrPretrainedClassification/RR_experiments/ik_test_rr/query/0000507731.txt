RulingByLowerCourt	The fact that there exists a bipartite settlement entered into by and between the <ORG>s and their workmen is not in dispute.
RulingByLowerCourt	The workman was all along aware about the said legal position inasmuch as, at all stages, viz., issuance of notices and memorandums, passing of the order of termination, the said settlement had been referred to.
RulingByLowerCourt	What fell for consideration before <ORG> was the interpretation and/or applicability of the said settlement.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG> committed an error of record insofar as it proceeded on the basis that the said settlement had not been proved.
RatioOfTheDecision	The settlement being an admitted d ocument should have been considered in its proper perspective by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause (2) of the said settlement is a complete code by itself.
RatioOfTheDecision	It lays down a complete machinery as to how and in what manner the employer can arrive at a satisfaction that the workman has no intention to join his duties.
RatioOfTheDecision	A bare perusal of the said settlement clearly shows that it is for the employee concerned to submit a proper application for leave.
RulingByLowerCourt	It is not in dispute that after the period of leave came to an end in June 1983, the workman did not report back for duties.
RulingByLowerCourt	He also did not submit any application for grant of further leave on medical ground or otherwise.
RulingByLowerCourt	It is in that situation the memorandum dated 2.11.1983 was issued and he was asked to joint his duties.
RulingByLowerCourt	It is furthermore not in dispute that despite receipt of the said memorandum, the workman did not join duties pursuant whereto he was served with a notice to show cause dated 31.12.1982.
RulingByLowerCourt	He was required to resume his duties by 15.1.1984.
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG> recei ved a telegram on 17.1.1984 and only about a month thereafter he filed an application for grant of leave on medical ground.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not the case of the workman that any leave on medical ground or otherwise was due to him.
RatioOfTheDecision	Opportunities after opportunities indisputably had been granted to the workman to explain his position but he chose not to do so except filing applications for grant of medical leave and that too without annexing proper medical certificates.
RatioOfTheDecision	The bipartite settlement is clear and unambiguous.
RatioOfTheDecision	It should be given a literal meaning.
RatioOfTheDecision	A bare perusal of the said settlement would show that on receipt of a notice contemplated thereunder, the workman must either : (1) report for duties within thirty days; (2) give his explanation for his absence satisfying the management that he has not taken any employment or avocation; and (3) show that he has no intention of not joining the duties.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is, thus, only when the workman concerned does not join his duties within thirty days or fails to file a satisfactor y explanation, as referred to hereinbefore, the legal fiction shall come into force.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the instant case except for asking for grant of medical leave, he did not submit any explanation for his absence satisfying the management that he has not taken up any other employment or avocation and that he has no intention of not joining his duties.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the aforementioned fact situation we do not see any reason as to why the <ORG> could not arrive at a satisfaction that the workman had no intention to join his duties.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is interesting to note that though the said order was passed on 17.5.1984, a representation to the <ORG> was made by the workman to reconsider the said decision after a period of 3 years and 2 months by a letter dated 31.7.1987 Yet again a dispute was sought to be raised by issuance of a legal notice on the <ORG> only on 6.4.1989.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mere sending of an application for grant of leave much after the period of leave was over as also the date of resuming duties cannot be said to be a bona fide act on the part o f the workman.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG>, as noticed hereinbefore, in response to the lawyer's notice categorically stated that the workman had been carrying on some business elsewhere.
RatioOfTheDecision	We cannot accept the submission of Mr. <NAME> that only because on a later date an application for grant of medical leave was filed, the same ipso facto would put an embargo on the exercise of the jurisdiction of the <ORG> from invoking clause (2 ) of the bipartite settlement.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may be true that in a case of this nature, the principles of natural justice were required to be complied with but the same would not mean that a full-fledged departmental proceeding was required to be initiated.
RatioOfTheDecision	A limited enquiry as to whether the employee concerned had sufficient explanation for not reporting to duties after the period of leave had expired or failure on his part on being asked so to do, in our considered view, amounts to sufficient compliance of the requirements of the principles of natural justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause (2) of the bipartite settlement raises a  legal fiction, which is of wide import.
RatioOfTheDecision	Once the action on the part of the employer is found to be fair, the court in view of such legal fiction would call upon the workman to prove contra.

RatioOfTheDecision	The notice dated 31.12.1983 refers to the said settlement by necessary implication, as on the failure of the workman to resume his duties by 15.1.1984, it was stated that he would be deemed to have been discharged from the services of the <ORG>.

RatioOfTheDecision	It was in that situation, the order dated 17.5.1984 was issued which again referred to the provisions contained in memorandum of settlement dated 8.9.1983.
RatioOfTheDecision	The workman ex facie appears to have accepted the said order as for a long period he maintained silence.
RatioOfTheDecision	Had he been interested in the <ORG>'s services, it was expected of him to resume his duties and/or file proper application for grant of medical leave with a valid medical certificate.
RatioOfTheDecision	The principle of natural justice, it is trite, is no unruly horse.
RatioOfTheDecision	When facts are admitted, an enquiry would be an empty formality.
