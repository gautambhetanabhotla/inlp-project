Fact	It is an admitted fact that the petitioners stood as sureties for releasing the accused in C.C.133/2007 on the file of <ORG>, Palakkad, and since he did not appear, his bail bond was cancelled and proceedings under Section 446(1) of the Code of Criminal Procedure was initiated against them and as per Annexure-1 order, the learned magistrate had Crl.M.C.No.3586/ 2014 imposed the entire bond amount of 25,000/- each as penalty.
Fact	It is also seen from the order that, they did not appear in spite of notice issued.
Fact	The petitioners challenged the same by filing Crl.Appeal No.189/2014 along with Crl.M.P.1600/2014 to condone the delay of 399 days in filing the appeal before <ORG>, Palakkad.
Fact	It is averred in the petition that, the appeal was filed on 26.05.2014, but without passing any orders in the delay petition, the case was adjourned to 29.07.2014.
Fact	In t he meantime, revenue recovery proceedings have been initiated against the petitioners for realisation of the amount.
Argument	It may be mentioned here that, when an appeal is filed with delay condonation application, where state alone is a party in a criminal proceedings, the court should have considered that application and disposed of as expeditiously as possible as without condoning the delay, no effective orders can be passed in the appeal.
Argument	In this regard, an oral report was called from the concerned court by telephonic Crl.M.C.No.3586/ 2014 message, and it is revealed that the application filed with delay condonation application on 26.05.2014 and it is posted to 29.07.2014.
Argument	So pendency of the delay condonation application to a longer date, when <ORG> alone is the party is not a good practice.
Argument	However, considering the fact that, without condoning the delay, no order of stay could be obtained by the petitioner, this court feels that, as a matter of interim arrangement, they can be directed to deposit some amount an d that can be taken for the purpose of staying the proceedings for a time being, till the appeal is disposed of by <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	So the petition is disposed of as follows:  If the petitioners deposits 4,000/- each before <ORG>, Palakkad, towards penalty amount imposed, then the execution proceedings initiated for recovery of the amount is directed to be kept in abeyance, till the appeal is disposed of by the Crl.M.C.No.3586/ 2014 Sessions Judge.
RulingByLowerCourt	The Sessions Judge is directed to expedite disposal of the delay petition and also the appeal filed by the petitioner, against the proceedings initiated under Section 446(1) of the Code of Criminal Procedure, immediately.
RulingByLowerCourt	With the above direction and observation, the criminal miscellaneous case is disposed of.
