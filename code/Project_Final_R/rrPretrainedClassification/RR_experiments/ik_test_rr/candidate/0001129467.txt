RatioOfTheDecision	While we agree fully with the reasoning and conclusion of <ORG>, we think it necessary at the same time to emphasise a few aspects relevant in the case of such contracts.
Fact	First, the relevant facts briefly.
Fact	For the excise year 1994-95, the appellant was the highest bidder for certain number of liquor shops.
Fact	His bid in a sum of Rs.11,67,00,000/- was accepted, payable in monthly instalments of Rs.97,25,000/- each.
Fact	After  making the necessary deposits and after complying with other conditions, licences were granted to him and he commenced the business with effect from 1.4.1995.
Fact	He failed to pay the monthly rental (licence fee) for the months of July and August, 1994.
Fact	Thereupon a notice dated 9.8.94 (exhibit R-3) was issued by the authorities intimating him that he was in arrears of the licence fee for the months of July and August and that if he does not pay the same, the shops would be reauctioned.
Fact	The appellant was asked to be present personally on 23.8.1994 for showing cause.
Fact	Though the appellant received the said notice, he did not appear before the specified authority.
Fact	He thus filed to avail of the opportunity of personal hearing afforded to him.
Fact	(Though the appellant disputed this fact, <ORG> has accepted the respondent's contention and has found as a fact that the appellant did fail to appear on the date of hearing.
Fact	We accept the said finding.)
Fact	On 2.9.94, a notification was issued - communicated to a large  number of excise officers and other departments and a copy of which was also sent to the appellant stating that if the appellant did not clear the arrears on or before 12.9.94, the shops would be sold by reauction to be held at 2.00 p.m. on 12.9.94.
Fact	The said notification is marked exhibit R-12.
Fact	It was also published in the daily newspaper, <ORG>, The notification mentioned the precise amount of arrears due from the appellant and stated that on the reauction being held, the existing licence in favour of the appellant shall stand cancelled.
Fact	Since the appellant failed to pay, reauction was held on 12.9.94, whereafter, a demand was made upon the appellant for loss of revenue resulting from reauction, as provided by sub-section (4)(b) of Section 31 of the Madhya Pradesh Excise Act.
Fact	We may set out sub-section (4) of Section 31 this stage:  "(4) Where a licence is cancelled or suspended under Clause (a), clause (b), clause (c) or clause  (e) of sub-section (1),  (a) the fee payable for the balance of the  period for which such licence would have been current but for such cancellation or suspension, may be recovered from the ex- licensee as excise-revenue;  (b) the Collector may take the grant under management or resell it at the risk and loss of the ex-licensee, but any profit realised by such management or resale which is not in excess of the amount recovered under clause (a) for such period shall be paid to the ex-licensee."
Fact	The appellant questioned the said demand by way of a writ petition in <ORG>.
Argument	He submitted that he was not given due opportunity before cancelling the licence as required by sub-section (1-A) of Section 31, that there was no order of cancellation of licence as such and that there was no proper publicity for the reauction conducted.
Argument	He submitted that on account of lack of due publicity, only one bidder was present at the reauction and that the shops were sold at a low price.
Argument	The learned Single Judge was impressed by the said contentions and allowed the writ petiti on.
Argument	On appeal, however, <ORG> rejected all of them.
RatioOfTheDecision	Before we set out the reasoning of <ORG>, it would be appropriate to set out sub- section (1-A) of Section 31.
Statute	It reads:  "(1-A) Before making an order cancelling ok suspending a licence, permit or pass under sub-section (1), the authority aforesaid shall record in writing the reasons for the proposed action, furnish to the holder thereof a brief statement of the same and afford him a reasonable opportunity of being heard."
Statute	<ORG> held that as held by that <ORG> in an earlier decision, "the court cannot take a hypertechnical view of the provisions and must understand the same in a reasonable manner.
RatioOfTheDecision	Substantial compliance with the requirements of the provisions would meet the ends of justice.
Fact	Where a specific order of cancellation of licence is not passed but if the requirements are substantially complied with, the court under Article 226 of the Constitution would not ordinarily interfere."
Fact	<ORG> referred t o the notice exhibit R-3 and found as a fact that it was received by the appellant.
Fact	The <ORG> also found that the notification (R-12) was duly communicated to all the Excise Officers, other departments, and to the appellant besides being published in the newspaper, <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	After referring to the contents of exhibit R-12, the <ORG> held that the said notification was both an order of cancellation of the appellant's licence as well as notice of reauction.
RulingByLowerCourt	It also found that there was adequate publicity of the said notification and the fact that only one bidder appeared at the reauction was no ground for holding that there was no proper publicity.
RulingByLowerCourt	It gave reasons why bidder generally do not attend reauctions.
RulingByLowerCourt	The Division <ORG> also found as a fact that the appellant failed to appear on the date of hearing specified in notice (R-3).
RulingByLowerCourt	In the light of the said findings, the Division <ORG> held, and in our opinion rightly, that the learned Single Judge way in error in allowing the writ petition.

RulingByLowerCourt	At the same time, it was observed that the licences are not precluded from seeking to enforce the statutory provisions governing the contract.
RatioOfTheDecision	lt must, however, be remembered that we are dealing with parties to a contract, which is a business transaction, no doubt governed by statutory provisions.
RatioOfTheDecision	While examining complaints of violation of statutory rules and conditions, it must be remembered that violation of each and every provision does not furnish a ground or the <ORG> to interfere.
RatioOfTheDecision	The provision may be a directory one or a mandatory one.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the case of directory provisions, substantial compliance would be enough.
RatioOfTheDecision	Unless it is establish ed that violation of a directory provision has resulted in loss and/or prejudice to the party, no interference is warranted.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even in the case of violation of a mandatory provision, interference does not fellow as a matter of course.
RatioOfTheDecision	A mandatory provision conceived in the interest of a party can be waived by that party, whereas a mandatory provision conceived in the interest of public cannot be waived by him.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other wards, wherever a complaint of violation of a mandatory provision is made, the <ORG> should enquire- in whose interest is the provision conceived.
RatioOfTheDecision	If it is not conceived in the interest of public, question of waiver and/or acquiescence may arise - subject, of course, to the pleadings of the parties.

RatioOfTheDecision	There is yet another fact.
RatioOfTheDecision	The contract provides for payment of monthly rental on or before a, particular date.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the amount of monthly rental not paid before the due date, the licence is liable to be cancelled as provided by sub-section (1) of Section 31.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that before cancelling the licence, an opportunity of hearing should be given as prov ided by sub-section (1-A).
RatioOfTheDecision	While the opportunity to be given should be reasonable, the reasonableness or otherwise of the opportunity given must be judged keeping in view the time-frame available.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is a case of a contract stipulating monthly payments.
RatioOfTheDecision	If there is a default in paying a month's rental, notice proposing cancellation may follow.
RatioOfTheDecision	The time given to the licencee to show cause would naturally be a short one for the reason that soon thereafter the next month's rental (licence fee) falls due and if that is not paid, another show cause notice may have to follow.
RatioOfTheDecision	(It must be remembered that in this case, the default was for two consecutive months, July and August.
RatioOfTheDecision	The authorities evidently did not act in haste.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even after one month's default, they waited hoping that he should pay.
RatioOfTheDecision	But when he defaulted for the next month also, they issued the notice proposing cancellation.)
RatioOfTheDecision	What we wish to emphasise is that the opportunity contemplated by sub- section (1-A) cannot be operated in a leisurely manner.
RatioOfTheDecision	A r ealistic view has to be taken while determining whether the opportunity given was reasonable or not.
RatioOfTheDecision	The object of all excise laws is two-fold viz., to raise revenue and to regulate the trade in liquors which is a noxious substance.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is no fundamental right to trade in liquor ( <CITATION_675008> 1995 (1) S.C.C.
RatioOfTheDecision	574).
RatioOfTheDecision	The only right of the licencee is to seek to enforce the terms of contract (which is statutory in nature) and the statutory provisions governing the contract.
RatioOfTheDecision	The considerations aforementioned should be kept in mind while examining complaints of violation of statutory Rules, conditions add terms of contract as well as complaints of jack of reasonable opportunity.
RatioOfTheDecision	Lastly, it is urged the before forfeiting the advance amount or the security deposit, a fresh opportunity of hearing ought to have been provided.
RatioOfTheDecision	From a perusal of the judgment under appeal, however, we do not find that any such contention was urged before it.
RulingByPresentCourt	For this reason, we decline to entertain this plea, which may involve i nvestigation of factual aspects.
