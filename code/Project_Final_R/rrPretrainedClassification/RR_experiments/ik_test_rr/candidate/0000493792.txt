RatioOfTheDecision	The scheme may be summarized thus.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Act applies to all establishments from the accounting year commencing on any day in the year 1964 and in respect of any subsequent year.
RatioOfTheDecision	Establishments having no prior history of bonus payment are governed by the provisions of the Act without the modifications contained in ss.
RatioOfTheDecision	33 and 34 .
RatioOfTheDecision	In respect of establishments with a prior history we ha ve two classes: establishments in which a dispute was pending on May 29, 1965 in respect of an accounting year not earlier than the accounting year ending in the year 1962 and those in which no such dispute was pending.

Statute	If there was a dispute pending in respect of an accounting year not earlier than an accounting year ending in the year 1962, the dispute is to be resolved as laid down in the Act with the special modifications made by s. 34 notwithstanding that there was no dispute in subsequent years and the bonus for the subsequent years is also to be calculated in accordance with the Act so modified.

RatioOfTheDecision	For this purpose the ratio between the bonus and the gross profits in the base year determines the proportion of allocable surplus must have to the gross profits of the account year.
RatioOfTheDecision	Gross profits are defined to mean gross profits reduced by direct taxes only.
RatioOfTheDecision	The payment is, however, subject to the maximum limit and the principle of set on.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this way the level of payment of bonus is maintained to what had been paid in the past as a result of agreement or award.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question is whether this classification is so arbitrary and creates such differen ces that it cannot be reasonably related to the object which the Bonus Act intends to achieve, namely, the settlement of all bonus disputes in future and to lay down a uniform formula which is considered reasonable both for the workmen as well as the employers so long as the Act remains in force.

RatioOfTheDecision	These sections are criticised on many grounds.
RatioOfTheDecision	Firstly, it is said that the Act creates inequality inasmuch as the formula under the Act is made applicable to cases pending for the application of the Full Bench Formula in respect of accounting years from 1962 onwards but leaves the establishments in which there was no dispute to be governed by the Full Bench Formula.
RatioOfTheDecision	This, it is submitted, is onerous to the establishments in which a dispute was pending.
RatioOfTheDecision	The onerous nature, it is submitted, arises from the fact that payment of minimum bonus even if there is a loss is compulsory, new categories of workmen have become entitled to bonus, "salary or wage" is made eq ual to wages plus dearness allowance and the employers lose the advantage of deductions on account of rehabilitation.
RatioOfTheDecision	A further criticism is that not only the year of dispute but all intervening years are brought under the Act even though there may be no dispute in those years.
RatioOfTheDecision	The object of the Bonus Act is to introduce a new uniform formula for calculation of bonus with limits of maximum and minimum and a principle of set on and set off to smoothen inequalities of payment over a number of years.
RatioOfTheDecision	One difficulty in the way of uniform law was the pendency of disputes at the time the Ordinance was promulgated.
RatioOfTheDecision	This would, of course, be the case whenever any law was introduced if a dispute was pending in respect of a prior year.
RatioOfTheDecision	There were two alternatives open.
RatioOfTheDecision	One was to leave the disputes to be decided by the <ORG> under the Full Bench Formula and the other was to apply the Act to the pending cases so that all decisions would be uniform and almost mechanical.
RatioOfTheDecision	If pending cases were to be treated as a clas s, special provision was required to deal with them.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Act chose to do away with the Fun Bench Formula from 1962.
RatioOfTheDecision	If it had been applied and no dispute was pending at all the matter was left there.
RatioOfTheDecision	For other cases there was a clear need for classification and classification was thus resorted to.
RatioOfTheDecision	Pending cases were brought under the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Act , of course, could not be applied without suitable modi- fications to remove hardships.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 34 , therefore, provided that the Act would apply to all cases as modified in the second subsection of s. 34 .
RatioOfTheDecision	That sub-section applied only to establishments in which there was a prior history of bonus payment and attempted to harmonize the application of the law to establishments in which disputes were pending and those in which there was no dispute.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are thus required to see the provisions of that sub-section before we can deal with the criticism against s. 33 .
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 34 deals with two matters.
RulingByPresentCourt	It deals with establishments in which a dispute, as laid down in s. 33  , was pending and also with old establishments in which there was payment of bonus in the past but no dispute was pending when the Ordinance was pro-  63  mulgated.
RulingByPresentCourt	It applies the Act to both sets of cases.
RulingByPresentCourt	It lays down a simple condition that the total bonus for any accounting year should correspond to the level of total bonus paid in a base year and for this purpose the allocable surplus in an accounting year dealt with under the Act must bear the same proportion to gross profits as the total bonus paid in the base year did to the gross profits of the base year, subject however to the maximum limit and the principle of set on.
RatioOfTheDecision	The base year was so defined that it would be a year in which there would be no dispute.
RatioOfTheDecision	In those cases in which a dispute was pending on May 29,1965 it meant an accounting year immediately preceding the year of dispute and in other cases a period of 12 months immediately preceding the period of accounting year in respect of which this Act became applicable.
RatioOfTheDecision	Gross profits were differe ntly defined for the purpose of the application of the subsection and meant gross profits as reduced by direct taxes payable in the year.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is obvious that this definition was evolved to avoid a clash between the Full Bench Formula and the formula under the Act.
RulingByLowerCourt	The provisions of s. 34(2) were specially enacted so that there might not be divergence in the payment of bonus over a number of years and to maintain the level of payment, as had existed in the past.
RulingByLowerCourt	In this way, three classes of cases were contemplated and we shall describe them more fully now.
RulingByLowerCourt	In the first class were put all establishments which.
RulingByLowerCourt	had no history of bonus payment.

RatioOfTheDecision	14 as we have already indicated above.

RatioOfTheDecision	No note was taken of cases pending before <ORG> and <ORG> because the jurisdiction of <ORG> and <ORG> is either supervisory or appellate and the intention was to cover cases in which no decisions of the authorities appointed under the law relating to industrial disputes was yet made.
RatioOfTheDecision	Disputes prior to 1962 were not taken note of because a date line had to be fixed and 1962 was the rational date to fix because <ORG> began its deliberations in that year.

RatioOfTheDecision	In several statutes a date is generally selected to demarcate pending cases and the selection of the date has never been challenged successfully if there is some rational ground for its selection.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the resolution of the dispute by the instrumentalit y of the Act was contemplated, the Act had also to say which dispute would be so resolved and the only rational date to select was the date on which the Ordinance was promulgated.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus the pendency of disputes with reference to the Ordinance and reopening of accounting years up to the year in which <ORG> began its deliberation was logical and not arbitrary.
RatioOfTheDecision	The provision with regard to the reopening of the intervening accounts year for refixation of bonus was also logical.

RatioOfTheDecision	The process of the Act is an integrated one and by the principle of set on and set off four accounting years are involved to avoid extraordinary results.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is said that two establishments equally situated are likely to be differently treated depending on the fortuitous circumstance of the existence of a dispute but is not this assumption  an imaginary one?
RatioOfTheDecision	the fact that in one there is a dispute and in the other there is not, clearly distinguishes the two establishments.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have explained in connection with s. 10 why we do not consider such comparison of any value and the same reasoning applies here.
RatioOfTheDecision	The distinguishing feature of the pendency of the dispute on the date of the promulgation of the Ordinance clearly demarcates a distinct class of cases and the classification made by the Act is a rational one., No doubt the liability for bonus under the Act may be more in some cases but it is likely to be less in others.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Act does not make any difference in treatment within the class it deals with.
RatioOfTheDecision	All establishments in which disputes were pending are treated alike.
RatioOfTheDecision	They are brought under the Act in the same manner without any discrimination.
RatioOfTheDecision	If they represent a class, the whole of the class is treated in the same way.

RulingByPresentCourt	Section 34(2) which is next criticised because it sacrifices all principles which this <ORG> had established in the past and fixes a ratio for all time to come is also not invalid.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Act was passed to make for greater certainty, for improving relations between the employers and the workmen and for the avoidance of disputes.
