Fact	1211 OF 2005 B --------------------------------------------------- Dated this the 12th June, 2009 JUDGMENT  <NAME>, J.    Appellant is the claimant in a petition filed under Section 166 of the Motor Vehicles Act.
Fact	The tribunal has awarded a total sum of Rs.2,75,000/=.
Fact	The Appeal is filed complaining of inadequacy of the compensation.
Fact	We heard the learned counsel for the appellant and also the learned counsel appearing on behalf of respondents 3 and 4.
Fact	2.
Fact	Learned counsel for the appellan t raised two contentions before us.
Fact	In the first place, it is pointed out that the Certificate issued by the Medical Practitioner would show that the appellant has suffered permanent disability of 35 per cent and against the same, 25 per cent alone is taken.
Fact	It is further contended that the multiplier is taken as 15, but actually, having regard to his age which was 23 years at the time of accident, the multiplier should have been 17.
Fact	The third contention is that there is shortening of the right leg by two inches and that he suffered disfiguration and no amount was granted towards the same.
Fact	Learned counsel for the third respondent supports the Award.
RulingByLowerCourt	3.
RulingByLowerCourt	As far as the question of income is concerned, admittedly there is oral evidence of PW1.
RulingByLowerCourt	We note that the accident was in the year 1996.
RulingByLowerCourt	Appellant does not have a case that he was working on monthly salary under any particular employer.
RulingByLowerCourt	Therefore, we would not think that any further increase is warranted.
RulingByLowerCourt	4.
RulingByLowerCourt	However, we feel that the appellant is entitled to  higher compensation on two grounds which we consider together.
RulingByLowerCourt	Appellant has been granted a total sum of Rs.67,500/= towards loss of earning power.
RulingByLowerCourt	In arriving at the same, the tribunal has taken the multiplier of 15.
RulingByLowerCourt	Appellant has produced Ext.A10, which is an attested copy of the licence.
RulingByLowerCourt	From the same, it is discernible that the age of the appellant was 23 years.
RulingByLowerCourt	This means that the applicable multiplier should have been taken as  17.
RulingByLowerCourt	Further, we notice that there is some substance in the contention that the per centage of disability should not have been reduced from 35 to 25.
RulingByLowerCourt	But, we cannot also overlook the fact that the appellant has not examined the Medical Practitioner.
RulingByLowerCourt	We also cannot overlook the reasoning of the tribunal that there was no treatment after 2001, when the disability was assessed and there is some improvement in the condition of the right leg.
RulingByLowerCourt	But, at the same time, we also cannot lose sight of the fact that the appellant was a driver.
RulingByLowerCourt	Having regard to the profession as a driver, the sh ortening of the right leg would assume greater significance.
RulingByLowerCourt	Having regard to the totality of the facts, we would think that the per centage of disability should be taken as 27 per cent instead of 25.
RulingByLowerCourt	On the basis of these changes in the factors, we arrive at a sum of Rs.82,620/=.
RulingByLowerCourt	When deducting the amount already granted, the appellant would be entitled to an enhancement of Rs.15,120/= towards loss of earning power which is rounded off to Rs.15,000/=.
RulingByLowerCourt	We further notice that the appellant who was 23 years of age at the time of accident did suffer disfiguration as a result of the shortening of the right leg by two inches and we feel that the appellant should be granted a sum of Rs.5,000/= more towards disfiguration.
RulingByLowerCourt	But, we notice that the tribunal has wrongly awarded a sum of Rs.5,000/= towards disability.
RulingByLowerCourt	Having regard to the law laid down by this Court, a further sum may not be warranted.
RulingByLowerCourt	The upshot of this discussion is that we find that the appellant is entitled to a sum of Rs.15,000/= more with 7.5 per c ent interest from the date of the petition till the date of realisation as against the third respondent.
RulingByLowerCourt	Accordingly, the Appeal is partly allowed and the appellant is allowed to realise a sum of Rs.15,000/= with 7.5 per cent interest more from the third respondent from the date of the petition till the date of realisation.
