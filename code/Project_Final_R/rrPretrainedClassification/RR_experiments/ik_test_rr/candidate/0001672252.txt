Statute	Power to recover damages:-Where an employer makes defaults in the payment of any contribution to the Fund (the Family Fund or the Insurance Fund) or in the transfer of accumulations required to be transferred by him under sub-section (2) of Section 15 (or sub- section (5) of Section 17 ) or in the payment of any charges payable under any other provision of thi s Act or of (any scheme or Insurance Scheme) or under any of the conditions specified under Section 17 , (the Central Provident Fund Commissioner, or such other officer as may be authorised by the Central Government, by notification in the Official Gazette in this behalf) may recover from the employer such damages, not exceeding the amount of arrear, as it may think fit to impose.
RatioOfTheDecision	Provided that before levying and recovering such damages, the employer shall be given a reasonable opportunity of being heard."
RatioOfTheDecision	The contention that section 14B confers unguided and uncontrolled discretion upon the Regional Provident Fund Commissioner to impose such damages 'as he may think fit' is, therefore, violative of Article 14 of the Constitution, cannot be accepted.
RatioOfTheDecision	Nor can it be accepted that there are no guide-lines provided for fixing the quantum of damages.
RatioOfTheDecision	The power of the Regional Provident Fund Commissioner to impose damages under s. 14B is a quasi-judicial function.
RatioOfTheDecision	It must be exercised after notice to the defaulter  and after giving him a reasonable opportunity of being heard.
RatioOfTheDecision	The discretion to award damages could be exercised within the limits fixed by the Statute.
RatioOfTheDecision	Having regard to the punitive nature of the power exercisable under s. 14B  84 and the consequences that ensue therefrom, an order under s. 14B must be a 'speaking order' containing the reasons in support of it.
RatioOfTheDecision	The guide-lines are provided in the Act and its various provisions, particularly in the word 'damages' the liability for which under s. 14B arises on the 'making of default'.
RatioOfTheDecision	While fixing the amount of damages, the Regional Provident Fund Commissioner usually takes into consideration, as he has done here, various factors viz.
RatioOfTheDecision	the number of defaults, the period of delay, the frequency of defaults and the amounts involved.
RatioOfTheDecision	The word 'damages' in s. 14B lays down sufficient guidelines for him to levy damages.
RatioOfTheDecision	Learned counsel for the petitioners, however, contends that in the instant case, the period of arrears varies from less than one month to more tha n 12 months and, therefore, the imposition of damages at the flat rate of hundred per cent for all the defaults irrespective of their duration, is not only capricious but arbitrary.
RatioOfTheDecision	The submission is that if the intention of the legislature was to make good the loss caused by default of an employer, there could be no rational basis to quantify the damages at hundred per cent in case of default for a period less than one month and those for a period more than 12 months.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is urged that the fixation of upper limit at hundred per cent is no guide-line.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the object of the Legislation is to be achieved, the guide-lines must specify a uniform method to quantify damages after considering all essentials like loss or injury sustained, the circumstances under which the default occurred, negligence, if any, etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is said that the damages under s. 14B which is the pecuniary reparation due must be correlated to all these factors.
RatioOfTheDecision	In support of his contention, he drew our attention to s. 10F of the Coal Mines Providen t Fund and Bonus Schemes Act, 1958, which uses the words 'damages not exceeding twenty-five per cent' like section 14B of the Act, and also to a tabular chart provided under that Act itself showing that the amount of damages was correlated to the period of arrears.
RatioOfTheDecision	We regret, we cannot appreciate this line of reasoning.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 10F of the Act of 1958 came up for consideration before this Court in <CITATION_1361667> .
RatioOfTheDecision	(1) This Court observed, firstly, that the determination of damages is not 'an in flexible application of a rigid formula', and secondly, the words 'as it may think fit to impose' show that the authority is required to apply its mind to the facts and circumstances of the case.
RatioOfTheDecision	The contention that in the absence of any guide-lines for the quantification of damages, s. 14B is violative of Article 14 of the Constitution, must, therefore, fail.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this connection, it was also urged that the absence of any provision for appeal, leaves the defaulting employer with no remedy.
RatioOfTheDecision	The conferral of arbitrary  and uncontrolled powers on the Regional Provident Fund Commissioner to quantify damages, it is said, without a corresponding right of appeal or revision, makes the provision contained in s. 14B per se void and illegal and it is liable to be struck down on that ground.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are afraid, the contention is wholly devoid of substance.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mere absence of provision for an appeal does not imply that the Regional Provident Fund Commissioner is invested with arbitrary or uncontrolled power, without any guide-lines.
RatioOfTheDecision	The conferral of power to award damages under s. 14B is to ensure the success of the measure.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is dependent on existence of certain facts, there has to be an objective determination, not subjective.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Regional Provident Fund Commissioner has not only to apply his mind to the requirements of s. 14B but is cast with the duty of making a "speaking order", after conforming to the rules of natural justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	This <ORG> has repeatedly laid it down that where the discretion to apply the provisions of a particular sta tute is left with the <ORG> or one of the highest officers, it will be presumed that the discretion vested in such high authority will not be abused.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> or such authority is in a position to have all the relevant and necessary information in relation to each kind of establishment, the nature of defaults made by the employer, and the necessity to decide whether the damages to be imposed should be exemplary or not: <CITATION_171861> .
RatioOfTheDecision	(1) It was stated in <CITATION_473037> (2) that when power as to be exercised by one of the highest officers, the fact that no appeal has been provided for 'is a matter of no moment'.
RatioOfTheDecision	The same view was reiterated in <CITATION_179636> .
RatioOfTheDecision	(3) There is always a presumption that public officials would discharge their duties honestly and in accordance with the rules of law.
RatioOfTheDecision	This was emphasised in <CITATION_1677837> ,(4) stress being laid on the power being vested not in any minor official but in top-ranking authority.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the circumstances, the absence of a provision for a ppeal or revision can be of no consequence.
Argument	Turning now to the main question, the contention is that s. 14B of the Act does not authorise levy of any penal damages, i.e., a penalty or fine but deals with the power to recover damages.
Argument	It is not the power to impose a penalty on the defaulting employer though the maximum amount of damages that can be recovered has been indicated in the section, it is submitted that the damages must have some correlation with the loss suffered as a result of delayed payments and the authority imposing damages must apply its mind to this aspect of the matter.
Argument	The defaulter under s. 14B is, therefore, liable to pay damages which represents the actual loss, but not anything more, as such recovery would amount to penalty and that is not permitted under the section.
Argument	In support of his submissions, he has referred to certain authorities.
Argument	It is argued that the damages referred to in s. 14B is different from penalty or fine and is intended to compensate the loss to the beneficiaries of  the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It has only the ordinary legal meaning of the term 'damages' viz.
RatioOfTheDecision	actual loss as in law of Contract or Tort.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus the award of damages under s. 14B must be, in essence, the pecuniary reparation for loss or injury sustained by one person through the fault or negligence of another.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is a conflict of opinion between different <ORG> as to the meaning of the word 'damages' in s. 14B of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to some of the <ORG>, the word 'damages' in s. 14B means actual loss to the beneficiaries.
RatioOfTheDecision	The view is that s. 14B clearly indicates that an employer is liable to pay damages, if he has made defaults in payment of the contribution.
RatioOfTheDecision	Any delay in paying the amount under s. 6 causes loss to the beneficiaries of the <ORG>, such as loss of the interest and the like.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is the loss that is sought to be recovered from the defaulting employer for the purpose of indemnifying the beneficiaries of the <ORG>, namely, the employees to the extent of the loss suffered by them.
RatioOfTheDecision	The defaulter u/s  14B is, therefore, liable to pay damages which represent the loss, but not anything more, as such recovery would amount to penalty, and that is not permitted under the section.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is, therefore, held by <ORG> that the damages to be imposed u/s 14B should have correlation with the loss suffered and that damages u/s 14B are intended to compensate the loss to the beneficiaries of the Scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	With respect, <ORG> have obviously fallen into an error in reading the word 'damages' in s. 14B in isolation, by trying to construe the word in a purely legalistic sense.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> have overlooked that we are not concerned in interpreting what damages means in the realm of Contract or Tort but the word had to be given its true meaning, in consonance with the objects and purpose of the Legislation.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned Additional Solicitor General brought to our notice the conflict of opinion between the different <ORG> on the construction of the word 'damages' used in s. 14B , and submitted that  this has given rise to confusion in the mind of those charged with the duty of administering the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	He wants that the conflict should be resolved by placing a proper construction on the word 'damages' in s. 14B , in the larger public interest, as the question is one of frequent occurrence.
RatioOfTheDecision	He rightly contends that the word 'damages' in s. 14B must, in the context in which it appears, means penal damages i.e.
RatioOfTheDecision	a penalty and not merely actual loss to the beneficiaries.
RatioOfTheDecision	He submits that if the word 'damages' appearing therein, were to mean actual loss to the beneficiaries and not anything more, as some of <ORG> have held, it would make the Act unworkable.
RatioOfTheDecision	He also points out that some of <ORG> have taken a view to the contrary.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to <ORG>, the expression 'damages' is, in substance, a penalty imposed on the employer for the breach of the statutory obligation.
RatioOfTheDecision	The object of the <ORG> in enacting s. 14B is clearly to punish the recalcitrant employers.
RatioOfTheDecision	The traditional v iew of damages as meaning actual loss, does not take into account the social content of a provision like s. 14B contained in a socio-economic measure like the Act in question.
RatioOfTheDecision	The word 'damages' has different shades of meaning.
RatioOfTheDecision	It must take its colour and content from its context, and it cannot be read in isolation, nor can s. 14B be read out of context.
RatioOfTheDecision	The very object of the Legislation would be frustrated if the word 'damages' appearing in s. 14B of the Act was not construed to mean penal damages.
RatioOfTheDecision	The imposition of damages u/s.
RatioOfTheDecision	14B serves a two-fold purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	It results in damnification and also serves as a deterrent.
RatioOfTheDecision	The predominant object is to penalise, so that an employer may be thwarted or deterred from making any further defaults.
RatioOfTheDecision	The expression 'damages' occurring in s. 14B is, in substance, a penalty imposed on the employer for the breach of the statutory obligation.
RatioOfTheDecision	The object of imposition of penalty u/s 14B is not merely 'to provide compensation for the employees'.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are clearly of the opinion  that the imposition of damages u/s 14B serves both the purposes.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is meant to penalise defaulting employer as also to provide reparation for the amount of loss suffered by the employees.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not only a warning to employers in general not to commit a breach of the statutory requirements of s. 6 , but at the same time it is meant to provide compensation or redress to the beneficiaries i.e.
RatioOfTheDecision	to recommence the employees for the loss sustained by them.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is nothing in the section to show that the damages must bear relationship to the loss which is caused to the beneficiaries under the Schemes.
RatioOfTheDecision	The word 'damages' in s. 14B is related to the word 'default'.
RatioOfTheDecision	The words used in s. 14B are 'default in the payment of contribution' and, therefore, the word 'default' must be construed in the light of Para 38 of the Scheme which provides that the payment of contribution has got to be made by the 15th of the following month and, therefore, the word 'default' in s. 14B must mean 'failure in performance' or 'failure to  act.'
RatioOfTheDecision	At the same time, the imposition of damages u/s 14B is to provide reparation for the amount of loss suffered by the employees.
RatioOfTheDecision	The construction that we have placed on the word 'damages' appearing in s. 14B of the Act, is in accord with the intent and purpose of the Legislation.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was brought on the statute book by Act 37 of 1953, the objects and reasons so far material, read:-  "There are also certain administrative difficulties to be set right.

RatioOfTheDecision	The object and purpose of the section is to authorise the Regional <ORG> Commissioner to impose exemplary or punitive damages and thereby prevent employers from making defaults.
RatioOfTheDecision	The provision for imposition of  damages at twenty- five per cent of the amount of arrear, however, did not prove to be effective.
RatioOfTheDecision	Accordingly, but Act 40 of 1973, the words 'not exceeding the amount of arrear' were substituted, for the words 'twenty-five per cent'.

RatioOfTheDecision	Each word, phrase or sentence is to be considered in the light of general purpose of the Act itself.
RatioOfTheDecision	A bare mechanical interpretation of the words devoid of concept or purpose will reduce most of legislation to futility.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is a salutary rule, well established, that the intention of the legislature must be found by reading the statute as a whole.
RatioOfTheDecision	There appears to be a misconception that the object of imposition of penalty under s. 14B is not 'to provide compensation for the employees' whose interest may be injured, by loss of interest and the like.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is also a misconception that the damages imposed under s. 14B are not transferred to <ORG> and <ORG>, of the employees who may be adversely affected, but the amount is transferred to <ORG> of the appropriate <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	We find that this assumption is wholly unwarranted.
RatioOfTheDecision	In assessing the damages, the Regional Provident Fund Commissioner is not only  bound to take into account the loss to the beneficiaries but also the default by the employer in making his contributions, which occasions the infliction of damages.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned Additional Solicitor General was fair enough to concede that the entire amount of damages awarded under s. 14B , except for the amount relatable to administrative charges, must necessarily be transferred to <ORG> and <ORG>.
