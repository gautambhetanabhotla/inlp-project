RulingByLowerCourt	But, on reading this affidavit as a whole and the affidavit of Mr. <NAME>, I am satisfied that in paragraph 14 of his affidavit the Chairman has set out all the material circumstances which bad emerged on an examination of the various materials before him.
RulingByLowerCourt	Briefly put, those circumstances are delay, bungling and faulty planning by the management resulting in double expenditure, huge losses, sharp fall in the price of the Company's shares and the resignation of some of the directors on account of differences in opinion with the managing director.
RulingByLowerCourt	I think that these circumstances, without more, cannot reasonably suggest that the business of the company was being conducted to defraud the creditors, members  and other persons or that the management was guilty of fraud towards the company and its members.
RulingByLowerCourt	No reasonable person who had given proper consideration to these circumstances could have formed the opinion that they suggested any fraud as mentioned in the order dated May 19, 1965.
RulingByLowerCourt	Had the Chairman applied his mind to the relevant facts, he could not have formed this opinion.
RulingByLowerCourt	I am, there- fore, inclined to think that he formed the opinion without applying his mind to the facts.
RulingByLowerCourt	An opinion so formed by him is in excess of his powers and cannot support an order under s. 237(b).
