Fact	Now, why does a case of under-assessment arise?
Fact	It is either because the assessee has not disclosed his proper income or because the income-tax officer has fallen into an error in computing it.
Fact	It may be caused by deliberate concealment on the part of the assessee or a mere accident or error either on his part or on the part of the officer carrying out the assessment.
Fact	Now take a very simple case.
Fact	The assessee discloses a certain income from his shop in Calcutta and no other and is assessed accordingly.
Fact	It is discovered next year that he had another shop at <ORG> and had not disclosed the income.
Argument	Under Section 34 , he can clearly be proceeded against.
Argument	The income from the <ORG> shop has escaped assessment, but the asses see has also been underassessed: Suppose he is proceeded against under Section 34 and his income from the <NAME> shop taken into consideration.
Argument	Two years later it is discovered that he had a third shop at Indore and had concealed the income.
Argument	How can it be said that the authorities are powerless to take proceedings in respect thereof?
Argument	14.
Argument	Then again suppose it is subsequently found that in computing the income of Calcutta shop, the assessee had1 concealed the fact that it dealt with lucrative <ORG> contract, from which he received a substantial income.
Argument	This income may be said to have escaped assessment and also the result is an under-assessment.
RatioOfTheDecision	Can it be said that because there had been these previous proceedings no further action can be taken?
RatioOfTheDecision	I think not.
RatioOfTheDecision	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	Take a further case.
RatioOfTheDecision	Suppose after all these proceedings had been completed, it is discovered that in adding up the income of the Calcutta shop, the income-tax officer had left out an item of a lakh of rupees.
RatioOfTheDecision	There has certainly been an under -assessment, but also this sum has escaped assessment.
RatioOfTheDecision	I do not think that the assessee can get away with this sum.
RatioOfTheDecision	And in all such cases, the assessment would be at too low a rate because the total world income of the assessee was previously calculated to be less than what it actually was.
RatioOfTheDecision	16.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is clear therefore that it is not possible to lay down water-tight frontiers between the several grounds upon which the income-tax officer can act, nor between the act of assessment and re-assessment.
RatioOfTheDecision	Assessment means the computation of the assessee's income (gain or profits) upon which attaches the incidence of taxation and includes the whole process which leads up to it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Re-assessment is only one of the processes by which the final goal is reached.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 34 , I. T. Act has been framed so as to enable the income-tax authorities to reach that goal.
RatioOfTheDecision	The section imposes a time limit (8 years in some case and 4 years in another).
RatioOfTheDecision	Within this time limit there is no restriction imposed as to the number of proceedings  that can be taken to re-open the assessment whether by way of assessment or re-assessment, computation or re-computation.
