Fact	JUDGMENT  1.
Fact	This Rule arises out of certain proceedings under the Land Acquisition Act .
Fact	2.
Fact	The petitioner alleged that he is the owner of a salt godown, for the efficient working of which the land in front is required, and that the Deputy Collector has acquired that frontal land without making a reference, as prayed, to <ORG> under Section 49 of the Land Acquisition Act I of 1894.
Fact	3.
Fact	We are invited in this Rule to set aside the order of the Deputy Collector dated the 22nd September 1910 and his proceedings up to, and including, an order of the 24th May 1911 wherein he refused to make a reference under Section 18 of the Act on the ground that the case had been completed and the award confirmed.
Fact	4.
Fact	It appears that the 19th September 1910 was the date fixed by the Deputy Collector for claims to be put in.
Fact	On that day, no claim was preferred nor was any petition for time filed.
Fact	The order-sheet of the Deputy Collector is exceedingly brief and does not embody the details mentioned in his letter of ex planation No.
Fact	1977 I.A., dated the 23rd August 1911, upon which the learned Senior Government Pleader showing cause relies.
Fact	However that may be, orders were reserved on the 29th September 1910, and the award in this connection was not made until the 21st November 1910.
Fact	The application of the petitioner praying for a reference under Section 49 of the Act bears date the 21st September 1910, and, apparently, reached the Deputy Collector through the post.
Fact	There are two orders on this petition, the first order is "File with the record," the second order which is over a date 22nd September 1910 which has been altered, or inked over, is to the following effect: "I have seen the land and godown.
Fact	The godown is now closed and no business is being done there now.
Fact	The land which is now under acquisition is not in the front of the building and its acquisition will not interfere with the business if the petitioner intends to start business at any future date.
Fact	The petition is, therefore, rejected.
Fact	Besides, the time for fili ng such petition has expired."
Fact	In this view of the matter, the Deputy Collector proceeded to complete the proceedings and possession was duly given of the land in question to <ORG>.
Fact	5.
Fact	The first question that arises upon this Rule is whether the Land Acquisition Deputy Collector is subject to the extraordinary jurisdiction of this Court.
Argument	6.
Argument	We have been referred to the cases of the Administrator-General of Bengal v. The Land Acquisition Deputy Collector 24-Pergannahs 12 C.W.N.
Argument	241 and <CITATION_887227> for India 8 Ind.
Argument	107 : 12 C.L.J.
Argument	505 : 15 C.W.N.
Argument	87 : 38 C. 230.
Argument	In the present state of the law, we cannot do otherwise than follow the decision of <NAME> and <NAME>, JJ., in Administrator-General of Bengal v. The Land Acquisition Deputy Collector 24 Pergannahs 12 C.W.N.
Argument	241.
Argument	It would, obviously, be unjust that the Deputy Collector should refuse to obey the provisions of the Act, and to provide no remedy for the correction of his mistaken action; where the law gives a right to a  party to a certain procedure, it must also be deemed to give a remedy for the rectification of any irregularities committed in that connection.
Argument	Section 49 of the Act clearly leaves no option to the Collector.
Argument	It says "he shall refer to the determination of such question (whether any land proposed to be taken under this Act does or does not form part of a house, manufactory or building) to <ORG>."
RatioOfTheDecision	He appears to have recognised the applicability of sect-ion 49 by framing the second order of the 22nd September 1910 which we have already re-produced.
RatioOfTheDecision	We entertain no doubt, therefore, that we have jurisdiction to set right the error committed by the Deputy Collector in not making a reference under Section 49 .
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
Fact	The second question involved in this Rule is whether the petitioner is an owner of any house, manufactory or building, a part of which house, manufactory or building is being acquired.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	The word owner is not defined in, the Act, but an owner must be declared to be one of the persons interest ed in the land being acquired [see Section 3(6) of Act I of 1894], Reading Section 10 of the Act we think that the proprietor, sub-proprietor, mortgagee, tenant or subtenant are all owners for the purposes of Section 49 .
RatioOfTheDecision	The petitioner is an under-tenant of some kind.
RatioOfTheDecision	He is, therefore, admittedly, interested in the acquisition of the land, which for the purposes of Section 49 , until <ORG> finds otherwise, may be presumed to be part of his salt go-down.
RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question for inquiry will be whether the piece of land in front of the godown forms the only means of approach to that godown or is reasonably necessary for the proper working of the salt business.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
RatioOfTheDecision	We, therefore, think that the petitioner was competent to apply -under Section 49 for a reference to <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	11.
Argument	The third contention of the learned senior Government Pleader is that the application for a reference was not filed in time.
Argument	The first proviso to Section 49 , however, says that the owner may, at any time before the <ORG> ctor has made his award, withdraw his application, and it would follow that it was equally open to the petitioner to make a substantive application for a reference at some time before the award was actually made, that is, before the 21st November 1910.
Argument	As a matter of fact, he made his application two months earlier, that is, on the 21st September 1910, and the Deputy Collector dealt with it on its merits by recording his second order after a local inquiry.
Argument	12.
Argument	It is unfortunate that the Deputy Collector overlooked the imperative language of the second proviso to Section 49 .
RatioOfTheDecision	We have no alternative but to set aside his proceedings from, and after, the 22nd September 1910, and to direct him to proceed in accordance with law under Section 49 of the Land Acquisition Act: whether any reference will be necessary under Section 13 is a matter contingent upon the decision of <ORG> as to whether the land does or does not form part of the petitioner's salt go-down.
RatioOfTheDecision	13.
RulingByPresentCourt	The Rule is made absolute.
RulingByPresentCourt	There will b e no costs in this matter.
