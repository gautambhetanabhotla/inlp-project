RatioOfTheDecision	12.
RatioOfTheDecision	The object of the Indian Act is to tax "income," a term which it does not define.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is expanded, no doubt, into "income, profits and gains," but the expansion is more a matter of words than of substance.
RatioOfTheDecision	Income, their Lordships think, in this Act connotes a periodical monetary return " coming in" with some sort of regularity, or expected regularity, from definite sources.
RatioOfTheDecision	The source is not necessarily one which is expected to be continuously productive, but it must be one whose object is the production of a definite return, excluding anything in the nature of a mere windfall.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus income has been likened pictorially to the fruit of a tree, or the crop of a field.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is essentially the produce of something, which is often loosely spoken, of as " capital."
RatioOfTheDecision	But  capital, though possibly the source in the case of income from securities, is in most cases hardly more than an element in the process of production.
