Fact	The petitioner, who was born on May 17, 1914, joined s ervice in <ORG> of the former State of Mysore on November 28, 1934.
Fact	In 1939 he was promoted as sub-inspector of police and in 1948, he became a police inspector.
Fact	On July 31, 1961 he was served with a notice issued by <ORG> informing him that <ORG> considered it was necessary in the public interest to retire him from service under note 1 to rule 285 of the rules and notice was accordingly given to him intimating to him that he will be retired as from November 15, 1961.
Argument	The petitioner contends that under rule 95(a) he was entitled to remain in service until he completed 55 years of age which means that he would have retired in normal course on May 17, 1969.
Argument	Since the notice served on him by <ORG> under the impugned note to rule 285 prematurely retires him, he contends that the said order as to premature retirement is invalid, because it purports to have been passed in exercise of the powers conferred on the <ORG> under note 1 to rule 285 and this l atter note is constitutionally invalid.
Argument	It appears that after the notice was served on the petitioner, he made a representation to the <ORG> through proper channel.
Argument	In this representation he urged that the action which the <ORG> intended to take against him was unjust and illegal and he pleaded that he had a very good record of service and did not deserve to have his service curtailed by nearly eight years by asking him to retire prematurely as from November 15, 1961.
Argument	The <ORG>, however, was not impressed by the plea made by the petitioner and on November 14, 1961 a final order was passed terminating the petitioner's service from November 15, 1961.
RatioOfTheDecision	That, in brief, is the background of the present petition.Rule 95(a) of the rules undoubtedly provides that the date of superannuation of a <ORG> servant is the date on which he attains the age of 55 years; and it authorizes the <ORG> to retain the <ORG> servant in service even after the date of superannuation, if the said servant is p hysically fit and if the continuance of the said <ORG> servant in service is found to be on public grounds.
RatioOfTheDecision	In such a case the grounds of public interest had to be mentioned in the order and the servant may be continued until he attains the age of 60 years.
Statute	Rule 285 deals with retiring pension and it provides that a retiring pension is granted to a <ORG> servant who is permitted to retire after completing qualifying service for thirty years or such less time as may, for any special class of <ORG> servants, be prescribed.
Statute	Note 1 to this rule provides inter alia that <ORG> may, in special cases, require any <ORG> servant to retire any time after he has completed 25 years' qualifying service or on attaining 50 years of age if such retirement is considered necessary in the public interest, provided that the appropriate authority shall give in this behalf a notice in writing to the <ORG> servant at least three months before the date on which he is required to retire.
Statute	The note further  provides that a <ORG> servant who retires or is retired, only in the manner indicated above, shall be granted a retiring pension not exceeding such proportion of average emoluments and subject to such maximum limits as are specified in Chap.
RatioOfTheDecision	XIX It would thus be clear that though the normal age of retirement under rule 95(a) is 55 years, under rule 285 it is competent to the <ORG> to retire, compulsorily, a <ORG> servant prematurely, if it is thought that such premature retirement is necessary in the public interest.
RatioOfTheDecision	This power can, however, be exercised only in cases where the <ORG> servant has completed 25 years' qualifying service or has attained 50 years of age.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words, ordinarily retirement by superannuation occurs after attaining 55 years or completing 30 years' service, while premature retirement can be forced on the <ORG> servant if he has either completed 25 years of service or has attained 50 years of age.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the case of premature compulsory retirement, the Governme nt servant is entitled to pension as indicated in note 1 to rule 285.Sri <NAME> contends that this rule is invalid, because it contravenes Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	14 as well as Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	16(1) of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, this contention can no longer be entertained, because it is concluded by a long series of decisions of this <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Recently, <ORG> of this <ORG> had occasion to consider the validity of rules 148(3) and 149(3) contained in the Indian Railway Establishment Code in <NAME> v. <ORG>-Civil Appeals Nos.
RatioOfTheDecision	711 to 714 of 1962 and 837 to 839 of 1963, dated 5 December 1963 1964 (2) LLJ 467 ].
RatioOfTheDecision	In dealing with the problem raised in that case, this <ORG> has made it perfectly clear that so far as the question of compulsory retirement is concerned, it must be taken to be concluded by several decisions of this <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	This <ORG> then examined the relevant decisions on this point beginning with the case of <CITATION_1262738> 1954 (2) LLJ 139 ] and it has observed that the  law in relation to the validity of the rules permitting compulsory premature retirement of <ORG> servants must be held to be well-settled by those decisions and need not be reopened.
RatioOfTheDecision	The only exception the majority judgment made in that behalf was that it may be necessary to consider whether such a rule of compulsory retirement would be valid if, having fixed a proper age of superannuation, it permits a permanent servant to be retired at a very early stage of his career.
RatioOfTheDecision	This consideration does not arise in the present case, because as we have already seen, note 1 to rule 285 requires that the <ORG> servant against whom an order of compulsory retirement is proposed to be passed must have completed either 25 years of active service or attained 50 years of age.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are, therefore, satisfied that the point which <NAME> wants to raise before us in the present petition is clearly concluded by the decisions of this Court and cannot be allowed to be reopened.<NAME>, how ever, attempted to argue that there are certain observations made by this <ORG> in the case of General Manager.
RatioOfTheDecision	<CITATION_1074353> 1962 AIR(SC) 36 ] which support his contention.
RatioOfTheDecision	In that case, this <ORG> was called upon to consider the validity of certain railway circulars and that needed an examination about the scope and effect of the provisions of the different clauses of Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	16 read with Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	14 of the Constitution; the question of compulsory retirement did not fall to be considered in that case.
RatioOfTheDecision	Dealing with the scope and effect of Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	16(1 ), it was observed that the narrow construction of the expression "matters relating to employment" occurring in Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	16(1) could not be accepted, because it would lead to anomalous results.
RatioOfTheDecision	In that connexion, it was pointed out that it would not be open to the <ORG> to prescribe different scales of salary for the same or similar posts, different terms of leaves or superannuation for the same or similar posts.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is on these observations that reliance is placed by <NAME> i <NAME>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is obvious that these observations have no relevance to the point with which we are concerned in the present petition.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are not dealing with different rules of compulsory retirement at all, the impugned note applies to all Government servants, and as such, is not open to a challenge under Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	14 or 16 (1).
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, we do not think Sri <NAME> is justified in contending that these observations support his argument.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that in his petition the petitioner has made certain allegations against the Home Minister <NAME> and has suggested that the order terminating his services prematurely is due to his whims and fancies, and he has characterized the said order as both arbitrary and illegal.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to the Petitioner, his past record is free from blemish and the Government was not, therefore, justified in coming to the conclusion that it was in the public interest to retire him compulsorily.
RatioOfTheDecision	The allegations made by the petitioner in that behalf are  very vague and unsatisfactory, and so, it would be idle to contend that if the impugned note is valid, the order terminating the services of the petitioner can still be challenged on the ground that it is not justified on the merits or is illegal or arbitrary.
RatioOfTheDecision	Whether or not the petitioner's retirement was in the public interest, is a matter for <ORG> to consider, and as to the plea that the order is arbitrary and illegal, it is impossible to hold on the material placed by the petitioner before us that the said order suffers from the vice of <NAME> fides.The result is, all the petitions Nos.
