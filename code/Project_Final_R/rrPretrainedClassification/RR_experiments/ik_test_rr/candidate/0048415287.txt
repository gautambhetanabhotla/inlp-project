Fact	(3) SCR 747 The following Order of the Court was delivered :  The respondent was initially granted Mining lease in the year 1966 for a period of 20 years to extract limestone in the forest area.
Fact	The Forest (Conservation) Act 69 of 1980 has come into force with effect from 25.10.80.
Fact	Under Section 2 of the Act grant or renewal after the Act has come into force shall be made, in the event of <ORG> deciding to grant lease or renewal, with the prior approval of <ORG>.
Fact	The object of the Act is to maintain ecology and preservation of the forest.
Fact	It is also the object under Section 3 to regenerate the forests by planting trees and the forest growth.
Fact	In 1986 after the expiry of the lease the respondent had approached the Government for renewal.
RulingByLowerCourt	Though the Government had taken t he decision and passed an order on February 7, 1986 to grant further renewal for a period of 20 years in terras of the original grant, it was objected to by <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	Consequently it was cancelled.
RulingByLowerCourt	The respondent had approached <ORG> in Miscellaneous Petition No.
RulingByLowerCourt	2758 of 89 and <ORG> by its order dated August 18, 1989 following the ratio in <ORG> v, <NAME> and Others, (A.I.R.
RulingByLowerCourt	1985 S.C. 814) directed to grant renewal on the ground that the part of the leased area had already been broken and, therefore, renewal should be granted.
RulingByLowerCourt	The appellant contends that this <ORG> in <CITATION_104313664> ., (A.I.R.
Precedent	1988 SC 2187) and <ORG> v. State of Gujarat and Others, (A.I.R.
Precedent	1987 SC 1073) had held that even the renewal of the lease cannot be granted without the prior occurrence of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	We find force in the contention.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is settled law that the grant or renewal is a fresh grant and mast be made consistent with law.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 2 prohi bited the grant or renewal.
RatioOfTheDecision	In case <ORG> decides to grant fresh lease or renewal of the lease it is mandatory that it should obtain prior approval of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Admittedly, no prior approval of <ORG> had been obtained under Section 2 of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> thus had realised the mistake in directing renewal when <ORG> had objected to the renewal of the lease in favour of the respondent".
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the cancellation of the order, before it came into effect by registering, had been properly made by the appellant.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> was, therefore, not right in directing grant of renewal of the lease.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is brought to our notice that under contempt proceedings the lease deed was directed to be registered and it was accordingly registered.
RulingByPresentCourt	It is declared that the registration is illegal and is invalid.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeal is accordingly allowed.
RulingByPresentCourt	No costs.
