Fact	JUDGMENT K.S.
Fact	<NAME>, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	In these appeals by certificate just one question arises for decision and that question is whether <ORG> was justified in refusing extension of time to the appellants to clear sugar released for sale in the open market?
Fact	2.
Fact	The Appellants (Companies incorporated under the Indian Companies Act ) carry on business inter aha of producing, manufacturing and selling sugar.
Fact	They have their factories either in Uttar Pradesh or in Bihar.
Fact	By a notification dated June 10, 1966 <ORG> in exercise of the powers conferred on it by <CITATION_158335608> of the Essential Commodities Act, 1955 (Act X of 1955) promulgated the Sugar (Control) Order, 1966.
Fact	Clause 4 of that Order restricted the sale of sugar by producers.
Statute	It reads:  No producer shall sell or agree to sell or otherwise dispose of sugar or deliver or agree to deliver sugar, or remove any sugar from the bonded godowns of the factory in which it is produced, except under and in accordance with a direction i ssued in writing by <ORG> or the Chief Director.
Statute	By Order dated November 16, 1967, the Sugar (Control) Order, 1966 was amended and the above mentioned Clause 4 was substituted by the following Clause:  <ORG> may direct that no producer shall sell or agree to sell or otherwise dispose of, or deliver or agree to deliver any land of sugar or remove any kind of sugar from the bond ed godowns of the factory in which it ft produced, except under and in accordance with a direction issued in writing by <ORG>.
Statute	On August 16, 1967, the Minister for Food and Agriculture announced the sugar policy for 1967-68.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to that policy quantity equal to 60 per cent of the production achieved in every factory from October 1, 1966 to September 30, 1967 will be procured from each of them at a fixed levy price.
RatioOfTheDecision	Factories will be free to sell the balance of their production at the free market price subject to releases sanctioned by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	A press note dated  October 21, 1967, was issued announcing the above policy.
Fact	By notification No.
Fact	1750/ESS.Com/Sugar dated November 20, 1967 the powers conferred by Clauses inter alia 4 and 5 of the Sugar (Control) Order 1966 are made exercisable by the Chief Director or Director in <ORG> and <ORG>-operation (<ORG>).
Fact	3.
Fact	In exercise of the powers conferred by Clause 5 of the Sugar (Control) Order, 1966 read with aforesaid notification dated 20th November 1966, Respondent No.
Fact	2, the Director issued release Orders dated December 23, 1967 whereby he permitted the appellants to sell the quantity of sugar mentioned in each one of those Orders in the open market.
Fact	Those Orders were received by the appellants on the 27th December, 1967.
Fact	Thereunder they were required to dispose of sugar released for sale in the open market on or before January 22, 1968.
Fact	Immediately after receiving the Order, the ap pellants entered into contracts with the  dealers for the sale of sugar.
Fact	They also applied for railway wagons for transport of that sugar to the States outside their own.
Argument	Most of the Sugar released for open sale was removed from their go downs within the time prescribed but each of the appellants was not able to put into open market a fraction of the sugar released for sale in the open market as the railway wagons were not made available to them in time due to some difficulty or other on the part of the <ORG>.
Argument	4.
Argument	It is satisfactorily proved that the appellants have taken every possible step dispose, of the sugar released for sale a the open market immediately after they received the release Orders.
Argument	If they have not been able to dispose of some portion of the sugar released, it is not due to any fault of theirs It is entirely due to circumstances beyond their control.
Argument	5.
Argument	It is admitted by the respondents that a decision had.
Argument	been taken to give the producers 30 days time for disposal of the sugar released for sale in the open market.
Argument	It is proved  that in the case of the appellants only 26 days had been given for the disposal of that sugar.
Argument	6.
RatioOfTheDecision	It must be remembered that right to trade is a guaranteed freedom.
RatioOfTheDecision	That right can be restricted only by law, considered by the <ORG> as reasonable in the circumstances.
RatioOfTheDecision	Not only the law restricting the freedom should be reasonable, the Orders made on the basis of that law should also be reasonable.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is clear that the sugar released for sale in the open market will have to bo ordinarily sent out of the States in which they are produced.
RatioOfTheDecision	For doing so, the concerned producers will have to enter into contracts with dealers at far off places.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thereafter the sugar will have to be transported to places of disposal mainly through railways.
RatioOfTheDecision	Taking all the circumstances into consideration, we do not think that the period of 30 days given for disposal of the sugar is in any manner generous.
RatioOfTheDecision	That being so, we are clearly of the opinion that the 28 days time given to the appellants for the disposal of the sugar cannot be  considered as reasonable.
RatioOfTheDecision	We think that the second respondent unreasonably rejected the apAppellants request to extend the time for the disposal of the sugar released for sale in the open market.
Fact	He appears to have acted mechanically.
Fact	7.
Fact	For the reasons mentioned above we allow these appeals and direct the respondents (in accordance with the Orders made in these appeals on June 6, 1969) to release to the appellants an equivalent quantity of sugar for free sale out of the appellants quota of levy sugar for the season 1968-69 in lieu of the sugar that they had to surrender from out of the quota released to them for sale in the open market for the year 1967-68.
Fact	The appellants are entitled to their costs both in this <ORG> as well as in the High <ORG>.
