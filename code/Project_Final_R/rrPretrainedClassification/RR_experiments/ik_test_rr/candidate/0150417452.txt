Fact	The petition er is before this <ORG> seeking for a direction to respondents No.2 to 6 herein not to insist upon the petitioner-Club to obtain the licence for the cultural activities carried on by the petitioner in the club premises as per the legal requirements.
Argument	3.
Argument	The petitioner claims to be a recreational club wherein the members of the club are permitted to play games of recreation such as rummy, carrom, chess etc.
Argument	It is therefore the contention that no unlawful activity is being carried on in the said premises.
Argument	The grievance of the petitioner is that the respondents are insisting on the petitioner to secure licence despite the same does not require a licence.
Argument	The petitioner is therefore before this <ORG>.
Argument	4.
RatioOfTheDecision	Having heard the learned counsel appearing for the parties, it is to be noticed that several matters of the like nature have arisen before this <ORG> and this <ORG> has consistently taken the view that in respect of the present nature of activities in a recreational club, the same does not require licence.
Fact	Hen ce, the respondents are not justified in insisting on the same.
Fact	However, the respondents would have the liberty of monitoring the activities of the club and taking action only if there are any illegal activities carried on in the premises.
Argument	5.
Argument	In that view of the matter, the petition is disposed of, with a direction to the respondents not to insist on the petitioner to secure a licence in respect of the activities stated to have been carried on by the petitioner.
Argument	However, it is made clear that the respondents would be entitled to monitor the activities and take action, if the petitioner indulges in any illegal activities in the club premises.
RulingByPresentCourt	With the above observations, the petition stands disposed of.
