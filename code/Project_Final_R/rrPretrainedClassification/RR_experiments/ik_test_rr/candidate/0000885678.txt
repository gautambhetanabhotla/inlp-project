Fact	JUDGMENT <NAME>, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	This is an application of one <NAME>, an employee in <ORG> of the State of Uttar Pradesh, for the issue of a writ of mandamus directing the State of Uttar Pradesh, the District Magistrate of Mirzapur and <ORG>r of Mirzapur, not to terminate the services of the applicant without complying with Article 311 , Sub-clause (2) of the Constitution of India, and praying further that the notice of termination of service dated 26th April 1951 given by the District Magistrate, Mirzapur, be declared to be inoperative and bad in law.
Fact	The facts disclosed in the affidavit submitted along with the application are as follows.
Fact	2.
Fact	The applicant is employed as supply inspector in <ORG> at Mirzapur.
Fact	On the 24th April 1951, the Hon'ble Sri <NAME>, Minister for Food and Civil Supplies, State of Uttar Pradesh visited the district of Mirzapur.
Fact	At the instance of the said Minister, the applicant was served with a  notice dated the 26th April 1951, under the signature of the District Magistrate, Mirzapur, notifying that the applicant's services were to be terminated on the expiry of one month's notice, that is, with effect from the 26th May 1951.
Fact	The notice was in the following terms :  "All the posts in <ORG> are temporary posts and these have been extended from lime to time according to the requirements.
Fact	The conditions of service of these posts stipulate that the services of the Incumbents are liable to termination with one month's notice.
Fact	2.
Fact	Due to his bad reputation in <ORG> it has been decided to retrench Sri <NAME>, Supply Inspector.
Fact	3.
RulingByLowerCourt	Article 311 of the Constitution of India requires that every person who holds any civil post under the <ORG> shall before dismissal or removal or reduction in rank be given an opportunity of showing cause against the action proposed to be taken in regard to him.
RulingByLowerCourt	It is not admitted that this provisi on has any application to the category of personnel in question, but assuming that it does, I am satisfied that in view of the person involved and the nature of reasons for retrenchment it does not seem reasonable to give the individual an opportunity to show cause why his services should not be terminated.
RulingByLowerCourt	4.
RulingByLowerCourt	It is, therefore, ordered that the services of Sri <NAME>, Supply Inspector, shall be terminated after a month of the service of this notice.
RulingByLowerCourt	One months's notice as stipulated in the condition of service is hereby given to him.
Fact	<NAME>, District Magistrate, Mirzapur.
Fact	April.
Fact	26, 1961."
Fact	3.
Argument	The applicant alleges that his reputation was not bad as the previous quarterly reports would show, that he made a representation to the District Magistrate on the 10th May 1951 questioning the basis of the charge of bad reputation against him and that the District Magistrate thereupon ordered the withdrawal of the charge of bad reputation while confirming the previous order of termination o f service.
Argument	The case for the applicant is that Article 311 of the Constitution applies to his case and that <ORG> was not entitled to terminate his services without following the procedure laid down in Clause (2) of that Article.
Argument	Clause (2) provides, "No such person as aforesaid (that is a person who is a member of a civil service of the <ORG> or an all-India service or a civil service of a <ORG> or holds a civil post under the <ORG> or a <ORG>) shall be dismissed or removed or reduced in rank until he has been given a reasonable opportunity of showing cause against the action proposed to be taken in regard to him."
Argument	There are three provisos to this clause under which the clause will not apply.
Argument	One of these provisos (b) is "Where an authority empowered to dismiss or remove a person or to reduce him in rank is satisfied that for some reason, to be recorded by that authority in writing, it is not reasonably practicable to give to that person an opportunity of showing cause."
Argument	4.
Argument	It is in order to  bring proviso (b) into operation that in the notice it was mentioned that "in view of the person involved and the nature of reasons for retrenchment it does not seem reasonable to give the individual an opportunity to show cause why his services should not be terminated."
Argument	The case of the applicant with regard to this clause is that the reasons given in the notice, for saying that it is not reasonably practicable to give the individual an opportunity of showing cause, are not sufficient in law to bring the case under proviso (b) to Clause (2) of Article 311 of the Constitution.
Argument	5.
Fact	The first point that is to be considered is whether the main Clause (2) of Article 311 at all applies to a case of this kind.
RulingByLowerCourt	It will be noticed that the applicant was a temporary employee whose contract of service laid down that his services were liable to termination with one month's notice.
RulingByLowerCourt	One month's notice was duly given to him and in accordance with the conditions of service, his services would terminate upon the expiry of t he period specified in the notice.
Fact	From the 26th May 1951 the applicant cannot claim to remain as an employee of the <ORG>.
Fact	He would no longer after that date hold a "civil post"--it is conceded that he is not a member of the civil service of the <ORG> or of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Article 311 applies only to a case in which a person is dismissed or removed or reduced in rank.
RatioOfTheDecision	These are technical words used in cases in which a person's services are terminated for misconduct.
RatioOfTheDecision	They do not apply to cases in which a person's period of service determines in accordance with the conditions of his service.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not so much a question of the post being held temporarily or it being of a permanent nature; the real question is whether a person's services are being dispensed with before his normal period of service has terminated by reason of misconduct on his part, or otherwise.
RatioOfTheDecision	If a person's services are sought to be terminated before the period of his service has expired, on account of some misconduct, then, whether the employee i s temporary or permanent, the procedure prescribed in Article 311 has to be followed unless of course the case falls within any one of the three provisos to Clause (2).
RatioOfTheDecision	If, on the other hand, a person's services are sought to be terminated at the expiry of the term for which he was engaged, or at the expiry of the period of notice by which, in accordance with the conditions of his service, his services could be terminated, there is no question of dismissal, removal or reduction in rank and Article 311 does not come into operation.
Argument	6.
Argument	Article 311 not being applicable to a case of this kind, it is not necessary to decide whether the reasons given in the notice, for not giving an opportunity to the applicant to show cause against the termination of his services, were sufficient or not, within the meaning of proviso (b) to Article 311(2).
Argument	7.
RatioOfTheDecision	We see no force in this application and dismiss it.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RulingByPresentCourt	We certify that this case involves a substantial question of law as to the interpretation of the Constitution.
