RulingByLowerCourt	As we have earlier stated, the <ORG> took the view that the <ORG> had not ceased to carry on business.
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG> observed that the question would depend on what the intentions of the <ORG> were.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG> was of the same opinion.
RulingByLowerCourt	We also think that that is the correct view.
RulingByLowerCourt	This postulates that the <ORG> was not in fact carrying on any business for if it was, it would be superfluous to enquire whether the <ORG> intended to carry on a business.
RulingByLowerCourt	The Courts below thought that the facts showed that the <ORG> intended to carry on business.
RulingByLowerCourt	The facts on which they relied were(1) the <ORG> did not sell its undertaking as a going concern; (2) it continued in possession of all assets of its undertakings other than those appertaining to the  Lahore electric supply undertaking; (3) it continued to hold deposits made by consumers of electricity supplied by the Lahore electric supply undertaking which had to be returned to them with interest; (4) it had no intention of going into liquidation; (5) the Directors' report showed that the Directors were "considering if they could possibly purchase some manufacturing concern which might become an additional source of profit to the shareholders"; and (6) there was nothing to show that there was permanent discontinuance of the business of the <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	So far as <ORG> is concerned, it appears to have held that the <ORG> was carrying on business because there was nothing to show that it intended to go into liquidation and because by keeping its staff and establishment it indicated that it would resume business, for otherwise it would not have retained them.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> also took into account the fact that during the larger part of the accounting periods the situation in the country was abnormal  as a result of the partition of India and that explained why the <ORG> had not commenced any fresh business.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, none of the grounds mentioned in the preceding paragraph leads to the conclusion that the <ORG> intended to carry on business.
RatioOfTheDecision	The facts found make it abundantly clear that since 1942 the only business of the <ORG> was to work the Lahore electric supply licence.
RatioOfTheDecision	It stopped that business on September 5, 1946 when the undertaking was taken over by the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thereafter, during the accounting years concerned, namely, 1947-48 and 1948-49 it had not started any other business.
RatioOfTheDecision	The mere fact that the <ORG> had not gone into liquidation would not establish that it had the intention to do business.
RatioOfTheDecision	If it were not so, then in the case of all trading companies it had to be held that they were always doing business.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is neither authority nor principle to support such a proposition.
RatioOfTheDecision	There was further no question of the <ORG>'s going into liquidation in the accounting years, .f or during that time it had not received from the Government the entire amount due to it as compensation for the acquisition of its Lahore electric supply undertaking.
RatioOfTheDecision	At the relevant time the <ORG> was not possessed of any commercial undertaking.
RatioOfTheDecision	What we have quoted earlier from the Directors' report would show that what was in the contemplation of the Directors was the purchase of a new concern.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Directors however had not stated that they did intend to do so.
RatioOfTheDecision	What they said was that they were considering whether they would do so or not.
RatioOfTheDecision	That does not express an intention to resume business.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is unnecessary to go into the question whether an expression of an intention to resume business in vacuo would amount to carrying on business.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is sufficient for the purpose of this case to state that even an intention to resume business has not been established.
RatioOfTheDecision	It would, therefore, appear that the business was closed and the <ORG> had not established an intention to resume it.
RatioOfTheDecision	That would be enough to show t hat no business was carried on and it would be irrelevant to enquire whether the business was permanently closed.
RatioOfTheDecision	We may add that we do not understand what was meant by saying that the <ORG> did not sell its undertaking as a going concern.
RatioOfTheDecision	The only going trading concern that it possessed was the Lahore electric supply undertaking and that it sold; it had no other commercial undertaking.
RatioOfTheDecision	After the sale of the Lahore electric supply concern all it did was to invest its moneys and the <ORG> has not found this activity to be a business.
RatioOfTheDecision	The facts that the <ORG> had to pay the <ORG> half share of the profits between November 27, 1942 and September 5, 1946 and that it had to return the consumers the deposits made by them would not indicate that it was carrying on a business.
RatioOfTheDecision	It would be laying down strange law to hold that where a business has in fact ceased to be run, it must be deemed as continuing because the outstanding liabilities of that business had not been liquidated.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question whether the  Company was carrying on business arises only because if it was, it would be entitled under s. 10 to deductions from its business income in regard to certain expenses incurred by it for the purpose of that business.
RatioOfTheDecision	Business as contemplated by that section is an activity capable of producing a profit which can be taxed.
RatioOfTheDecision	Payment of outstanding liabilities is not an activity which can ever produce such a result.
RatioOfTheDecision	It cannot be said, therefore, that because liabilities of a closed business were outstanding, it has to be held that either the business was continuing or that an intention to resume business must be inferred : see commissioners of <ORG> v. <ORG>).
