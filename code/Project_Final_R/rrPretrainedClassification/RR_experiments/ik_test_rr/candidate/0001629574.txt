Fact	1.
Fact	The respondent filed an application for winding up <ORG> (hereinafter referred to as "the company").
Fact	The petition was admitted by an order dated March 6, 1990.
Fact	An appeal was preferred against the order by the company.
Fact	This application has been filed in the appeal for stay of the order dated March 6, 1990.
Fact	More than two months after the filing of the appeal, a reference was made by the company under Section 15 of the Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act to <ORG> (hereinafter referred to as "the <ORG>") constituted under the Act.
Fact	The reference has been registered by the secretary of the <ORG> under regulation 19 of <ORG> Regulations, 1987 (hereinafter referred to as "the Regulations"), on June 25, 1990.
Fact	These facts were brought on record by way of a supplementary affidavit filed by the company.
Fact	2.

Fact	No other point has been put forward in support of the stay application on behalf of the company.

RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are of the view that the contentions of Mr. <NAME> must be accepted.
RatioOfTheDecision	6.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not think that, after the registration of a reference under Section 15 of the Act, the <ORG> has no option but to hold an enquiry.
RatioOfTheDecision	The word "may" in Section 16(1) would indicate that the <ORG> can refuse to hold an enquiry.
RatioOfTheDecision	This power must, in any event, be taken to be a necessary corollary to the power to hold an enquiry.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is possible that if the <ORG> finds a reference to be frivolous, it may exercise its option of not holding an enquiry at all.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are fortified in our finding by  the three unreported decisions relied upon by Mr. <NAME>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Although this issue was not specifically raised before <ORG> in <NAME> case, , we cannot lose sight of the observations of <ORG> in paragraph 10 of the decision, viz.
RatioOfTheDecision	(at page 173 of 71 Comp Cas) :  "As soon as the enquiry under Section 16 is ordered by the <ORG>, the various proceedings set out under Sub-section (1) of Section 22 would be deemed to have been suspended."
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, in our view, it is immaterial whether the <ORG> has an option to hold or not to hold an enquiry under Section 16 of the Act in view of our interpretation of Section 22(1) .
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
Statute	Section 22(1) in so far as it is material provides as follows :  "22(1) Where, in respect of an industrial company, an inquiry under Section 16 is pending or ... .where an appeal under Section 25 relating to an industrial company is pending, then .... no proceedings for the winding up of the industrial company .... shall be proceeded with further, except with th e consent of the <ORG> or, as the case may be, the appellate authority."
RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are of the view that the phrase "enquiry pending under Section 16 " does not include a reference under Section 15 .
RatioOfTheDecision	We are supported in our view by the scheme of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
RatioOfTheDecision	The scheme of the Act would show that a distinction is drawn between a reference, enquiry and scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	Apart from the heading of Chapter III of the Act, the regulations would also show that a distinction is drawn between a reference and an enquiry.
RatioOfTheDecision	Regulation.
RatioOfTheDecision	19 deals with references under Section 15 while regulation 20 deals with enquiries under Section 16 .
RatioOfTheDecision	An inquiry under Section 16 is, therefore, only a stage in the proceedings contemplated under the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	If one were to adopt the analogy of a suit as submitted by Mr. <NAME>, the inquiry could be equated with the actual trial.
RatioOfTheDecision	A trial cannot be said to be pending merely because a suit is filed.
RatioOfTheDecision	Similarly, an enquiry under Section 16 cannot be said to be pending merely because a reference is filed,  11.
RatioOfTheDecision	In  any event, the words of the statute are explicit.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 22 refers to an enquiry under Section 16 .
RatioOfTheDecision	Had it been the intention of the <ORG> to extend the provisions of Section 22 to a reference made under Section 15 , the <ORG> would have provided for that expressly.
RatioOfTheDecision	This the <ORG> has not chosen to do.
RatioOfTheDecision	12.
RatioOfTheDecision	The use of the words "under Section 16 " limits the words "enquiry pending" in Section 22(1) .
RatioOfTheDecision	The provisions of Section 22(1) apply only to such enquiries and to no other.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is well-established that, if the language is clear, the court must give effect to it, for "the words of the statute speak the intention of the <ORG>".
RatioOfTheDecision	In these circumstances, the case of In re Eippon  1 All ER 676, as well as the two passages from <NAME> cited by Mr, <NAME> can have no application.
RatioOfTheDecision	13.
RatioOfTheDecision	In view of the express wording of the statute, there is no question of the application of the principles of beneficial construction.
RatioOfTheDecision	In any event, Section 2 of the Act indicates that the Act has been enacted  for giving effect to the directive principles as contained in Articles 39(b) and (c).
RatioOfTheDecision	of the Constitution of India.
RatioOfTheDecision	The principles contained in Articles 39(b) and (c) relate to subserving the common good as opposed to concentration of wealth.
RatioOfTheDecision	14.
RatioOfTheDecision	It would appear from the Statement of Objects and Reasons of the Act that one of the objects of the Act is to salvage the productive assets and realise the amount due to the banks and other financial institutions to the extent possible from the non-viable industrial companies through liquidation of those companies.
RatioOfTheDecision	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words, the benefit of the legislation is not aimed merely at reviving a sick industry but also to put an end to a non-viable sick industry with a view to protecting investment of public funds.
RatioOfTheDecision	16.
RatioOfTheDecision	At the time of the registration of a reference, the secretary is expected only to see whether the documentation is in order.
RatioOfTheDecision	The secretary is not required to come to any decision regarding the merits of the reference.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is necessarily some hia tus between the registration of the reference and the commencement of the enquiry.
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, one of the objects of the Act is to protect public investment made through financial institutions.
RatioOfTheDecision	If we accept the construction of Section 22(1) put forward by Mr. <NAME>, it would defeat the objectives sought to be achieved by the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	For example, a company, with the mischievous intent of avoiding its creditors, could file a reference under Section 15 with untruthful but correct documentation.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may be that the circumstances of the case may not warrant the filing of a reference in fact.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to Mr. <NAME>, the documentation being in order, the secretary would register the reference under regulation 19 and the enquiry under Section 16 would be pending from that date.
RatioOfTheDecision	The result would be that recovery proceedings by a financial institution would be stalled for a crucial period allowing the dishonest company time to siphon off its assets.
RatioOfTheDecision	This cannot have been the intention of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	17.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this  background, the contention of Mr. <NAME> that any ambiguity in the statute must be resolved in favour of the company is also not tenable.
RatioOfTheDecision	18.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 16(3) of the Act also indicates that the enquiry is to be commenced by the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> is required to complete- the enquiry within 60 (sixty) days from the commencement of the enquiry.
RatioOfTheDecision	This cannot mean that the <ORG> must complete the enquiry within 60- (sixty) days from the registration of the reference but must be construed as meaning from when the <ORG> first applies its mind.
RatioOfTheDecision	We also accept Mr. <NAME>'s submissions that mere registration of a reference by the secretary to the <ORG> cannot mean any application of mind by the <ORG> itself.
RatioOfTheDecision	19.
RatioOfTheDecision	Furthermore, it will appear from Section 22(1) that proceedings may be continued against the industrial company with the consent of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	This would suggest that the <ORG> would have to apply its mind for the purpose of determining whether the consent should be granted or not.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the <ORG> has not commenced an y enquiry at all, it would not be in a position to accord such consent.
RatioOfTheDecision	20.
RatioOfTheDecision	The final contention of Mr. <NAME>, namely, that because an appeal may lie from the decision of the Board not to hold an enquiry, therefore, the phrase "pending enquiry under Section 16 " in Section 22(1) of the Act must include references registered under Section 15 is, in our view, a non sequitur.
RatioOfTheDecision	Merely because <ORG>, in its wisdom, has made Section 22(1) applicable to appeals and permitted proceedings against the company to be stayed pending such appeal, that cannot lead to the conclusion that the phrase "on enquiry under Section 16 " in Section 22(1) must include a reference under Section 15 of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not wish to speculate as to the reason why <ORG> included the pendency of appeals as a situation warranting stay of all proceedings.
RatioOfTheDecision	Suffice it to say that, in view of the clear wording of Section 22(1) , there is no need to embark upon an enquiry into the intention of <ORG>.
