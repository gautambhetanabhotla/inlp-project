RatioOfTheDecision	(30) However, in view of the nature of the business being identical, coupled with the further fact that even the office of both the parties at least in Delhi are located within close vicinity of each other, it is difficult to rule out the possibility of confusion.
RatioOfTheDecision	As already observe d, the use of the word'Prakash' in the name of the defendant gives an impression even to an educated or qualified person that the defendant company belongs to the same group as the plaintiff.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, I prima facie feel that the balance of convenience is also in favor of the plaintiff and demands that an injunction be granted in favor of the plaintiff to save its business and goodwill, <ORG> case reported in 1989(2) AII.E.R.650 cited by the defendant in support of its case does not help the defendant.
RatioOfTheDecision	The facts of the case are totally different.
RatioOfTheDecision	In <ORG> case, a bank incorporated in Switzerland with word <NAME> in its name was sought to be restrained in an action for passing off by <ORG>) incorporated in Pakistan from using the words <NAME> in its operations in England on the ground that the overseas bank had the exclusive right in England to the reputation in the <NAME> name.
RatioOfTheDecision	The action failed on the ground that the goodwill and reputation of the plaintiff's branch in England did  not become exclusive property of that branch.
RatioOfTheDecision	The plaintiff therefore, could not claim on behalf of the overseas bank, the exclusive right to use the name <ORG> in respect of banking business in England.
RatioOfTheDecision	The facts of the case in hand are totally different.
RatioOfTheDecision	Here both the parties are engaging in business in the same territories and are in direct competition.
RatioOfTheDecision	(31) It further follows from the above discussion that unless the plaintiffs right to its trade name is protected the plaintiff is likely to suffer an irreparable loss and injury.
RatioOfTheDecision	(32) This brings me to the fourth contention of the defendant.
RatioOfTheDecision	The defendant has referred to certain pages from the telephone directory to show that under the category of transporters there are other concerns mentioned therein who use the word 'Prakash' with their names.
RatioOfTheDecision	On this basis, it is submitted that the plaintiff can not be allowed to pick on the defendant alone and restrain the defendant from doing its business.
RatioOfTheDecision	This argument to my mind has no force.
RatioOfTheDecision	Merely because no ac tion is taken against certain other parties, it does not mean that the plaintiff is not entitled to take action against the defendant.
RatioOfTheDecision	The other parties may not be affecting the business of the plaintiff.
RatioOfTheDecision	They may be small-time operators who really do not matter to the plaintiff.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the plaintiff may not chose to take any action against them.
RatioOfTheDecision	On the contrary the plaintiff feels danger from defendant in view of the fact that the defendant's promoters are the ex Directors/employees of the plaintiff who are fully in the know of the business secrets of the plaintiff.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the mere fact that the plaintiff has not chosen to take any action against such other parties cannot disentitle the plaintiff from taking the present action.
RatioOfTheDecision	This contention is, therefore, prima-facie without any merit and is rejected.
RatioOfTheDecision	(33) Now, only the last point raised by counsel for the defendant remains to be dealt with.
RatioOfTheDecision	Great emphasis has been put on the conduct of certain present directors of the plaintiff in wresting contr ol of its management from Mr.<NAME> who founded the plaintiff.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not disputed that those in control of the plaintiff and the defendant are the members of the same larger family or a closely related group.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is also not disputed that groups have emerged within that larger family leading to inter se rivalry and tug of war regarding control of management of th this litigation is pending in other courts.
RatioOfTheDecision	It has also been urged that Shri <NAME> the founder of the plaintiff company has been stabbed in the back and deprived of the position of Chairman of the plaintiff company.
RatioOfTheDecision	Be that as it may, in the present case I feel I am concerned with the legal rights of the plaintiff in protecting its name and goodwill.
RatioOfTheDecision	I have dealt with the matter purely from that angle.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, inter se disputes or pending litigation in other Courts I do not consider relevant.
RatioOfTheDecision	(34) It has also been mentioned that the present suit is a counter blast to a similar suit pending in Bombay for passing off filed by a sister  concern the defendant having word 'Prakash' in its name against a sister concern of the plaintiff.
RatioOfTheDecision	I feel that the said suit will be decided on its own merits and pendency of that suit can not be a ground to non-suit the plaintiff before me.
RatioOfTheDecision	(35) For the same reason, non-disclosure in the plaint about the pendency of the litigation between the family members at Bangalore and the passing off suit at Bombay, can not be fatal to plaintiffs case.
RatioOfTheDecision	The plaintiff has come to this Court on the basis of a specific case claiming an exclusive right in the use of the word 'Prakash' in its name in relation to transport business.
RatioOfTheDecision	I have prima-facie found that the plaintiff has such a right.
RulingByPresentCourt	Therefore, the plaintiff is entitled to succeed at this stage.
