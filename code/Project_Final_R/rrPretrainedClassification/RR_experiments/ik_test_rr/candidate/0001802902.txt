Fact	These two appeals arise out of two writ p etitions filed in <ORG> which were disposed of by a common judgment delivered by a learned single Judge on December 5, 1969.
RulingByLowerCourt	The appeals have been brought on certificate granted suo motu by the learned single Judge under Art.
RulingByLowerCourt	132(1) of the Constitution of India.
Argument	While granting the certificate, the learned single Judge observed that at an earlier stage in the case, request was made to him to refer these two petitions for decision to a <ORG>, because important questions were involved in them.
Argument	At that time, the learned Judge felt that since there was a <ORG> decision of <ORG> in <CITATION_742804> (') there was no need to refer the matter to a larger <ORG>.
Argument	The learned Judge goes on to say in the order granting certificate that at the time of the hearing, he found that several questions arose before him which were not covered by the Rajasthan decision.
Argument	He probably felt that as the points which he had to decide were not considered by this <ORG> in any earlier decision  and as he had decided the case sitting singly, he should grant certificate suo motu for appeal to this <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, and we say it respectfully, the learned Judge was in error in not making a reference to a <ORG> when he found that important questions of law were arising before him.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even if he did not make any reference, he should have allowed the parties to take an appeal in the High <ORG> itself under the provisions pertaining to appeals against decisions of a single Judge.
RatioOfTheDecision	The practice of deciding the case sitting singly and giving a certificate under Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	132(1) for appeal to this <ORG> although technically correct, is an improper practice.
RatioOfTheDecision	If is the right of the party to file an appeal in the High <ORG> itself against the decision of the single Judge and that right should not be short-circuited by passing on the case to the Supreme <ORG> for decision.
RatioOfTheDecision	We think we should not endorse this practice which would create a bad precedent in India.
RulingByPresentCourt	We accordingly cancel the certificate and leave the par ties free to file an appeal in <ORG>, it they so desire.
