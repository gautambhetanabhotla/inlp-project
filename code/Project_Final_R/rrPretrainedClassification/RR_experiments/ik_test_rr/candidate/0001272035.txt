RatioOfTheDecision	The intention of the legislature in regard to what is vegetables is shown by its specifying vegetables and betel leaves as separate items in the <ORG> exempting articles from <ORG>.
Fact	Subsequently betel leaves were removed from the <ORG> which is indicative of the legislature's  intention of not exempting betel leaves from the imposition of the tax.
Fact	But it was submitted that betel leaves are vegetables and therefore they would be exempt from <ORG> under item  6.
Fact	Reliance was placed on the  dictionary meaning of the word "vegetable" as given in <ORG> where the word is defined as "of or pertaining to, comprised or consisting of, or derived, or obtained from plants or their parts".
RatioOfTheDecision	But this word must be construed not in any technical sense nor from the botanical point of view but as understood in common parlance.
RatioOfTheDecision	It has not been defined in the Act and being a word of every day use it must be construed in its popular sense meaning "that sense which people conversant with the subject matter with which the statute is dealing would attribute to it."
Precedent	It is to be construed as understood in common language; Caries on Statute Law, p. 153 (5th Ed.).
Precedent	It was so' held in <ORG> v. The King (1).
Precedent	This interpretation was accepted by <ORG> dhya Pradesh in Madhya Pradesh Pan Merchants' Association, Santra Market, Nagpur v. The State of Madhya Pradesh (<ORG>) (2) where it was observed:-  "In our opinion, the word "vegetables" cannot be given the comprehensive meaning the term bears in natural history and has not been given that meaning in taxing statutes before.
Precedent	The term "vegetables" is to be understood as commonly understood denoting those classes of vegetable matter which are grown in kitchen gardens and are used for the table."
