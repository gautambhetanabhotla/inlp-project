Fact	ORDER Sinha, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	This is an application for an amendment of the plaint.
Fact	The plaintiff instituted this suit on 26-5-1952 against the defendant.
Fact	It is alleged in the plaint, as filed, that on or about 30-3-1949, it was agreed by and between the plaintiff and the defendant by means of letters exchanged between the parties that the plaintiff would sell and the defendant would purchase 100 tons of imported continental paper upon certain terms specified in the plaint.
Fact	It is then stated that the plaintiff had supplied the defendant and the defendant had accepted 40 tons 756 lbs.
Fact	of the contracted goods, as a result of which a sum of Rs.
Fact	80,663/8/3 became due and payable, out of which the plaintiff had been paid a sum of Rs.
Fact	58,943/14/3 on or about 14-11-1949, but the defendant had failed and neglected to pay the balance.
Argument	In the alternative the plaintiff stated that he had supplied the said goods to the defendant not intending to do so gratuitously and that the defendant was liable to compensate the plaintiff for  the benefits enjoyed.
Argument	By the amendment the plaintiff wishes to add a paragraph making an alternative case that if the agreement between the plaintiff and the defendant as mentioned in para 1 of the original plaint be discovered void, the defendant was bound to compensate the plaintiff for advantages enjoyed by the defendant under the contract.
Argument	2.
RatioOfTheDecision	I shall proceed on the footing that the plaintiff wishes to introduce an alternative cause of action and for that purpose requires fresh leave under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent.
RatioOfTheDecision	I might mention that in the original plaint leave under Clause 12 was asked for and obtained at the time of the filing of the plaint.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the short point that arises is as to whether it is possible at this stage to add by way of amendment an alternative cause of action during the pendency of the suit, a cause of action which requires fresh leave under Clause 12 to be granted.
RatioOfTheDecision	3.
Fact	<CITATION_581086> ', 34 Cal 619 (A), a Special Bench of <ORG> presided over by <NAME>  held that leave of the <ORG> under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent had to be obtained before the institution of the suit.
Fact	In that case the leave had been granted by the Registrar of this <ORG> in its Original Side, but it was held that the Registrar had no jurisdiction to grant such leave and the defects could Rot be cured by grant of leave by a Judge at that stage of the litigation, namely, after the suit had already been instituted.
Fact	The plaint was, there-lore, directed to be taken off the file.
Fact	4.
Fact	In -- 'Rampurtab Samruthroy v. Premsukh Chandamal', 15 Bom 93 (B) it was held that the leave granted under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent was confined to the cause or causes of action set forward in the plaint at the time when leave was granted; hence the plaint could not be amended so as to alter the original cause of action or to introduce a different cause of action which would require leave under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent.
Fact	The <ORG>could not try such a different cause of action except in another suit  duly instituted.
Fact	5.
Fact	<CITATION_976179> ', AIR 1939 Bom 345 (C), a suit was originally instituted in the name of a firm in which there were six partners.
Fact	The defendant took the defence that he had not entered into any transaction with the firm but individually with one of the partners.
Fact	Thereupon, the plaint was amended by substituting that partner in his individual capacity as the plaintiff.
Fact	In the plaint as originally filed leave under Clause 12 had been obtained.
Fact	But when the amendment was made no leave was asked for or granted.
Argument	It was held that the Court had no jurisdiction to try the suit inasmuch as the leave originally granted related to a cause of action which had been completely changed.
Argument	<NAME>, J., said as follows :  "The cause of action which is a right to sue vested in six persons alleged to be doing business together, is not the same as the right which exists in one individual.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the first case these persons are together appointed agents for the defendant and the act of one or the other of them wil l bind the rest.
RatioOfTheDecision	They will all be liable and can only give a joint discharge.
RatioOfTheDecision	When the claim is made by <NAME>, he alone is alleged to be agent appointed by defendant, he alone is under the obligation to carry out instructions and he alone is liable to the defendant and can enforce the claim for damages or indemnity, if any, against the defendant.
RatioOfTheDecision	The two causes of action being thus entirely different and no leave having been obtained when <NAME>, the individual, desired to carry on the suit, this issue must be found against the plaintiff and the suit should therefore tie dismissed with costs."
RatioOfTheDecision	It will be observed that the learned Judge does not decide whether leave could properly have been obtained when the amendment was made.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mr. <NAME> argues that the case is an authority for the proposition that such leave' could have been obtained if asked for.
RatioOfTheDecision	Obviously, that is not so.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned Judge finds that the leave originally obtained could not be made to serve the purpose of the suit as it was framed wh en it came up for hearing.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned Judge did not deal with the question as to whether the plaintiff could have obtained such leave at the stage if he had asked for it.
RatioOfTheDecision	6.
Precedent	The next case to be considered is --' <CITATION_574042> , 24 Parganas', AIR 1946 Cal 23 (D).
Precedent	In this case the plaintiff company sued <ORG> upon six agreements by which it was alleged that the <ORG> guaranteed to supplement the net earnings of the <ORG>.
Argument	Leave was obtained under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent at the time of the institution of the suit.
Argument	When the suit came to a hearing before <NAME>, the point was taken on behalf of the defendant that the agreements were not executed according to law.
Argument	Thereupon, both the plaint and the written statement were ordered to be amended.
Argument	By the amendment the plaintiff took alternative pleas under Sections 65, 70 and 72, Contract Act , but no fresh leave was asked for or obtained.
Argument	The learned Judge said as follows : "The wording and meaning of Clause 12 of the Letters Patent, 18 65, is clear, namely, that the <ORG>'s leave shall previously be obtained before it has jurisdiction to receive a suit for which leave is required.
Fact	The grant of leave is a condition precedent to the <ORG> having jurisdiction to receive such a suit.
Fact	An amendment to a plaint raising a new cause of action upon which a claim is made is not a fresh suit but is an additional claim made in an existing suit.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG>'s leave is previously required in respect of a cause of action before the <ORG> can have jurisdiction to receive the suit in which it is alleged.
RatioOfTheDecision	Further in the present case leave to sue was not sought with respect to the cause of action in the amendment either at the time application was made to amend or when leave was given for the amendment or when the plaint with the amendment was presented to the <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	............
Argument	In my opinion, the <ORG> has no jurisdiction to receive, try and determine the claims upon the causes of action in the amendment to the plaint since leave was not previously obtained in  respect of those causes of action before the suit in which those claims are made was instituted in this <ORG>."
Argument	In this case the amendment was allowed but leave although necessary was not asked for.
Argument	Therefore, the <ORG> had not been called upon to decide whether such leave could have been asked for.
Argument	Mr. <NAME> argues that the words "Further, in the present case leave to sue was not sought with respect to the cause of action in the amendment ............... when leave was given for the amendment or when the plaint with the amendment was presented to the <ORG>"show that the <ORG> considered that such leave might have been granted if asked for.
RatioOfTheDecision	I do not think that is a correct reading of the judgment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned Judge was no doubt referring to the fact that leave had not been asked for although required in respect of the amendment, but he also distinctly held that an amendment to a plaint, raising a new cause of action upon which a claim was made, was not a fresh suit but was an additional claim made in an  existing suit, so that leave under Clause 12 could not be granted at any stage subsequent to the institution of the suit.
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
Fact	The next case cited is --'Benoy Shankar v. <ORG>', (E).
Fact	In this case the plaintiff sued the defendant for partition o£ joint family properties, a part of which was situated outside jurisdiction so that the suit required leave under Clause 12 which had been obtained.
Fact	During the pendency of the suit, certain terms of settlement were arrived at, not only between the parties on record but other parties who were by the compromise decree itself joined as defendants.
Argument	At the time the compromise was filed no fresh leave under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent was obtained.
Argument	It was held that the effect of adding new parties by an amendment was to enlarge the scope of the suit and, therefore, fresh leave had to be obtained as condition precedent to a, <ORG> obtaining jurisdiction against the added parties.
RulingByLowerCourt	<NAME>, J., said as follows :  "It is not disputed that out of the four properties three we re situated outside and one within the jurisdiction of <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	"Leave was, therefore, necessary for filing a suit for partition, which is a suit for land against these minors.
RulingByLowerCourt	No such leave was obtained.
Argument	It is now established that leave originally obtained at the time of the filing of a suit does not cover an amended plaint.
Argument	Where the defendant is added fresh leave must be obtained when the suit was originally filed.
Argument	It is contended that there was no change in the cause of action.
Argument	Even so, if new parties are joined against whom it intended to proceed in the suit and to obtain a decree, the obtaining of the leave is imperative, because it is the foundation of the jurisdiction of the <ORG>.
Argument	The <ORG> gets jurisdiction to decide the suit against the parties only if it grants leave which is condition precedent.
Argument	No leave was obtained to proceed against the newly added defendants and it follows that the <ORG> never get any jurisdiction to make any decree or order against them in the suit."
Argument	8.
Argument	There is no d oubt that according to the learned Judge, when fresh parties are added it is permissible to the <ORG> to grant fresh leave.
Argument	Mr. <NAME> argues that if fresh leave could be granted during the pendency of the suit in the case of additional parties, there does not seem to be any objection to the granting of such leave in the case of an additional cause of action.
RatioOfTheDecision	In my opinion, he is not right.
RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to Section 22 , Limitation Act , where, after institution of a suit, a new plaintiff or defendant was substituted or added, it not being a case of the assignment or devolution of interest during the pendency of the suit or transposition of a party, the suit is to be regarded, as against the added party to have been instituted when he is made a party.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the case of the addition of a party during the pendency of the suit stands on quite a different footing to the addition of a different cause of action against the same party.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
Fact	The point was recently considered by <NAME>, J., in an unreported jud gment dated 25-7-1951, <ORG> v. The State of West Bengal (F).
Fact	The application was in a sense remarkable.
Fact	The suit had been filed without any leave under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent on 14-5-1950.
Fact	Long after the suit had been filed, an application was made, not in the suit, but entitled "In the matter of clause 12 of the Letters Patent etc.
Fact	", for grant of leave under Clause 12.
Argument	The learned Judge relying on --' <CITATION_581086> ) (ibid) held that leave could only be granted in respect of, the suit at the time of the presentation of the plaint and not afterwards.
Argument	The learned Judge also relied on a similar view in an unreported judgment delivered on 11-8-1950 by <NAME>, J., in --'Sree Sree Iswar Jew v. <NAME>', in Suit No.
Fact	3797 of 1949 (G).
RatioOfTheDecision	The result of all these authorities may be summarised as follows :  1.
RatioOfTheDecision	Where leave under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent is necessary, the granting of such leave is the condition precedent to the <ORG> having jurisdiction to  entertain the suit.
RatioOfTheDecision	2.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, such leave is to be obtained at the time of the institution of the suit.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the Original Side of <ORG>, the practice is to present the plaint before the Master who places it before a Judge of this Court for granting the requisite leave.
RatioOfTheDecision	It does not mean, however, that such leave is granted after the institution of the suit.
RatioOfTheDecision	For the purpose of limitation the date of the presentation of the plaint may be relevant but the leave when granted is nevertheless leave granted prior to the institution of the suit.
RatioOfTheDecision	3.
RatioOfTheDecision	Where by amendment of a plaint, a cause of, action is altered or a new cause of action I added, it is not a new suit but the old suit in a new form.
RatioOfTheDecision	In such a case, no amendment can be ordered if it requires leave to be granted under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent at the time of the amendment.
RatioOfTheDecision	4.
RatioOfTheDecision	It follows that leave under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent cannot be granted in such a case, at any stage after the institution of the original suit.
RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	But where  a new party is added, the suit as regards the added party must be deemed to have been commenced on the date when he was so added.
RatioOfTheDecision	Consequently, if the cause of action against the added party requires leave under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent it is open to the <ORG> to grant such leave, if, asked for at the time of the amendment.
RatioOfTheDecision	In fact, if such leave has not been asked for or obtained in such a case, the <ORG> has no jurisdiction to entertain the suit against such an added defendant.
RatioOfTheDecision	11.
RatioOfTheDecision	I must admit that these rules are quite artificial and there does not seem any logical reason why the <ORG> should not be able to grant fresh leave under Clause 12 of the Letters Patent at the time of amendment where a new or alternative cause of action is introduced.
RatioOfTheDecision	But that is a question of altering the law and the present case must be dealt with ac cording to the <ORG> as it stands.
RulingByPresentCourt	For the reasons aforesaid, this application fails and must be dismissed with costs.
