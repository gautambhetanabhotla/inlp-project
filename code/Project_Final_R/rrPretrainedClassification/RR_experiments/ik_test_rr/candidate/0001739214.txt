Fact	The respondent filed a complaint before <ORG> (District Forum) under Section 12 of the Consumer Protection Act, 1986 (for brevity 'the Act') praying for settlement of an insurance claim at Rs.75,000/- along with interest at the rate of 18% per annum.
Fact	The appellant repudiated the claim on the ground that damage caused to the building and printing press of the respondent was not covered by Clause 5 of the insurance policy.
Fact	<ORG> accepting the contention urged on behalf of the appellant held that there was no deficiency of service on the part of the appellant and dismissed the complaint as not maintainable.
Fact	The respondent filed appeal before <ORG> (<ORG>) against the  order of <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG>, on interpretation of the word "impact" contained in Clause 5 of the insurance policy, allowed the appeal, set aside the order of <ORG> and granted relief to the respondent directing the appellant to pay a sum of Rs.75,000/- with interest at the rate of 12% per annum with effect from 18.10.1994 till the date of payment.
RulingByLowerCourt	The appellant, dissatisfied with the order of <ORG>, filed revision petition before <ORG> (<ORG>).
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG>, while accepting the interpretation given by <ORG>, however, reduced the amount of payment to the respondent from Rs.75,000/- to Rs.56,000/-.
Fact	Aggrieved by said order of the <ORG>, this appeal is brought before this Court by the appellant.
Fact	Before us, learned counsel for the parties in their arguments reiterated their respective contentions, which were urged before all the forums.
RulingByLowerCourt	In order to consider the respe ctive contentions urged on behalf of the parties, it is both necessary and useful to quote the relevant portions from the insurance policy: -  "IN CONSIDERATION OF THE insured named in the Schedule hereto having paid to <ORG> (hereinafter called THE COMPANY) the premium mentioned in the said schedule.
RulingByLowerCourt	Till company agrees, (subject to the condition and exclusion contained herein or endorsed or otherwise expressed hereon) that if after payment of premium the property insured described in the said schedule or any part of such property, be destroyed or damaged by the following: -  1.
RulingByLowerCourt	.......  2.
RulingByLowerCourt	.......  3.
RulingByLowerCourt	.......  4.
RulingByLowerCourt	.......  5.
RulingByLowerCourt	Impact by any rail/road vehicle or animal."
RulingByLowerCourt	In the order of <ORG> it is noticed that the appellant contested the claim by filing written objection contending that the damage caused due to vibration from the operation of bulldozer was not an incident of impact by any road vehicle, as per Clause 5 of the insurance policy for risk, and so the  complaint was not maintainable.
RulingByLowerCourt	Para 4 of the order of <ORG> reads: -  "4.
RatioOfTheDecision	Neither party led any evidence because it was admitted by <ORG> that in connection with a road construction with the help of a bulldozer near the complainant's printing press in question there was damage to that building.
RatioOfTheDecision	And, both parties agreed that it all depends upon the interpretation of the term (5) of the Insurance Policy."
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, from the order of <ORG> it is clear that the appellant did not dispute as to damage caused to the building and machinery of the respondent on account of the bulldozer driven close to the building on the road for the purpose of road construction and that both the parties agreed that the sustainability of the claim depended upon the interpretation of Clause 5 of the insurance policy.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> took a narrow view that the word "impact" contained in clause 5 of the insurance policy covered risk of only contingent impact of a road vehicle forcibly coming in co ntact with another.
RatioOfTheDecision	It held that the damage caused to the building and machinery in the instant case was not due to such forcible contact but it was due to the consequential effect of vibration on account of operating of a bulldozer by the side of the respondent's printing press building and as such it was not covered by clause 5 of the insurance policy; thus, there being no deficiency of service on the part of the appellant the complaint filed by the respondent was not maintainable.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to <ORG> the only point, which arose for decision in the appeal was whether the damage caused to the building and the machinery of the respondent was the resultant of the impact by the bulldozer.
RatioOfTheDecision	Considering the meaning of the word "impact" given in various dictionaries <ORG> took the view that when the word "impact" has got meanings more than one and the word "impact" not only means "coming forcibly in contact with another", it also means "to drive close", "effective action of one thing u pon another" and "effect of such action".
RatioOfTheDecision	The "impact" covered damage caused to the building and machinery in view of the admitted fact that such damage was caused because of close drive by the bulldozer on the road.
RatioOfTheDecision	Expressing thus <ORG> set aside the order of <ORG> and granted relief to the respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> concurring with the view expressed by the State commission interpreting the expression "impact" observed that the said word has to be construed liberally and in its wider sense.
RatioOfTheDecision	The only point that arises for consideration is whether the word "impact" contained in clause 5 of the insurance policy covers the damage caused to the building and machinery due to driving of the bulldozer on the road close to the building.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is evident from the terms of the insurance policy that the property was insured as against destruction or damage to whole or part.
RatioOfTheDecision	The appellant company agreed to pay towards destruction or damage to the property insured to the extent of its l iability on account of various happenings.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case both the parties relied on clause 5 of the insurance policy.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause 5 is also subject to exclusions contained in the insurance policy.
RatioOfTheDecision	That a damage caused to the building or machinery on account of driving of vehicle on the road close to the building is not excluded.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause 5 speaks of "impact" by any rail/road vehicle or animal.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the appellant company wanted to exclude any damage or destruction caused on account of driving of vehicle on the road close to the building, it could have expressly excluded.
RatioOfTheDecision	The insured possibly did not understand and expect that the destruction and damage to the building and machinery is confined only to the direct collusion by vehicle moving on the road to the building or machinery.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the ordinary course, the question of a vehicle directly dashing the building or the machinery inside the building does not arise.
RatioOfTheDecision	Further, "impact" by road vehicle found in the company of other words in the same clause 5 normally ind icates that damage caused to the building on account of vibration by driving of vehicle close to the road is also included.
RatioOfTheDecision	In order to interpret this clause, it is also necessary to gather the intention of the parties from the words used in the policy.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the word "impact" is interpreted narrowly the question of impact by any rail would not arise as the question of a rail forcibly coming to the contact of a building or machinery would not arise.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the absence of specific exclusion and the word "impact" having more meanings in the context, it cannot be confined to forcible contact alone when it includes the meanings "to drive close", "effective action of one thing upon another" and "the effect of such action", it is reasonable and fair to hold in the context that the word "impact" contained in clause 5 of the insurance policy covers the case of the respondent to say that damage caused to the building and machinery on account of the bulldozer moving closely on the road was on account of its "impact".
RatioOfTheDecision	It is al so settled position in law that if there is any ambiguity or a term is capable of two possible interpretations one beneficial to the insured should be accepted consistent with the purpose for which the policy is taken, namely, to cover the risk on the happening of certain event.
RatioOfTheDecision	Although there is no ambiguity in the expression "impact", even otherwise applying the rule of contra proferentem, the use of the word "impact" in clause 5 in the instant policy must be construed against the appellant.
RatioOfTheDecision	Where the words of a document are ambiguous, they shall be construed against the party who prepared the document.
RatioOfTheDecision	This rule applies to contracts of insurance and clause 5 of the insurance policy even after reading the entire policy in the present case should be construed against the insurer.
Precedent	<ORG> of this Court in <CITATION_1435269> .
Precedent	[1966 (3) SCR 500] has expressed that "in a contract of insurance, there is requirement of uberrima fides, i.e.
Precedent	good faith on the part of the assured and the contract is lik ely to be construed contra proferentem i.e.
Precedent	against the company in case of ambiguity or doubt."
RatioOfTheDecision	In the light of what is stated above, no fault can be found with the impugned order.
RatioOfTheDecision	The interpretation placed by <ORG> as well as by <ORG> in relation to the expression "impact" is in order and appropriate.
RulingByPresentCourt	Hence the point is answered in the affirmative.
RulingByPresentCourt	Under the circumstances we find no merit in the appeal.
RulingByPresentCourt	Consequently it is dismissed.
RulingByPresentCourt	No costs.
