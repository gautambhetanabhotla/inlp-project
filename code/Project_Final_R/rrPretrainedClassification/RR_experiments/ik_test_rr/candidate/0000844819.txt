Statute	When any tax, penalty or interest is due in consequence of any order passed under or in pursuance of this act, the Income-tax Officer shall serve upon the assessee or other person liable to pay such tax, penalty or interest a notice of demand in the prescribed form specifying the sum so payable."
RatioOfTheDecision	25.
RatioOfTheDecision	Under this section, if a tax is due in consequence of an order from an assessee, the Income-tax Officer us under a duty to serve on him a notice of demand.
RatioOfTheDecision	Pursuant to the directions given by the tribunal the Income-tax Officer made fresh calculations under the head "Capital gains", and pursuant to the said calculation, he should have passed an order and issued the Income-tax Officer did not discharge his duty which he was boun d to do under the Act; with the result he had become amendable to a writ of mandamus directing him to do what he should have done under the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	26.
RulingByPresentCourt	In the result, the order of the high Court is set aside and we issue a writ of certiorari quashing the order of the commissioner and a writ of mandamus directing the Income-tax Officer to pass as order and issue a notice in accordance with law.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appellant will have his costs throughout.
Precedent	27.
RulingByPresentCourt	Appeal allowed.
