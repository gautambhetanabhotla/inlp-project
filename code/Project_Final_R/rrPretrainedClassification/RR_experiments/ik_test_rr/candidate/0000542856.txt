RulingByPresentCourt	We will in the course of this judgment analyse the award and consider whether the award furnishes reasons for the amount awarded.
RulingByPresentCourt	But before we do so, we wish to remind ourselves of the basic limitations of the judicial interference in arbitration award.
Precedent	The need to confine to the limitations was placed in perspective by <ORG> in <CITATION_1337022> AIR 1989 SC 1263 and <CITATION_1873931> AIR 1989 SC 268.
Precedent	The propositions emerging from these decisisons are summarised below :  (i) While the functions of the courts of la w is to oversee that the arbirators act within the norms of justice, the courts should as far is possible give effect to the award, and if the arbitrator acts within the norms of justice, the courts should compel obedience to the decision of the chosen adjudicator.
Precedent	(ii) Where reasons have been given for the award, the challenge to the award cannot be sustained unless, the arbitrator has taken a view of the matter which could not be sustained on any view of the matter.
Precedent	iii) judicial interference with the arbitrator's award is justified only if it is shown that the arbitrator has reached a decision which no reasonable arbitrator could have done.
Precedent	iv) If the conclusion of the arbitrator is based on a possible view of the matter the court should not interfere with the award.
Precedent	6.
