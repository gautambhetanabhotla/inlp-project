Fact	ORDER <NAME>, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	The appellant-union had filed a complaint before <ORG> under the Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 (the 'Act') complaining of the breach of Items 1(a), (b), 4(a), (f) and 6 of Schedule II and Items 7, 9 and 10 of Schedule IV of the said Act.
Fact	The case of the complainant-union was that the 21 workmen who were working in one of the canteens of the respondent-company, were not given the service conditions as were available to the other workmen of the company and there was also a threat of termination of their services.
Fact	It is an admitted fact that these workmen were employed by a contractor who was given a contract to run the canteen in question.
Fact	The com plaint, however, was filed on the footing that the workmen were the employees of the company and, therefore, the breach committed and the threats of retrenchments were cognizable by <ORG> under the said Act.
Fact	Even in the complaint, there was no case mad e out that the workmen in question had ever been accepted by the company as its employees.
Fact	However, the complaint proceeded on the basis as if the workmen were a part of the work-force of the company.
Fact	The facts on record reveal that the workmen were never recognised by the respondent- company as its workmen and it was the consistent contention of the company that they were not its employees.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG> dismissed the complaint holding that since the workmen were not the workmen of the respondent-company, the complaint was not maintainable under the said Act.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG> in writ petition confirmed the said finding and dismissed the petition also on the same ground.
RulingByLowerCourt	Hence the present appeal.
RulingByLowerCourt	2.
RulingByLowerCourt	As pointed out both by <ORG> and <ORG>, it was not established that the workmen in question were the workmen of the respondent-company.
RulingByLowerCourt	In the circumstances, no complaint could lie under the Act as is held by the two courts below.
RatioOfTheDecision	We, therefore, find nothing wrong in the decision impu gned before us.
RatioOfTheDecision	The workmen have first to establish that they are the workmen of the respondent-company before they can file any complaint under the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Admittedly, this has not been done.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is open for the workmen to raise an appropriate industrial dispute in that behalf if they are entitled to do so before they resort to the provisions of the present Act.
RulingByPresentCourt	3.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeals, therefore, stand dismissed with no order as to costs.
