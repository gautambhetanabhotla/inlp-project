RatioOfTheDecision	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	It has been argued on behalf of the appellants that Section 190A is an independent provision which must receive full effect and cannot, in any way, be subordinated to any thing contained in Section 39.
RatioOfTheDecision	The respondents' contention has clearly been that in view of the provision contained in Section 39, they cannot be asked to return any part of the duty which was refunded to them, after the expiry of three months from the date of refund.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is to be recalled that a sum of Rs.
RatioOfTheDecision	12,855 was refunded to the respondents On the 13th January, 1958.
RatioOfTheDecision	The contention is that even if this amount was erroneously refunded to them, no proceedings can be taken for recovery of the amount unless they were commenced by a notice of demand issued in terms of Section 39 within a period of three months from the date of refund.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this case, the notice by which the respondent firm were called upon to show cause against the proposed order of repayment of the amount refunded was clearly time-barred.
RatioOfTheDecision	16.
RatioOfTheDecision	The appellants' contention has been that Section 39 is completely out of the way.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is irrelevant in the present context since what the <ORG> authorities proposed to do was to revise under Section 190A of the Act the order, of refun d which had been previously made; that order was, in the opinion of the authorities, an erroneous order which was fit to be revised.
RatioOfTheDecision	The proceeding commenced or intended to be commenced by the notice dated the 15th September was a proceeding in revision in terms of Section 190A which is a complete code in itself and under which any order could be revised within a period of two years.
RatioOfTheDecision	The effect of this contention is that even though Section 39 might have imposed a limit of time within which a notice of demand has to be issued, a proceeding commenced under Section 190A is not controlled by what is provided for in Section 39.
RatioOfTheDecision	The last named Section has its own period of limitation which regulates a proceeding taken under it, but the present proceeding being one under Section 190A is not governed by Section 39.
Argument	17.
Argument	The learned trial Judge rejected the contention on behalf of the <ORG> authorities and held that Section 190A is a procedural Section and it could not be said that it is independent of Section 39.
Argument	He observed that "the bar of limitation under Section 39 must be applied to the facts of this case and the Additional Collector of <ORG> did not act in accordance with law in an attempt to re-open the matter under Section 190A of the Sea <ORG> Act."
RatioOfTheDecision	We find ourselves in complete agreement with the view taken of the matter by the learned Judge.
RatioOfTheDecision	18.
RatioOfTheDecision	It seems plain that Section 190A would be nearly void of content if the substantive provisions in the Act were to be read completely divorced from it.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, Section 190A is entirely procedural.
RatioOfTheDecision	It gives wide powers to the Chief <ORG> authority to call for and examine the records of any proceeding for the purpose of satisfying himself as to the legality or propriety of any decision or order made by a subordinate officer of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Reading the Section as it is, it seems that it provides for correcting mistakes and errors committed by officers subordinate to the Chief <ORG> authority.
RatioOfTheDecision	But such correction Or revision is permitted within a period  of two years.
RatioOfTheDecision	But where, however, in consequence of such revisional proceeding any order is likely to be made which may prove prejudicial to any person, the Section provides that no such order can be made adverse to that person unless reasonable opportunity has been given to the person concerned of making a representation against the proposed order.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may well be that a person aggrieved may apply to <ORG> for relief under Section 190A in a given case and if he so applies, <ORG> would be quite entitled to give him relief provided there is no bar to the grant of such relief.
RatioOfTheDecision	For example, where a person has been charged to duty, in excess of what he is required to pay under the Act, he may apply to <ORG> for redress Or <ORG> may of his own motion give him redress; but in such cases it would not be possible to give relief or to make any adverse order unless the application for such relief Or the step in aid of such adverse o rder is made or taken within the limit of time prescribed by the Statute.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this case, <ORG> could certainly ask for repayment of an amount erroneously refunded provided the notice of demand wag issued within the period of three months prescribed in Section 39.
RatioOfTheDecision	Similarly, under Section 40 of the Act which speaks of refund of charges overpaid to the <ORG>, no relief can be granted to the person concerned unless the application is made within a period of three months,  19.
RatioOfTheDecision	The power to reopen a case may reside in the Chief <ORG> authority in terms of Section 190A.
RatioOfTheDecision	That power can be exercised within a period of two years but in a case like the one before us which is completely covered by the specific provision contained in Section 39 of the Act, it cannot be said that the limit of time prescribed in the Section is wiped out or superseded by the period of limitation prescribed in Section 190A.
RatioOfTheDecision	There can be no question that Section 39 is a specific provision under which the respondents' case c learly falls.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to the appellants, a sum of money had been erroneously refunded to them.
RatioOfTheDecision	In order that the amount thus erroneously paid might be recovered, a notice of demand has to issue in terms of Section 39 of the Sea <ORG> Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, it is idle to contend that since Section 190A prescribes a period of two years within which the revisional powers of the Chief <ORG> authority may be exercised, that has the effect of destroying the provision of Section 39 which is a clear and specific provision in the same Statute.
RatioOfTheDecision	20.
RatioOfTheDecision	It seems clear that when <ORG> duty, after having been levied, has been erroneously refunded owing to one or other of the causes mentioned in Section 39, the person to whom such refund has been erroneously made shall repay the amount on a notice of demand being issued to him within three months from the date of refund.
RatioOfTheDecision	Similarly, by Section 40, the Act provides that <ORG> duty which was erroneously paid for similar causes will be refunded only if the claim for refund i s made within three months from the date of such payment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The point to note is that the claim for levy of duty by the <ORG> authorities or claim for refund against them has to be made within the prescribed period.
RatioOfTheDecision	These provisions are unambiguous.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Courts have to construe them as they find them.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG>' right to levy duty unpaid or short-paid or the citizen's right to refund of duty erroneously paid or overpaid have been placed on the same footing.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question then arises, whether by recourse to Section 190A, the time for levying of duty not paid or under-paid or erroneously refunded can be enlarged in favour of the <ORG> authorities.
RatioOfTheDecision	The direct effect of such enlargement of time will be to destroy completely the earlier provision contained in Section 39, limiting the time for the <ORG> to claim payment of duty not levied or short levied or erroneously refunded.
RatioOfTheDecision	If such was the result intended by the legislature, it would amount to taking away something with the left hand which was given with t he right hand.
RatioOfTheDecision	There can be no extension of the <ORG>' rights without corresponding extension of the citizen's obligations.
RatioOfTheDecision	Interest of revenue cannot be pleaded in extension of such right in favour of the <ORG> authorities.
RatioOfTheDecision	Equity has no place in the law of limitation and we are to construe these provisions strictly.
RatioOfTheDecision	It can never be right to think that the legislature intended to keep alive in favour of the <ORG>, in this indirect manner a claim already extinguished by efflux of time.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, there is no real conflict between Section 39 and Section 190-A; and even if there was one, it would be the business of the <ORG> to harmonise the two provisions so as to provide a workable and well-reasoned basis on which each might fulfil its purpose in aid of the object of the enactment.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 190-A, which is a new provision introduced in 1955, embodies the experience of the working of the Act Words of wide import appear to have been deliberately chosen so as to give the Chief <ORG> authority power to  exercise effective control over all matters, administrative or other, with the very inteligible safeguard that no one can be adversely affected in consequence of the exercise of such power without the person concerned being first heard.
RatioOfTheDecision	The very amplitude of power given by the <ORG> would be a reason to guard against its abuse; and we consider an attempt to enlarge time for claim to payment of duty not levied or short-levied or erroneously refunded, by appealing to its provisions, would be an instance of such abuse.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> cannot be read so as to destroy or defeat the earlier express provision contained in <ORG> 39 of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	21.
RatioOfTheDecision	The rule is well-known that where a general intention is expressed and the Act expresses also a particular intention, incompatible with the general intention, the particular intention is to be considered in the nature of an exception.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> endorsed this view in the case of <CITATION_424874> .
RatioOfTheDecision	.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, the specific provision relating to limitation cont ained in Section 39 cannot possibly be destroyed or over riden by the provision in Section 190A whereby the <ORG> has been given power to revise orders previously made within, a period of two years.
RatioOfTheDecision	There can, in our view, be no question that the period of limitation prescribed in Section 39 must prevail.
RatioOfTheDecision	22.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, the present case is completely covered by Section 39 of the Sea <ORG> Act winch is a specific provision.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to the notice issued by the <ORG> authorities, a part of the duty was erroneously refunded.
RatioOfTheDecision	Consequently if action was to be taken, it was required to be taken under Section 39 within the period of three months prescribed in the Section itself.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Section speaks of "relevant date".
RatioOfTheDecision	That expression is defined and it means, in this instance, the date of refund.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have indicated that the date of issue of notice was long after the date of refund.
RatioOfTheDecision	We cannot accept the contention that since the notice speaks of action proposed to be taken in terms of Sectio n 190A, the period of limitation prescribed in Section 39 of the Act is wiped out, to the detriment of the person called upon to show cause against the proposed recovery of the amount said to have been erroneously refunded.
RatioOfTheDecision	There can be no question that different periods of limitation have been prescribed for different purposes.
RatioOfTheDecision	In order to enable <ORG> to exercise a general power of superintendence over decisions and orders made by subordinate officers of the <ORG>, a longer period of limitation, has been prescribed.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is quite understandable, but surely the period of limitation in Section 190A cannot override the express provision in Section 39 which limits the issue of notice of demand for payment of duties not levied or short-levied or erroneously refunded to a period of three months.
