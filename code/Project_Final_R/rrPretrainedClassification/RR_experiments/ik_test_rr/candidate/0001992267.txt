Precedent	In connection with land acquisition proceeding whenever the provision of Section 17 and its various sub-sections including Section 17(4) is used in the name of taking urgent or emergent action and the right of hearing of the land hol der under  Section 5A is dispensed with, the <ORG> is called upon to consider a few fundamentals in the exercise of such powers.
Precedent	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	Admittedly, the Land Acquisition Act , a pre-Constitutional legislation of colonial vintage is a drastic law, being expropriatory in nature as it confers on the <ORG> a power which affects person's property right.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even though right to property is no longer fundamental and was never a natural right, and is acquired on a concession by the <ORG>, it has to be accepted that without right to some property, other rights become illusory.
RatioOfTheDecision	This <ORG> is considering these questions, especially, in the context of some recent trends in land acquisition.
RatioOfTheDecision	This <ORG> is of the opinion that the concept of public purpose in land acquisition has to be viewed from an angle which is consistent with the concept of a welfare <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	16.
RatioOfTheDecision	The concept of public purpose cannot remain static for all time to come.
RatioOfTheDecision	The concept, even though sought to be defined under Section 3(f) of the Act, is not capable  of any precise definition.
RatioOfTheDecision	The said definition, having suffered several amendments, has assumed the character of an inclusive one.
RatioOfTheDecision	It must be accepted that in construing public purpose, a broad and overall view has to be taken and the focus must be on ensuring maximum benefit to the largest number of people.
RatioOfTheDecision	Any attempt by the <ORG> to acquire land by promoting a public purpose to benefit a particular group of people or to serve any particular interest at the cost of the interest of a large section of people especially of the common people defeats the very concept of public purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even though the concept of public purpose was introduced by pre-  Constitutional legislation, its application must be consistent with the constitutional ethos and especially the chapter under Fundamental Rights and also the Directive Principles.
Precedent	17.
Precedent	In construing the concept of public purpose, the mandate of Article 13 of the Constitution that any pre-constitutional law cannot in any way take away or abridge rights conferred und er Part-III must be kept in mind.
Fact	By judicial interpretation the contents of these Part III rights are constantly expanded.
RatioOfTheDecision	The meaning of public purpose in acquisition of land must be judged on the touchstone of this expanded view of Part-III rights.
RatioOfTheDecision	The open-ended nature of our Constitution needs a harmonious reconciliation between various competing principles and the overhanging shadows of socio-economic reality in this country.
RatioOfTheDecision	18.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the concept of public purpose on this broad horizon must also be read into the provisions of emergency power under Section 17 with the consequential dispensation of right of hearing under Section 5A of the said Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> must examine these questions very carefully when little Indians lose their small property in the name of mindless acquisition at the instance of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	If public purpose can be satisfied by not rendering common man homeless and by exploring other avenues of acquisition, the <ORG>, before sanctioning an acquisition, must in exercise of its  power of judicial review, focus its attention on the concept of social and economic justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	While examining these questions of public importance, the <ORG>, especially the Higher <ORG>, cannot afford to act as mere umpires.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this context we reiterate the principle laid down by this <ORG> in <CITATION_763510> reported in , wherein this <ORG> held:  "......It is true that Judges are constitutional invigilators and statutory interpreters; but they are also responsive and responsible to Part IV of the Constitution being one of the trinity of the nation's appointed instrumentalities in the transformation of the socio-  economic order.
RatioOfTheDecision	The judiciary, in its sphere, shares the revolutionary purpose of the constitutional order, and when called upon to decode social legislation must be animated by a goal-oriented approach.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is part of the dynamics of statutory interpretation in the developing countries so that courts are not converted into rescue shelters for those who seek to defeat agrarian justice by cute  transactions of many manifestations now so familiar in the country and illustrated by the several cases under appeal.
Precedent	This caveat has become necessary because the judiciary is not a mere umpire, as some assume, but an activist catalyst in the constitutional scheme."
Precedent	13  19.
Precedent	In other words public purpose must be viewed through the prism of Constitutional values as stated above.

RatioOfTheDecision	The question is whether in the admitted facts of this case, invoking the urgency clause under Se ction 17 (4) is justified.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the writ petition before <ORG>, the petitioners have given the details of the land holding, and it has also been stated that the entire holding of petitioners 2, 5, 7, 9, 10, 11 and 13 have been acquired, and as a result of such acquisition, the petitioners have become landless.
RatioOfTheDecision	From the various facts disclosed in the said affidavit it appears that the matter was initiated by the <ORG>'s letter dated 4th of June, 2008 for issuance of Section 4(1) and Section 17 notifications.
RatioOfTheDecision	A meeting for selection of the suitable site for construction was held on 27th June, 2008, and the proposal for such acquisition and construction was sent to the Director, Land Acquisition on 2nd of July, 2008.
RatioOfTheDecision	This was in turn forwarded to the State <ORG> by the Director on 22nd of July, 2008.

RatioOfTheDecision	These notificati ons were published in local newspapers on 24th of September, 2008.
RatioOfTheDecision	23  Thereafter, over a period of 9 months, <ORG> deposited 10% of compensation payable to the landowners, along with 10% of acquisition expenses and 70% of cost of acquisition was deposited, and the proposal for issuance of Section 6 declaration was sent to the Director, Land Acquisition on 19th of June, 2009.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Director in turn forwarded all these to <ORG> on 17th July, 2009, and <ORG> finally issued the Section 6 declaration on 10th of August, 2009.

RatioOfTheDecision	39.
RatioOfTheDecision	In paragraph 15 of the writ petition, it has been clearly stated that there was a time gap of more than 11 months between Section 4 and Section 6 notifications, which demonstrates that there was no urgency in the <ORG> action which could deny the petitioners their right under Section 5A .
RatioOfTheDecision	In the counter which was filed in this case by the <ORG> before <ORG>, it was not disputed that the time gap between Section 4 notification read with Section 17 , and Section 6 notification was about 11 months.
RatioOfTheDecision	40.
RatioOfTheDecision	The construction of jail is certainly in public interest and for such construction land may be acquired.
RatioOfTheDecision	But such acquisition can be made only by strictly following the mandate of the said Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the facts of this case, such acquisition cannot be made by invoking emergency provisions of Section  17.
RatioOfTheDecision	If so advised, Government can initiate acquisition proceeding by following the provision of Section 5A of the Act and in accordance with law.
RatioOfTheDecision	41.
RatioOfTheDecision	For the reasons a foresaid, we hold that <ORG> was not justified, in the facts of this case, to invoke the emergency provision of Section 17(4) of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The valuable right of the appellants under Section 5A of the Act cannot flattened and steamrolled on the `ipsi dixit' of the executive authority.
