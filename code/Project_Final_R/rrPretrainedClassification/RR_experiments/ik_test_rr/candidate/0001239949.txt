RatioOfTheDecision	7.
RatioOfTheDecision	The language of Section 4A clearly indicates that the Section by itself does not premat urely terminate any mining lease.
RatioOfTheDecision	A decision in this regard has to be taken by <ORG> after considering the circumstances of each case separately.
RatioOfTheDecision	For exercise of power it is necessary that the essential condition mentioned therein is fulfilled, namely, that the proposed action would be in the interest of regulation of mines and mineral development.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question of <ORG> granting a fresh mining lease in favour of <ORG> or a <ORG> arises only after a decision to terminate the existing mining lease is arrived at and given effect to.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Section does not direct termination of all mining leases, merely for the reason that <ORG> or <ORG> has equipped itself for the purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Section was enacted with a view to improve the efficiency in this regard and with this view directs consulation between <ORG> and <ORG> to be held.
RatioOfTheDecision	The two Governments have to consider whether premature termination of a particulare minin g lease shall advance the object or not, and must, therefore, take into account all considerations relevant to the issue, with reference to the lease in question.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not correct to say that an existing mining lease can be terminated merely for the reason that a Government Company or <ORG> is ready to undertake the work.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	Considered in this light, the <ORG> must be interpreted to imply that the person who may be affected by such a decision should be afforded an opportunity to prove that the proposed step would not advance the interest of mines and mineral development.
