RatioOfTheDecision	Procedural fairness embodying natural justice is to be implied when ever action is taken affecting the rights of parties.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may be that the opportunity to be heard may not be pre-decisional; it may necessarily have to be post- 678 decisional where the danger to be averted or the act to be prevented is imminent or where the action to be taken can brook ne delay.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may not oven be necessary in some situations to give pre-decisional opportunity of making a representation but it would be sufficient but obligatory to consider any representation that may be made by the aggrieved person and that would satisfy the requirements of procedural fairness and natural justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	There can be no tape-measure of the extent of natural justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may and indeed it must vary from statute to statute, situation to situation and case to case.
RatioOfTheDecision	Pre-decisional natural justice is not usually contemplated when the decisions taken are of an interim nature pending investigation or enquiry.
RatioOfTheDecision	Ad- i nterim orders may always be made ex-parte and such orders may themselves provide for an opportunity to the aggrieved party to be heard at a later stage.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even if the interim orders do not make provision for such an opportunity, an aggrieved party has, nevertheless, always the right to make an appropriate representation seeking a review of the order and asking the authority to rescind or modify the order.
RatioOfTheDecision	The principles of natural justice would be satisfied if the aggrieved party is given an opportunity at his request.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is no violation of a principle of natural justice if an ex-parte ad-interim order is made unless of course, the statute itself provides for a hearing before the order is made.
RatioOfTheDecision	Natural justice will be violated if the authority refuses to consider the request of the aggrieved party for an opportunity to make his representation against the ex- parte ad-interim orders.
RatioOfTheDecision	(700H ; 701A-F) There is no rule of justice of fair play which requires the authority to seek the comments of the person concer ned before embarking upon an investigation.
RatioOfTheDecision	Investigation commences as soon as the authority concerned to take the first step whether by way of seeking evidence or by way of seeking an explanation from the person concerned.
RatioOfTheDecision	(699F) In some cases, ex-parte interim orders may be made pending a final adjudication.

RatioOfTheDecision	Some orders of that nature, intended to prevent further mischief of one kind.
RatioOfTheDecision	may themselves be productive of greater mischief of another kind.
RatioOfTheDecision	An interim order of stay or suspension which has the effect of preventing a person, however, temporarily, say, from pursuing his profession or line of business, may have substantial, serious and even disastrous consequences to him and may expose him to grave risk and hazard.
