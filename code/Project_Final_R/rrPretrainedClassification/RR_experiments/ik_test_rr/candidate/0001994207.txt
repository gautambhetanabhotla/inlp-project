Fact	HEADNOTE: The first respondent filed a writ petition in the <ORG> challenging the grant of a mining lease to the appellant on the ground of a direct.
Fact	infringement of his right to be granted a mining lease over an area for which he applied for a mining lease and which, according to him, formed part of the area for which the appellant was given the lease.
Fact	But in fact, the first respondent's application was not in respect of any part of the area for which the appellant was granted a mining lease.
Fact	Though the appellant was made party to the proceeding, he did not appear as notices were not served on him.
Fact	The <ORG>  allowed the petition in the view that there was a violation: of s. 31 of the Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act , 1957, and rr.
Fact	58 and 59 of the Mineral Concession Rules 1960.
Fact	Allowing the appeal to this Court, HELD : The first respondent had no interest in the subject- matter of the lease, and the petition was not maintainable.
Fact	[618 G-H] Though this contention was not urged before <ORG> as the appellant did not appear in <ORG>, this Court, in appeal, can not only determine the soundness of the decision, but has jurisdiction to determine any point raised before, it, such as, whether the appeal is competent, whether a party has a locus standi to present the petition and whether the petitioner was maintainable.
Fact	Ordinarily, the foundation for exercising the jurisdiction under Article 32 or Article 226 , is the personal or individual right of the petitioner himself, though in cases of writs of habeas corpus or quo warranto, the rule may be relaxed.
Fact	In respect of persons who are  not aggrieved and who seek to invoke the jurisdiction of <ORG> or this <ORG>, the matter rests ultimately on the discretion of the <ORG>, and depends on the nature and extent of the right or interest said to have been infringed and whether the infringement affects the petitioner in some way.
Fact	[619 A-B, D, G-H] In the present case, the first respondent only alleged direct infringement of his right,but it was found that no right of his had been affected.
Fact	He was neither aparty nor a person aggrieved or affected and hence had no locus standi to file the petition.

Fact	The appellant was granted a mining lease on August 30, 1969 by the State of Bihar (Respondent  3) with the prior approval of <ORG> (Respondent 2) for winning a mineral known as Apatite over as area of 1999.634 acres.
Fact	Respondent 1 filed a writ petition on September 15, 1969 challenging the lease on the ground that he had earlier on March 22, 1965, applied for a mining lease over an area of 280.62 acres in certain villages of Singhbhum District which was included in the lease granted to the appellant, but as no orders were passed by <ORG> within the statutory period the application was ,deemed 'to have been rejected.
Fact	He thereafter file d a revision petition to <ORG> which called for the comments of <ORG>.
Fact	<ORG> intimated to <ORG> that it wanted to work the area itself and for that reason had in fact rejected all the applications for this area including that of the first respondent.
Fact	On receipt of this comment, <ORG> rejected the revision petition of the first respondent.
Fact	It appears that the appellant had pursuant to an advertisement in the newspapers applied along with others for the grant of a mining lease for phosphatic rock (Apatite) over an area of 4.1 sq.
Fact	miles in village Khajurdari in Singhibhum District.
Fact	But all 'the applications were rejected as <ORG> had by then decided to work the phosphatic bearing areas in the public sector.
Fact	Later, however, as 3rd respondent felt that such a venture could be better undertaken by a private party rather than <ORG> in view of the dispersed nature of the deposits, whose concentrated and effi cient supervision may not be possible through the public sector, it decided to release the area in question to be worked in the private sector.
Fact	Accordingly permission was sought from <ORG> and an advertisement published in the newspapers for the general information of the interested parties who may be willing to set up a benefication plant for upgrading the low grade Apatite to ensure its use for the production of phosphatic fertiliser and who were capable of making an investment to the extent of Rs.
Fact	40 to 50 lakhs.
Fact	The appellant who is reported to be financially sound submitted a scheme for setting up a benefication plant for upgrading the Apatite.
Fact	In view of the financial solvency, of the appellant his application was recommended to <ORG>.
Fact	<ORG> accepted this recommendation and directed the grant of the mining lease in the following terms :  " <ORG> in the interest of mineral development, in exercise of the powers conferred by subrule (2) o f rule 58 of the Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, 61 7 hereby authorise <ORG> to grant mining lease for apatite over the area to Dr. <NAME> without following the procedure laid down in sub-rule (1) of the said Rule 58 of the Mineral Concession Rules, 1960.
