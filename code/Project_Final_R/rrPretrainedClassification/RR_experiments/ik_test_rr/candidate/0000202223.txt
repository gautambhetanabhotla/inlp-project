RatioOfTheDecision	Section 101 of the Evi- dence Act lays down that whoever desires any Court to, give judgment as to any legal right or liability dependent on the existence of facts which he asserts, must prove that those facts exist, and s. 102 provides that the burden of proof in a suit or proceeding lies on that person who would fail if no evidence at all were given on either side.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 103 provides that the burden of proof as to any particular fact lies on that person who wishes the 'Court to believe in its existence, unless it is provided by any law that the proof of that fact shall lie on any particular person.

RatioOfTheDecision	But, considering the nature of the objections which are capable of being successfully taken under section 5A , it is diffi- cult to see why the summary enquiry should not be concluded quite expeditiously.
RatioOfTheDecision	In View of the authorities of this Court, the existence of what are prima facie public pur- poses, such as the one present in the cases before us, cannot be successfully challenged at all by objectors.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is rare to find a case in which.
RatioOfTheDecision	objections to the validity of a public purpose of an acquisition can even be stated in a form in which the challenge could succeed.
RatioOfTheDecision	Indeed, questions relating to validity of the notification on the ground of malafides do not seem to US to be ordinari- ly open in a summary enquiry  under section 5A of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence, there seems to us to be little difficulty in completing enquiries contemplated by section 5A of the Act very expeditiously.
RatioOfTheDecision	Now, the purpose of section 17(4) of the Act is, obvi- ously, not merely to confine action under it to.
RatioOfTheDecision	waste and arable land but ,also to situations in which an inquiry under section 5A will serve no useful purpose, or, for some overriding reason, it should be dispensed with.
RatioOfTheDecision	The mind of the Officer or authority concerned has to be applied to the question whether there is fan urgency of such a nature that even the summary proceedings under section 5A of the Act should be eliminated.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not just the existence of an urgency but the need to dispense with an inquiry under section 5A which has to be considered.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 17(2) deals with a case in which an enquiry under section 5A of 'the Act could not possibly serve any useful purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	Sudden change of the course of a river would leave no option if essential communications have to be maintained.
RatioOfTheDecision	It r esults in more or less indicating, by an operation of natural physical forces beyond human control, what land should be urgently taken possession of.
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence, it offers no difficulty in applying section 17(4) in public interest.
RatioOfTheDecision	And, the particulars of'.
RatioOfTheDecision	what is .obviously to be done in public interest need not be concealed when its validity is questioned in <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Other cases may raise questions involving consideration of facts which are especially within the knowledge of the authorities concerned.
RatioOfTheDecision	And, if they do not discharge their special burden, imposed by section 106 Evidence Act, without even disclosing a sufficient reason for their abstention from disclosure, they have to take the consequences which flow from the non-production of the best evidence which could be produced on behalf of the <ORG> if its stand was correct.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the case before us, the public purpose indicated is the development of an area for industrial and residential pur- poses.
RatioOfTheDecision	.This in itself, on the face of it, does not cal l for any such action, barring exceptional circumstances, as to make immediate possession, without holding even a summary enquiry under section 5A of the Act, imperative.
RatioOfTheDecision	On the other hand, such schemes generally take sufficient period of time to enable at least summary inquiries under section 5A of the Act to be completed without any impediment whatso- ever to the execution of the scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the very statement of the public purpose for which .the land was to be 'acquired indicated the absence of such urgency, on the apparent facts of the case, as to require the elimination of an enquiry under ' section 5A of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Again, the uniform and set recital of a formula, like a ritual or mantara, apparently applied mechanically to every case, itself indicated that the mind of the Commissioner concerned was only applied to the question whether the land was waste or arable and whether its acquisition is urgently needed.
RatioOfTheDecision	Nothing beyond that seems to have been considered.
RatioOfTheDecision	The recital itself shows that the min d of the Commissioner was not applied at all to the question whether the urgency is of such a nature as to require elimination of the enquiry under section 5A .of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	If it was, at least the notifications gave no inkling of it at all.
RatioOfTheDecision	On the other hand, its literal mean- ing was that nothing beyond matters stated there were con- sidered.
RatioOfTheDecision	All schemes relating to development of industrial and residential areas must be urgent in the context of the country's need for increased production and more residential accommodation.
RatioOfTheDecision	Yet, the very nature of such schemes of development does not appear to demand such emergent action as to eliminate summary enquires under section 5A of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is no indication whatsoever in the affidavit filed on behalf of the <ORG> that the mind of the Commis- sioner was applied.
RatioOfTheDecision	at all to the question whether it was a case necessitating the elimination of the enquiry under section 5A of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The recitals in the notifications, on the other hand, indicate that elimination of t he enquiry under section 5A of the Act was treated as an automatic consequence of the opinion formed on other matters.
RatioOfTheDecision	The recital does not say at all that any opinion was formed on the need to dispense with the enquiry under section 5A of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is certainly a case in which' the recital was atleast defective.
RatioOfTheDecision	The burden, therefore, rested upon the <ORG> to remove the defect, if possible, by evidence to show that some exceptional circumstances which necessitated the elimination of an enquiry under section 5A of the Act and that the mind of the Commissioner was applied to this essen- tial question.
RatioOfTheDecision	It seems to us that <ORG> correctly applied the provisions of section '106 of the Evidence Act to place the burden upon the <ORG> to prove those special circumstances.
RatioOfTheDecision	although it also; appears to us.
RatioOfTheDecision	that <ORG> was not quite correct in stating its view in such a manner as to make it appear that some part of the initial burden of the petitioners under sections 101 and 102 of the Evidence Act h ad been displaced by the failure of the <ORG>, to discharge its duty under' section 106 of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The correct way of putting it would have been to say that the failure of the <ORG> to produce the evidence of facts espe- cially' within the knowledge of its officials, which rested upon it under section 106 of the Evidence Act, taken together with the attendant facts gnu circumstances, includ- ing the contents of recitals, had enabled the petitioners to discharge their burdens under sections 101 and 102 of the Evidence Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	We may also observe that if, instead of prolonging litigation by appealing to this Court, the <ORG> Government had ordered expeditious enquiries under section 5A of the Act or even afforded the petitioners some opportunity of being heard before acting under section 17(4) of the Act, asking them to show cause why no enquiry under section 5A of the Act should take place at all, the acquisition proceed- ings need not have been held up so long.
RatioOfTheDecision	In fact, we hope that the acquisition proceedings  have not actually been held up.
RatioOfTheDecision	784  On the view we take of the cases before us, we find no force in either the appeals by the owners of land or in those preferred by the State of Maharashtra.
RulingByPresentCourt	Consequently, we dismiss all the nine appeals before us.
