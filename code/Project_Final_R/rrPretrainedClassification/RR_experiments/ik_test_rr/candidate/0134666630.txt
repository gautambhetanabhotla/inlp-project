RatioOfTheDecision	Having read and analyzed the evidence led by both the sides before the learned <ORG>, I am not persuaded, as I find no valid reason, to disagree with the conclusion recorded by the learned <ORG> on various issues of fact.
RatioOfTheDecision	Learned <ORG> has correctly examined the driving licence of the offending driver and found that it authorized him to drive light motor vehicle, light transport vehicle, public service vehicle and heavy goods vehicle.
RatioOfTheDecision	At the same time, learned <ORG> has rightly noticed that the licence was not renewed after 6 29.12.2006 and therefore, not valid and effective as on the date of the accident.
RatioOfTheDecision	Likewise, I am not persuaded to disagree with the finding recorded by the learned <ORG> that a person having been engaged as a labourer with a tractor would fall within the definition of a person meant for operation and maintenance of the tractor for the reason that his role would be loading, unloading and fitting o f trollies and ploughs to the tractor, which are the activities connected with operation of a tractor.
RatioOfTheDecision	I have noticed that there is nothing in the evidence led before the learned <ORG> that the offending tractor at the time of accident was being used for any commercial purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	Learned <ORG> has correctly taken note that the policy of insurance covered legal liability of the insured in respect of one person engaged for operation/maintenance of the tractor and held the deceased to be a person falling in that category.

RatioOfTheDecision	Last question, but not the least, however, is, whether the learned <ORG> had the jurisdiction and was legally right in foisting the liability on the appellant/insurer to satisfy the award with liberty to recover the awarded amount from the insured/owner?
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is now well settled that in an appropriate case, where the insurer has issued the policy of insurance 7 but has succeeded in defending its liability by proving breach of a specified condition of the policy of insurance in term s of section 149(2) of the Act, the insure can still be directed to satisfy the award and to recover the awarded amount from the insured.
RatioOfTheDecision	In <ORG> SC 1531 <ORG> has observed that the liability of the insurance company to satisfy the decree at the first instance and to recover the awarded amount from the owner or driver thereof has been holding the field for a long time. <ORG>, however, has hastened to add in paragraph 102 of the reporting that (SC): 102.
RatioOfTheDecision	the <ORG> and the court must, however, exercise their jurisdiction to issue such a direction upon consideration of the facts and circumstances of each case and in the event such a direction has been issued despite arriving at a finding of fact to the effect that the insurer has been able to establish that the insured has committed a breach of contract of insurance as envisaged under subclause  (ii) of clause (a) of sub-section (2) of Section 149 of the Act, the insurance company  shall be entitled to realise the awarded amount from the owner or driver of the vehicle, as the case may be, in execution of the same award having regard to the provisions of Sections 165 and 168 of the Act .
RatioOfTheDecision	However, in the event, having regard to the limited scope of inquiry in the proceedings before the <ORG> it had not been able to do so, the insurance company may initiate a separate action therefore against the owner or the driver of the vehicle or both, as the case may be.
RatioOfTheDecision	Those exceptional cases may arise when the evidence becomes available to or comes to the notice of the 8 insurer at a subsequent stage or for one reason or the other, the insurer was not given opportunity to defend at all.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such a course of action may also be resorted when a fraud or collusion between the victim and the owner of the vehicle is detected or comes to the knowledge of the insurer at a later stage. The view taken by <ORG> in <NAME> has been referred to with approval lately in <CITATION_111485285 > AIR (2011) SC 1234.

RatioOfTheDecision	I agree with the view taken by the learned <ORG> as I find no good reason to disagree.
RatioOfTheDecision	Besides, I find another good reason for upholding the order of the learned <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	On reading of the impugned judgment and award, it is seen that even tho ugh the appellant/insurer had succeeded in proving and 9 learned <ORG> held that the driving licence of the offending driver, having not been renewed after 29.12.2006, was not valid and effective at the time of accident, neither has the appellant proved nor the learned <ORG> accorded consideration to the fact as to whether the breach of a specified condition of the policy of insurance was on the part of the insured/owner.
RatioOfTheDecision	To defend its liability under section 149(2) of the Act the insurer has to prove not only the breach of a specified condition of the policy of insurance but also that the breach had been on the part of the insured.
RatioOfTheDecision	The insurer has to prove by leading evidence that there was conscience breach on the part of the insured.
RatioOfTheDecision	This legal position is discernible in <ORG> (supra) where their Lordships have held that the insurance companies with a view to avoid their liability must not only establish the available defence(s) raised in the said proceedings but must also establish breach on  the part of the owner of the vehicle; the burden of proof wherefore would be on them.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is not even a whisper in the evidence led by the appellant/insurer that the owner of the offending tractor was aware of the fact that the driver had not got his licence renewed.
RatioOfTheDecision	11.
RatioOfTheDecision	The insurance company in this case having failed to establish that breach of insurance policy, that is, 10 driving licence of the offending driver being not renewed as at the time of accident, was on the part of the owner of the offending tractor, I have my reservation in regard to <ORG> allowance to the appellant/insurer to recover the awarded amount from the insured.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, this aspect of the impugned judgment and award having not been assailed, I would prefer not to devolve upon the same any more.
RatioOfTheDecision	Nonetheless I would say that appellants grievance cannot be taken as well founded and hold that this is a fit case where the insurer should satisfy the award and avail the benefit of recovery as allowed by the learned <ORG> .
