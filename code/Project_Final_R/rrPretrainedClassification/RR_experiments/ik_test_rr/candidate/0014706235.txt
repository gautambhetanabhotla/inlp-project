RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG>.

RatioOfTheDecision	The purpose for which the lands are being utilized by a governmental agency is also a public purpose and as we have noticed earlier, would come within the ambit of the public purpose declared in Section 4 , Notification.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the acquisition cannot be challenged on the g round that the acquired lands are not being utilized for the declared public purpose.

RatioOfTheDecision	In the matter of computing the period of limitation three situations may be visualized, namely - (a) where the <NAME> mitation Act applies by its own force; (b) where the provisions of the Limitation Act with or without modifications are made applicable to a special statute; and (c) where the special statue itself prescribes the period of limitation and provides for extension of time and or condonation of delay.
RatioOfTheDecision	The instant case is not one which is governed by the provisions of the Limitation Act .
RatioOfTheDecision	The Land Acquisition Collector in making an award does not act as a Court within the meaning of the Limitation Act .
RatioOfTheDecision	It is also clear from the provisions of the Land Acquisition Act that the provisions of the Limitation Act have not been made applicable to proceedings under the Land Acquisition Act in the matter of making an award under Section 11A of the Act.
Statute	However, Section 11A of the Act does provide a period of limitation within which the Collector shall make his award.
Statute	The explanation thereto also provides for exclusion of the period during which any action or proceeding to be taken in pursuance of the declaration is stayed  by an order of a court.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such being the provision, there is no scope for importing into Section 11A of the Land Acquisition Act the provisions of Section 12 of the Limitation Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The application of Section 12 of the Limitation Act is also confined to matters enumerated therein.
RatioOfTheDecision	The time taken for obtaining a certified copy of the judgment is excluded because a certified copy is required to be filed while preferring an appeal/revision/review etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	challenging the impugned order.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus a court is not permitted to read into Section 11A of the Act a provision for exclusion of time taken to obtain a certified copy of the judgment and order.
RatioOfTheDecision	The court has, therefore, no option but to compute the period of limitation for making an award in accordance with the provisions of Section 11A of the Act after excluding such period as can be excluded under the explanation to Section 11A of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	45.
RatioOfTheDecision	Our conclusion finds support from the scheme of the Land Acquisition Act itself.
Statute	Section 11A of the Act was inserted by Act 6 8 of 1984 with effect from 24.09.1984.
Statute	Similarly, Section 28A was also inserted by the Amendment Act of 1984 with effect from the same date.
Statute	In Section 28A the Act provides for a period of limitation within which an application should be made to the Collector for re-determination of the amount of compensation on the basis of the award of the <ORG>.
Statute	The proviso to Sub-section 1 of Section 28A reads as follows:  Provided that in computing the period of three months within which an application to the Collector shall be made under this sub-section, the Page 1928 day on which the award was pronounced and the time requisite for obtaining a copy of the award shall be excluded.
Statute	It will thus be seen that the legislature wherever it considered necessary incorporated by express words the rule incorporated in Section 12 of the Limitation Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	It has done so expressly in Section 28A of the Act while it has consciously not incorporated this rule in Section 11A even while providing for exclusion of time under the explanati on.
RatioOfTheDecision	The intendment of the legislature is therefore unambiguous and does not permit the <ORG> to read words into Section 11A of the Act so as to enable it to read Section 12 of the Limitation Act into Section 11A of the Land Acquisition Act.
