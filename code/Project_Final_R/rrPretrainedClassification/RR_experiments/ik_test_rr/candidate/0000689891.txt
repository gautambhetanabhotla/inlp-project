RatioOfTheDecision	This reasoning of <ORG> cannot be faulted for the simple reason that the <ORG>, who has to carry out the planned development of the industrial estate, is in the best position to judge as to which land can be exempted from the acquisition without jeopardizing the development scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not possible for the court to sit in appeal over the exercise of such satisfaction by the <ORG> vested with the task of implementing the development plan.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned counsel for the appellants urged that the decision taken for exempting <ORG> <ORG> <ORG>, <ORG> <ORG> and <ORG> K.C.
RatioOfTheDecision	Fibre Ltd. was not a principled one and that there was no uniform yardstick applied for exemption of the said units from acquisition.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was urged that, although <ORG> had ostensibly decided to exempt the said three units on the basis of construction put up and industrial units being set up, this was really not true in the case of these three units.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, it is unnecessary for us to enter into this controversy.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even if we assume that the three units, who were exempted, did not qualify under the standard adopted by <ORG> for exemption, at the highest, it would make the exemption granted to them vulnerable.
RatioOfTheDecision	None of them was made party to the writ petitions filed before <ORG>, nor was any relief claimed against them.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even assuming that the exemption granted to the said three units was erroneous and illegal, Article 14 does not mandate that the appellants should be granted similar illegal and unjustified relief.
RatioOfTheDecision	As said by this Court in <CITATION_178226192> .
RatioOfTheDecision	and Anr.
RatioOfTheDecision	, to which one of us, (<NAME>, J.)
RatioOfTheDecision	was a party, (vide Para 13):  "..It is not necessary to deal with that aspect because two wrongs do not make one right.
RatioOfTheDecision	A party cannot claim that since something wrong has been done in another case direction should be given for doing another wrong.
RatioOfTheDecision	It would not be setting a wrong right, but would be perpetuating another wrong.
RatioOfTheDecision	In such matters there is no discrimination involved.
RatioOfTheDecision	The concept of equal treatment on the logic of Article 14 of the Constitution of India (in short "the Constitution) cannot be pressed into service in such cases.
RatioOfTheDecision	What the concept of equal treatment presupposes is existence of similar legal foothold.
RatioOfTheDecision	It does not countenance repetition of a wrong action to bring both wrongs on a par.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even if hypothetically it is accepted that a wrong has been committed in some other cases by introducing a concept of negative equality the respondents cannot strengthen their case.
RatioOfTheDecision	They have to establis h strength of their case on some other basis and not by claiming negative equality."
RatioOfTheDecision	It is trite law that not only land but also structure on land can be acquired under the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	As to whether in a given set of circumstances certain land should be exempted from acquisition only for the reason that some construction had been carried out, is a matter of policy, and not of law.
RatioOfTheDecision	If after considering all the circumstances, <ORG> has taken the view that exemption of the lands of the appellants would render askew the development scheme of the industrial estate, it is not possible for <ORG> or this <ORG> to interfere with the satisfaction of the concerned authorities.
RatioOfTheDecision	We see no ground on which the appellants could have maintained that their lands should be exempted from acquisition.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even if three of the parties had been wrongly exempted from acquisition, that gives no right to the appellants to seek similar relief.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is rightly pointed out by <ORG> that, merely because a representat ion was made by the <ORG>, that upon gift of certain land to <NAME> for widening of the passage, permission for change of user of land would be granted, such a promise is not one capable of being enforced against the State Government.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> has rightly pointed out that, if the appellants are so desirous, they may seek invalidation of the gifts in favour of <NAME> on the ground of failure of the <ORG> to fulfil his commitment.
RatioOfTheDecision	That, however, does not render the acquisition proceedings illegal.
RatioOfTheDecision	No other ground has been made out.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, therefore, no fault can be found with the judgment rendered by <ORG>.
RulingByPresentCourt	We find no merit in the appeals, which are hereby dismissed.
