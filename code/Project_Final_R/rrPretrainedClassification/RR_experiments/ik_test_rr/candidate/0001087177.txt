Fact	CASE NO.
Fact	: Appeal (civil) 3375 of 1998 PETITIONER: <NAME> Vs. RESPONDENT: <ORG> & ANR.
Fact	DATE OF JUDGMENT: 09/01/2001 BENCH: <NAME>, <NAME> JUDGMENT:  L.....I.........T.......T.......T.......T.......T.......T..J J U D G M E N T S. N. VARIAVA, J.
Fact	This Appeal is against an Order passed by <ORG> on 20th August, 1996.
Argument	Briefly stated the facts are as follows: The Appellants claim to be a <ORG> representing the workmen of a <ORG> run by the Respondents.
Argument	The Appellant <ORG> claimed that even though the Appellants are actually the employees of the Respondents, the Respondents are not treating them at par with other employees and have notionally engaged contractors to run the canteen.
Argument	As the Respondents were not accepting the Appellants' claim to treat them as their employees, the Appellant filed a Complaint under Section 28(1) of the Maharashtra Recognition of Trade <ORG>s & Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 (hereinafter called the MRTU & PULP Act) allegi ng that the Respondents had engaged in unfair labour practices under Item Nos.
Argument	1, 1(a), 1(b), 4, 4(a) of Schedule II and Items 3, 5, 6, 7, 9 and 10 of Schedule IV of the MRTU & PULP Act.
Argument	This Complaint came to be dismissed by the impugned Order dated 20th August, 1996.
Argument	<ORG> has filed an SLP directly in this Court against this Order as <ORG> of Bombay, in the case of <CITATION_850959> reported in (1993) 1 CLR Page 1002, has already held that <ORG> cannot in a complaint under MRTU & PULP Act abolish contract labour and treat employees as direct employees of the company.
Argument	At this stage it must be mentioned that this Court has also in the case of <CITATION_130103> .
Argument	reported in (1995) 2 LLJ 765, held that where the workmen have not been accepted by the <ORG> to be its employees, then no complaint would lie under the MRTU & PULP Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are in full agreement with the above mentioned view.
RatioOfTheDecision	The provisions of MRTU & PULP Act can only be enforced by persons who admittedly are wo rkmen.
RatioOfTheDecision	If there is dispute as to whether the employees are employees of the <ORG>, then that dispute must first be got resolved by raising a dispute before the appropriate forum.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is only after the status as a workmen is established in an appropriate Forum that a complaint could be made under the provisions of MRTU & PULP Act.
Argument	Faced with this situation it was submitted that the Respondent <ORG> had always recognised the members of <ORG> to be their own workmen.
Argument	It is submitted that a formal denial was taken only to defeat the claim.
RatioOfTheDecision	We see no substance in this submission.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the written statement it has been categorically denied that the members of <ORG> were employees of the Respondent <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question has been agitated before <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> has given a finding, on facts, that the members of <ORG> were not employees of the Respondent <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is a disputed fact and thus till the Appellants or their members, get the question  decided in a proper forum, this complaint was not maintainable.
RatioOfTheDecision	Accordingly, we dismiss this Appeal on the ground that the complaint was not maintainable.
RatioOfTheDecision	We clarify that it is open for the Appellant or their members to raise dispute in this behalf before an appropriate forum provided they are entitled to do so.
RatioOfTheDecision	If they get a declaration to the effect that they are employees of <ORG>, then it may be open to them to file such a complaint.
RulingByPresentCourt	It is also clarified that if a dispute as to their status is raised in an appropriate forum then the same will be decided on merits without taking into consideration any observations made or finding given by <ORG> in the impugned Order.
