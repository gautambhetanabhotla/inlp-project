Fact	The scheme envisaged grant of temporary status to casual labourer who had worked at least 240 days in a year (206 days in the case of offices observing 5 days a week).

Argument	In support of the appeal, learned counsel for the appellant submitted that the <ORG> was not justified in its view and <ORG> did not take note of the submissions made, and by a cryptic non-reasoned order has dismissed the writ petition.
Argument	Learned counsel for the respondent on the other hand submitted that undisputed position being that i n 1998 the respondent had rendered service for more than 206 days, no exception can be taken to the orders of the <ORG> and <ORG>.
Argument	The controversy can be resolved on the basis of the interpretation of clause 4 of the Scheme.
Argument	As already noticed, the Scheme came into effect from 1-9-1993.
Statute	Clause 4(1) of the Scheme reads as follows:  "4.
Statute	Temporary status:-(1) `temporary' status would be conferred on all casual labourers who are in employment on the date of issue of this OM and who have rendered a continuous service of at least one year, which means that they must have been engaged for a period of at least 240 days (206 days in the case of offices observing 5 days' week)."
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause 4 of the Scheme is very clear that the conferment of "temporary" status is to be given to the casual labourers who were in employment as on the date of commencement of the Scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> has taken the view that this is an ongoing scheme and as and when casual labourers complete 240 days of work in a year or 206 days (in case  of offices observing 5 days a week), they are entitled to get "temporary" status.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not think that clause 4 of the Scheme envisages it as an ongoing scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	In order to acquire "temporary" status, the casual labourer should have been in employment as on the date of commencement of the Scheme and he should have also rendered a continuous service of at least one year which means that he should have been engaged for a period of at least 240 days in a year or 206 days in case of offices observing 5 days a week.
RatioOfTheDecision	From clause 4 of the Scheme, it does not appear to be a general guideline to be applied for the purpose of giving "temporary" status to all the casual workers, as and when they complete one year's continuous service.
RatioOfTheDecision	Of course, it is up to <ORG> to formulate any scheme as and when it is found necessary that the casual labourers are to be given "temporary" status and later they are to be absorbed in <ORG> 'D' posts.
RatioOfTheDecision	A similar controversy was examined by this Court in <CITATION_714743> .,   4 SCC 573 and a similar view was expressed in paragraph 6 of the judgment.
RatioOfTheDecision	That being so, the <ORG>'s order is clearly untenable.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> unfortunately did not examine the real issue involved and by a very cryptic order summarily dismissed the writ petition.
RatioOfTheDecision	The order of <ORG> is accordingly set aside.
