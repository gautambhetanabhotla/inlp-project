Argument	1,70,245, being the profit on sales of import entitlements.

Argument	The <ORG> excluded the same on the ground that this did not have any relation to the manufacturing operations of the assessee.
Argument	This view was confirmed by the <ORG> and the <ORG>.
Argument	Under s. 80E , in order to get the benefit of deduction, the profits and ga ins should be attributable to the business of manufacture or sale.
Argument	The <ORG> held that in order to make profits "attributable" to the manufacturing activity, there should be some direct nexus with the profit earned and the manufacturing activity.
Argument	As in the view of the <ORG>, there is no such nexus in the profit earned by sale of import entitlement, for the purpose of the relief under s. 80E the profits on sale of import entitlements had to be excluded.
Precedent	<ORG> in <CITATION_81055> , while interpreting the words "attributable to" occurring in s. 80E , observed as follows (p. 93) :  "As regards the aspect emerging from the expression 'attributable to' occurring in the phrase profits and gains attributable to the business of' the specified industry (here generation and distribution of electricity) on which the learned Solicitor-General relied, it will be pertinent to observe that the legislature has deliberately used the expression 'attributable to' and not the expression 'derived from'.
RatioOfTheDecision	It cannot  be disputed that the expression 'attributable to' is certainly wider in import than the expression 'derived from'.
RatioOfTheDecision	Had the expression 'derived from' been used, it could have with some force been contended that a balancing charge arising from the sale of old machinery and buildings cannot be regarded as profits and gains derived from the conduct of the business of generation and distribution of electricity.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this connection, it may be pointed out that whenever the legislature wanted to give a restricted meaning in the manner suggested by the learned Solicitor-General, it has used the expression 'derived from', as, for instance, in section 80J .
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, since the expression of wider import namely, 'attributable to', has been used, the legislature intended to cover receipts from sources other than the actual conduct of the business of generation and distribution of electricity."
RatioOfTheDecision	3.
RatioOfTheDecision	The import entitlement in this case was given to the assessee on the basis of its export of the goods manufactured by them.
RatioOfTheDecision	Certainly, therefore, the import entitlement was directly attributable to the export of goods manufactured and the business of sale of forgings and stampings.
