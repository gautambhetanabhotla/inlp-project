RatioOfTheDecision	Before we proceed to deal with the contentions raised by counsel for the parties, it will be convenient to consider the relevant provisions of section 10 .
RatioOfTheDecision	Omitting the part not material for the purpose of this case, the said provisions read :  "10.
RatioOfTheDecision	(1) Business.
Statute	- The tax shall be payable by an assessee under the head 'Profits and gains of business, profession or vocation' in respect of the profits or gains of any business, profession or vocation carried on by him.
Statute	(2) Such profits or gains shall be computed after making the following allowances, namely :-.....  (vi) in respect of depreciation of such buildings, machinery, plant or furniture being the property of the assessee, a sum equivalent.... to such percentage on the written down value thereof as may in any case or class or cases be prescribed....
Statute	Provided that -  (a) the prescribed particulars have been d uly furnished.....  (5) In sub-section (2).....'written down value' means -  (a) in the case of assets acquired in the previous year, the actual cost to the assessee......  (b) in the case of assets acquired before the previous year the actual cost to the assessee less all depreciation actually allowed to him under this Act, or any Act repealed thereby, or under executive orders issued when the Indian Income-tax Act , 1886 (II of 1886), was in force."
Statute	10.
Statute	These provisions indicate that in the computation of the tax payable by an assessee under the head "Profits and gains" of the business, profession vocation, the assessee is entitled to certain deductions, by way of certain allowances and one of the allowances is depreciation allowance in respect of buildings, machinery, plant and furniture, which is the property of the assessee and which he has used for the purpose of his business during the assessment year.
Statute	The allowance allowable is at a certain prescribed percentage on the written down value.
RatioOfTheDecision	The method  for computation of the written down value is two-fold as provided in clauses (a) and (b) of sub-section (5) of section 10 of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	In determining the written down value of the assets acquired in the previous year, it is the actual cost to the assessee and in the case of the assets acquired before the previous year the actual cost less the depreciation actually allowed.
RatioOfTheDecision	"Actual cost", however, is not defined in the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The ordinary dictionary meaning of the word "actual" is "existing in fact or fact as opposed to imaginary or past state of things".
RatioOfTheDecision	The dictionary meaning of the word "cost" is "what is laid out or suffered to obtain anything."
RatioOfTheDecision	Depreciation allowance as understood in the commercial world is deduction from profits, which a businessman ordinarily makes with a view to making good the capital investment, which, spooner or later, will have to be replaced on account of the wear and tear of the assets during the course of the business.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, therefore, the meaning of the expression "actua l cost to the assessee" as used in sub-section (5) of section 10 of the Act would be what the assessee his, in fact, expended or laid out for the purpose of acquiring the depreciable assets.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is in the light of the view we have taken of the expression "actual cost to the assessee" that the agreement of date 4th June, 1948, will have to be scrutinised to ascertain whether any of the services rendered by <NAME> would reasonably be attributable towards the cost of the depreciable assets.
