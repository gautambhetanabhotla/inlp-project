Fact	Heard counsel on both sides.
Argument	The appellant, <ORG> (hereinafter referred to as <ORG>") seems to be more aggrieved by the following pungent/harsh observations made by <ORG> against the officials of <ORG> and in particular against its Chairman and Secretary :  "Probably the authorities of <ORG>, think themselves above the State or the least a State within the State.
Argument	It seems that they are under an illusion that if they flout the directions of <ORG>, there would be no forum where the person can seek redress.
Argument	They failed to realise that <ORG> acting within its powers, sanctioned by law, had the authority  to issue directions, particularly in matters, where a policy is involved leaving no option to them except to comply with those directions.
Argument	As they refused to comply with the directions of <ORG> in a most reckless and indiscreet manner, this <ORG>, has no option except to force them to act, within the boundaries of law.
Argument	But before doing so, I am constrained to say, that the Chairman and Secretary of <ORG>, considering themselves, a rare species like white Elephants, tried to ransack and destroy, circumvent the directions of <ORG>.
Argument	I am definitely of the view that the aforesaid officers are not only guilty of flouting the directions of <ORG>, but are responsible for harassing, a retrenched employee who deserved sympathy and compassion.
Argument	However, it is for <ORG> to consider, as to whether such persons should be allowed to continue on such higher positions."
Argument	The circumstances under which the above observations came to be made can  be briefly noted :  <ORG> took a policy decision to absorb the employees of the <ORG>s/ Undertakings which have been wound up due to recurring losses in other Departments/Undertakings of the <ORG>.
Argument	One such <ORG> owned <ORG> which ended in winding up was <ORG>.
Argument	After giving notice steps were taken to terminate the services of the employees of that <ORG> and on humanitarian ground, the <ORG> decided to take steps for absorption of the employees of this <ORG> in other Departments/Organisations under the <ORG>.
Argument	Out of 600 employees of <ORG>, only few had been without absorption in other departments, one among those left out was the first respondent herein.
Argument	Pursuant to the policy as stated above, the <ORG> asked <ORG> to appoint the first respondent as Electrician.
Argument	In spite of several such requests from the <ORG>, the appellant <ORG> took a stand that <ORG> being an  autonomous body cannot be directed by the <ORG> absorb/appoint the first respondent in <ORG> as Electrician.
Argument	Finding that <ORG> was not prepared to appoint/absorb him, the first respondent moved <ORG> for the issue of a Writ of Mandamus commanding <ORG> to appoint/absorb him on the post of Electrician and for consequential reliefs.
Argument	Even before <ORG>, the appellant took the same stand by placing reliance on Sections 5 and 78A of the Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 The learned Single Judge after looking into the various correspondence that passed between the <ORG> and <ORG> made the above observations.
Argument	Learned counsel appearing for the appellant <ORG> fairly and rightly stated that in compliance of the order of <ORG>, the first respondent has been appointed/absorbed and the appellant has decided to continue his services irrespective of the result of this case.
Argument	Therefore, so far as the question of la w is concerned, we need not go into that in view of the fair and reasonable attitude taken by the appellant <ORG>.
Argument	However the learned counsel for the appellant was particular about the deletion the pungent observations extracted above.
RulingByLowerCourt	<CITATION_1923688> (AIR 1992 SC 1348) this Court had occasion to consider the scope of Section 78A of the Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948.
RulingByLowerCourt	Considering Section 78A of the Act, the Court observed as under :-  "78A.
RulingByLowerCourt	Directions by <ORG>.-  (1) In the discharge of its functions, the <ORG> shall be guided by such directions on question of policy as may be given to it by <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	(2) If any dispute arises between the <ORG> and <ORG> as to whether a question is or is not a question of policy, it shall be referred to the <ORG> whose decision thereon shall be final.
Argument	The above provision clearly lays down that the <ORG> shall be guided by such directions on questions of policy as may be given to it by <ORG>.
Argument	In th e circumstances of the case before us the directions given under letter dated 18.7.1988 and 5.
Argument	5.
Argument	1989 cannot be considered as directions on any questions of policy.
Argument	So far as the appointment of staff is concerned, S.15 empowers the <ORG> to appoint such officers and employees as may be required to enable the <ORG> to carry out its functions under the Act."
Argument	In view of the law laid down by this <ORG> as above, one cannot find fault with the appellant for taking a stand as they took before the High <ORG>, namely, that the Government have no authority to give directions to the Electricity <ORG> to appoint/absorb the first respondent as Electrician in the <ORG>.
Argument	The further question whether the directions in the facts and circumstances of this case will fall within the scope of Section 78-A is a debatable question and, therefore, the appellant can not be condemned for taking such stand before the High <ORG>.
Argument	This <ORG> has time and again observed that making stringent observations/strictures must be avoided by  the courts as far as possible and only in exceptional circumstances such observations can be made.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not think the case on hand presents any exceptional circumstances.
RatioOfTheDecision	For the reasons stated above, We set aside the observations extracted at the beginning from the judgment of <ORG> appealed against and dispose of the appeal accordingly.
