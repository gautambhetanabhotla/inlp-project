RatioOfTheDecision	If, instead of the expression "in respect of', the word "on" were there, the intention of the rule  would be manifest and the answer to the question raised would be obvious.
RatioOfTheDecision	The excise duty paid by the respondent was only on the raw tobacco and not on the goods' sold by it and, therefore, the said duty was not deductible thereunder.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far there is no dispute.
RatioOfTheDecision	But it was said that the expression "in respect of" made all the difference.
RatioOfTheDecision	The words "in respect of", it was said, meant "attributable" and, therefore, the argument proceeded, the excise duty paid on the tobacco, though it was not paid on the goods sold by the respondent, was attributable to the said goods sold.
RatioOfTheDecision	The object of the concession is presumably to avoid payment of tax on tax in respect of the same goods.
RatioOfTheDecision	If excise duty was paid by a dealer on certain goods, it would be deducted from the gross turnover of the dealer in regard to the said goods, as otherwise, in effect, sales-tax would have to be paid on the amount paid towards excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	This concession could have no relevance if the goods subjected to excise duty were different from the  goods sold.
RatioOfTheDecision	Raw tobacco, when converted by a process of manufacture into chewing tobacco, be comes a different marketable product.
RatioOfTheDecision	There will be no comparison between the raw tobacco and the chewing tobacco in the matter of demand or even price.
RatioOfTheDecision	Duty on raw tobacco may have some effect on the cost of the manu- factured product, but it cannot possibly be said that the said duty is paid in respect of the manufactured product.
RatioOfTheDecision	Rule 5(1)(i) of the Rules, therefore, permits deduction from the gross turnover of the dealer only the excise duty paid by him in respect of the same goods sold by him.

Statute	When the said rule says "excise duty paid in respect of the goods", the excise duty referred to is the excise duty paid under s. 3(1) , read with the Schedule, of the Central Excises and Salt Act , 1944 .
Statute	Under the, said section, read with the Schedule, excise duty is levied on the goods described in the Schedule.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, when r. 5(1)(i) of the Rules refers to the duty paid in respect of the goods to <ORG>, it necessarily refers to the duty paid on the goods mentioned in the Schedule.
RatioOfTheDecision	As the duty exempted from the gross turnover is the duty so paid under the Central Act , read with the Schedule, th e expression "in respect of" in the context can only mean excise duty paid on goods.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, the expression "in respect of the goods" in r. 5(1)(i) of the Rules means only "on the goods".
RatioOfTheDecision	Even if the word "attributable" is substituted for the words "in respect of", the result win not be different, for the duty paid shall be attributable to the goods.
RatioOfTheDecision	If it was paid on the raw material it can be attributable only to the raw material and not to the goods.
RulingByPresentCourt	We, therefore, hold that only excise duty paid on the goods sold by the assessee is deductible from the gross turnover under r. 5(1)(i) of the Rules.
RulingByPresentCourt	We cannot, therefore, agree with the construction of r. 5(1)(i) of the Rules accepted by <ORG>.
