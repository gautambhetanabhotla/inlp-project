Fact	In appeal to this Court, setting aside the acquittal, HELD : (1) A criminal trial is not like a fairy tale wherein one is free to give flight to one's imagination and phantasy.
Fact	It concerns itself with the question as to whether the accused arraigned at the trial is guilty of the crime with which he is charged.
Fact	Crime is an event in real life and is the product of interplay of different human emotions.
RatioOfTheDecision	In arriving at the conclusion about the guilt of the accused charged with the commission of a crime, the court has to judge the evidence by  the yardstick of probabilities, its intrinsic worth and the animus of witnesses.
RatioOfTheDecision	Every case in the final analysis would have to depend upon its own facts.
RatioOfTheDecision	Although the benefit of every reasonable doubt should be given to the accused, the courts should not at the same time reject evidence which is ex facie trustworthy on grounds which are fanciful or in the nature of conjectures.
RatioOfTheDecision	[337F-H] In the present case, <ORG> has rejected the prosecution evidence which was ex facie of a convincing nature on grounds which partake of the nature of conjectures and surmises.
RatioOfTheDecision	The view taken by <ORG> is manifestly unreasonable and has resulted in miscarriage of justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	A perusal of the judgment of <ORG> shows that in acquitting the accused respondents, <ORG> approached the entire matter in a spirit of distrust and suspicion of the various officers who dealt with the case.
Fact	[337H-338B] (2) <ORG>, while rejecting the prosecution evidence, made unwarranted criticism couched in harsh la nguage about the magistrate who received the copy of F.I.R., the doctor, the investigating officer and the Sessions Judge who tried the case.
Fact	[337E-F] (3) In view of the fact that more than two years had elapsed since <ORG> acquitted the respondents, it would be more appropriate to sentence the accused to imprisonment for life instead of to the extreme penalty.
