Fact	The respondent instituted a suit in the Court of the senior Subordinate Judge, Patiala on June 8, 1965 for a declaration that the order dated October 27, 1964 was illegal and void having been made without giving him an opportunity as required by Article 11(2) of the Constitution and that he should be deemed as continuing in service and therefore entitled to all rights, benefits and privileges available to members of that service.
RulingByLowerCourt	The trial court considering the circumstances of the case was of opinion that the services of plaintiff were terminated by way of punishment and the impugned order was based on misconduct and inefficiency on the part of the plaintiff."
RulingByLowerCourt	The trial court further held that on the facts, stated above, the provisions of Article 311(2) of the Constitution were attracted and the impugned order was void, having been passed in violation of that Article.
RulingByLowerCourt	On this view the trial court decreed the suit quashing the order  of termination and also granted the other declarations prayed for.
RulingByLowerCourt	On appeal by the State of Punjab and Additional District Judge, <NAME>, on a consideration of the material on record observed: "I have no doubt left in my mind that the plaintiff was visited with evil consequences due to an alleged misconduct".

RatioOfTheDecision	3.
RatioOfTheDecision	An order terminating the services of a temporary servant or a probationer, if it is by way of punishment, will attract Article 311 of the Constitution; the form (if the order is not conclusive, it is the substance of the matter that has to be looked into.
RatioOfTheDecision	Whether or no t an order of termination is by way of punishment would depend on the facts and circumstances of each case.
RatioOfTheDecision	This well established position has been reiterated by this <ORG> recently in the case of <NAME> etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	v. State of Punjab and Anr.
RatioOfTheDecision	on a review of the earlier decision on the point.
RatioOfTheDecision	The trial court and the first appellate court both took the view that the order of termination in the instant case was by way of punishment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The High <ORG> dismissed the second appeal in limine apparently in view of this concurring finding.
RatioOfTheDecision	The application for leave to file a Letters Patent appeal must therefore be held to have been rightly dismissed, there being no ground on which leave could have been granted.
RulingByPresentCourt	This appeal has no merit and is dismissed with costs.
