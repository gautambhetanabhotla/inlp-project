Argument	11.
Argument	Learned counsel contends that when the Government noticed that a mistake had crept in the English version of the Notification in regard to the date of appointment of the Public Analyst, it issued this corrigendum.
Argument	12.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is some force in the contentions raised by the learned counsel.
RatioOfTheDecision	But in a criminal case, law cannot be interpreted in a manner as to turn an innocent act into an offence.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Notification giving August 3, 1959 as  the date of appointment was published in the <ORG> dated August 1, 1959 while the Corrigendum was published several months after on January 11, 1960.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the circumstances, appellant cannot take advantage of the Corrigendum for investing legality in the report of the Public Analyst, which, on the face of the original Notification is clearly illegal.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 18(5) of the Act cannot be harnessed for reading in evidence the report of a person who was not a Public Analyst within the meaning of Section 8 of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	I agree with the observations of <NAME>, J. in the above noted criminal revision, that the second Notification cannot be read retrospectively to validate an act which was wholly void.
RatioOfTheDecision	13.
Argument	Learned counsel further contended that as the same notification of 27th July, 1959 published in Hindi, version of the gazette gives the 3rd of April, 1959 as the date of appointment of the Public Analyst, it should be preferred to the notification published in the English gazette because Hindi in Deonagri characters  being the official language of the <ORG>, Hindi version of the gazette is more authoritative than the English version.
RatioOfTheDecision	I do not find any substance in this argument.
RatioOfTheDecision	14.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the case of <CITATION_973037> , AIR 1962 All 240 a Full Bench of this Court has held that:  "In case of conflict or divergence between the two versions of the official gazette the English version may reign supreme and supersede the Hindi one."
RatioOfTheDecision	I am bound by that decision.
RatioOfTheDecision	15.
Fact	Learned counsel for the appellant invited my attention to three <ORG> notifications.
Fact	Government Notification No.
Fact	10656/XVI(PH)-9722-55-published in U. P. <ORG> of February 18, 1956, to show that two persons, viz., Dr. <NAME> and <NAME> were appointed as Public Analysts for the whole of Uttar Pradesh within the meaning of Section 8 of the Prevention of Food Adulteration Act.
Fact	<NAME>, Public Analyst to <ORG>) was granted leave on average pay for 4 months preparatory to retirement from April 3, 1957 under Government Notification No.
Fact	4177/XVI-(PH)-908-57-dated 24th May, 1957, and Dr. <NAME>, Assistant Public Analyst, was appointed under <ORG> Notification No.
Fact	4177(i)/XVI(PH)-908-57 of the same date to officiate as Public Analyst to <ORG> (II) vice Sri <NAME> granted leave.
Fact	On the basis of these <ORG> notifications <NAME>, learned counsel, contended that Sri <NAME> was a Public Analyst to <ORG> on 30-7-1959 when he gave the report in this case.
Fact	I do not find myself able to agree with this contention.
Fact	Sri S. C. <NAME> was granted leave for 4 months only and Dr. <NAME> was appointed to officiate as Public Analyst only for that period.
Fact	It cannot be assumed, therefore, that he continued to act as Public Analyst even after the expiry of the four months from April, 3, 1957.
Fact	This becomes clear from the fact that the <ORG> issued a notification dated July 27, 1959, appointing Sri <NAME> as Public Analyst, which would not have been necessary if Sri <NAME> had continued as Public Analyst b y virtue of the notification dated May 24, 1957, referred to above.
Fact	16.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this view of the matter, I do not find any force in this appeal.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeal is dismissed.
