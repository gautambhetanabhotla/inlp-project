Fact	ORDER <NAME>, C.J.
RulingByLowerCourt	1.
Fact	Pursuant to directions given by this <ORG> in in ITC No.44/92 under Section 256(2) of the Income-tax Act, 1961 (in short the 'Act') following question has been referred for opinion of this <ORG> by <ORG>.
Fact	Delhi Bench 'C' (in short the <ORG>):  "Whether on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, <ORG> was justified in holding that the Trust settled for the benefit of the sole beneficiary first son of <NAME> who was unborn at the time of creation of the Trust, is a valid trust?"
Fact	2.
Fact	Factual background in nutshell is as follows:  assessed settled 60,000 equity shares of <ORG> of the face value of Rs.5/- each for the benefit of first son of <NAME>.
Fact	Son of late Col. <NAME>.
Fact	For this purpose a trust was created on 2.5.1974.
Fact	Income-tax Officer (in short the 'ITO') was of the view that the trust was not valid and effective as there was no existence of any beneficiary.
Fact	Incom e arising out of the aforesaid shares was included in the income of the assessed.
Fact	Matter was carried in appeal before the Commissioner of Income-tax (Appeals)(in short the 'CIT(A)').
Fact	Adopting the view expressed earlier, in some cases the CIT(A) deleted the addition.
Fact	Matter was carried in appeal before the <ORG> by the <ORG>.
Fact	It was revenue's stand that in the absence of a definite or known beneficiary, creation of the trust itself was invalid.
Fact	<ORG> did not accept the stand.
Fact	A prayer for reference under Section 256(1) of the Act was turned down.
Fact	Subsequently, pursuant to the directions of this <ORG>, the aforesaid question has been referred for opinion along with statement of case.
Fact	3.
Fact	We have heard learned counsel for the parties.
Fact	Stand of learned counsel for <ORG> is that in the absence of known beneficiary, a trust is a misconceived idea.
Fact	According to him, for constituting a valid trust there must be a definite and an existent beneficiary.
Fact	According to learned counsel for the assessed, if the des cription of the beneficiary is there, it would be sufficient compliance with the requirements of law.
Fact	4.
Fact	In order to constitute a valid trust the author of the trust must indicate with reasonable certainty:  (a) the intention on his part to create a trust, (b) the purpose of the trust, (c) the beneficiaries, (d) the trust property and (e) transfer of property to the trust.
Fact	According to learned counsel for the <ORG> except condition (c) all other conditions are present here.
RatioOfTheDecision	We find that it is not a case where the beneficiary was unknown or indefinite.
RatioOfTheDecision	In fact it was clearly stipulated that the beneficiary was the first son of <NAME>.
RatioOfTheDecision	At this juncture, it would be also relevant to take note of Section 13 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 (in short 'T.P.Act').
RulingByLowerCourt	Section 13 reads as follows:  "13.
RulingByLowerCourt	Where, on a transfer of property, an interest therein is created for the benefit of a person not in existence at the date of the transfer, subject to a prior interest created by the same transfer, the i nterest created for the benefit of such person shall not take effect, unless it extends to the whole of the remaining interest of the transferor in the property."
RulingByLowerCourt	<N<N<ORG>ME>ME>s per the illustration appended to the provision where <N<N<ORG>ME>ME> transfers his property to B in trust for <N<N<ORG>ME>ME> and his prospective wife successively for their life and after the death of the survivor, for the eldest son of the intended marriage for life and after his death for <N<N<ORG>ME>ME>'s second son, interest created for the benefit of the eldest son does not take effect for the reason that it does not extend to the whole of <N<N<ORG>ME>ME>'s remaining interest in the property.
RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	<N<N<ORG>ME>ME> transfer cannot be made directly to an unborn person, for the definition of transfer in Section 5 is limited to living persons.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such transfer can only be made by the machinery of trusts.
RatioOfTheDecision	Possibly, to express this distinction, the expression "for the benefit of" has been used, since trustees being the transferees hold the property for the benefit of the unborn person.
RatioOfTheDecision	6.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the case at hand the entir e interest in the property goes to the benefit of the child to be born to <NAME>.
RatioOfTheDecision	As the facts go to show, in fact a child was born in August 1977, What would have happened if <NAME> did not get a male child is also spelt out in the trust deed.
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
RatioOfTheDecision	Above being the position the <ORG> was justified in its conclusion.
RulingByPresentCourt	The question referred is answered in the affirmative, in favor of the assessed and against the <ORG>.
RulingByPresentCourt	8.
RulingByPresentCourt	The reference stands disposed of.
