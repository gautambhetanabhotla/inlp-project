RatioOfTheDecision	The action for infringement is a statutory remedy conferred on the registered proprietor of a registered trade mark for the vindication of "the exclusive right to the use of the trade mark in relation to those goods" while an action for passing off is a common law remedy, being in substance an action for defunct, that is.
