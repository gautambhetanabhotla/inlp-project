Argument	4.
Argument	The present appellant approached <ORG> in Writ Petition No.
Argument	610 of 1974 under Article 227 of the Constitution.
Argument	<ORG> declined to interfere with the order of the learned District Judge even though it was satisfied that the appellant had established that it was prevented by a sufficient cause from preferring the appeal in time.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is frankly un-understandable and exhibits a rigid and inflexible view of jurisdiction under Article 227 ultimately leading to injustice.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is what <ORG> says :  May be, if I had considered the matter as the court of first instance I might have taken a different view, but unless it can be held that the view taken by the learned District Judge was not a plausible view that cannot be reversed in exercise of the powers under Article 226 of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	Article 227 or Article 226 were devised to advance justice and not to thwart it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, accepting the finding of <ORG> that sitting as  trial court, it was satisfied that the appellant had made out sufficient cause for condoning the delay, we must interfere in this appeal so as to advance justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	5.
