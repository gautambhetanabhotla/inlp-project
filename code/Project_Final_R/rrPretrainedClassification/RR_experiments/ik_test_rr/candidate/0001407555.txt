RatioOfTheDecision	It was contended that in view of Section 11-A of the Act the entire land acquisition proceedings lapsed as no award under Section 11 had been made within 2 years from the date of commencement of the Land Acquisition Amendment Act , 1984.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are of the view that t he above plea has no force.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this case, the <ORG> had taken possession of the land in question under Section 17(1) of the Act.It is not open to the <ORG> to withdraw from the acquisition ( Section 48 of the Act).
RatioOfTheDecision	In such a case, Section 11-A of the Act is not attracted and the acquisition proceedings would not lapse, even if it is assumed that no award was made within the period prescribed by Section 11-A of the Act.
Argument	Delivering the Judgment of a Three Member Bench of this Court, in <CITATION_1653863> , 1993 (4) sc 369, s.p.
Argument	<NAME>, J., at page 374, paragraph 15, stated the law thus:  "Ordinarily, the <ORG> can take possession of the land proposed to be acquired only after an award of compensation in respect thereof has been made under section 11 .
Argument	Upon the taking of possession, the land vests in the <ORG>, that is to say, the owner of the land loses to the <ORG> the title to it.This is what section 16 states.
Statute	The provisions of section 11-A are intended to benefit the landowner and  ensure that the award is made within a period of two years from the date of the section 6 declaration.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the ordinary case, therefore, when <ORG> fails to make an award within two years of the declaration under section 6 , the land has still not wasted in the <ORG> and its title remains with the owner, the acquisition proceedings are still pending, and by virtue of the provisions of section 11-   A, lapse.
RatioOfTheDecision	When section 17(1) is applied by reason of urgency, <ORG> takes possession of the land prior to the making of the award under Section 11 and thereupon the owner is diversted of the title to the land which is vested in the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 17(1) states so in unmistakable terms.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clearly, section 11- A can have no application to cases of acquisition under Section 17 , because the lands have already vested in the <ORG> and there is no provision in the said Act by which land statutorily vested in the <ORG> can revert to the owner."
RatioOfTheDecision	(Emphasis supplied) We, therefore, hold that the l and acquisition proceedings in the instant case did not lapse.
