RatioOfTheDecision	To judge this issue, primarily, the general nature of cases upon which <ORG> placed its reliance need to be looked into.

RatioOfTheDecision	At the outset it is to be mentioned that Article 311 cannot be automatically invoked in all the instances where a person is not given an opportunity of hearing.
RatioOfTheDecision	Article 311 confers certain safeguards upon persons employed in civil capacities under <ORG> or a <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Only persons who are holding "civil posts" can claim the protection provided under Article 311.
RatioOfTheDecision	The 1st Respondent could claim the protection of Article 311 only if she holds a 'civil post'.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> of this Court in <CITATION_125960> AIR 1967 SC 884 has explained the meaning of 'civil post'.
RatioOfTheDecision	Here it was held that:  "There is no formal definition of 'post' and 'civil post'.
RatioOfTheDecision	The sense in which they are used in the Services Chapter of Part XIV of the Constitut ion is indicated by their context and settinga civil post means a post not connected with defence outside the regular services.
RatioOfTheDecision	A post is a service or employment.
RatioOfTheDecision	A person holding a post under a <ORG> is a person serving or employed under the <ORG> There is a relationship of master and servant between the <ORG> and a person holding a post under it.
RatioOfTheDecision	The existence of this relationship is indicated by the <ORG>'s right to select and appoint the holder of the post, its right to suspend and dismiss him, its right to control the manner and method of his doing the work and the payment of his wages or remuneration.
RatioOfTheDecision	A relationship of master and servant may be established by the presence of all or some of these indicia, in conjunction with other circumstances and it is a question of fact in each case whether there is a relation between the <ORG> and the alleged holder of the post."
RatioOfTheDecision	[Para 9, <CITATION_125960> it was also held that "a post is an employment but every employment is not a post."
RatioOfTheDecision	While dealing with the ter mination of an employee, another Constitution Bench of this Court looked into the applicability of Article 311 in <CITATION_1924249> .
RatioOfTheDecision	.

RatioOfTheDecision	Considering this and other aspects it is ruled that <ORG> Company is not a <ORG> of the purpose of Article 311.
RatioOfTheDecision	Reverting back to the case in hand, Section 4 of The Post Graduate Institute of Medical Education & Research, Chandigarh Act , 1966 [<ORG> Act] says that <ORG> is a 'body corporate which is having a perpetual succession and a common seal with power.'

RatioOfTheDecision	There is als o no master and servant relationship between the <ORG> and an employee of <ORG>, which is a separate legal entity in itself.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is a settled position that a person cannot be said to have a status of holding a 'civil post' under <ORG> merely because his salary is paid from the <ORG> fund or that the <ORG> exercises a certain amount of control over the post.

RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore the employees of <ORG> cannot avail the protection of Article 311 since the same can be claimed only by the members of a civil service of the <ORG> or of <ORG> or of a civil service of a <ORG> or by persons who hold a civil post under the <ORG> or a <ORG>.
