RulingByLowerCourt	HEADNOTE: The Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act 1971 inter alia introduced s. 11A into the parent Act of 1947.
RulingByLowerCourt	The new section provided that if in the course of the adjudication of an Industrial dispute relating to the discharge or dismissal of a workman a La bour <ORG>, <ORG>, or National <ORG> is satisfied that the order of discharge or dismissal was not justified, it may, by its award, set aside the order of discharge or dismissal and direct reinstatement of the workmen or reduce punishment etc.
RulingByLowerCourt	The proviso to the section laid down that in any proceeding under the section the <ORG> or <ORG> in question shall rely only on the materials on record and shall not take any fresh evidence in relation to the matter.
RulingByLowerCourt	The section came into force with effect from December 15, 1971.
RulingByLowerCourt	In the present appeals by special leave the two questions that arose for consideration were : (i) whether s. 11 A had made any changes in the existing legal situation as laid down by this <ORG> and if so, to what extent-, (ii) whether the section was applicable to industrial disputes which had been already referred for adjudication and were pending as on December 15, 1972.
RatioOfTheDecision	HELD : (i) The statement of objects and reasons cannot be taken into account for the purpose of interpreting the p lain words of the section.
RatioOfTheDecision	But it gives an indication as to what the <ORG> wanted to achieve.
RatioOfTheDecision	[608 A-B] (ii) The Act is a beneficial piece of legislation enacted in the interest of employees.
RatioOfTheDecision	it is well settled that in construing the provisions of a welfare legislation, courts should adopt, what is described as a beneficent rule of construction.
RatioOfTheDecision	If two constructions are reasonably possible to be placed on the section, it follows that the construction which furthers the policy and object of the Act and is more, beneficial to the employees has to be preferred.
RatioOfTheDecision	The interpretation must be liberal enough to achieve the legislative purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	It must still be in accordance with the plain words, of the statute or the section and must not do violence to the language used by the legislature.
RatioOfTheDecision	it will further have to be found from the words of the section whether it has altered the entire law as laid down in the existing decisions and, if so, whether there is a clear expression of that intention in the language of t he section.
RatioOfTheDecision	[608 G-H; 609 A-C] (iii)Both in respect of cases where a domestic enquiry has been held as also in cases when the <ORG> considers the matter on the evidence adduced before it for the first time, the satisfaction under s. 11A , about the guilt or otherwise of the workmen concerned is that of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It has to consider the evidence and come to a conclusion one way or other.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even in cases where an enquiry has been held by an employer and a finding of misconduct arrived at, the <ORG> can now differ from that finding in a proper case and hold that no misconduct is proved.
RatioOfTheDecision	[611 A-C] The contention that the stage for interference under section 11A by the <ORG> is reached only when it has to consider the punishment after 588 having accepted the finding of guilt recorded by the employee, could not be accepted., The <ORG> under s. 11A can consider the question of guilt as well as of punishment.
RatioOfTheDecision	It can also alter the punishment imposed by an employer.
RatioOfTheDecision	[611 C-H; 612 A-B] It was specifically  contended before the <ORG> in <ORG>, that when an employer had held no enquiry as required by the Standing Orders, it was not open to him to adduce evidence before the <ORG> for the first time and justify the order of discharge.
RatioOfTheDecision	This contention was rejected by this <ORG> and it was held that if the enquiry was defective or no inquiry had been held, as required by the Standing Orders, the entire case would be open before the <ORG> and the, employer would have to justify, on evidence as well that its order of dismissal or discharge was proper.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is no provision either in the Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1948 or in the Industrial Disputes Act which states that an order of dismissal or discharge is illegal if it is not recorded by a proper and valid domestic enquiry.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the contention that such 'an enquiry being illegal, the <ORG> has now under s. 11A no alternative but to order reinstatement could not be accepted.
RatioOfTheDecision	Moreover the Ind ustrial Disputes Act cannot be differently applied to employees who are governed by the Stand Orders Act and those who are not governed by it.
RatioOfTheDecision	[612 H; 613 A-G] The expression 'materials on record' occurring in the proviso to s. 11A cannot be confined only to the materials which were available at the domestic enquiry.
RatioOfTheDecision	On the other hand the 'materials on record' in the proviso must be held to refer to materials on record before the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	They take in (1) the evidence taken by the management at the enquiry and the proceedings of the enquiry, or (2) the above evidence and in addition any further evidence led before the <ORG>, or (3) evidence placed before the <ORG> for the first time in support of the action taken by the employer as well as the evidence adduced by the workmen contra.
RatioOfTheDecision	The expression 'fresh evidence' has to be read in the context in which it appears, namely, as distinguished from the expression 'materials on record'.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG>, for the purpose of determining the question of misconduct  or punishment or relief to be granted to workmen, has to act only on the basis of the , materials on record' before it and.
RatioOfTheDecision	cannot call for fresh evidence as an appellate authority can normally do.
RatioOfTheDecision	[613H; 614 A-H; 615A-D] (iv)The words "in a proceeding under this section" in the proviso.
RatioOfTheDecision	to s. 11A are very significant.
RatioOfTheDecision	There cannot be a "proceeding under this section" before the section has come into force.
RatioOfTheDecision	A proceeding under that section can only be on or after 15-12-1971.
RatioOfTheDecision	T at also gives an indication that section 11A applies only to disputes which are referred for adjudication after the section has come into force.
RatioOfTheDecision	There are no express words in s. 11A which would make it applicable even to disputes referred before it came into force nor could such an inference be gathered by necessary intendment, The section therefore does not apply to proceed- ings before 15-12-1971.

Precedent	We have held that the previous law, according to the decisions of this <ORG>, in cases where a proper domestic enquiry had been held, was that the <ORG>, had no jurisdiction to interfere with the finding of misconduct except under certain circumstances.
RatioOfTheDecision	The position further was that the <ORG> had no jurisdiction to interfere with the punishment imposed by an employer both in cases where the misconduct is established in a proper domestic enquiry as also in cases where the <ORG> finds such misconduct proved on the basis of evidence adduced before it These limitations on the powers of the <ORG>s were recognised by this <ORG> mainly on the basis that the power to take disciplinary action and impose punishment was part of the managerial functions.
RatioOfTheDecision	That means that the law, as laid down by this <ORG> over a period of years,' had recognised certain managerial rights in an employer.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have pointed out that this position has now been changed by section 11 A.
RatioOfTheDecision	The section has the effect of alte ring the law by abridging the rights of the employer inasmuch as it gives power to the <ORG> for the first time to differ both on a finding of misconduct arrived at by' an employer as well as the punishment imposed by him.
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence in order to make the section applicable even to disputes, which had been referred prior to the coming into force of the section, there should be such a clear, express and manifest indication in the section.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is no such express indication.
RatioOfTheDecision	An inference that the section applies to proceedings, which are already pending, can also be gathered by necessary, intendment.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the case on hand, no such inference can be drawn as the indications are to the contrary.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have already referred to the, proviso to section 11A which states 'in any proceeding under this section'.
RatioOfTheDecision	A proceeding under the section can only be after the section has come into force.
RatioOfTheDecision	Further the section itself was brought into force some time after the Amendment Act was passed.
RatioOfTheDecision	These circumstances as well as the scheme  of the section and particularly the wording of the Proviso indicate that section 11A does not apply to disputes which had been referred prior to 15-12- 1971.
RulingByPresentCourt	The section applies only to disputes which are referred for adjudication on or after 15-121971.
RulingByPresentCourt	To conclude, in our opinion, section 11A has-no application to disputes referred prior to 15-12-1971.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such disputes have to be dealt with according to the decisions of this <ORG> already referred to.
Precedent	In Civil Appeal No.
Precedent	1461 of 1972, <ORG> had considered only the question regarding- the applicability of the section to disputes which had been referred before the section came into force.
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG> has held that the section does not apply to such disputes.
RulingByLowerCourt	This view is in accordance with our decision and as such is correct.
RulingByLowerCourt	This appeal is hence dismissed.
Precedent	In the three other orders, which are the subject of consideration in Civil Appeals Nos.
Precedent	1995 of 1972, 1996 of 1972 and 2386 of 1972, the Labour <ORG>, Bombay has held that section 11A app lies even to disputes which had been referred prior to 15-121971 This view, according to our judgment, is erroneous.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> has also-expressed some views on the construction to be placed on section 11 A.
RatioOfTheDecision	Part of the views expressed therein is correct; but the rest are wrong.
RulingByPresentCourt	To the extent that the decision of <ORG> in the three orders are contrary to our decision on both the points, they are set aside add the appeals allowed to that extent.
RulingByPresentCourt	The <ORG> and <ORG>s concerned in all these appeals, will proceed with the adjudication of the disputes in accordance with the views expressed in this judgment.
RulingByPresentCourt	There will be no order as to costs in these appeals.
