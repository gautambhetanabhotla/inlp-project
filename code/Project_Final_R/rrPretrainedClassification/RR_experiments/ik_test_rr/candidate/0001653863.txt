Argument	<ORG> noted that it was the appellants' own case that more than two years had elapsed since the date of issue of the notification under Section 4 .

Statute	Section 4 of the said Act requires the publication of a notification that it appears to the appropriate <ORG> that certain land is needed or is likely to be needed for a public purpose.
Statute	Thereupon an officer authorised by the <ORG> may enter upon the land to survey it and do all other acts necessary to ascertain whether the land is suitable for the public purpose.
Statute	Section 5 requires the payment of estimated compensation to the owner for damage done in enter ing upon the land and doing such acts as are necessary to ascertain whether it can be used for the public purpose.
Statute	Under the provisions of Section 5-A any person interested in the land may raise objections to the proposed acquisition.
Statute	Upon considering the report of the Collector who hears such objections, if the <ORG> is satisfied that the land is needed for the public purpose, a declaration to that effect shall be made under the provisions of Section 6 .
Statute	Section 9(1) contemplates the issue of a notice that the <ORG> intends to take possession of the land and it must invite claims for compensation for all interests in the land.
Statute	The Collector must inquire into the claims under the provisions of Section 11 and make an award of compensation in favour of the persons found interested in the land.
Statute	Section 16 states that the Collector may, after he has made an award under Section 11 , "take possession of the land which shall thereupon vest absolutely in the <ORG> free form all encumbrances."
Statute	Section 1 1-A was inserted in the said Act by Act 68 of 1984 and it reads thus:  11-A.
Statute	Period within which an award shall be made.
Statute	The Collector shall made an award under Section 11 within a period of two years from the dale of the publication of the declaration and if no award is made within that period, the entire proceedings for the acquisition of the land shall lapse :  Provided that in a case where the said declaration has been published before the commencement of the Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act , 1984, the award shall be made within a period of two years from such commencement.
Statute	The provisions of Section 48(1) may also be noted.
Statute	It states that "the <ORG> shall be at liberty to withdraw from the acquisition of any land of which possession has not been taken.
Statute	13.
Statute	Section 17 provides from cases where there is urgency.
Statute	The relevant provisions for our purposes read thus :  17.
Statute	Special powers in cases of urgency - (1) In cases of urgency, whenever the appropriate <ORG> so directs, the Collector, though  no such award has been made, may, on the expiration of fifteen days from the publication of the notice mentioned in Section 9 , Sub-section (1), take possession of any land needed for public purpose.
Statute	Such land shall thereupon vest absolutely in the <ORG>, free from all encumbrances.
Statute	(3-A) Before taking possession of any land under Sub-section (1) or Sub-section (2), the Collector shall, without prejudice to the provisions of Sub-section (3),.
Statute	(a) tender payment of eighty per centum of the compensation for such land as estimated by him to the persons interested entitled thereto,  (b) pay it to them, unless prevented by some one or more of the contingencies mentioned in Section 31 , Sub-section (2), and where the Collector is so prevented, the provisions of Section 31 , Sub-section (2), (except the second proviso thereto), shall apply as they apply to the payment of compensation under that section, (3-B) The amount paid or deposited under Sub-section (3-A) shall be taken into account for determining the  amount of compensation required to be tendered under Section 31 , and where the amount so paid or deposited exceeds the compensation awarded by the Collector under Section 11 , the excess may, unless refunded within three months from the date of the Collector's award, be recovered as an arrear of land revenue.
Statute	(4) In the case of any land to which, in the opinion of the appropriate <ORG>, the provisions of Sub-section (1) or Sub-section (2) are applicable, the appropriate <ORG> may direct that the provisions of Section 5-A shall not apply, and, if it does so direct, a declaration may be made under Section 6 in respect of the land at any time after the date of the publication of the notification under Section 4 , Sub-section (1).
RatioOfTheDecision	14.
RatioOfTheDecision	There are two judgments of this Court which we must note.
RatioOfTheDecision	<CITATION_37963> .
RatioOfTheDecision	, it was held that <ORG> could not withdraw from acquisition under Section 48 once it had taken possession of the land.
RatioOfTheDecision	<CITATION_54770> , it was held that "after possession has been taken  pursuant to a notification under Section 17(1) the land is vested in the <ORG> and the notification cannot be cancelled under Section 21 of the General Clauses Act, nor can the notification be withdrawn in exercise of the powers under Section 48 of the Land Acquisition Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Any other view would enable the State <ORG> to circumvent the specific provision by relying upon a general power.
RatioOfTheDecision	When possession of the land is taken under Section 17(1) , the land vests in the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is no provision by which land statutorily vested in the <ORG> reverts to the original owner by mere cancellation of the notification."
RatioOfTheDecision	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	Ordinarily, the <ORG> can take possession of the land proposed to be acquired only after an award of compensation in respect thereof has been made under Section 11 .
RatioOfTheDecision	Upon the taking of possession the land vests in the <ORG> that is to say, the owner of the land loses to the <ORG> the title to it.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is what Section 16 states.
RatioOfTheDecision	The provisions of Section 11-A are  intended to benefit the land owner and ensure that the award is made within a period of two years from the date of Section 6 declaration.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the ordinary case, therefore, when <ORG> fails to make an award within two years of the declaration under Section 6 , the land has still not vested in the <ORG> and its title remains with the owner, the acquisition proceedings are still pending and, by virtue of the provisions of Section 11-A , lapse.
RatioOfTheDecision	When Section 17(1) is applied by reason of urgency, <ORG> takes possession of the land prior to the making of the award under Section 11 and thereupon the owner is divested of the title to the land which is vested in the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 17(1) states so in unmistakable terms.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clearly, Section 11-A can have no application to cases of acquisition under Section 17 because the lands have already vested in the <ORG> and there is no provision in the said Act by which land statutorily vested in the <ORG> can revert to the owner.
RatioOfTheDecision	16.
Statute	Further, Section 17  (3-A) postulates that the owner will be offered an amount equivalent to 80 per cent of the estimated compensation for the land before the Government takes possession of it under Section 17(1) .
Statute	Section 11-A cannot be so construed as to leave the Government holding title to the land without the obligation to determine compensation, make an award and pay to the owner the difference between the amount of the award and the amount of 80 per cent of the estimated compensation.
RatioOfTheDecision	17.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the instant case, even that 80 per cent of the estimated compensation was not paid to the appellants although Section 17 (3-A) required that it should have been paid before possession of the said land was taken but that does not mean that the possession was taken illegally or that the said land did not thereupon vest in the Ist respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is, at any rate, not open to the third respondent, who, as the letter of the Special Land Acquisition Officer dated 27th June, 1990 shows, failed to make the necessary monies available and who h as been in occupation of the said land ever since its possession was taken, to urge that the possession was taken illegally and that, therefore, the said land has not vested in the first respondent and the first respondent is under no obligation to make an award.
RatioOfTheDecision	18.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is no merit whatsoever in the submission that compensation can be awarded to the appellants under Section 5 .
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 5 postulates payment of compensation for damage done to land during the course of surveying it and doing all other acts necessary to ascertain whether it is capable of being adapted for a public purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 5 has no applicable to the instance case.
RatioOfTheDecision	19.
