RatioOfTheDecision	To accept it would be, apart from making a depar- ture from the rules o f grammar, (for doing which no valid grounds exist), making not only the rule in question but also s. 3 of the Defence of India Act where similar language is used almost ineffective.
RatioOfTheDecision	What has, however, to be considered is his other argument.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question posed by the argument is whether an authority competent to make an order under the aforesaid provision can make such an order on the ground that the authority feels it necessary to prevent a person from acting in any manner prejudicial to the maintenance of law and order.
RatioOfTheDecision	The expression "law and ordee' does not find any place in the rule and is not synonymous with "public order'.
RatioOfTheDecision	It s to me that "law and order" is a comprehensive expression in which would be included not merely public order, but matters such as public peace, tranquillity, orderliness in a locality or a local area and perhaps some other matters.
RatioOfTheDecision	"Public order" is something distinct from order or orderliness in a local area.
RatioOfTheDecision	Under r. 30(1) no power is conferred upon that authority to detain a p erson on the ground that it is necessary so to do in order to prevent that person from acting in a manner prejudicial to the main- tenance of order in a local area.
RatioOfTheDecision	What is it that the District Magistrate, Patna had in mind when he ordered the detention of the petitioner ?
RatioOfTheDecision	Was the apprehension entertained by the District Magistrate that Dr. <NAME>, if left at large, was likely to do some thing which will imperil the maintenance of public order generally or was it that he apprehended that Dr. <NAME>'s activities may cause disturbances in a particular locality?
RatioOfTheDecision	There is thus an ambiguity on the face of the order and, therefore, the order must be held to be bad.
RatioOfTheDecision	No doubt, the order also refers to the apprehension felt by the District Magistrate about Dr. <NAME>'s acting in a manner prejudicial to public safety.
RatioOfTheDecision	But then the question arises, what is it that weighed with the District Magistrate, the apprehension regarding public safety or an apprehension regarding the maintenance of law and order ?
RatioOfTheDecision	Again, would the D istrict Magistrate have made the order solely on the ground that he felt apprehension re- garding the maintenance of public safety because of the activities in which he thought Dr. <NAME> might indulge ?
RatioOfTheDecision	It could well be that upon the material before him the District Magistrate would have refrained from making an order under r. 30 solely upon the first ground.
RatioOfTheDecision	Or on the other hand he would have made the order solely upon that ground.
RatioOfTheDecision	His order, however, which is the only material on the basis of which we can properly consider the matter gives no indication that the District Magistrate would have been prepared to make it only upon the ground relating to public safety.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the circumstances I agree with my brethren <NAME> and <NAME> that the order of detention cannot be sustained.
