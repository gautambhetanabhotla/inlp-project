RatioOfTheDecision	During the last few decades ques-  308  tion of payment of compensation for accidents has assumed great importance, which is co-related with the accidents which have touched a new height not only in India but in different parts of the world.
RatioOfTheDecision	Initially, the theory of payment of compensation was primarily linked with tort compensation only if the injury or damage was caused by someone's fault, of late the injury or damage being caused by someone's fault is being read as b ecause of someone's negligence or carelessness.
RatioOfTheDecision	That is why any damage caused by negligent conduct is generally actionable irrespective of the kind of activity out of which the damage arose.
RulingByLowerCourt	Even in an action based on the tort, the applicant has to show that the defendant was negligent i.e.
RulingByLowerCourt	there was a failure on his part to take that degree of care which was reasonable in the circumstances of the case.
RulingByLowerCourt	There has never been any doubt that those using the highways are under a duty to be careful and the legal position today is quite plain that any person using the road as a motorist will be liable, if by his action he negligently causes physical injuries to anybody else.

RulingByLowerCourt	9.
RulingByLowerCourt	Broadly speaking while fixing an amount of compensation payable to a victim of an accident, the damages have to be assessed separately as pecuniary damages and special damages.
RulingByLowerCourt	Pecuniary damages are those which the victim has actually incurred and which is capable of being calculated in terms of money-, whereas non-pecuniary damages are those which are incapable of being assessed by arithmetical calculations.
RulingByLowerCourt	In order to appreciate two concepts pecuniary damages may, include expenses incurred by the claimant : (i) medical attendance; (ii) loss of earning of profit upto the date of trial; (iii) other material loss.
RulingByLowerCourt	So far non-pecu- niary damages are concerned, they may include (i) damages for mental and physical shock, pain suffering, already suffered or likely to be suffered in future; (ii damages to compensate for the loss of amenities of life which may include a va-  309  riety of matters i.e.
RatioOfTheDecision	on  account of injury the claimant may not be able to walk, run or sit; (iii) damages for the loss of expectation of life, i.e.
RatioOfTheDecision	on account of injury the normal longevity of the person concerned is shortened; (iv) inconvenience, hardship, discomfort, disappointment, frustration and mental stress in life.

Fact	Money cannot renew a broken and shattered physical frame.
