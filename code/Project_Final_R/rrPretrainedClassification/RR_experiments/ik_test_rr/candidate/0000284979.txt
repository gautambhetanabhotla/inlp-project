Fact	The short question arising for our decision in this Appeal is, whether the Order by which <ORG> cancelled the select list of candidates for appointment as Conductors in the Chandigarh Transport Undertaking (<ORG>) prepared by <ORG> constituted therefor, because of its view of that select list not having been prepared in a fair and judicious manner, was liable to be interfered with by <ORG> (<ORG>) on the ground of that Order having not been made after affording an opportunity of hearing thereon to the members of the concerned <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The facts giving rise to the said question lie in a narrow compass.
Fact	In the year 1989, there arose 32 vacancies of conductors in <ORG> of Chandigar h was required to sponsor the names of eligible candidates while a three-member <ORG> constituted by <ORG> was required to prepare a select list of 32 candidates out of such candidates.
Fact	That selection Board interviewed as many as 446 candidates so sponsored by <ORG> and prepared a select list of 32 candidates on the basis of marks awardable for their educational qualifications plus the marks awarded for their performance at the interview, a criteria which was said to have been followed by a Selection board constituted for a similar purpose in the Year 1953.
Fact	That criteria, although required the award of marks for the educational qualification possessed by a candidate upto 110, enabled every member of <ORG> to award marks for such candidate's performance at the interview upto 20.
Fact	The select list of 32 candidates meant to fill the 32 vacancies of conductors in <ORG>, when was announced on September 11,1989, it invited severe criticism f rom the members of both the public and the <ORG> as to the role of the members of t he Selection Board in the matter of its preparation.
Fact	The select list, according to the criticism, was the amalgum of favourtism.
Fact	nepotism and even corruption resorted to by the members of <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG> which could not ignore such criticism, got examined the select list with reference to the marks awardable to the candidates for their educational qualification and the marks awarded by the members of <ORG> to the candidates for their performance at the interview had brought into select list the least qualified candidates who had been awarded least marks for marks for their educational qualifications.
RulingByLowerCourt	Such examination also revealed that uniform standards had not been applied to app candidates by <ORG> in their selection.
RulingByLowerCourt	These revelations, compelled <ORG> to conclude that the select list of candidates for appointment as conductors in <ORG> had  not been prepared by the members of <ORG> fairly and judiciously in that those members had taken undue advantage of the marks awardable by them at the interview to favour the candidates of their choice although there was no clinching evidence of corruption attributable to the members.
RulingByLowerCourt	This situation made <ORG> to think of cancellation of the dubious select list prepared by <ORG> and of the constitution of a new <ORG> to prepare a fresh select list on the basis of only 15 per cent interview marks awardable to candidates as against 30 per cent interview marks awardable earlier, lest the power of <ORG> to award interview marks may be utilised either to pull up unmerited candidates or pull down the merited candidates.
RulingByLowerCourt	Consequently, <ORG> made an order of cancelling the select list of candidates for appointment as conductors prepared by <ORG> and published on <ORG>ptember 11,1989, and constituted a new <ORG> lection Board to prepare a fresh select list of candidates including those who had been interviewed by the earlier <ORG>, according to the fresh selection criteria with 85 per cent marks awardable for educational qualifications of candidates and 15 per cent marks awardable for their performance at interview.
RulingByLowerCourt	The newly constituted <ORG> when was about to interview the eligible candidates for selection as conductor for <ORG>, the Respondents in this Appeal, whose names had found places in the cancelled select list of candidates, filed applications before <ORG> seeking the setting aside of t he aforesaid order made by <ORG> by which it had cancelled the select list prepared by the earlier <ORG> and directed the newly constituted <ORG> to prepare a fresh select list of candidates on the basis of altered criteria of marks.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG> which entertained those applications, has by its Judgment dated May 27,1991 not merely set aside that part of the impugned order  of <ORG> by which it had cancelled the earlier select list but also directed <ORG> by which it had cancelled to earlier select list but also directed <ORG> to appoint in the available vacancies of conductors in <ORG> the candidates from the cancelled select list in preference to candidates selected as conductors in the select list prepared by the fresh <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG>, which felt aggrieved by this Judgment of <ORG> has preferred this Appeal by special leave.
RulingByLowerCourt	In its judgment under Appeal, the <ORG> has, no doubt, expressed its reactions to the views of <ORG> as to t he charge of corruption levelled against the members of the earlier <ORG> in the matter of preparation of select list of candidates by it and the percentage of marks awardable to candidates for their performance at interview while examining the challenge directed against the order made by <ORG>  cancelling that select list and requiring the preparation of a fresh select list.
RulingByLowerCourt	Yet, those reactions are not made use of by the <ORG> as grounds for setting aside the order of <ORG> impugned before it.
RulingByLowerCourt	The sole ground, has been from the Judgment, which has weighed with the <ORG> for setting aside that part of the impugned order of <ORG> by which it had can called the select list of candidates prepared by the earlier <ORG> in the non-affording by the <ORG> of an opportunity of hearing to the members of its <ORG> before cancelling the select list of candidates prepared by them.
RulingByLowerCourt	It was contended on behalf of the Appellant - the <ORG>-that affording of an opportunity of hearing to the members of the earlier <ORG> before cancelling their dubious select list of candidates for appointment as conductors in <ORG>, being neither a requirement of law nor a requirement of any principle of natural Justice, the <ORG> co uld not have made it the sole ground for setting aside of the order by which the Appellant had cancelled such select list and hence the Judgment of <ORG> under appeal based on such untenable ground required to be set aside.
RulingByLowerCourt	The learned counsel for Respondents-Candidates in the select list cancelled by <ORG>, however, did not choose to urge that the ground of non- affording of an opportunity by <ORG> to the members of <ORG> before ordering cancellation of their select list, was a valid ground on which the <ORG> could have rested its Judgment under appeal.
RulingByLowerCourt	But, he contended, rather very streneously, that the Judgment of <ORG> under appeal was required to be sustained for the reason that the cancellation of the select list of candidates prepared by the earlier <ORG> had been made by the Appellant (<ORG>) without proof of corruption charges levelled against the members of that <ORG> in that matter of selection of candi dates and further without affording an opportunity of hearing to the candidates in the select list to sustain the same.
RulingByLowerCourt	We shall now proceed to examine the sustainability or otherwise of the rival contentions.
RulingByLowerCourt	The Judgment of <ORG> itself refers to the enquiry got conducted by <ORG> about the select list of 32 candidates as prepared by its <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	Such enquiry revealed that the members of the <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	Such enquiry revealed that the members of the <ORG> had made use of the interview marks awardable by them for performance of candidates at interview to eliminate merited candidates from the list and to bring in unmerited candidates, is a matter adverted to in the said Judgment.
RulingByLowerCourt	No doubt, the <ORG> (Appellant) has found that there was no direct evidence of corruption produced against the members of the <ORG> for the favour they had shown in the matter of awarding high interview marks to unmerited candidates.
RulingByLowerCourt	Yet, having regard to the syst ematic manner of award by <ORG> of high interview marks to candidates with low marks got for their educational qualifications and of low interview marks to candidates with high marks got for their educational qualifications, <ORG> discerned the tilting of balance by <ORG> in favour of candidates with poor qualifications and against candidates with high qualifications.
RulingByLowerCourt	Consequently, it concluded that <ORG> had not prepared to select list of candidates for appointment as conductors in <ORG>, in a fair and judicious manner.
RulingByLowerCourt	When the select list prepared by <ORG> was thus regarded by <ORG> as a dubious select list, it cancelled that select list and constituted a new <ORG> to prepare a fresh select list of candidates out of the competing candidates including the candidates whose cases were considered by the earlier <ORG>, on a fresh selection criteria which provided for award of as large as 85  percent marks for educational qualifications of candidates and for award of as little as 15 percent for performance of candidates at interview, by making an order in that regard.
RatioOfTheDecision	The order so made by <ORG> cannot but be regarded as the right and the just one.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such an order, as is held by <ORG>, cannot be vitiated on the ground that it had been made without affording an opportunity of hearing to the members of <ORG> who had prepared it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Further, such an order cannot be vitiated either because no direct evidence was made available to prove corruption charges against the members of <ORG> in the matter of award of interview marks by them so as to tilt the balance in favour of candidates with poor educational qualifications and against the candidates with high educational qualifications or because there was no opportunity of hearing afforded to the candidates in the select list to sustain it before its cancellation by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Affording of an  opportunity of hearing by an <ORG> to the members of <ORG> Constitute by it, before cancelling a dubious select list of candidates for appointment to civil posts prepared by such <ORG> is not and cannot be requirement of either law or any principle of natural justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is so for the reason that no member of <ORG> acquires any vested right or interest in sustaining a select list prepared by the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Besides, there is no personal right or interest of any member of <ORG> which could be adversely effected, by the <ORG> cancelling a select list of candidates prepared by <ORG> when it is found to have been prepared by <ORG> in unfair and injudicious manner.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, there can arise no need to any <ORG> to afford an opportunity of hearing to the members of the <ORG> before cancelling a dubious select list of candidates for appointment to civil posts prepared by it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence, we must hold that the  <ORG> was wholly wrong in setting aside <ORG> order by which the dubious select list of candidates for appointment as conductors in <ORG> prepared by <ORG> constituted by it had been cancelled, on its erroneous view that non-affording of an opportunity of hearing tot the members of the <ORG> before cancelling its select list had vitiated that Order.
RatioOfTheDecision	This would be our answer tot he question adverted to at the outset.
RatioOfTheDecision	Coming to the contentions of the learned counsel for Respondents (selectees in the cancelled select list) that <ORG> when had found no direct evidence which could establish charges of corruption levelled against the members of the <ORG> in the matter of preparation of select list of conductors for appointments as conductors in its <ORG>, it should not have made an order cancelling the select list, all that could be said is, that failure on the part of complainants to establish charges of corruption levelled against the memb ers of <ORG> could not have saved the select list, if it was otherwise found to be dubious.
RatioOfTheDecision	The select list, which was cancelled by <ORG> was found by it to have been prepared in unfair and injudicious manner, in that the interview marks purported to have been awarded by the members of <ORG> for the performance of candidates at their interview were either inflated to push up the candidates who had got poor marks for their educational qualifications or deflated to pull down the candidates who had got high marks for their educational qualifications.
RatioOfTheDecision	That select list was also found to have been prepared without adopting common eligibility criteria, for all candidates.
RatioOfTheDecision	When the said reasons formed the basis for <ORG> to cancel the select list of <ORG>, the fact that charges of corruption levelled against the members to <ORG> in the preparation of that select list had not been established by direct evidence prod uced in that regard, can make no difference.
RatioOfTheDecision	What remains for our consideration is that contention of the learned counsel for Respondents that the Respondents who were the selectes in the select list should have been heard by <ORG> before it cancelled that list as a dubious one.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to learned counsel, non-affording of an opportunity of hearing to the Respondents- Selectees before the select list in which they had found places as selected condidates for appointment in the vacant civil posts of conductors in <ORG> should be regarded by us a s a sufficient ground not to disturb the Judgment of the <ORG> under appeal, although the Judgment itself is not rendered on that basis.
RatioOfTheDecision	The contention of learned counsel on our view, misconceived and hence calls to be rejected.
Precedent	<CITATION_1222533> , reported in JT (1991) 2 SC 380, <ORG> of this Court which had occasion to examine the question whether a candidate seeking appointment to a civil post can be regarded to have acquired  an indefeasible right to appointment in such post merely because of the appearance of his name in the merit list (select list) of candidates for such post has answered the question in the negative by enunciating the correct legal position thus:  "it is not correct to say that if a number of vacancies are notified for appointment and adequate number of condidates are found fit, the successful candidates acquire an indefeasible right to b appointment which cannot be legitimately denied.
RatioOfTheDecision	Ordinarily the notification merely amounts to an invitation to qualified candidates to apply for recruitment and on their selection they do not acquire any right to the post.
RatioOfTheDecision	Unless the relevant Recruitment Rules so indicate, the <ORG> is under no legal duty to fill u p all or any of the vacancies.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, it does not mean that the <ORG> has the licence of acting in an arbitrary manner.
RatioOfTheDecision	The decision not to fill up the vacancies has to be taken bona fide for appropriate reasons.
RatioOfTheDecision	And if the vacancies or any of them are filled up,  the <ORG> is bound to respect the comparative merit of the candidates, as reflected at the recruitment test, and no discrimination can be permitted.
RatioOfTheDecision	This correct position has been consistently followed by this <ORG>, and we do not find any discordant note in the decisions in the <CITATION_470118> .
RatioOfTheDecision	1 SCR 165; <CITATION_1049711> ,  4 SCC 268, or <NAME> and others v. <ORG> of Punjab and others:  1 SCR 899."
RatioOfTheDecision	If we have regard to the above enunciation that a candidate who finds a place in the select list as a candidate selected for appointment to a civil post, does not acquire an indefeasible right to be appointed in such posting the absence of any specific Rule entitling him for such appointment and he could be aggrieved by his non- appointment only when the <ORG> does so either arbitrarily or for no bona fide reasons, it follows as a necessary concomitant that such candidate even if has a legitimate expectation of being appointed in such posts due to his name finding a place in the select li st of candidates, cannot claim to have a right to be heard before such select list is cancelled for bona fide and valid reasons and not arbitrarily: In the instant case, when <ORG> which received the complaints about the unfair and injudicious manner in which select list of candidates for appointment as conductors in <ORG> was prepared by <ORG> constituted for the purpose, found those complaints to be well founded on an enquiry got made in that regard, we are unable to find that <ORG> had acted either arbitrarily or without bona fide and valid reasons in cancelling such 0dubious select list.
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence, the contentions of the learned counsel for the Respondents as to the sustainability of the Judgment of <ORG> under appeal on the ground of non-affording of an opportunity of hearing to the Respondents (candidates in the select list) is a misconceived one and is consequently rejected.
RulingByPresentCourt	In the result, we allow this appeal, set aside the Judgment under appeal,  and reject the applications made by Respondents before <ORG>, Chandigarh.
