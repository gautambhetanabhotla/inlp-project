Fact	1.
Fact	Unless the oral application for an adjournment of the hearing of a previous execution petition, made by the decree-holder on the 7th August 1908 is held to be an application to take a step in aid of execution, the present execution application of 3rd July 1911 is clearly barred by limitation.
Fact	2.
Fact	The question is not free from difficulty.
Fact	<CITATION_863927> (1900) I.L.R., 27 Calc., 285, there is an obiter dictum showing that an application for adjournment to enable the decree-holder to conduct his petition further with effect is not an application to take a step in aid of execution.
Fact	3.
Fact	A different view was taken in <NAME> v. <NAME> (1909) 14 C.W.N., 486, where the point directly arose.
Fact	4.
Fact	The learned vakil for the judgment-debtors (appellants before us) sought to distinguish <NAME> v. <NAME> (1909) 14 C.W.N., 486 from the present case on two grounds:  (a) that the application for adjournment relied on i n <NAME> v. <NAME> (1909) 14 C.W.N., 486 was in writing and not oral;  (b) that the application in that case was an application for an adjournment to enable the decree-holder to produce an affidavit as evidence to carry on those execution proceedings further, whereas it was not so in the present cast).
Fact	5.
Fact	I think that neither of these contentions is sound.
Fact	There is nothing in Article 179 of the Limitation Act which requires the application to take some step in aid of execution to be in writing.
Fact	<NAME> v. <NAME> (1880) I.L.R., 3 All., 39 and <NAME> v. <NAME> (1891) I.L.R., 15 Bom., 405 at p. 407 are direct authorities to the contrary and I am prepared to follow them.
Argument	6.
Argument	Then as regards the other distinction sought to be made, I am unable to see that the application for an adjournment to enable the decree-holder to produce affidavit-evidence in aid of further proceedings [which was the application in <NAME> v. <NAME> h (1909) 11 C.W.N., 486], stands on a better footing than an application for an adjournment to enable the decree-holder to produce an encumbrance certificate in respect of the attached property in aid of further proceedings in execution.
Argument	7.
Fact	Then reliance is placed by the appellant's vakil on the reason given in the obiter dictum in <CITATION_863927> (1900) I.L.R., 27 Calc.
RatioOfTheDecision	285.
RatioOfTheDecision	That reason is that an application for adjournment is in retardation of the execution proceedings and not in aid of the execution proceedings.
RatioOfTheDecision	I think that there is a fallacy in this reasoning.
RatioOfTheDecision	When an application for adjournment is made by the judgment-debtor, it is almost invariably to retard the execution proceedings.
RatioOfTheDecision	As regards an application by the decree-holder it may be one of three things:  (a) It may be to get an order in aid or  (b) it may be to get an order in retardation or  (c) it may be to get an order which is neither.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	An application by the decree-holder to give time to the judgment-debtor merely as a matter of grac e is a step in retardation.
RulingByPresentCourt	An application for an adjournment to enable the decree-holder to produce records or evidence necessary to effectively conduct the execution proceedings further will be an application to get an order in aid.
Fact	<NAME> v. <NAME> (1912) 14 Bom.
Fact	L.R., 1204; <CITATION_1805304> (1912) I.L.R., 36 Bom., 638; <CITATION_254377> (1907) I.L.R., 29 All., 301 at p. 302 and <CITATION_1220020> (1882) I.L.R., 5 Mad., 141.
Argument	9.
Argument	An application by the decree-holder to draw money deposited in <ORG> or to obtain copies of sale lists (without anything to indicate that they were necessary to aid further execution) will be application neither in aid nor in retardation.
Argument	10.
Fact	In the present case, I think that the application for an adjournment was for an order in aid.
RatioOfTheDecision	11.
RatioOfTheDecision	I think that the <ORG> is a little harsh on decree-holders in fixing the date of applying for execution as one of the starting points for limitation for calculating the 'three years' period for the next subsequent applicati on in execution instead of the date on which the proceedings in the previous application for execution terminated, and I should be glad if the Limitation Act is amended so as to fix the latter date.
RatioOfTheDecision	But the harshness is mitigated to some extent by allowing the date of applying to take a step in aid to be also a starting point and I think that if even an oral application is really for an order which will be a step in aid (and not merely for an order which will be indifferent or retarding) a liberal interpretation should be put on the Article 179 so as to enable a decree-holder to obtain the fruits of his decree.
RatioOfTheDecision	12.
RulingByPresentCourt	In the result I would dismiss the appeal with costs.
