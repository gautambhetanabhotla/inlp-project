Fact	271  The Magistrate must then consider all the documents and after making such examination, if any, of the accused, as the Magistrate thinks necessary and after giving the prosecution and the accused' in opportunity of being heard, the Magistrate must consider whether a charge should be framed against the accused or not.

RatioOfTheDecision	Under s. 253 , Criminal Procedure Code , if, upon taking all the evidence referred to in the section just quoted and making such examination, if any, of the accused as the Magistrate thinks necessary, he finds that no case against the accused has been made out which, if unrebutted, would warrant his conviction, the Magistrate can discharge him.
