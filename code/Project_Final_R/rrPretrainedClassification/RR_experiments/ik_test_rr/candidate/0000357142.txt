Fact	REPORTABLE IN THE SUPREME COURT OF INDIA CRIMINAL APPELLATE JURISDICTION CRIMINAL APPEAL NO.
Fact	580 OF 2001 State of A.P.
Fact	...Appellant Versus <NAME> ...Respondent J U D G M E N T  Dr. <NAME>, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	Challenge in this appeal is to the judgment of a learned Single Judge of <ORG> directing acquittal of the respondent who faced trial for alleged commission of offence punishable under Section 13(2) read with Section 13(1)(d)(i) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 (in short the `Act').
Fact	The trial Court i.e.
Fact	the Court of Principal Special Judge for SPE & ACB Cases, Hyderabad, found the accused guilty.
Fact	He was convicted and sentenced to two years rigorous imprisonment under each count.
Fact	Both the sentences were directed to run concurrently.
Fact	He was also fined with Rs.1000/- under each count with default stipulation.
Fact	2.
Fact	Background facts in a nutshell are as follows:  One Gande Vaikuntam (PW-1) was sanctioned a loan of Rs.15,000/- in the year 1986 by <ORG> llage Industries Board, Sangareddy.
Fact	But due to some unavoidable circumstances he could not avail the facility.
Fact	After about three years between April and May 1989 he approached the respondent-accused officer who was the Development Officer of <ORG> and requested him for revival of the lapsed loan.
Fact	According to the defacto complainant, respondent dodged him on 25th May, 1989 when he went to his office at Sangareddy and asked him to see him in <ORG> of <ORG> at Hyderabad on 27th May, 1989.
Fact	Accordingly, the complainant and one <NAME> (PW-2) met the respondent at <ORG> in Hyderabad on 27.5.1989.
Fact	The respondent demanded bribe of Rs.500/- from him so that his loan case could be revived.
Fact	He also told PW-1 to meet him on 31st May, 1989 at 8.00 a.m. at his residence.
Fact	PW-1 approached the Dy.
Fact	Superintendent of Police, <NAME> and gave a report.
Fact	On 31.5.1989 at about 7.25 a.m. PW-1 met the respondent who demanded and accepted th e sum of Rs.500/- from him in presence of <NAME> and <NAME>.
Fact	The respondent was caught red handed and bribe money was recovered in presence of mediators from the respondent.
Fact	To prove the accusations the prosecution examined eight witnesses and 31 exhibits were exhibited.
Fact	Accused examined one witness and exhibited 2 exhibits.
Fact	<ORG> found the evidence of PWs 1 and 2 to be clear and cogent and accordingly recorded the conviction and sentence as afore- stated.
Fact	In appeal before <ORG> it was submitted that the accusations have not been established so far as the respondent is concerned.
Fact	<ORG> held that according to the evidence of prosecution the application given by PW-1 was found in the brief case of the accused person.
Fact	But the High court observed that normally one would expect that if the accused officer kept the application in the brief case he would also keep the money in the brief case and it is not expected that he would keep the money on a tea-pot and the  application in the brief case.
Fact	This according to <ORG> created doubt whether any money was demanded and received by the accused person.
Fact	It was further observed that the evidence of PW-1 was not corroborated.
Fact	Although there was an independent witness available, he was not examined.
Fact	<ORG> also observed that non examination of one <NAME> rendered the prosecution version fragile.
Fact	Accordingly, the conviction was set aside and acquittal was directed.
Fact	Questioning the acquittal, the State of Andhra Pradesh has filed this appeal.
Fact	3.
Fact	Learned counsel for the appellant-State submitted that <ORG> by a cryptic order has set aside the well reasoned judgment of the trial Court.
Fact	Merely because some persons were not examined, same cannot be a ground to discard the evidence of a reliable witness.
Fact	It is pointed out that the bribe money purported to have been given by <NAME> was also seized.
Fact	The Investigating Officer had clearly stated the reasons for the non examination of <NAME>  <NAME>.
Argument	It was stated that he had joined naxalites.
Argument	The presumption available under Section 20 of the Act was not kept in view by <ORG>.
Argument	It is submitted that <ORG>'s conclusions are based on surmises and, therefore, the judgment of acquittal cannot be maintained.
Argument	4.
Argument	Learned counsel for the respondent on the other hand submitted that <ORG> has analysed the evidence in great detail to find that the prosecution version is not believable.
Argument	That being so, there was no scope for any interference in this appeal.
Argument	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is to be noted that the evidence of PW-1 has not been discarded by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	But it is observed by <ORG> that there was no corroboration to the evidence of PW-1 and therefore it recorded the order of acquittal.
RatioOfTheDecision	The evidence of PW-1 does not suffer from any infirmity.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mere non- examination of any other person would not render his evidence suspect.
RatioOfTheDecision	The IO has categorically stated that Narsimha <NAME> was not available to be examined as a witness.
RatioOfTheDecision	Further, there w as no suggestion given by the accused that money was forced on his hands and thereafter he put it on the table.
RatioOfTheDecision	No such suggestion was given and for the first time during examination under Section 313 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (in short the `Code') such a stand was taken.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> has also not considered the effect of the presumption flowing from Section 20 of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not understood as to the basis on which <ORG> found that accused would not put the application form and the money in different places.
RatioOfTheDecision	The conclusion has no basis.
RatioOfTheDecision	The accused did not dispute that the application form (Ext.P5) was found in a brief case.
RatioOfTheDecision	In fact the bribed money from <NAME> was also seized.
RatioOfTheDecision	It has been clearly indicated by the witness that the money given by PW-1 and money given to <NAME> were kept side by side and were not mixed up.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case, the trial Court had elaborately dealt with the evidence to record conviction.
RatioOfTheDecision	The High court has not indicated any reason as t o how the conclusions of the trial Court are wrong.
RatioOfTheDecision	In any event, <ORG> by a cryptic conclusion held that the evidence led was not sufficient.
Fact	As noted above, reasons for <NAME> non examination has been disclosed by the prosecution.
RulingByLowerCourt	6.
RulingByLowerCourt	Above being the position, the judgment of <ORG> is clearly unsustainable and set aside.
RulingByLowerCourt	Considering the background facts, one year custodial sentence with fine of Rs.1,000/- as was imposed by the trial Court are imposed.
RulingByLowerCourt	The respondent shall surrender to custody forthwith to serve the remainder of sentence.
RulingByLowerCourt	7.
RulingByLowerCourt	The appeal is allowed to the aforesaid extent.
