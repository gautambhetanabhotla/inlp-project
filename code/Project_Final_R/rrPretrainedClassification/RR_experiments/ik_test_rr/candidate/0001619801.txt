RatioOfTheDecision	There was, however, nothing to prevent those whose books were submitted for selection from pointing out this fact to <ORG> rnment so that it could amend its Resolution by appointing a substitute or substitutes, as the case may be.
RatioOfTheDecision	There was equally nothing to prevent such non-official author-members from resigning from the committee on the ground of their interest in the matter.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as the fifth and last ground on which <ORG> rested its decision is concerned, it must be held that <ORG> was justified in refusing to grant relief on this ground in respect of the books selected for the years 1980, 1981 and 1982.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are also faced with a similar fait accompli as <ORG> was, not only in respect of the books selected and purchased for those years but also with respect to the books selected and to be purchased from the <ORG> grant because these books have also by now been purchased and distributed among the various school and college libraries.
RatioOfTheDecision	All that we can, therefore, do in this Appeal is to lay down guide-lines which should be followed in the future in selecting not only books for libraries  in educational institutions but also in prescribing text-books and in constituting committees for these purposes.
Fact	It was, however, submitted on behalf of the Respondents that it was not necessary for this <ORG> to lay down any guide-lines inasmuch as after the Special Leave to Appeal was granted in this case, <ORG> had issued a fresh Government Resolution dated November 24, 1983, whereby it constituted a new <ORG> consisting of Government officials and non- official members, clause (8) of which Resolution satisfied the principles of fair play and natural justice by eliminating the possibility of any author-member of the committee influencing author-members in selecting his book.
Fact	Clause (8) of the said Resolution reads as follows :  "No member of <ORG> shall remain present in discussion while considering a book in which he/she is interested as author/editor/publisher."
Fact	In the alternative, it was submitted that if the <ORG> desi res to lay down guide-lines, it should do so by adopting clause (8) of the said Government Resolution dated November 24, 1983.
Fact	We are unable to accept either of the above two submissions.
Fact	Several books would come up for consideration before the <ORG>ee, one or more of them by one of the members and the other or others by some of the other members.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mere non-participation in the discussion by the member concerned or even his withdrawing from the deliberations of the <ORG>ee while his or her book or books are being considered is not sufficient because the evil of quid pro quo cannot be eliminated by this.
RatioOfTheDecision	Members deliberating would bear in mind that the turn for selecting their books would also come and the concerned member who had not participated or had withdrawn would not then be favourably inclined to select their books.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was suggested on behalf of the Appellants that in laying down the guide-lines we should provide that if a publisher has published a book written by one of the members of the <ORG> ee or sub-committee concerned with the selection of books, such publisher should not be permitted to submit any book for selection even though no book by that author-member had been submitted for selection.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was urged that in such a case the author-member would be favourably inclined to select or approve the book of that publisher in order to maintain good relations with him.
RatioOfTheDecision	We find this suggestion to be unrealistic.
Fact	Many a person falls a victim to that disease which <NAME> called cacoethes scribendi(the writer's itch), for as <NAME> said in his 'English Bards and Scottish Reviewers' :  "'Tis pleasant, sure, to see one's name in print; A book's a book, although there's nothing in't."
RatioOfTheDecision	It would, therefore, be unfair to prohibit publishers from submitting books for selection merely because they had at one time published a book written by one of the members of the committee or sub-committee concerned with the selection of books The number of publishers is large but good publishers are few and such publishers  will, therefore, be publishing the majority of books.
RatioOfTheDecision	To lay down such a guide-line would be to eliminate a large number of books which may be worthy of selection.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the light of the above discussion we lay down the following guide-lines to be adopted by <ORG>, governmental authorities and all committees constituted for the selection to textbooks as also books for libraries of educational institutions whether such committee be called a committee or sub-committee or be described by some other nomenclature :  (1) The committee should not consist merely of Government officials or have a preponderance of Government officials on it, for Government officials, with few exceptions, have by and large only administrative experience.
Fact	In addition to Government officials, therefore, the committee should also consist of men eminent in the particular fields of knowledge for which the books are to be selected.
Fact	Non-official members should not be appointed as a matter of political considerations or on party li nes but should be appointed only on merit.
Statute	(2) No member of the committee, a book written or edited by whom is submitted either by himself or his publisher for approval or selection; should continue to remain a member of the committee.
Statute	If he is a non-official member, he should submit his resignation from the committee on this ground.
Statute	If he is a <ORG> official, he should intimate to the <ORG> or the authority appointing him on that committee the fact that a book written or edited by him has been submitted for approval or selection and the <ORG> or the concerned authority should substitute in place of such member another person, whether official or non- official, none of whose books has been submitted for approval or selection.
Statute	(3) No publisher of books or his representative should be appointed a member of the committee or be allowed to remain present at or participate in the deliberations of the committee.
RatioOfTheDecision	The guide-lines we have laid down above are not intended to be exhaustive but contain t he bare essentials of what is required.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are conscious that no guide-lines laid down by a court can ensure the selection of really worthwhile books.
RatioOfTheDecision	This must necessarily depend upon the social consciousness and moral fibre of the members of the committee.
RatioOfTheDecision	Similarly, no judgment of a court can eliminate the evil of behind-the-scene influence.
RatioOfTheDecision	Here, one must perforce trust the sense of responsibility of the members of the committee in the discharge of the important duty with which they are entrusted.
RulingByPresentCourt	For the reasons mentioned above, we allow this Appeal and direct the State of Orissa to amend suitably the Government Resolution dated November 24, 1983, or to issue a fresh notification in supersession of that notification, incorporating the guide-lines laid down by us above, as expeditiously as possible and in any event before the next selection of books is made, without affecting any selection already made.
RulingByPresentCourt	In the particular facts and circumstances of this case there will be no order as to the costs of this  Appeal.
