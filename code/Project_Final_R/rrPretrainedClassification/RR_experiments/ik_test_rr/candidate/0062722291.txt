RulingByLowerCourt	The application is liable to be dismissed on that ground alone.
Fact	The Bowbazar property has admittedly been under <ORG> for about two decades.
Fact	The partition suit is still pending, as also the application taking exception to the report of the Special Referee.
Argument	As rightly argued by Mr. <NAME>, the petitioner not being a party to the suit, it was necessary for the petitioner to get himself examined pro interesse suo, in or der to obtain discharge of the Joint <ORG>.
Argument	This Court is not inclined to discharge the Joint <ORG>, on the application of a stranger to the suit.
Fact	The Bowbazar property has been under <ORG> for several years.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is immaterial that the application for discharge of the Joint Receiver is being opposed by only one heir of a deceased defendant.
RatioOfTheDecision	The suit being for partition of joint properties, it is immaterial that the heirs of <NAME>, since deceased, have not been brought on record.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even one interested heir might oppose an order that might be against the interest of the estate.
RatioOfTheDecision	As observed above, the partition suit has not finally been decreed.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question of whether the Bowbazar property constitutes part of the joint properties of the parties to the partition suit or is the self acquired property of Late <NAME> has not finally been adjudicated.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mere allowing of an amendment of the plaint does not tantamount to a judicial verdict and/or pronouncement on the status o f the properties sought to be incorporated in the plaint by amendment, or of title thereto.
RulingByPresentCourt	If this Court accepts the contention of the Bowbazar property being the self-acquired property of Late <NAME>, the transfer deeds executed by the persons claiming joint ownership thereof would be rendered unenforceable, ineffective, and liable to cancellation.
RulingByPresentCourt	Discharge of the Joint Receivers, at this stage would in any event, render the application taking exception to the report of the Special Referee infructuous.
RulingByPresentCourt	If the application taking exception to the report of the Special Referee has not been disposed of for 10 years, the applicant is not to suffer.
RulingByPresentCourt	In my view, that the prayer for discharge of Joint Receivers cannot be entertained until the application taking exception to the report of the Special Referee is disposed of.
RulingByPresentCourt	This application is, thus dismissed.
