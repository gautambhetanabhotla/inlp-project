Fact	JUDGMENT <NAME> and <NAME>, JJ.
Fact	1.
Fact	In this case the landlord petitioners made an application under Section 27 of the Chota Nagpur Tenancy Act (Bengal Act VI of 1908) for the enhancement of the rent paid by the tenants opposite-parties in respect of their holding.
Fact	2.
Fact	The application was heard by a Deputy Collector empowered to discharge the functions of a Deputy Commissioner under Section 27 and the following sections of the Act.
Fact	After a prolonged enquiry the Deputy Collector on the 12th February, 1912, made an order enhancing the rent of the tenants from Rs.
Fact	2-8 to Rs.
Fact	32-10-5 per annum.
Fact	Against this order the tenants preferred an appeal under the provisions of Section 215 (1) (iv) to the Deputy Commissioner who, after commenting on the proceedings of the Deputy Collector in terms which should not find a place in the judgment, of any <ORG>, dismissed the landlords application on the ground that in his opinion the questions at issue should be decided only "after a fall and fair trial" or "by the Settlement D epartment in the village."
Argument	3.
Argument	It is not suggested that, in the area with which we are here concerned any order for the preparation of a record-of-rights had been issued, and obviously if in the opinion of the Deputy Commissioner the case had not been fully and fairly tried his proper course was to make or direct such further inquiry as might be necessary.
Argument	4.
Argument	It therefore cannot, be and has not been disputed before us that in omitting to deal with the appeal before him on the merits the Deputy Commissioner has failed to exercise a jurisdiction vested in him by law.
Argument	But on behalf of the tenants opposite-parties it has been contended that <ORG> when dealing with applications for the enhancement of rent under the provisions of Sections 27 to 30 of the Chota Nagpur Tenancy Act are not Courts subject to the appellate jurisdiction of this Court within the meaning of Section 15 of the Indian High Courts Act 1861, that superintendence over Deputy Commissioners in the performance of their  duties under the Act is by its express provisions vested in the Commissioner and <ORG> and that, therefore, this <ORG>, has no jurisdiction, or at least should not interfere.
Argument	5.
Argument	These contentions are based on the provisions of Section 215 (2) (which in terms relates to suits only), Section 217 and Section 270 of the Act.
Argument	6.
RatioOfTheDecision	From the very nature of the proceedings themselves, and also from the provisions of the Act as contained, for instance, in Chapter XVI it is clear that proceedings on applications for enhancement of rent are judicial proceedings, and in view of the express provisions of Section 224 (2) which allows in certain cases a second appeal to this <ORG>, it cannot in our opinion be contended that Deputy Commissioners in the performance of their judicial duties under the Chota Nagpur Tenancy Act are not <ORG>s subject to the appellate jurisdiction of this <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
RatioOfTheDecision	No doubt, by the provisions of the sections we have already cited, powers of revision direction and control are vested  in the Commissioner and <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	But these sections merely reproduce in practically identical terms the provisions of Sections 151 and 152 of Act X of 1859, and notwithstanding the existence of these provisions in that Act, this Court in a long series of decisions we need here refer only to the case of <CITATION_1871184> (1911) I.L.R.
RatioOfTheDecision	38 Calc 832 has held that it has jurisdiction and has interfered in cases where <ORG> have either exceeded the jurisdiction or failed or refused to exercise the jurisdiction vested in them by the said Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RulingByPresentCourt	We are therefore of opinion that in the present case we have jurisdiction and should interfere.
RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RulingByPresentCourt	We accordingly make this Rule absolute, pet aside the order of the Deputy Commissioner dated the 7th June, 1912, and direct him to proceed with and determine the appeal before him on the merits.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
RulingByPresentCourt	We make no order as to costs.
