Statute	Section 66 (1) requires the applic ation to be made within 60 days of the date on which the applicant is served with notice of an order under sub- section (4) of section 33 .
Argument	It was held that the granting of an application for rectification under section 35 and correcting the error in the order was not an order under section 33 (4) and, therefore, was not one in respect of which section 66 (1) permitted a case to be stated.
Argument	It was further held that if <ORG> improperly or incorrectly made a reference in violation of the provisions of the statute, <ORG> was capable of entertaining an objection to the statement of the case and that, if it camp to the conclusion that the case should not have been stated, <ORG> was not compelled to express an opinion upon the question referred.
Argument	in the case before us there is no question that the present application was not made within time, but the contention is that section 66 (1) only contemplates an application for a reference of a question of law arising out of " such order" w hich clearly means an order made under section 33 (4), _and, therefore, if there is no valid order under that section no question of law can be said to arise out of "such order" and consequently <ORG> can have on jurisdiction to make any reference to <ORG> under section 66(1) .
Statute	Section 66 (2) provides that if on any application being made under sub-section (1) <ORG> refuses to state the case on the ground that no question of law arises, the assessee or the Commissioner may, within the time specified therein, apply to <ORG> and <ORG> may, if it is not satisfied of the correctness of the decision of <ORG>, require <ORG> to state the case and to refer it.
RatioOfTheDecision	The jurisdiction given to <ORG> under this sub-section is conditional on an application under sub-section (1) being refused by the- Appellate Tribunal.
RatioOfTheDecision	This clearly presupposes that the application under sub-section (1) was otherwise a valid application.
RatioOfTheDecision	If,  therefore, an application under sub- section (1) was not well founded in that there was no order which could properly be said to be an order under sub- section (4) of section 33 then the refusal of <ORG> to state a case on such misconceived application on the ground that no question of law arises will not authorise <ORG>, on an application under sub-section (2) of section 66 , to direct the <ORG> to state a case.
RatioOfTheDecision	The jurisdiction of the <ORG> and of <ORG> is conditional on there being an order by <ORG> which may be said to be one under section 33 (4) and a question of law arising out of such an order.
RatioOfTheDecision	The only question for our consideration, therefore, is whether in this case any question of law arose out of an order which can properly be said to have been made by <ORG> under sub-section (4) of section 33 , for if it did not, then <ORG> -would have no jurisdiction under sub-section (1) of section 66 to refer a case, no r would <ORG> have jurisdiction under sub-section (2) of that section to direct the <ORG> to do so.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was at one stage suggested by the learned AttorneyGeneral that we should in the first instance remit the matter - to <ORG> for their decision on this question but as the question is one of law depending on the construction of the relevant sections of the Act it will save time if it is decided by us here and now.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not disputed that we have the power, on the hearing of this appeal, to decide this question.
Argument	It will be recalled that when on 19th November, 1945, the Appellate Assistant Commissioner declined to admit the appeal, the assessee did not prefer any appeal but only made a miscellaneous application before the Appellate <ORG>.
Argument	There is no provision in the Act permitting such an application.
Argument	Indeed, in the statement of the case the Appellate <ORG> states that in entertaining that application and correcting theerror of the Income-tax Officer it acted in exercise ofwhat it r egarded as its inherent powers.
Argument	There being no appeal under section 33 (1) and the order having been made in exercise of its supposed inherent jurisdiction., the order cannot possibly be regarded as one under section 33 (4) and there being no order under section 33 (4) there could be no reference under section 66 (1) or (2) and the appellate Court properly refused to entertain it.
Argument	The learned Attorney-General submits that this Court should not take such a narrow and technical view but should treat that miscellaneous application as really an appeal under section 33 .
Statute	Turning now to section 33 we find that any assessee objecting to an order passed by an Appellate Assistant Commissioner under section 28 or section 31 may appeal to <ORG> within the time specified in sub-section (1) which time, however, may be extended by the <ORG> under sub-section (2A).
Statute	Under sub-section (4) <ORG> is given power, after giving both parties to.
Statute	the appeal an opportunity to be heard, to pass su ch order thereon as it thinks fit.
Statute	It is thus clear that <ORG> can make an order under section 33 (4) only on an appeal from an order passed by the Appellate Assistant Commissioner under section 28 or section 31 .
Statute	If, therefore, there is no order which may properly be said to have been made by the Appellate Assistant Commissioner under section 28 or section 31 then there can be no appeal under section 33 (1) and consequently there can be no order under section 33 (4).
Statute	Section 28 is not relevant for our present purpose.
Statute	Section 30 provides for filing of appeals against assessments made under the Act.
Statute	Sub-section (1) of that section prescribes the different decisions against which an appeal will lie.
Statute	Sub-section (2) prescribes the time within which the appeal is to be filed.
Statute	Subsection (3) prescribes the form in which the appeal is to be made.. Then comes section 31 which gives power to the Appellate Assistant Commissioner to hear and dispose of such appeal.
Statute	Sub- section (3) of section 31 empow ers the Appellate Assistant Commissioner in disposing of an appeal under section 30 to make one or other order under one or other of the several clauses of that' sub-section.
Argument	It is, therefore, clear that in order that the Appellate Assistant Commissioner may exercise his jurisdiction and make an order under section 3  1 , there must be an appeal as contemplated by section 30 .
Argument	The learned AttorneyGeneral only relies on the opening part of sub-section (1) of section 30 and contends that the appeal before the Appellate Assistant Commissioner was with respect to the amount of income assessed under section 23 or section 27 .
Argument	It will be recalled that <ORG> held that the two sums claimed by the assessee would be allowed to him and concluded by saying that the appeal was partly allowed.
Argument	- The power of <ORG> under section 33(4)  474 is indeed wide, for on an appeal properly before it, it can make such order as it thinks fit.
Argument	Therefore, it be order made by <ORG> in th is case on August 20, 1943, must be read and construed as a direction to the Income-tax Officer to carry out the directions by allowing the two deductions in question.
Fact	When the matter again came before the Income-tax Officer his function was only to carry out the order of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	He could not otherwise reopen the assessment already made by him under section 23 .
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, in carrying out the directions of the <ORG> and in doing what be aid on September 26, 1945, the Income-tax Officer cannot be regarded as having acted under section 23 or section 27 of the Act and that being the position no appeal lay from that order of the Income-tax Officer under section 30 (1) of the Act, I The result of it was that there was no proper appeal before the Appellate Assistant Commissioner such as is contemplated by section 30 (1) and, therefore, the order made by the Appellate Assistant Commissioner cannot be regarded as an order made by him under section 31 (3), for an order under section 31 (3) can only  be made in disposing of an appeal properly' filed under section 30 , and consequently no further appeal lay to <ORG> under section, 33 (1) so as to enable <ORG> to make an order under sub- section (4) of that section.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the premises, there being no order which may properly be said to have been made under section 33 (4), no question of law can be said to arise out of an order made under section 33 (4) and consequently there can be no valid reference under section 66 , subsection (1) or sub-section (2).
RatioOfTheDecision	If, therefore, the reference was incompetent for want of jurisdiction both under section 66 (1) or section 66 (2) surely <ORG> could decline to entertain it as it did.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even if the order dated September 26, 1945, made by the Income-tax Officer after the matter came back to him to give effect to the decisions of <ORG> be regarded as an order made by him under  section 23 or section 27 and as such appealable under section 30 (1) then the order made by th e Appellate Assistant Commissioner on November 19, 1946, declining to admit the appeal clearly amounted to a refusal on his part to exercise the jurisdiction vested in him by law.
RatioOfTheDecision	An order thus founded on an error as to his jurisdiction way conceivably be corrected by appropriate proceedings but it cannot certainly be regarded as such an order as is contem- plated by any of the sub-sections of section 31 .
RatioOfTheDecision	Such an order not coming within the purview of section 28 or section 31 , no, appeal lay therefrom to <ORG> under section 33 (1) and if no such appeal properly came before <ORG> it could not properly make an order under section 33 (4) and if there was no order under section 33 (4) there could be no reference under section 66 , sub- section (1) or sub-section (2).
RatioOfTheDecision	It follows, therefore, that the order of <ORG> correcting the order of the Income-tax Officer directing that the sum of Rs.
RatioOfTheDecision	13,541 should not be included in the assessment cannot be regarded as an  order passed by <ORG> under section 33 (4) so as to attract the operation of section 66 .
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned Attorney-General urged that having under section 66 (2) of the Act directed <ORG> to state a case <ORG> could not afterwards refuse to answer the question thus referred to it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Whether <ORG> was so precluded or not requires no decision on this occa- sion, for even conceding but ;not deciding that <ORG> was so precluded, this Court, at any rate, can surely entertain the question of the competency of the reference.
RulingByPresentCourt	The result, therefore, is that we dismiss these appeals with costs.
RulingByPresentCourt	Appeals dismissed.
