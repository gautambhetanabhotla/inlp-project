RatioOfTheDecision	A reading of the above clauses of the scheme shows that according to the scheme, the entire undertaking of <ORG> shall be transferred to <ORG> with effect from the transferred date and that <ORG> shall be amalgamated with <ORG> with effect from the said date.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause (6) states clearly that the implementation of the said scheme "is conditional upon the scheme being sanctioned under Section 391 of the Act and the appropriate orders for the implementation of this scheme being made under Section 394 of the Act by <ORG> Tamil Nadu and Calcutta".
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause (8) further provides that the implementation of the said scheme "is conditional also upon shareholders holding not less than nine-tenths in value of the shares in the Subsidiary company becoming shareholders of <ORG> by virtue of the amalgamation".
RatioOfTheDecision	It is one the basis of the language  of clauses (7) and (8) that <ORG> has opined that the scheme takes effect only on and from the date it was sanctioned by <ORG>s of Madras and Calcutta coupled with the date on which the shareholders of <ORG> becomes the shareholders of <ORG> as provided in the sub-clauses.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> has opined that the transfer date mentioned in the scheme viz., January 1, 1982 is "totally artificial and arbitrary" [for the reason that on the said date neither the company nor their shareholders had even thought of amalgamation] and that it has no legal significance.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to <ORG>, therefore, the date on which the amalgamation should be deemed to have come into being is not January 1, 1982 but January 20, 1984/February 24, 1984, on which dates the Madras and Calcutta <ORG> respectively approved the scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words, <ORG> has taken the view that in the absence of any date being specified in the order of <ORG> as the date of amalga mation, the date of the order of <ORG> [Company <ORG>s] shall be taken as the date of amalgamation.
RatioOfTheDecision	For arriving at the said view, <ORG> followed an earlier <ORG> decision of that <ORG> in <CITATION_469965> [(1950) 20 Company Cases 214].
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> also opined that the decision of <ORG> in <ORG> is of no assistance to the appellant.
RatioOfTheDecision	On this basis, <ORG> has upheld the validity of the notices issued by the Income Tax Officer, which notices were impugned in the writ petition, and dismissed the writ petition.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question is whether the view taken by <ORG> is correct.
RatioOfTheDecision	Every scheme of amalgamation has to necessarily provide a date with effect from which the amalgamation/transfer shall take place.
RatioOfTheDecision	The scheme concerned herein does so provide viz., January 1, 1982.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that while sanctioning the scheme, it is open to the <ORG> to modify the said date and prescribe such date of amalgamation/transfer as it thinks appropriate in  the facts and circumstances of the case.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the <ORG> so specifies a date, there is little doubt that such date would be the date of amalgamation/date of transfer.
RatioOfTheDecision	But where the <ORG> does not prescribe any specific date but merely sanctions the scheme presented to it - as has happened in this case - it should follow that the date of amalgamation/date of transfer is the date specified in the scheme as "the transfer date".
RatioOfTheDecision	It cannot be otherwise.
RatioOfTheDecision	It must be remembered that before applying to the <ORG> under Section 391(1) , a scheme has to be framed and such scheme has to contain a date of amalgamation/transfer.
RatioOfTheDecision	The proceedings before the court may take some time; indeed, they are bound to take some time because several steps provided by Sections 391 to 394-A and the relevant Rules have to be followed and complied with.
RatioOfTheDecision	During the period the proceedings are pending before the <ORG>, both the amalgamating units, i.e., <ORG> and <ORG> may carry on business, as has happened in t his case but normally provision is made for this aspect also in the scheme of amalgamation.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the scheme before us, clause 6(b) does expressly provide that with affect from the transfer date, <ORG> (Subsidiary Company) shall be deemed to have carried on the business for and on behalf of <ORG> (Holding Company) with all attendant consequences.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is equally relevant to notice that the <ORG> have not only sanctioned the scheme in this case but have also not specified any other date as the date of transfer amalgamation.
RatioOfTheDecision	In such a situation, it would not be reasonable to say that the scheme of amalgamation takes effect on and from the date of the order sanctioning the scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are, therefore, of the opinion that the notices issued by <ORG> (impugned in the writ petition) were not warranted in law.
RatioOfTheDecision	The business carried on by <ORG> (Subsidiary Company) should be deemed to have been carried on for and on behalf of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is  the necessary and the logical consequence of the court sanctioning the scheme of amalgamation as presented to it.
RatioOfTheDecision	The order of the <ORG> sanctioning the scheme, the filing of the certified copies of the orders of the court before the Registrar of Companies, the allotment or shares etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	may have all taken place subsequent to the date of amalgamation/transfer, yet the date of amalgamation in the circumstances of this case would be January 1, 1982.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is also the ratio of the decision of <ORG> in <CITATION_52599> .
RatioOfTheDecision	[A.I.R.1919 P.C.9].
Argument	Counsel for the <ORG> contended that if the aforesaid view is adopted when several complications will ensue in case the <ORG> refuses to sanction the scheme of amalgamation.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not see any basis for this apprehension.
RatioOfTheDecision	Firstly, an assessment can always be made and is supposed to be made on <ORG> taking into account the income of both the Transferor and Transferee Company.
RatioOfTheDecision	Secondly, and probably the more advisable course from the point of view of  the <ORG> would be to make one assessment on <ORG> taking into account the income of both of Transferor or Transferee Companies and also to make separate protective assessments on both the Transferor and Transferee Companies separately.
RatioOfTheDecision	There may be a certain practical difficulty in adopting this course inasmuch as separate balance-sheets may not be available for the Transferor and Transferee Companies.
RatioOfTheDecision	But that may not be an insuperable problem inasmuch as assessment can always be made, on the available material, even without a balance- sheet.
RatioOfTheDecision	In certain cases, best-judgment assessment may also be resorted to.
RatioOfTheDecision	Be that as it may, we need not purpose this line of enquiry because it does not arise for consideration in these cases directly.
