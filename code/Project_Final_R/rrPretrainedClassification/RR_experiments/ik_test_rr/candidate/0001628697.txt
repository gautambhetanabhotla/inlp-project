RulingByLowerCourt	It was urged that since excise duty is essentially a tax on production and manufacture of excisable goods, no duty could be levied in respect of goods manufactured and produced during the period when excise duty was not leviable.
Fact	According to the petitioners molasses produced prior to the date when Item No.
RatioOfTheDecision	15CC was introduced in the Schedule to the Central Excises and Salt Act was exempt from excise duty under the notification dated 30th April, 1975.
RatioOfTheDecision	The demands made by the respondents are illegal.
RatioOfTheDecision	We find no merit in the contention.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that an excise duty by its very nature is on the production or manufacture of goods but as pointed out by <ORG>,in <CITATION_264716> (A.I.R.
Statute	1977 S.C. 1459) the excise duty can be levied at a convenient stage so long as the character of the impost, i.e., it is a duty on the manufacture or production, is not lost.
Statute	The method of collection does not affect the essence of the duty, but only rela tes to the machinery of collection for administrative convenience.
Statute	Laws are to be found which impose a duty on excise at stages subsequent to the manufacture or production.
Statute	5.
Statute	Rule 9 of the Central Excise rules provides that no excisable goods shall be removed from any place where they are produced, cured or manufactured or any premises appurtenant thereto, which may be specified by the Collector in this behalf, whether for consumption, export, or manufactures or any other commodity in or outside such place, until the excise duty leviable thereon has been paid at such place and in such manner as is prescribed in the rules or as the Collector may require.
Statute	Rule 9-A provides that the rate of duty and tariff valuation, if any, applicable to any excisable goods shall be the rate and valuation in force (i) in the case of goods cleared from the premises of a curer on payment of duty on the date on which the duty assessed ; and (ii) in the case of goods cleared from a factory or a warehouse, subject to certain other  rules, which are not relevant for our purpose, on the date of the actual removal of such goods from such factory or warehouse.
Statute	Rule 49 also makes it clear that excise duty is chargeable on removal of the goods from the factory premises or from an approved place of storage.
Statute	It clearly provides that payment of duty shall not be required in respect of excisable goods made in a factory until they are about to be issued out of the place or premises specified under Rule 9 or are about to be removed from a store-room or other place of storage approved by the Collector under Rule 47.
RatioOfTheDecision	These rules leave no room for doubt that excise duty is leviable not with reference to the date of manufacture of the excisable goods but at the time and at the rate on the date of removal of goods from the place of manufacture or from the approved place of storage.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus the duty is linked in point of a time to the date of removal and not to the date of manufacture.
RatioOfTheDecision	The fact that in the present cases molasses was produced prior to 18th  June, 1980 would not affect the liability of the petitioners for excise duty on the stock of molasses available with the petitioners when excise duty was levied on the molasses under the Finance Act , 1980.
RatioOfTheDecision	6.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was urged that molasses manufactured by the petitioners was exempt from excise duty under the Notification dated 30th April, 1975 and since that notification has not been withdrawn, the petitioners were entitled to claim exemption from excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	The argument is untenable.
RatioOfTheDecision	By the aforesaid notification <ORG> exempted goods falling under Item No.
RatioOfTheDecision	68 of the First Schedule to the Central Excises and Salt Act in certain circumstances.
RatioOfTheDecision	At that stage molasses was not listed as a separate item in the Schedule but was treated to fall under residuary Tariff Item No.
RatioOfTheDecision	68 of the Schedule.
RulingByLowerCourt	When the Finance Act , 1980 brought molasses under a specific item (No.
RulingByLowerCourt	15-CC) it ceased to fall under the residuary Tariff Item No.
RulingByLowerCourt	68 with the result that the notification dated 30th April, 1975 ceased to  apply to molasses.
RulingByLowerCourt	No advantage can, therefore, be taken of the exemption granted under the aforesaid notification which has ceased to cover molasses now.
Argument	7.
Argument	<NAME> appearing for <ORG> contended that since molasses in question was produced at the time when it was exempt from excise duty and the Finance Act of 1980, under which molasses was introduced as a specific item in the Schedule, did not give retrospective operation, this stock could not be subjected to excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	Learned counsel, however, could not point out any provision in the Act or the <ORG> that excise duty was leviable with reference to the date of production.
RatioOfTheDecision	As pointed out earlier, although excise duty is on the production or manufacture of goods, it can be imposed at a stage subsequent to the manufacture or production as held in <CITATION_1225724> (A.I.R.
RatioOfTheDecision	1976 S.C. 182).
RatioOfTheDecision	This dictum has been approved in the case of <ORG> (supra).
