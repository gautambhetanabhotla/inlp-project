Fact	JUDGMENT  1.
Fact	An ex parte decree for rent; was obtained against the present appellants and they succeeded in having that decree set aside.
Fact	On the rehearing of the case the suit against them was dismissed with costs.
Fact	They applied to <ORG> by one application for execution of the final decree for costs and for restitution of the amount of the ex parte decree which had been satisfied by them before it was set aside.
Fact	2.
Argument	<ORG> has held that although the application was in time as regards costs, it was not in time as regards the refund, because the appellants were entitled to apply for the refund immediately on the setting aside of the ex parte decree.
Argument	The date on which the ex parte decree was set aside has not been stated; but, as <ORG> says, it must have been more than three years before the date on which the application in question was made.
Argument	The learned Judge has accordingly held, applying Article 178 of the second schedule of the Limitation Act , that  that application was barred so far as the refund was concerned.
Argument	3.
Argument	The main contention before us, and in fact, the contention on which all the other arguments which have been addressed to us depend, is that <ORG> was in error in holding that, the right to apply accrued on the setting aside of the ex parte decree, and that it should have held that the right accrued on the passing of the final decree dismissing the suit against the appellants, because, until the suit was finally dismissed, the appellants could not tell whether they might not ultimately be obliged to satisfy the decree.
RatioOfTheDecision	We think that there is nothing in this contention.
RatioOfTheDecision	The decree which had been satisfied was the ex parte decree.
RatioOfTheDecision	Since that decree was set aside, the appellants were entitled to a refund.
RatioOfTheDecision	They were in no way bound to allow the amount which they had already paid to remain in the decree-holder's hand in case the suit should eventually be decreed against them.
Argument	4.
Argument	The question has been raised whether Article 178  or Article 179 of the second schedule of the Limitation Act properly applies to an application such as that in question.
Argument	It has been pointed out that in the case of <CITATION_655064> (1886) I.L.R.
Argument	8 All.
Argument	545 where a refund was sought in execution of a decree of <ORG>, modifying the decree of <ORG>, the application was held to be an application made according to law in the proper <ORG> in the sense of Article 179 of the Limitation Act .
Argument	5.
Argument	On the other hand, the learned pleader for the respondent has referred to the case of <NAME> v. <NAME> (1886) I.L.R.
Argument	10 Mad.
Argument	66 in which the learned Judges, who disposed of the case, were inclined to think that an application for refund after a decree passed in appeal was governed by Article 178 , since it was not one for execution of a decree or order, but to enforce a benefit by way of restitution under a decree passed in appeal.
Argument	6.
RatioOfTheDecision	It does not appear that in the Allahabad case the question was considered whether Art icle 179 was the one most properly applicable to the case, and we are disposed to agree with the learned Judges of <ORG> in considering that Article 178 is that which applies.
RatioOfTheDecision	In either case the real question is as to the starting point from which limitation began to run, and, whether Article 178 or Article 179 be applied, the appellants would be out of time.
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeal is accordingly dismissed with costs.
