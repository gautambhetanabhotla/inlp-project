Fact	O R D E R The following order of the Court was delivered: W I T H WRIT PETITION (C) NOT.
Fact	171 OF 1996 Learned Attorney General submits that the perception of <ORG> is as under:  1.
Fact	It has been estimated by the <ORG> that about 1.20 lakhs cubic meters of illicitly felled seized timber, belonging to <ORG> is lying in the forests and depots for varying periods of time between 1 to 2 years and is thereby getting degraded on account of decay and rotting of the wood.
Fact	It is necessary to dispose it off at the earliest to minimise any further loss in the monetary value.
Fact	There is, in addition, considerable quantity of timber claimed by the private industry and local people.
Fact	In view of the approaching monsoon season (April 98) all such timber needs to be disposed off with urgency to save further loss in quality, as also i n value, albeit with proper checks and balances.
Fact	2.
Fact	Given the weak infrastructure in the North-Eastern region, it does not seem feasible to transport such huge quantities of timber for auction in markets outside the region in a short time.
Fact	Moreover, there would be uncertainty of the response in timber markets far away from the source of timber which has been subject to elements of degradation in varying degrees.
Fact	There is also the likelihood of local resentment, in an otherwise sensitive area, if all such material is removed from the region without processing and value addition, which could be conceived as creating an adverse effect on the region's economy.
Fact	3.
Fact	Even though the proliferation of wood-based industries has been the main cause of degradation of forests in the North-Eastern States, considering the extent of forests (64% of the geographical area) and the dependence of the local people on the forest resources in the region it is neither feasible, nor desirable, to ban completely either the timer trad e or running of the wood based industries.
Fact	However, their numbers and capacities need to be regulated qua the sustainable availability of forest produce and they are also required to be relocated in specified industrial zones.
Fact	Moreover, the industrial requirements have to be subordinated to the maintenance of environment and ecology as well as bonafide local needs.
Fact	4.
Fact	There shall be no fresh fellings in the forests belonging to the <ORG>, <ORG> <ORG> till the disposal of their existing stocks of legal and illegal timber.
Fact	5.
Fact	In view of the multidimensional issues impinging upon forest protection, foolproof institutional arrangements need to be put in place, and made functional under the strict supervision of <ORG> (<ORG>).
Fact	Technical backstopping in the forestry matter will be provided by <ORG> by opening a separate cell in the Ministry under an officer of the rank of <ORG> and starting a satellite office of <ORG> at Shillong.
