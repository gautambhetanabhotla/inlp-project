RatioOfTheDecision	4.
RatioOfTheDecision	The observations in each individual judgment have to be read in the light of facts found in the case, and under the circumstances it does not appear to me that any of the three cases relied upon by the plaintiff sustains the contention that after the mortgage debt has become due the mortgagor is entitled to file a suit to restrain the mortgagee from exercising the power of sale without offering to redeem and tendering the amount.

RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	The contract between the parties is found in the indenture of mortgage.
RatioOfTheDecision	By that document the plaintiff had stipulated that he would pay the mortgage amount on July 18, 1940, to the mortgagees.
RatioOfTheDecision	The present demand on the part of the plaintiff is to enforce his right under that contract although he has committe d a breach of one of the most important terms of the same.
Argument	The argument of the defendants is that as the plaintiff has committed a breach of the contract he has no right to come to <ORG> and seek to enforce it against the defendants.
Argument	It is argued that relief by way of injunction is granted on the same principles on which the <ORG> will order specific performance, and in this case if the plaintiff had asked for specific performance of this contract the <ORG> would have refused it because the plaintiff himself had committed a breach thereof.
Argument	The <ORG> should similarly refuse the relief by way of injunction.
RatioOfTheDecision	In my opinion there is considerable force in this contention.
RatioOfTheDecision	The relief by way of injunction is always discretionary and the <ORG> will be slow to grant such a relief to a party who admits that he has committed a breach of the contract and at the same time wants to prevent the opposite party from enforcing his rights under the contract.
RatioOfTheDecision	Moreover the relief by way of injunction should in this case be very we ll and properly accompanied by a prayer for redemption.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the plaintiff chooses to refrain from carrying out his obligations and prays only for an injunction, it is difficult for the Court to exercise a discretion in his favour.
RatioOfTheDecision	To permit the plaintiff to do so would mean that the plaintiff can persist in his breach and prevent the defendants from enforcing their rights under the contract.
RatioOfTheDecision	Moreover in this case the express terms of the contract contained in the mortgage deed show that if the power of sale was exercised improperly or irregularly or that the same was unnecessary, the remedy of the plaintiff in respect of any breach of the proviso was to be in damages only.
RatioOfTheDecision	That is the usual clause in a mortgage deed and is inserted for the benefit of the mortgagor as well as the mortgagees.
RatioOfTheDecision	Without such a clause if an objection is taken to the sale at the time of the auction, the purchaser may be scared away and may not offer a proper price.
RatioOfTheDecision	In order, therefore, that the purchaser may give a proper value this  clause is found in mortgage deeds.
RatioOfTheDecision	By that clause it is clear that the parties had stipulated that in the event of an improper, irregular or unnecessary exercise of the power of sale the remedy of the aggrieved party was to be only in damages.
RatioOfTheDecision	Having regard to this clause the <ORG> will be most reluctant to give an injunction to the mortgagor.
RatioOfTheDecision	In my opinion, the suit as framed is not maintainable and the mortgagor should pray for redemption before asking the <ORG> to intervene and grant an injunction restraining the defendants from exercising their power of sale.
RatioOfTheDecision	This leaves the question whether the power of sale is wholly illegal.

RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RatioOfTheDecision	The argument of the plaintiff based on the construction of the words used in this case is also unsound.
RatioOfTheDecision	A power of sale must include all steps which are necessary to be taken in that connection.
RatioOfTheDecision	In order to make the power bad words must be pointed out in the section which render the whole power invalid according to law.
RatioOfTheDecision	It should be remembered that as between adults the law permits the greatest freedom of contract unless it is expressly taken away.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore if the plaintiff contends that this particular clause restricts in any way the power of the parties to enter into a contract, the burden is on him to show that the words of the section prevent an agreement between the parties as embodied in the document.
RatioOfTheDecision	I am unable to find any words in Section 69 which prevent the parties from entering into the contract found in this mortgage deed.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was argued that under Section 69 a mortgagee had no power to postpone the sale.
RatioOfTheDecision	I am unable to accept that contention.
RatioOfTheDecision	A power to sell must necess arily include a power to postpone the sale.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is a power to sell either now or at a later date.
RatioOfTheDecision	I do not think that expression is against the words used in Section 69 .
RatioOfTheDecision	It was contended that the power to buy in, or rescind or vary any contract for sale was bad and against the words of Section 69 .
RatioOfTheDecision	This argument is based on a misapprehension.
RatioOfTheDecision	The words used are not "buy out" but "buy in."
RatioOfTheDecision	No mortgagee has a right to buy the mortgage premises without an express authority from the Court.
RatioOfTheDecision	If he attempted to buy directly or indirectly, on proof that the transaction was of that nature, the <ORG> have always held that the mortgage subsisted and the transaction of sale was a nullity.
RatioOfTheDecision	I do not think the expression "buy in" has ever been intended to give the mortgagee that right.
RatioOfTheDecision	It should be realised that if at an auction sale there are several intending purchasers and the highest bid is very inadequate, a way has to be found by which the property is not sold to the highest bidder and cause a tremendous loss both to  the mortgagor and the mortgagee.
RatioOfTheDecision	The expression "buy in" is therefore used so that in effect the party at whose instance the auction is held takes the property out of the auction.
RatioOfTheDecision	The only way in which the auction can be closed is by such an offer being given and the property not being knocked down to an outsider.
RatioOfTheDecision	The expressions used after the words "buy in" make this position very clear.
RatioOfTheDecision	The mortgagee is given a right to buy in or to rescind i.e.
RatioOfTheDecision	to set aside the contract of buying or vary any contract for sale and to resell the same.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore when an auction sale is found not to result in realising the proper value, the mortgagee may either postpone the sale, or if he finds that he is unable to do so because the auction sale had started, he has to resort to the second way to prevent the property being knocked down at an undervalue and to resell the same.
RatioOfTheDecision	I do not think, on a true interpretation, the clause relating to the power of sale gives the mortgagees a right to buy the property for themselves.
RatioOfTheDecision	Unle ss that construction is put on this clause there is nothing to violate the words "power to sell" used in Section 69 .
RatioOfTheDecision	In my opinion, therefore, this clause does not violate Section 69 in any manner and the power is valid.
