Argument	In this regard he also argued that the ratio of <NAME> (supra) should be construed to be that courts have the power to modify terms of the scheme to re move impediments and the like to make the scheme function properly so long as the basic fabric of the scheme is not affected.
Precedent	According to <NAME>, the judgment of this <ORG> in <ORG> (supra) sets out the correct position in which it was stated in para 54 that:    "...
Precedent	Section 392 of the Act... only gives power to the <ORG> to make such modifications in the compromise or arrangement as it may consider necessary for the proper working of the compromise or arrangement... it cannot be understood as a power to make substantial modifications in the scheme approved by the members in a meeting called in terms of Section 391 of the Act."
Precedent	153.
RatioOfTheDecision	However wide the powers of the courts may be, they cannot be so wide as to order supply of gas in contravention of government policies, the constitutional obligations that the <ORG> must bear in mind when formulating such policies and in contravention of broader public interest.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> erred by holding that certain quantum of natural gas stood all ocated to <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The error is on account of both a misinterpretation of the <ORG> and also public law.
RatioOfTheDecision	Apart from that, both the Learned Single Judge and the <ORG> below have erroneously held that the <ORG>'s gas supply section be read into the <ORG> thereby effectively substituting the phrase "suitable arrangements" in Clause 19 to mean the gas supply provisions of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	We hold that those conclusions were erroneous.
RatioOfTheDecision	We disagree with the propositions of Learned Counsel for <ORG> that the ratio in <NAME> (supra) would support such a result.
RatioOfTheDecision	154.
RatioOfTheDecision	The ratio of <NAME> (supra) is that under Section 392 the <ORG> have the duty of continuous supervision to make the <ORG> workable by removing the hitches, obstacles or impediments as necessary to ensure the proper functioning of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Further, while the <ORG> does state that the powers of the court are of the widest amplitude, including the power to modify a provision of the scheme, it also does hold that the same can only be exercised so as  to ensue the proper working of the Scheme and further, that such powers may not be exercised in a manner that would alter the "basic fabric" of the scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	The removal of obstacles, impediments or hitches cannot be held to mean wholesale changes in the scheme itself and go beyond the confines of what the shareholders, the stakeholders and the courts that sanctioned the scheme would have understood the provisions of the scheme to mean.
RatioOfTheDecision	155.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that in paragraph 26 of the said decision it was stated that if "something can be omitted or something can be added to a scheme of compromise by the <ORG>, on its own motion or on the application of a person interested in the affairs of the company" then there ought not to be any justification for restricting the meaning of the word of modification and whittle down the powers of the court.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, the next paragraph holds the key to the judgment that the "basic fabric" of the scheme ought not to be changed.
RatioOfTheDecision	The limit on the powers of the <ORG> to modify by way  of even additions or omissions as contemplated is that the "basic fabric" of the <ORG> cannot be changed; and according to the said decision, even before a court could embark upon a mission of suggesting modifications it has to first determine what "modifications are necessary to make the compromise or arrangement workable."
RatioOfTheDecision	Any such determination first has to arrive at a conclusion that the <ORG> has become unworkable in its entirety or in a portion thereof.
RatioOfTheDecision	Arrangements, by their very nature are complex processes involving many elements that may or may not work.
Precedent	In fact in <NAME> (supra) this court recognized that to be the very reason why the legislature in India has given such a power to the courts; and such power can be exercised only to order those minimal modifications that would bring the aspect that is not working into a functional zone, with the proviso that at any rate such a modification cannot lead to a change of the "basic fabric" of the <ORG>.
Precedent	156.
Precedent	What does the expression "basic fabri c" mean?
RatioOfTheDecision	"Fabric" can imply both the end result, and also equally importantly, the processes, procedures and steps that were taken to weave the "fabric" of the Scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	During the course of weaving of the "fabric", decisions could be taken to leave out certain aspects as unacceptable to the Board or the shareholders and stakeholders or the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Further, those processes necessarily involve certain steps in obtaining shareholders permissions.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such processes are the very essence of the fabric and not just some technicalities that are to be consigned to history and ignored in making modifications.
RatioOfTheDecision	Whatever changes are made can only be minor ones which would not tamper with the essence of the scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	157.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this Scheme, the shareholders & stakeholders of <ORG> would have broadly understood from the Scheme two things: (1) that <ORG> was to engage in the business of supply of many different kinds of fuels, in which supply of natural gas to its affiliate companies is one; and (2)  that <ORG> will engage in the business of promoting energy generation business, from using any and all fuels, including natural gas, both from <ORG> and also from other sources.
RatioOfTheDecision	Nowhere did the <ORG> state that the only fuel that <ORG> would deal with would be natural gas from <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	To change that meaning would be to begin the process of tearing apart the "basic fabric" of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	158.
RatioOfTheDecision	"Basic fabric" of a scheme also implicates the essentiality of common interests between the class of members who have voted together, thinking that they all have the same level of information and the same understanding of the entire class of members as to what the <ORG> entails.
RatioOfTheDecision	That understanding would certainly not have comprehended the claims that <ORG> is putting forward in these proceedings: (i) that the intent was to actually share the benefits of the production and exploration activities, including the benefit of internal use of natural gas; (ii) that b ecause the same was not possible on account of statutory and contractual problems, the gas supply agreement was a way out; (iii) that the gas be supplied in accordance with the commercial terms regarding quantity, price and tenure in the <ORG> which were never revealed to them; (iv) that the burden of gas supply would involve the transgression of the boundaries of the <ORG> from which the value flows to <ORG>; and  (v) that the burden would extend to <ORG> subsidizing <ORG> if it were required to pay a much higher value to <ORG> than what it receives from <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	In contrast to the foregoing, all that the class of members who approved the scheme and the court which sanctioned it would have understood was that normal commercial agreements of supply, that would protect the interests of both parties and also including the clauses of governmental agreements, would be put in place.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such a conclusion would also follow from the main tenet of the Scheme that the two groups were to function independently of each other.
RatioOfTheDecision	159.
RatioOfTheDecision	If th e question regarding what would make the <ORG> work had been framed properly by the courts below and they had appreciated the role of the courts better then this case would not have taken the twists and turns that it has.
RatioOfTheDecision	The first question would have been whether the <ORG> itself has become unworkable?
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG>'s arguments that the gas supply is integral to the whole <ORG> are simply an unsustainable proposition.
RatioOfTheDecision	Gas supply is but a part of the <ORG> as a whole.
RatioOfTheDecision	The fact remains that <ORG> can supply gas to <ORG> provided appropriate governmental approvals, pursuant to constitutionally permissible utilization policies, are in place; and moreover, the commitment to supply gas in the <ORG> was to established gas based energy generating power plants.
RatioOfTheDecision	That possibility still remains.
RatioOfTheDecision	We fail to see where even that aspect of the <ORG> has failed to work.
RatioOfTheDecision	We were given to understand that in fact one of the gas based power generating power plants associated with <ORG> and <ORG> is in fact being supplied natural ga s, all in accordance with the utilization policies set in place by the GoI.
RatioOfTheDecision	If that be the case, then the conclusion that even this small part of the Scheme is not working is completely unwarranted and would not even merit a second look at.
RatioOfTheDecision	160.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Learned Counsel for <ORG> objected to reliance of RIL on the ratio of <NAME> (supra), on the ground that it only pertains to the situation at the time of sanction of the scheme and that the ratio of <ORG> (supra) cannot be relied upon as <NAME> (supra) a three judge decision, suggests otherwise.
RatioOfTheDecision	In light of the discussion above we do not see how, in the context of this case, the ratio of <NAME> (supra) is different from that of <ORG> (supra): they both speak of the same thing, that the basic fabric of the scheme cannot be changed.
RatioOfTheDecision	Which aspect of that basic fabric the courts may deal with could vary, but certainly the processes that protect the shareholders, their rights to know what is being transferred and the sanctity of the cla ss of members who have voted together cannot be derogated from.
RatioOfTheDecision	161.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the instant case by importing the gas supply section into the <ORG>, in the guise of interpreting it, the phrase "suitable arrangements" was transformed into "suitable arrangements as agreed upon by the promoters in the gas supply section of the <ORG>".
RatioOfTheDecision	Such a modification necessarily tears apart the basic fabric and cannot be permitted.
RatioOfTheDecision	162.
RatioOfTheDecision	For the view that we have taken it is not necessary to go into the protested points regarding the Identity of the Buyer, Definition of Affiliate and Limitation of Liability.
RatioOfTheDecision	CONCLUSIONS:  163.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the result, we hold that:  (i) both the learned Single Judge and <ORG> committed a serious error in exercising jurisdiction in the manner they did under Section 392 of the Companies Act, 1956, for such interference has resulted in the provisions of a document (<ORG>) which was not before the shareholders supersede the <ORG> of Arrangement.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such a document could not have been read into and incorp orated in the Scheme propounded by the <ORG>, approved by the shareholders and sanctioned by <ORG>;  (ii) the courts below having rightly directed the parties to negotiate, and further having rightly refused to grant the prayers in the Company Application, however, fell into error directing the MoU to be binding and the basis for further negotiations between the parties.
RatioOfTheDecision	MoU is a private pact between the members of <ORG> family which is not binding on <ORG>;     (iii) the <ORG> decisions, regarding the utilization of the natural gas and the price formula/basis etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	do not suffer from any legal or constitutional infirmities.
RatioOfTheDecision	They shall apply to all supplies of natural gas under the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The parties are bound by the governmental policy and approvals regarding price, quantity and tenure for supply of gas;  (iv) under the <ORG> in issue the Contractor (<ORG>) does not become the owner of natural gas, and there is nothing like specified physical quantities of natural gas to be shared by the <ORG> and the Contr actor;  (v) we, accordingly, direct the parties to renegotiate as to the suitable arrangements for supply of gas de-hors the MoU.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such renegotiations shall be within the framework of governmental policy and approvals regarding price, quantity and tenure for supply of gas.
RatioOfTheDecision	The renegotiations shall commence within eight weeks from today at the initiative of <ORG> and shall be completed within a period of six weeks from the day of commencement of negotiations.
RulingByPresentCourt	Accordingly, the judgments of the learned Single Judge and the Division Bench of <ORG> are set aside and we dispose of all the appeals without any order as to costs.
RulingByPresentCourt	Intervention Applications do not require any adjudication.
RulingByPresentCourt	They are also accordingly disposed of.
RatioOfTheDecision	164.
RatioOfTheDecision	Before we part with the case, we consider it appropriate to observe and remind the <ORG> that it is high time it frames a comprehensive policy/suitable legislation with regard to energy security of India and supply of natural gas under production sharing contracts.
