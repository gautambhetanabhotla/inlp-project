Precedent	In <NAME> and Anr.

Precedent	J.
Argument	(1954) SCR 904 speaking for the <ORG>, held :  ....
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that there is substantial difference between the two sections but they also to some extent overlap and it is a question to be determined on the facts of each case whether the charge under Section 149 overlaps the ground covered by Section 34 .
RatioOfTheDecision	If the common intention, then the substitution of Section 34 for Section 149 might result in prejudice to the accused and ought not therefore be permitted.
RatioOfTheDecision	B ut if the facts to be proved and the evidence to be adduced with reference to the charge under Section 149 would be the same if the charge were under Section 34 .
RatioOfTheDecision	could not result in any prejudice and in such cases the substitution of Section 34 for Section 149 must be held to be a formal matter.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is no such broad proposition of law that there can be no recourse to Section 34 when the charge is only under Section 149 .
RatioOfTheDecision	Whether such recourse can be had or not must depend on the facts of each case.
RatioOfTheDecision	13.
RatioOfTheDecision	The findings of <ORG> as confirmed by <ORG> would clearly show that the appellant had the common intention.
RatioOfTheDecision	The facts proved and the evidence adduced would have been the same if the appellant had been charged under Section 302 read with Section 34 and we do not see how the appellant was prejudicied.
RatioOfTheDecision	14.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have no hesitation in holding that <ORG> was right in its conclusion that as the appellant had common intention with <NAME> to murder <NAME>, there was no illegality  in convicting him under Section 302 read with Section 34 .
