RatioOfTheDecision	The argument is somewhat ingenious but there are obvious difficulties in the way of its acceptance.

RatioOfTheDecision	Section 101 merely imposed a restriction upon the power of <ORG> to make laws for an acceding State inconsistent with the stipulations contained in the Instrument of Accession.
RatioOfTheDecision	When that section along with the rest of the Government of India Act, 1935, was repealed by the new Constitution, which has created new legislatures with power to make retroactive laws, it is idle to suggest that ri ghts or privileges acquired while the old Constitution Act was in force are preserved for ever-for that must be the result of the argument-by section 6 of the General Clauses Act, which can have no application to such cases.
RatioOfTheDecision	Furthermore, it will be recalled that the Proclamation made by the <NAME> as Ruler of Rajasthan on 23rd November, 1949, declared and directed that the Constitution of India when brought into force "shall be- the Constitution for the Rajasthan State" and it expressly "superseded and abrogated all other constitutional provisions inconsistent therewith" which were then in force.
