Fact	ORDER Hiremath, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	This application under Order 41 Rule 19 read with Section 151 C.P.C.
Fact	is filed in the following circumstances.
Fact	2.
Fact	The appellant/applicant challenged the decree of the first <ORG> in R.A. No.
Fact	22/1987 on certain substantial questions of law formulated by him in the appeal memo as required.
Fact	The defendant in the Original Suit is the appellant and he suffered a decree in the trial Court for declaration of title of the plaintiff/respondent to the suit property and for delivery of vacant possession of the area under dispute.
Fact	The parties are said to be brothers.
Fact	The first <ORG> confirmed the decree.
Fact	When the appeal came up for admission on 15-4-1991, the learned Single Judge made the following Order:  "None appears for the appellant.
Fact	This matter was heard on 8-2-1991 and notice directed to the other side.
Fact	The respondent/ plaintiff has appeared through Counsel who is present in Court today.
Argument	I have satisfied myself that no injustice is caused by dismissing this appeal for non -prosecution as it is on the concurrent findings of the <ORG> below and no substantial question of law arises for consideration."
Argument	Though in the memorandum of facts in support of <ORG> it is stated that the Counsel appearing for the appellant was seriously ill and unable to move out of the house due to acute diabetic nuerosis and because of his physical inability he could not go to the <ORG> and represent the matter.
RatioOfTheDecision	None appeared before <ORG> when the matter was taken up for admission.
RatioOfTheDecision	3.
RatioOfTheDecision	The point now for consideration is whether this application under Order 41 Rule 19 C.P.C.
Argument	is maintainable as this <ORG> in the absence of the Counsel appearing for the appellant proceeded to observe that no substantial question of law arises for consideration, which ultimately may mean that the appeal was not found fit for admission on merits.
Argument	The respondent supports the order contending that the Judgment being one passed on merits, application under Order 41 Rule 19 is not maintainable.
Argument	4.
Argument	Order 41 Rule 11 (1) reads th us:  "<ORG>, after sending for the record if it thinks fit so to do, and after fixing a day for hearing the appellant or his pleader and hearing him accordingly if he appears on that day, may dismiss the appeal without sending notice to, the <ORG> from whose decree the appeal is preferred and without serving notice on the respondent or his pleader."
Argument	Under Rule 11 (2):  "If on the day fixed or any other day to which the hearing may be adjourned the appellant does not appear when the appeal is called on for hearing, the <ORG> may make an order that the appeal be dismissed."
Statute	Under Rule 11 (3):  "The dismissal of an appeal under this rule shall be notified to the <ORG> from whose decree the appeal is preferred."
Statute	Thus Rule 11 is attracted to a stage Where the appeal is not yet admitted and posted for hearing oh merits.
Statute	Rule 16 relates to the procedure on hearing.
Statute	Explanation to Order 17(1) states that nothing in Sub-rule (1) shall be construed as empowering the <ORG> to dismiss the appeal on meri ts.
Statute	Under Sub-rule (1) of Rule 17 where on the date fixed or on any other date to which hearing may be adjourned; the appellant does not appear when the appeal is called for hearing, the <ORG> may make an Order that appeal be dismissed.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus this is the stage when the appeal is posted for hearing after it is admitted to hear on the substantial questions of law arising in the appeal.
Statute	Rule 19 relates to re-admission of appeal dismissed for default.
Statute	It is necessary to note that Rule 19 does not confine itself to Rule 17 only.
Argument	On the other hand, it applies to Rule 11 as well when it states that where an appeal is dismissed under Rule 11 Sub-rule (2) of Rule 17 or Rule 18 the appellant may apply to the Appellate <ORG> for re-admission of the appeal.
Argument	These are the relevant provisions which are now required to be considered in this application.
Argument	Rule 19 makes reference to Sub-rule (2) of Rule 11 which states that if on the day or any other date to which the hearing may be adjourned, the appellant does not appear when  the appeal is called on for hearing, the <ORG> may make an order that the appeal be dismissed.
Argument	This is analogous to the provisions of Rule 17(1).
Argument	It is contended that the Explanation to Rule 17 may as well attract the provisions of Rule 11 though it is not expressly stated so under Rule 11 that the <ORG> has no power to dismiss the appeal on merits in the absence of the appellant.
Argument	5.
Argument	My attention is also invited to Order 42 Rule 2 C.P.C.
Argument	which refers to power of the <ORG> to direct that the appeal be heard on the questions formulated by it.
Argument	It says that at the time of making an order under Rule 11 of Order 41 for the hearing of the second appeal, the <ORG> shall formulate the substantial question of law as required by Section 100 and in doing so, the <ORG> may direct that the second appeal be heard on the question so formulated and it shall not be open to the appellant to urge any other ground in the appeal without the leave of the <ORG> given in accordance with the provisions of Section 100.
Argument	Thus Rule 2 of  Order 42 refers to Rule 11 of Order 41 as well.
RatioOfTheDecision	What is required to be done before admitting an appeal is stated in Rule 11 which means hearing the appellant or his pleader and then coming to the conclusion if the substantial question of law arises or not.
RatioOfTheDecision	Incidentally the respondent's Counsel invited my attention to Chapter II of the Rules framed by <ORG> which relates to definitions and interpretations.
RatioOfTheDecision	Rule 1 (p) reads thus:  "To admit a case" means to decide to issue notice to respondent or direct issue of notice to respondent after preliminary perusal of papers or preliminary hearing under the provisions of Order 41 Rule 11 of C.P.C.
RatioOfTheDecision	or Section 421 of .
RatioOfTheDecision	or any other like provision to any other law for the time being in force;  The words "Admission", "For Admission" or similar expressions shall be construed accordingly.
RatioOfTheDecision	Where upon such preliminary perusal or hearing the <ORG> decides not to issue notice, the case is said to be dismissed summarily."
RatioOfTheDecision	Referring to this definition, it was urged t hat the <ORG> has power to peruse the papers as a preliminary step or give a preliminary hearing to decide to issue notice to respondent or direct issue of notice to respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	Issuing of notice to respondent may be at a stage where the <ORG> has not yet decided to hear the appeal on admission.
RatioOfTheDecision	This however is not in conflict with Rule 11 of Order 41 which speaks of hearing the appellant or his pleader before dismissing the appeal without sending notice to lower <ORG> or issuing notice to the respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	That being so, the preliminary stage or preliminary hearing contemplated under this Rule 1(p) cannot be construed as hearing for admission itself.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, whether the <ORG> proceeds to hear under Rule 11 or Rule 17, it must be deemed that the said hearing is either to decide or determine any substantial question of law involved in that appeal or on merits respectively.
RatioOfTheDecision	Undoubtedly, the stage under Rule 17 had not reached and the <ORG> was at the stage under Rule 11.
RatioOfTheDecision	Broadly speaking, it is apparently clea r from reading of Rule 11 of Order 41 or Order 42 Rule 2 that the appellant's Counsel or appellant must be heard before the <ORG> decides whether there is substantial question of law involved or not in the appeal.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> sitting in the second appeal has the task of perusal of the.
RatioOfTheDecision	Judgments of the trial <ORG> and the first Appellate <ORG> before deciding whether the appeal involves substantial questions of law or not, as the question of fact decided in the first Appellate <ORG> could not be reopened unless appreciation of evidence is perverse.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, for that reason perhaps Rule 11 lays down that after fixing the date for Rearing the appellant or his pleader and hearing him accordingly if he appears on that day, may dismiss the appeal.
RatioOfTheDecision	If he does not appear Sub-rule 2 gives power to dismiss the appeal and this cannot be construed as giving power to the <ORG> to dismiss the appeal on merits.
RatioOfTheDecision	When the appeal reaches the High <ORG> it has to apply its mind while considering the appeal for admission and he aring the appellant's Counsel who would assist the <ORG> in determining the questions involved in the appeal.
RatioOfTheDecision	That being so, it cannot be said that the order passed by this <ORG> in the absence of the appellant should be construed as an order passed on hearing the appellant and on merits.
RatioOfTheDecision	It thus follows that even at the stage of admission the appeal cannot be dismissed on merits stating that no substantial question of law is involved.
RatioOfTheDecision	6.
RatioOfTheDecision	What should happen to the order that is already passed by this <ORG> is the next point for consideration.
RatioOfTheDecision	When this <ORG> finds that even at the admission stage an appeal could not be dismissed on merits, the <ORG> has broad discretion of rectifying its own order as the Apex <ORG> of the State to impart justice and to prevent failure of justice that may occasion if its order were to stand.
RatioOfTheDecision	In my view, therefore, the order now in question cannot be deemed to have been passed on merits as the same was passed without hearing the appellant's Counsel and considering the grounds  made out in the memorandum of facts that the Counsel was seriously ill and was not in a position to attend the Court on the date fixed for admission as constituting sufficient cause for the Advocate not being present, the order in question dated 15-4-1991 is set aside holding the same to be an order passed for default of the appellant's Counsel and the Appeal is restored to file, The same shall be posted for admission.
