Fact	JUDGMENT <NAME>, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	This appeal has been filed by the State of Maharashtra against an order of <ORG> directing <NAME>, Inspector of Police, <ORG>, Sholapur, the fourth respondent before <ORG>, to pay an amount of Rs.
Fact	10,000/- by way of compensation to the respondent herein, an undertrial prisoner, on the ground that the said police officer was guilty of violation of fundamental right of an undertrial prisoner under Article 21 of the Constitution of India.
Fact	It further directed that an entry should be made in his service record.
Fact	The facts that give rise to this appeal are as follows.
Fact	2.
Fact	One <NAME> was murdered on 2nd August, 1989.
Fact	During the investigation, the police suspected that the respondent herein was a party to the said murder.
Fact	He was arrested in Karnataka State and was brought to Sholapur in the early hours of 17th August, 1989.
Fact	A local paper called <ORG> published from Sholapur, carried in its issue of 17 th August, 1989 a news item which stated that the respondent, an undertrial prisoner, would be taken in a procession or a parade from Faujdar Chavadi Police Station through the main squares of the city for the purpose of investigation.
Fact	On 17th August, 1989, the respondent herein was handcuffed and both his arms were tied by a rope and he was taken through the streets and the same is not in dispute.
Fact	The respondent herein filed a writ petition seeking a censure of the police officer and to award damages.
Argument	<ORG> of <ORG> having exonerated <NAME> and other respondents, held that the 4th respondent <NAME>, Inspector of Police, who is one of the appellants before us, has subjected the undertrial prisoner to an unwarranted humiliation and indignity which cannot be done to any citizen of India and accordingly directed him to pay the compensation and he was also censured as mentioned above.
Argument	3.
Argument	It is submitted before as that the respondent had a long crim inal record and that the murder of <NAME> was as a result of enmity between the two gangs and the respondent belonged to one gang and the situation required that he should be taken after being handcuffed.
Argument	<ORG> elaborately dealt with this aspect and held that the explanation given by <NAME> is wholly unacceptable.
Argument	On behalf of the respondent, reliance is placed on some of the decisions of this <ORG> on the aspect of handcuffing and violation of Article 21 of the Constitution of India.
Argument	<CITATION_162242> .
Argument	, <ORG> of this <ORG> held that "the convicts are not wholly denuded of their fundamental rights.
Fact	Prisoners are entitled to all constitutional rights unless their liberty has been constitutionally curtailed."
RulingByLowerCourt	<CITATION_853252> , this <ORG> observed that "To be consistent with Articles 14 and 19 handcuffs must be the last refuge as there are other ways for ensuring security.
RulingByLowerCourt	No prisoner shall be handcuffed or fettered routinely or merely for the convenience of t he custodian or escort."
RulingByLowerCourt	<CITATION_1745823> .
RulingByLowerCourt	, this Court again reiterated following the principles laid down in <NAME> case1 and other cases held that handcuffing is an act against all norms of decency and amounts to violation of principles underlying Article 21.
Fact	This Court also directed <ORG> to take appropriate action against the erring officials for having unjustly and unreasonably handcuffed the arrested persons.
Fact	4.
RatioOfTheDecision	Having gone through the entire record we are unable to disagree with some of the findings of <ORG> regarding the handcuffing and we do not propose to interfere with the order directing the payment of compensation.
RatioOfTheDecision	But we think that <NAME>, Inspector of Police, 2nd appellant herein, cannot be made personally liable.
RatioOfTheDecision	He has acted only as an official and even assuming that he has exceeded his limits and thus erred in taking the undertrial prisoners handcuffed, still we do not think that he can be made personally liable.
RulingByLowerCourt	<CITATION_810491> .
RulingByLowerCourt	, this Co urt directed the <ORG> to pay compensation to the person illegally detained.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG> also having noted this decision observed that the court can order payment of compensation either by the <ORG> or persons acting on behalf of the <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	Having so observed, <ORG>, however, held <NAME>, Inspector of Police personally liable and directed him to pay the compensation.
RulingByLowerCourt	We are of the view that in the instant case also a similar order as one passed in <NAME> case4, will meet the ends of justice.
RulingByLowerCourt	Then <ORG> has also directed that an entry should be made in his service record to the effect that he was guilty of violation of fundamental right of an undertrial prisoner.
RulingByLowerCourt	So far this direction is concerned, it is submitted that such an adverse entry cannot straightway be made without giving <NAME>, 2nd appellant herein, an opportunity of being heard.
RulingByLowerCourt	We find considerable force in this submission and accordingly we modify the order of <ORG> as follows.
RulingByLowerCourt	5.
RulingByLowerCourt	The compensation of Rs.
RulingByLowerCourt	10,000/- as awarded by <ORG> shall be paid by the State of Maharashtra.
RatioOfTheDecision	The concerned authorities may, if they think it necessary, hold an enquiry and then decide whether any further action has to be taken against <NAME>, Inspector of Police.
RulingByPresentCourt	Subject to the above directions, this appeal is disposed of.
