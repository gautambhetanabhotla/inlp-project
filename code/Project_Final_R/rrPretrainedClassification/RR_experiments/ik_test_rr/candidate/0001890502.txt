Fact	JUDGMENT  1.
Fact	Special Leave granted.
Fact	Intervenor's application filed in Special Leave Petition No.
Fact	6121 of 1990 is also allowed.
Fact	2.
Fact	This appeal concerns with the problem of regularisation of substituted/ad hoc teachers in <ORG> of the State of Manipur.
Fact	They have been in service for the past several years.
Fact	<ORG> however has refused to regularise their services, instead they took steps for direct recruitment.
Fact	The services of the members of the appellants' association were also sought to be terminated.
Fact	Thereupon the appellants moved <ORG> for relief under Article 226 for staying the direct recruitment and also seeking a direction to regularise their services.
Fact	<ORG> issued a rule nisi in the writ petition but refused to grant any interim relief.
Fact	3.
Fact	This appeal has been preferred against the order of <ORG> denying the interim relief to the appellants.
Fact	During the pendency of this appeal, there were some more writ petitions filed before <ORG>.
Fact	In  one of those cases, <ORG> has directed the <ORG> to publish the result of the direct recruitment, and in another case, it appears that <ORG> has issued an order staying the direct recruitment.
Fact	4.
Fact	It is said that there are more than one thousand substituted teachers who have been recruited from 1981-82.
Fact	It appears that they were allowed to appear before the <ORG> for direct recruitment and in that process 23 of them were selected by the <ORG> for direct recruitment, but they could not also be regularly appointed in view of the stay order of <ORG>.
Fact	5.
Fact	If the direct recruitment takes place on one hand and substituted teachers are also directed to be regularised subsequently, it would create an enormous problem for the department to accommodate both the categories of persons.
Fact	Taking all these factors into consideration, this <ORG> made an order dated 2nd May 1990 directing <ORG> to consider the case of regularisation of the appellants before making direct recruitment .
Fact	But the Government did not take any action.
Fact	On 9th October, 1990 the <ORG> made a further order to implement the, order dated 2nd May 1990 within two weeks from that date failing which the Secretary, <ORG> should be present personally for facing contempt proceedings.
Fact	Pursuant thereto, the Secretary of <ORG> is personally present before us today.
Fact	6.
Fact	Mr. <NAME>, learned Senior Advocate appeared for the <ORG>.
Fact	He also explained the genesis of the case and the problem of the <ORG> for regularising the services of all the substituted teachers.
Fact	Having heard counsel on both sides, it appears to as that it is necessary to make the following order to avoid further litigation and also to avoid seemingly conflicting interim orders issued by the High <ORG>.
Fact	1.
Fact	All substituted/ad hoc teachers who have put in five years of service or more as on October 1, 1990 shall be regularised without pay D.P.C.
Fact	This regularisation would be subject to their possessing the required qualifications at the ti me of their initial appointment.
Fact	2.
Fact	The substituted/ad hoc teachers who have rendered less than five years of service as on October 1, 1990 shall be allowed to appear before the <ORG> for selection.
Fact	The <ORG> shall be constituted exclusively for them within three months from today.
Fact	Those who are selected by the <ORG> shall be regularised immediately thereafter.
Fact	3.
Fact	Twenty three substituted/ad hoc teachers who have been already selected by the <ORG> shall be regularly appointed forthwith retaining their present seniority.
Fact	4.
Fact	All the substituted/ad hoc teachers who ace in service as on today shall be allowed to continue in service till the <ORG> declares its result of the selection.
Fact	The services of those who do not appear before <ORG> or could not be selected by the <ORG> could be terminated unless their services are required for a further period.
Fact	5.
Fact	It is said that the <ORG> earlier convened has selected some persons for direct recruitment.
Fact	If there are additional vacancies in addition to those whic h are being occupied by the substituted/ad hoc teachers, they may straightway be appointed against those vacancies and the remaining if any may be appointed after the <ORG> completes the, process of selection of substituted/ ad hoc teachers for regularisation depending upon the vacancy position.
Fact	6.
Fact	As to the seniority between the direct recruits and the regularised candidates, we make it clear that the direct recruits other than those mentioned in Para (3) above, shall be ranked below all the regularised candidates.
Argument	7.
Argument	This order shall govern all those who are in service as substituted/ad hoc teachers.
Argument	It shall also govern all pending litigations before <ORG> or any other <ORG>.
RulingByPresentCourt	The concerned parties shall move <ORG> or the <ORG> to dispose of the pending litigations accordingly.
