RatioOfTheDecision	Now, it is clear that in the present case those considerations have no application.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is clear that the decree-holders could, notwithstanding the order in the claim case, have prosecuted their application for execution against the one-third share which was not released then quite as well as they can do so now.
Fact	Their present application is for the sale of that third share of the property; there was no bar then to their enforcing the execution of the decree, and there has been no subsequent removal of  that bar.
RatioOfTheDecision	The reason of the decisions not applying to the present case, they cannot afford any ground for holding that the present application is a continuation of the application of the 11th June 1883.
RatioOfTheDecision	3.
RatioOfTheDecision	Then, as regards the second ground, no doubt the case cited in the argument, <ORG> v. <NAME> 8 W.R. 99, lends some support to it, but that was a case under the old Limitation Law (Act XIV of 1859), the language of which was very different from that of the present law.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause 4, Article 179 of the second schedule of the Limitation Act provides, that the three years may be reckoned from the date of applying, in accordance with law, to the proper <ORG> for execution, or to take some steps in aid of execution of any decree or order," etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	Now, seeing that the Limitation Act XV of 1877 draws a clear distinction between suits and applications, it would be difficult to construe these words to include a suit for setting aside an order in a claim case.
RatioOfTheDecision	"Proper <ORG>," again, as defined by Explanation  II of that article, means the <ORG> whose duty it is (whether under Section 226 or Section 227 of the Code of Civil Procedure or otherwise) to execute the decree or order," Therefore, evidently the provision of law just referred to contemplates an application made in the course of execution to the <ORG> whose duty it was to execute the decree, and cannot be taken to include a suit to have an order in a claim case set aside.

RulingByPresentCourt	We therefore think that the Lower Appellate <ORG> was right, and that this appeal must be dismissed with costs.
