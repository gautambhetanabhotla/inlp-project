Fact	P-7 relates to a small plot and the land in question acquired is about 84 acre.
Fact	This land comprising of large area is not developed although it has potential value for residentia l and commercial purposes.
RatioOfTheDecision	In order to develop this land, roads were to be laid, provisions for drainage was to be made and certain area was to be earmarked for other civic amenities.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, after leaving the area in the land required for the purposes mentioned above, plots were to be made for residential and commercial purposes by incurring expenditure for other developmental works, such as providing electricity, water, etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	The acquired land is not small plot located in such a way that no other development was required at all and it could be utilized as it is as a developed building site.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is well-settled that in respect of agricultural land or undeveloped land which has potential value for housing or commercial purposes, normally 1/3 amount of compensation has to be deducted out of the amount of compensation payable on the acquired land subject to certain variations depending on its nature, location, extent of expenditure involved for development and the area required for roads and other civic amenities t o develop the land so as to make the plots for the residential or commercial purposes.
RatioOfTheDecision	A land may be plain or uneven, the soil of the land may be soft or hard bearing on the foundation for the purpose of making construction; may be the land is situated in the midst of a developed area all around but that land may have a hillock or may be low-lying or may be having deep ditches.
RatioOfTheDecision	So the amount of expenses that may be incurred in developing the area also varies.
RatioOfTheDecision	A claimant who claims that his land is fully developed and nothing more is required to be done for developmental purposes, must show on the basis of evidence that it is such a land and it is so located.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the absence of such evidence, merely saying that area adjoining his land is developed area, is not enough particularly when the extent of the acquired land is large and even if a small portion of the land is abutting the main road in the developed area, does not give the land character of a developed area.
RatioOfTheDecision	In 84 acres of land acquired even if one porti on on one side abuts the main road, the remaining large area where planned development is required, needs laying of internal roads, drainage, sewer, water, electricity lines, providing civic amenities etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, in cases of some land where there are certain advantages by virtue of the developed area around, may help in reducing the percentage of cut to be applied, as the developmental charges required may be less on that account.
RatioOfTheDecision	There may be various factual factors which may have to be taken into consideration while applying the cut in payment of compensation towards developmental charges, may be in some cases it is more than 1/3 and in some cases less than 1/3.
RatioOfTheDecision	It must be remembered that there is difference between a developed area and an area having potential value, which is yet to be developed.
RatioOfTheDecision	The fact that an area is developed or adjacent to a developed area will not ipso facto make every land situated in the area also developed to be valued as a building site or plot, particularly when vast tracts a re acquired, as in this case, for development purpose.

RatioOfTheDecision	On facts and in the light of the legal position emerging from the various decisions referred to above, it is not possible for us to say that cut of 20% adopted by the learned Single Judge as affirmed by <ORG> in the impugned judgment is wrong or unsustainable.
RatioOfTheDecision	It appears to us having regard to facts and circumstances of the case that <ORG> has applied cut of 20% as against the normal 1/3 deduction.
RatioOfTheDecision	We find that <ORG> was right and justified in doing so.
RatioOfTheDecision	The decision of <NAME> (supra) does not help the appellants as the said decision was rendered on the facts of that case.
RatioOfTheDecision	As already noticed above, the said decision was referred to in earlier decisions of this <ORG> and distinguished.
RatioOfTheDecision	That was a case of a fully developed land having all amenities and situated in an advantageous position.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the context of the facts of that case , in para 11, it is stated thus:-  "The principle of deduction in the land value covered by the comparable sale is thus adopted in order to arrive at the market value of the acquired land.
RatioOfTheDecision	In applying the principle it is necessary to consider all relevant facts.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not the extent of the area covered under the acquisition which is the only relevant factor.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even in the vast area there may be land which is fully developed having all amenities and situated in an advantageous position.
RatioOfTheDecision	If smaller area within the large tract is already developed and suitable for building purposes and have in its vicinity roads, drainage, electricity, communications etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	then the principle of deduction simply for the reason that it is part of the large tract acquired, may not be justified."
RatioOfTheDecision	(emphasis supplied) In that case deduction was not given on the ground that even in the vast area there may be land, which is fully developed having all amenities and situated in an advantageous position; if smaller area within the large trac t is already developed and suitable for building purposes and have in its vicinity roads, drainage, electricity, communication, etc., then the principle of deduction simply for the reason that it is part of the large tract acquired, may not be justified.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case the situation is entirely different.
RatioOfTheDecision	The area acquired is not a small area; it was not developed; may be it had some advantages; a small portion of the large tract was abutting the main road; it was also not the case that any smaller area within the large tract of land acquired was fully developed having all facilities as in the case of Bhagwathula Samanna (supra).
RatioOfTheDecision	The appellants herein did not establish that the entire area of 84 acres of land acquired was fully developed having all the facilities such as roads, drains, sewers, water, electricity lines and civic amenities.
RatioOfTheDecision	In order to convert the land into plots for the purpose of construction of residential and commercial buildings certain area was to be earmarked for the abovementioned  purposes in accordance with the law governing in the matter of creating layouts in addition to incurring of expenditure for the development area.
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence the claim of the appellants that there should have been no deduction out of the compensation amount determined for the entire area acquired is unsustainable.
RatioOfTheDecision	May be the acquired land with potentiality for construction of residential and commercial buildings had some advantages, which aspect is taken note of by <ORG> in giving cut of only 20% as against 1/3 normal deduction.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not find any force in the contention that the <ORG> has made unjust enrichment by collecting more money from the allottees after the compensation amount was enhanced by the District Judge and that neither the <ORG> nor the <ORG> will be put to any loss as they have collected money from the allottees.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not the case where collection of any tax is involved to bring in the theory of unjust enrichment.
RatioOfTheDecision	Be that as it may, we are not concerned in these cases as to what happened  between the <ORG> and the allottees.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question for consideration is as to the determination of amount of compensation for the acquired land.
RatioOfTheDecision	Once the proper amount of compensation is finally determined, the land owners will be entitled only to that amount.
RatioOfTheDecision	Having regard to all these aspects, we find no merit in these appeals.
