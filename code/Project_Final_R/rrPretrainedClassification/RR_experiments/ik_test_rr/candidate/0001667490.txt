RatioOfTheDecision	This has been the consistent view of this <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 11A of the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 (for short 'the Act') confers a wide power upon the <ORG>, <ORG> or the National <ORG> to give appropriate relief in case of discharge or dismissal of workmen.
RatioOfTheDecision	While adjudicating on a reference made to it, the Labuor <ORG>, <ORG> or the National <ORG>, as the case may be, if satisfied that the order of discharge or dismissal was not justified, it may, while setting aside the same, direct reinstatement of the workman on such terms and conditions, if any, as it thinks fit, or give such other relief to the workman including the award of any lesser punishment in lieu of discharge or dismissal as the circumstances of the case may require.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, only in a case where the satisfaction is reach ed by <ORG> or the <ORG>, as the case may be, that an order of dismissal was not justified, the same can be set aside.
RatioOfTheDecision	So long as the same is not set aside, it remains valid.
RatioOfTheDecision	But once whether on the basis of the evidences brought on record in the domestic inquiry or by reason of additional evidence the employer makes out a case justifying the order of dismissal, we fail to understand as to how such order of dismissal can be given effect to only from the date of the award and not from the date of passing of the order of punishment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The distinction r-ought to be made by this <ORG> in some of the matters including <ORG> (supra), in our opinion, is not based on a sound premise, particularly when the binding decisions of this <ORG> in <ORG> case (supra) and Workmen of Messrs <ORG> (P.) <CITATION_1747159> .,  3 SCR 587, have not been taken note of.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case, we find that the charges were proved before the Labour <ORG> and,  thus, <ORG> was correct in holding that the order of termination would relate back to the date of original order.
