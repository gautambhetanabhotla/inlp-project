Fact	HEADNOTE: The respondent used to manufacture certain goods for sale in India by Messers <ORG> (for short <ORG>'s) under certain trade marks in respect of which the latter was registered as the sole registered user in India.
Fact	The goods were supplied to <ORG>'s at wholesale price on rail at Moga or free o n lorry at factory.
Fact	The respondent disputed the value of the goods determined by the excise authorities for the purpose of the levy under the Central Excises and Salt Act , 1944 and ultimately the respondent filed writ petitions in <ORG>.
Fact	<ORG> allowed the writ petitions holding that the value of the trade marks cannot form a component of the value of the goods for the purpose of assessment of excise duty.
Argument	In appeal to <ORG>, the appellant contended that the value of the goods sold by the respondent to <ORG> should, for the purpose of levy of excise duty, include the value of the trade marks under which the goods are sold in the market and that the value of such trade marks should be added to the wholesale price for which the goods are sold by the respondent to <ORG>.
Argument	Dismissing the appeal.
Argument	^ HELD: The value of <ORG> trade marks cannot be added to the wholesale price charged by the respondent to <ORG> for the purpose of computing the value of the goods manufactured by  the respondent in the assessment to excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	[168 C-D] In the instant case, what are sold and supplied by the respondent are goods manufactured by it with the trade marks 166 affixed to them and it is the whole sale price of goods that must determine the value for the purpose of assessment of excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is immaterial that the trade marks belong to <ORG>'s.
RatioOfTheDecision	What is material is that <ORG>'s have authorised the respondent to affix the trade marks on the goods manufactured by it and it is the goods with the trade marks affixed to them that are so sold by the respondent to <ORG>'s.
RatioOfTheDecision	There can therefore be no doubt that the wholesale price at which the goods with the trade marks affixed to them are sold by the respondent to <ORG>'s as stipulated under the agreements would be the value of the goods for the purpose of excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	That is the price at which the respondent sells the goods to <ORG>'s in the course of wholesale trade.

Fact	The Judgment of the Court was delivered by <NAME> This appeal by special leave is directed against the judgment and order dated August 31, 1976 of <ORG> and haryana allowing a Group of writ petitions filed by the respondent against the levy of excise duty.
Fact	The respondent, Messrs. Food Specialities Limited, Moga is a company registered under the Indian Companies Act , 1956.
Fact	It entered into a number of agreements with Messrs. Nestle's <ORG>, a subsidiary of a foreign company, Messrs. Nestle's holdings Limited, to manufacture for and on behalf of Messrs. Nestle's <ORG> (hereinafter  referred to as <ORG>'s ) sweetened condensed milk, soluble coffee, <NAME> milk food, milk powders and infant cereal foods for sale in India by <ORG>'s under certain trade-marks in respect of which the latter was registered as the sole registered user in India.
Fact	The agreement stipulated that the respondent would manufacture the goods and supply them to <ORG>'s in such quantities as <ORG>'s might specify from time to time subject to a prescribed minimum and maximum, and <ORG>'s was obliged to buy the products so manufactured by the respondent.
Fact	The products were manufactured by the respondent in accordance with detailed quality specifications supplied by <ORG>'s, and the price of the products was determined under the agreements free on rail at Moga or free on lory at factory.
Fact	The respondent enjoyed no interest in the trade marks and labels and undertook not to sell any of those products to any person other than <ORG>'s.
Fact	The products manufactured by the respondent were subject to excise duty under the said  <ORG>s and Salt Act , 1944.
Fact	The respondent disputed the value of the goods determined by the excise authorities for the purpose of the levy, and a number of questions were raised in that context.
Fact	The controversy was processed through the statutory channels provided by the Act and ultimately the respondent filed writ petitions in <ORG>.
Fact	<ORG> found in favour of the respondent and quashed the orders of the <ORG> authorities and <ORG> impugned in the writ petitions.
Fact	In these appeals the only question pressed before us by the learned Attorney General on behalf of the appellant is that the value of the goods sold by the respondent to <ORG> should, for the purpose on levy of excise duty, include the value of the trade marks under which the goods are sold in the market and that the value of such trade marks should be added to the wholesale price for which the goods are sold by the respondent to <ORG>.
Argument	It is urged by the lea med Attorney General that <ORG> erred i n holding that the value of the trade marks cannot form a component of the value of the goods for the purpose of assessment of excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are of opinion that <ORG> was right in the view it took and the appeal must rail.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may be noticed that the respondent manufactures the goods according to the specifications supplied by <ORG> and affixes the trade marks of <ORG> on the goods and supplies the same to <ORG> at a wholesale price free on rail at Moga or free on lorry at factory stipulated under the agreements with <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	What are sold and supplied by the respondent are goods manufactured by it with the trade marks affixed to them and it is the whole sale price of such goods that must determine the value for the purpose of assessment of excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is immaterial that the trade marks belong to <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	What is material is that <ORG> have authorised the respondent to affix the trade marks on the goods manufactured by it and it is the goods with the trade marks affixed to them th at are sold by the respondent to <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	There can therefore be no doubt that the wholesale price at which the goods with the trade marks affixed to them are sold by the respondent to <ORG> as stipulted under the agreements would be the value of the goods for the purpose of excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	That is the price at which the respondent sells the goods to <ORG> in the course of wholesale trade and we fail to see how the value of the trade marks could be added to the wholesale price for the purpose of determining the value of the goods for the purpose of levy of excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are satisfied upon the particular facts of this case that the value of <ORG> trade marks cannot be added to the wholesale price charged by the respondent to <ORG> for the purpose of computing the value of the goods manufactured by the respondent in the assessment to excise duty.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeals are dismissed with costs.
