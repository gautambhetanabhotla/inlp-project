Statute	'Factory' is defined in Section 2(a) of the Act as follows:  "Factory" means any permises, including the precints thereof wherein or in any part of which excisable goods other than salt are manufactured of wherein or in any part of which any manufacturing process connected with the production of there good is being carried on or is ordinarily carried on."
Statute	The term "excisable goods" is denned in Section 2(d) of the Act as the following:  "Excisable goods" means goods specified in the First Schedule as being subject to a duty of excise and includes salt."
Argument	6.
Argument	It is the contention of the learned Counsel for the petitioner-society that on the exemption of cotton fabrics by Notification No.
Argument	47, dated 24.4.1962, the goods ceased to be "Excisable goods" within, the meaning of the term in Section 2(d) , According to the learned Counsel, in order to be satisfied in the First Schedule, th ey should also IK subject to a duty of excise.
Argument	He also contends that any other construction will make the words "as being subject to a duty of excise "redundant,, and it would have been enough to define excisable goods specified in the First Schedule.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words, specification of the goods as an Item in the First Schedule as also subjecting them to a duty of excise are to be satisfied as cumulative conditions in order to bring the goods within the definition of "excisable goods".
RatioOfTheDecision	The Learned Counsel relies upon the Notification exempting goods from the levy of excise duty as amounting to taking away the goods from the category of "excisable goods".
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are unable to agree with the contention of the learned Counsel.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are of the view that the words "as being subject to a duty of Excise" in the definition of the term "excisable goods" are only descriptive of the goods specified in the First Schedule, and have no reference to the factum of their Liability to duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	In fact, it is seen that some of the rat e of duty as 'NIL'.
RatioOfTheDecision	It could not be contended that these goods are not 'excisable goods'.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this connection, we may also refer to Section 3 , which is the charging section, which provides for the levy and collection of excise duty on all excisable goods.
RatioOfTheDecision	Produced or manufactured in India at the rate set forth Schedule, thereby showing that excisable goods in the, definition in the section refer only to the description of the goods in column (2) of the First Schedule, and not to the rate of duty in column (3) of that Schedule.
RatioOfTheDecision	The exemption granted by the Notification of the Central Government made on 24.4.1962. only exempts handloom fabrics from the levy of excisable duty and it does not change the nature and character of the goods as excisable goods within the meaning the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Notification proceeds on the assumption that the handloom fabrices are excisable goods.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the handloom fabrice are not excisables goods, there was no need or occasion for exempting the same from the levy of excise duty.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are un able to agree with the learned Counsel for the petitioner that once the handloom fabrics are exempted from excise duty, they cease to be excisable goods.
RatioOfTheDecision	As we have already pointed out, the character as excisable goods does not depend on the actual levy of duty, but on the description as excisable goods in the First Schedule to the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are, therefore, unable to interefere with the orders of the respondents.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
