RatioOfTheDecision	For example, in this very case, the <ORG> made some changes in the time frame origi-  242  nally prescribed.
RatioOfTheDecision	These changes affected all intending applicants alike and were not objectionable.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the same way, changes or relaxations in other directions would be unobjectionable unless the benefit of those changes or relaxations were extended to some but denied to others.
RatioOfTheDecision	The fact that a document was belatedly entertained from one of the applicants will cause substantial prejudice to another party who wanted, likewise, an extension of time for filing a similar certificate or document but was declined the benefit.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may perhaps be said to cause prejudice also to a party which can show that it had refrained from applying for the tender documents only because it thought it would not be able to produce the document by the time stipulated but would have applied had it kno wn that the rule was likely to be relaxed.
RatioOfTheDecision	But neither of these situations is present here.
RatioOfTheDecision	Sri <NAME> says that in this case one of the appli- cants was excluded at the preliminary stage.
RatioOfTheDecision	But it is not known on what grounds that application was rejected nor has that party come to Court with any such grievance.
RatioOfTheDecision	The ques- tion, then, is whether the course adopted by the <ORG> has caused any real prejudice to the appellant and other parties who had already supplied all the documents in time and sought no extension at all?
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that the relaxations of the time schedule in the case of one party does affect even such a person in the sense that he would otherwise have had one competitor less.
RatioOfTheDecision	But, we are inclined to agree with the respondent's contention that while the rule in <NAME>'s case (supra) will be readily applied by <ORG> to a case where a person complains that a departure from the qualifi- cations has kept him out of the race, injustice is less apparent where the attempt of the applicant be fore Court is only to gain immunity from competition.
RatioOfTheDecision	Assuming for pur- poses of argument that there has been a slight deviation from the terms of the <ORG>, it has not deprived the appellant of its right to be considered for the contract; on the other hand, its tender has received due and full consideration.
RatioOfTheDecision	If, save for the delay in filing one of the relevant docu- ments, <ORG> is also found to be qualified to tender for the contract, no injustice can be said to have been done to the appellant by the consideration of its tender side by side with that of the <ORG> and in the <ORG> going in for a choice of the better on the merits.
RatioOfTheDecision	The appellant had no doubt also urged that the <ORG> had no experience in this line of work and that the appellant was much better quali- fied for the contract.
RatioOfTheDecision	The comparative merits of the appel- lant vis-a-vis <ORG> are, however, a matter for the <ORG> (counselled by the <ORG>) to decide and not for the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	We were, therefore, rightly not called upon to go into this que stion.
