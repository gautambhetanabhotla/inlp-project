Precedent	19.

Precedent	20.

Precedent	21.
Precedent	The post of Principal whether it is of Class II or Class I is a post of higher responsibilities.
Precedent	The administrative qualities are mixed and blended with the academic achievements of the candidates and, therefore, it has been specifically provided in the Rules, specially the Schedule appended thereto, that the candidates, whether they are to be appointed by direct recruitment or by promotion, must possess a Degree or Diploma in Engineering.
Precedent	22.
Argument	Learned counsel for the respondent contended that since he had already been promoted to the post of principal, Class II and was also placed on the post of principal, Class I before the promulgation of the Rules, his promotion as Principal cannot be disturbed.
Argument	It is contended  that the Rules would apply to a situation where the post was lying vacant and was intended to be filled up after the promulgation of the Rules.
Argument	It is also contended that having worked on the post of Principal since 1976, he cannot be shifted to the post of Vice-Principal after such a long period of dedicated service on the higher post.
RatioOfTheDecision	These contentions are devoid of merit.
RatioOfTheDecision	23.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not disputed that the respondent was promoted to the post of Principal, Class II for a short period of six months or till the availability of candidates duly selected by the commission, whichever was earlier.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is also not disputed and the <ORG> itself has found it as a fact that the respondent was placed on the post of principal only in an ad hoc capacity.
RatioOfTheDecision	Consequently, the post, having not been filled up on a regular basis in accordance with the Rules, was rightly treated by the appellant to be vacant.
RatioOfTheDecision	That being so, the respondent had only ad hoc status which he would continue to hold till it was altered by the appointin g authority.
RatioOfTheDecision	24.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> service is essentially a matter of status rather than a contract.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> of this Court in <NAME> Vs. Union of India (1968) 1 SCR 185 had observed as under:  " It is true that the origin of <ORG> service is contractual.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is an offer and acceptance in every case.
RatioOfTheDecision	But once appointed to his post or office the <ORG> servant acquires a status and his rights and obligations are no longer determined by consent of both parties, but by statute or statutory rules which may be framed and altered unilaterally by the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words, the legal position of a <ORG> servant is more one of status than of contract.
RatioOfTheDecision	The hall-mark of status is the and duties imposed by the public law and not by mere emolument of the <ORG> servant and his terms of service are governed by statute or statutory rules which may be unilaterally altered by the <ORG> without the consent of the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that Art, 311 imposes constitutional restoratio ns upon the power of removal granted to the President and the Governor under Art, 310.
RatioOfTheDecision	But it is obvious that the relationship between the Government and its servant is not like an ordinary contract of service between a master and servant.
RatioOfTheDecision	The legal relationship is something entirely different, something in the nature off status.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is much more than a purely contractual relationship voluntarily entered into between the parties.
RatioOfTheDecision	The duties of status are fixed by the law and in the enforcement of these duties society has an interest.
Precedent	In the language of jurisprudence status is a condition of membership of a group of which powers and duties are exclusively determined by law and not by agreement between the parties concerned.
Precedent	25.
Precedent	These observations were quoted with approval by <ORG> in <CITATION_1134697> , AIR 1985 SC 1416 = 1985 (3) SCC 398 = (1985) Suppl 2 SCR 131.
Precedent	A 9- judge Bench in <CITATION_268805> .
Precedent	AIR 1991 SC 101 = 1991 (Supp.1) SCC 600 = (1990) Supp.
RatioOfTheDecision	1 SCR 142 also approved the pr inciples laid down in <NAME> case (supra) that the legal relationship between the <ORG> and its servants is something entirely different.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is much more than a purely contractual relationship and is in the nature of 'status'.
RatioOfTheDecision	26.
RatioOfTheDecision	Whether a persons holds a particular pos in a substantive capacity or is only temporary or ad hoc is a question which directly relates to his status .
RatioOfTheDecision	It all depends upon the terms of appointment.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not open to any <ORG> employee to claim automatic alteration of status unless that result is specifically envisaged by some provision in the statutory rules.
RatioOfTheDecision	Unless, therefore, there is a provision in the statutory rules for alteration of status in a particular situation, it is not open to any <ORG> employee to claim a status different than that which was conferred upon him at the initial or any subsequent stage of service.
RatioOfTheDecision	27.
RatioOfTheDecision	Applying these principles to the instant case, since the respondent, admittedly, was appointed in an ad hoc capacity, he would c ontinue to hold the post in question in that capacity.
RatioOfTheDecision	On the promulgation of Rules, therefore, the post of Principal which he was holding could not be treated to have been filled up on regular basis and had to be treated as vacant.
Precedent	In order to make regular appointment by promotion on that post, the eligible candidates were considered and the respondent, not possessing the required educational qualification, was not found fit or suitable for the post of principal and was consequently directed to the appointed on regular basis as Vice-principal as he was found suitable only for that post principally for the reason that he did not possess a Degree or Diploma in Engineering.
Precedent	28.
Argument	It is next contended by the learned counsel for the respondent that although the respondent does not possess a Degree or Diploma in Engineering, he has been working on the post of principal for a long time and since he has acquired sufficient experience on the post he need not, in the circumstances, be disturbed by reverting him as Vice  Principal.
Precedent	This plea is also without merits.
Precedent	29.
Precedent	Rule 8(2), which provides for Educational qualification, is in mandatory terms and it is mentioned therein that "the candidates must possess the educational qualification prescribed for the service as shown in Schedule III."
Precedent	In column 2 of Schedule III, the posts of Principal Class I and principal Class II are mentioned and in column 5 thereof, it is again mentioned in mandatory terms that "the candidates must possess a Degree in Engineering from any recognised <ORG> or must possess a Diploma in engineering from any recognised <ORG> or <ORG> along with five years' experience of working in any Training Institute or in any reputed business concern."
Precedent	30.
Precedent	We have already held above that educational qualifications mentioned in column 5 of Schedule III for the post of Principal class I or Principal class II are also applicable to appointments by promotion and that the applicability of column 5 of Schedule III is not restricted to direct appointments.
Precedent	In  this situation, therefore, before a person is eligible for being promoted to the post of Principle class II or Principal Class I, he must possess a Degree or Diploma in Engineering.
Precedent	31.
RatioOfTheDecision	The plea that the <ORG> should have a "human approach" and should not disturb a person who has already been working on this post for more than a decade also cannot be accepted as the <ORG>s are hardly swayed by emotional appeals.
RatioOfTheDecision	In dispensing justice to the litigating parties, the courts not only go into the merits of the respective cases, they also try to balance the equities so as to do complete justice between them.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus the courts always maintain a human approach.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the instant case also, this approach has not been departed from.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are fully conscious that the respondent had worked on the post in question for quite a long time but it was only in ad hoc capacity.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are equally conscious that a selected candidate who also possesses necessary educational qualification is available.
Precedent	In this situation, if the respondent is  allowed to continue on this post merely on the basis of his concept of "human approach", it would be at the cost of a duly selected candidate who would be deprived of cleared the selection.
Precedent	In fact, it is the "human approach" which requires us to prefer the selected candidate over a person who does not possess even the requisite qualification.
Precedent	The <ORG> as also the <ORG> have no power to override the mandatory provisions of the Rules on sympathetic consideration that a person, though not possessing the essential educational qualifications.
Precedent	should be allowed to continue on the post merely on the basis of his experience.
Precedent	Such an order would amount to altering or amending the Statutory provisions made by the <ORG> under Article 309 of the Constitution.
Precedent	32. "
Precedent	Experience" gained by the respondent on account of his working on the post in question for over a decade cannot be equated with Educational Qualifications required to be possessed by a candidate as a condition of eligibility for promotion to highe r posts.
Precedent	If the <ORG>, in exercise of its executive power, has created certain posts, it is for it to prescribe the mode of appointment or the qualifications which have to be possessed by the candidates before they are appointed on those posts.
Precedent	The qualifications would naturally vary with the nature of posts or the service created by the <ORG>.
Precedent	33.
RatioOfTheDecision	The post in question is the post of Principal of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> has prescribed a Degree or Diploma in Engineering as the essential qualification of this post.
RatioOfTheDecision	No one who does not possess this qualification can be appointed on this post.
RatioOfTheDecision	The educational qualification has direct nexus with the nature of the post.
RatioOfTheDecision	The principal may also have an occasion to take classes and teach the students.
Precedent	A person who does not hold either a Degree or Diploma in Engineering cannot possibly teach the students of <ORG> the technicalities of the subject of Engineering and its various branches.
Precedent	34.
Precedent	The respondent ha ving worked in an ad hoc capacity on the post of principal might have gained some administrative experience but the same cannot be treated as equivalent to his knowledge in the field of Engineering.
Precedent	A compounder, sitting for a considerably long time with a Doctor practicing in Modern medicine, may have gained some experience by observing the medicine prescribed by the Doctor for various diseases or ailments but that does not mean that he, by that process, acquires knowledge of the Human Anatomy or Physiology or the principles of pharmacology or the field of action of any particular medicine or its side effects.
Precedent	The Compounder cannot, merely on the basis of experience, claim a post meant exclusively for persons having MBBS or other higher degrees in medicine or surgery.
Precedent	The plea of experience, therefore, must fail.
Precedent	Moreover, this would amount to a relaxation of Rule relating to educational qualification.
Precedent	Power to relax the Rule vests exclusively in the Governor as provided by Rule 21.
Precedent	This power cannot be usur ped by the <ORG> or the <ORG>.
Precedent	35.
