RatioOfTheDecision	To this preliminary objection there is to my mind a very short answer for the purposes of this reference.
RatioOfTheDecision	The scheme of Section 66 of the Income-tax Act is that under Sub-section (1) either the assessee or the Commissioner may apply to the <ORG> to refer to <ORG> any question of law arising out of such order and the <ORG> may draw up a statement of the case and refer it to t he <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	If they do so, and they have done so in the present reference, then it is the duty of the <ORG> under Sub-section (5) thereof to decide the question of law raised by the case stated.
RatioOfTheDecision	The section does not confer power either on the assessee or the Commissioner to go to <ORG> and urge that the case should not have been stated or no question of law should have been raised.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is only if the <ORG> takes the view that no question of law arises and refuses to state a case that either party is entitled to apply to <ORG> under Sub-section (2) for an order requiring the <ORG> to state a case.
RatioOfTheDecision	That is not the position before us.
RatioOfTheDecision	Since the <ORG> has, although the question was not argued before it, raised the question of law and deferred it to us, we are bound to determine it under Sub-section (5).

RatioOfTheDecision	These words would, in my opinion, fall to be determined only when an application under Section 66(2) is made requiring the <ORG> to state a case.
RatioOfTheDecision	I, therefore, refrain from expressing any opinion on the correct interpretation to be placed on these words.
