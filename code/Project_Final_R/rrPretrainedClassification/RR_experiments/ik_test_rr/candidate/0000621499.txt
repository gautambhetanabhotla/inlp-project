RatioOfTheDecision	14.
RatioOfTheDecision	The scheme and the provisions of the Act clearly indicate that the person who receives income by way of salary is primarily liable to pay the tax, though, generally speaking, the employer, that is, the person responsible for paying the salary, has a duty to collect the tax on the estimated income and pay over the same to the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Where the employer fails to do so, he is deemed to be an assessee in default with certain consequences.
RatioOfTheDecision	The amount of tax so in default is to be recovered from him.
RatioOfTheDecision	The scheme contained in Sections 192 , 201 and the connected sections is only a mode of recovery of tax on the estimated income at the time of payment of salary to the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	The obvious purpose of the scheme is to facilitate recovery of tax under the Act .
RatioOfTheDecision	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	Nevertheless, income-tax is a tax on the income derived by the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is a charge on the income.
RatioOfTheDecision	The liability of the person who derives income subsists in spite of the liability cast on the employer to deduct tax at source and pay over the same to the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	What is to be deducted at source and paid over to the <ORG> is tax computed on the estimated income.
RatioOfTheDecision	Income estimated may or may not, ultimately, in the assessment to be made in regard to the employee, be found to be correct.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is for the employee to submit an annual return showing his total income for the purpose of taxation and the <ORG> has a duty to complete the assessment, with statutory rights provided in the Act to the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	The regular assessment may be preceded by a provisional assessment.
RatioOfTheDecision	In either event, the tax deducted at the source and paid over to the <ORG> is to be given credit to in the tax liability fixed on the employee for the assessment year.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the tax deducted at source and paid over to the <ORG> is f ound to be in excess, that has to be refunded to the person on whose behalf it was deducted, viz., the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	If any excess is found due, the liability is on the employee to pay the same.
RatioOfTheDecision	16.
RatioOfTheDecision	At the same time, we notice that the failure to make deduction or pay over the same to the <ORG> is also visited with certain consequences.
RatioOfTheDecision	17.
RatioOfTheDecision	As we have pointed out, the provisions regarding deduction of tax at source and payment of tax so deducted to the <ORG> lay down only a mode of recovering tax due from the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words, the duty on the employer is not an end in itself.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is only a means to an end, viz., recovery of tax payable by the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	Tax paid over to the <ORG> after deduction by the employer is for and on behalf of the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is subject to the ultimate assessment to be made on the employee and the tax so deducted and paid is to go in adjustment of the employee's liability.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words, the liability of the employer to make deduction at source and pay over the tax to  the <ORG> is not independent of the liability of the employee to pay tax.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is dependent entirely on the liability of the employee to pay tax.
RatioOfTheDecision	If, on the estimated income of the employee, no tax is due, the employer has no liability to deduct tax at the source.
RatioOfTheDecision	The liability of the employer and the employee is interconnected and not independent of each other.
RatioOfTheDecision	Where the assessment in relation to an employee has been completed and has become final and no further tax is found due from the employee, that puts an end to the liability of the employer.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thereafter, it cannot be said that the liability of the employer survives.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are here dealing with a case where the assessment on the employee has become final.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is admitted that it cannot be reopened under any of the provisions of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The present discussion is confined to such cases.
RatioOfTheDecision	18.
RatioOfTheDecision	Any other view would lead to absurdity.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the view is taken that irrespective of the finality (followed by non-reopenability) attached to the assessment completed with  reference to the employee, the employer can be deemed to be an assessee in default, it would follow that the employer is liable to pay the deficit to the <ORG> and that too with simple interest at 15 per cent.
RatioOfTheDecision	per annum from the date on which tax was deductible.
RatioOfTheDecision	Had the liability of the employee to pay the alleged deficit been alive, he would be liable to pay the deficit with simple interest at 15 per cent.
RatioOfTheDecision	per annum not from the date on which the tax was deductible but only from the day commencing after the end of the period mentioned in Sub-section (1)of Section 220 , that is, the expiry of 35 days of the service of demand notice under Section 156 , which notice could be served only after completing the assessment.
RatioOfTheDecision	This would lead to absurd results.
RatioOfTheDecision	19.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the view is taken that the employer is deemed to be an assessee in default even though the assessment on the employee has become final, it would naturally follow that the employer would be under a duty to pay over the deficit to the <ORG>, fail ing which the <ORG> would be entitled to recover the same in the manner provided in the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Assuming that the deficit tax is so collected from the employer by the <ORG>, the amount so collected would go to the credit of the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	But the assessment made on the employee showing that no further tax was due from him having become final, the employee has no liability in regard to any further amount.
RatioOfTheDecision	Naturally, the alleged deficit amount collected from the employer must be refunded to the employee.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not think that the provisions of the Act could be interpreted in such a manner as to lead to such patently absurd consequences.
RatioOfTheDecision	20.
RatioOfTheDecision	We, therefore, hold that the employer cannot be deemed to be an assessee in default once the assessment on the employee is complete and cannot be reopened in accordance with any of the provisions of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the act of the Income-tax Officer in the instant case treating the employer as an assessee in default is invalid and without jurisdiction.
RatioOfTheDecision	21.
RatioOfTheDecision	Learned cou nsel for the <ORG> placed reliance on the decision of <ORG> in <CITATION_596612>  33 ITR 245.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not think that either the ratio of, or the observations in, the judgment have any impact on the question we have considered in this case.
