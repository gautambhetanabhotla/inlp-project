RatioOfTheDecision	It may be stated that <NAME> was himself injured during the course of occurrence and there can be hardly any doubt regarding his presence at the spot.
RatioOfTheDecision	Likewise, the other four witnesses have satisfactorily accounted for their presence at or about the place of occurrence.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
Fact	Mr. <NAME> has referred to the fact that injuries were found on the persons of the two of the accused, namely, <NAME> and <NAME>.
Fact	It is stated that these injuries have not been satisfactorily explained by the prosecution witn esses.
RatioOfTheDecision	No question, however, appears to have been put to any of the prosecution witnesses regarding the injuries caused to <NAME> and <NAME> accused.
RatioOfTheDecision	As such, there arose no occasion for the prosecution witnesses to explain the injuries on the persons of the two of the accused.
RatioOfTheDecision	Apart from that, we find that a cross case was started against the prosecution witnesses in this case regarding the injuries to <NAME> and <NAME> accused.
RatioOfTheDecision	The accused in the cross case, we have been informed by Mr. <NAME>, were acquitted on appeal by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It appears that the witnesses refrained from referring to the injuries on the persons of <NAME> and <NAME> because of fear of being implicated in the cross case.
RatioOfTheDecision	Looking to the circumstances of the case, we are of the opinion that the non-mention of the injuries on the persons of the two accused in the prosecution evidence would not justify, interference with the appraisement of the evidence by the trial court and <ORG> t.  11.
