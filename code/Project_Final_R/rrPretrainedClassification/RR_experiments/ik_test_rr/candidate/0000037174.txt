Precedent	35.One of the proven methods of examining the legislative competence of a legislature with regard to an enactment is by the application of the doctrine of pith and substance.
Precedent	This doctrine is applied when the legislative competence of the legislature with regard to a particular enactment is challenged with reference to the entries in various lists.
Precedent	If there is a challenge to the legislative competence, the courts will try to ascertain the pith and substance of such enactment on a scrutiny of the Act in question.
Precedent	In this process, it is necessary for the courts to go into and examine the true character of the enactment, its object, its scope and effect to find out whether the enactment in question is genuinely referable to a field of the legislation allotted to the respective legislature under the constitutional scheme.
Precedent	This doctrine is an established principle of law in India recognized  not only by this <ORG>, but also by various High <ORG>s.
Precedent	Where a challenge is made to the constitutional validity of a particular State Act with reference to a subject mentioned in any entry in List I, the <ORG> has to look to the substance of the State Act and on such analysis and examination, if it is found that in the pith and substance, it falls under an entry in the State List but there is only an incidental encroachment on any of the matters enumerated in the Union List, the State Act would not become invalid merely because there is incidental encroachment on any of the matters in the Union List.
Precedent	36.A Constitution Bench of this <ORG> in <CITATION_1057797> [AIR 1957 SC 297], held as under:  "8.
Precedent	...
Precedent	But then, it must be remembered that we are construing a federal Constitution.
Precedent	It is of the essence of such a Constitution that there should be a distribution of the legislative powers of the Federation between the <ORG> and the Provinces.
Precedent	The scheme of distribution has varied with different Constitutions,  but even when the Constitution enumerates elaborately the topics on which the Centre and the States could legislate, some overlapping of the fields of legislation is inevitable.
Precedent	The British North America Act, 1867, which established a federal Constitution for Canada, enumerated in Sections 91 and 92 the topics on which the Dominion and the Provinces could respectively legislate.
Precedent	Notwithstanding that the lists were framed so as to be fairly full and comprehensive, it was not long before it was found that the topics enumerated in the two sections overlapped, and <ORG> had time and again to pass on the constitutionality of laws made by the Dominion and Provincial Legislatures.
Precedent	It was in this situation that <ORG> evolved the doctrine, that for deciding whether an impugned legislation was intra vires, regard must be had to its pith and substance.
Precedent	That is to say, if a statute is found in substance to relate to a topic within the competence of the legislature, it should be held to be intra vi res, even though it might incidentally trench on topics not within its legislative competence.
Precedent	The extent of the encroachment on matters beyond its competence may be an element in determining whether the legislation is colourable, that is, whether in the guise of making a law on a matter within it competence, the legislature is, in truth, making a law on a subject beyond its competence.

Precedent	To examine whether a legislation has impinged on the field of other legislatures, in fact or in substance, or is incidental, keeping in view the true nature of the enactment, the courts have evolved the doctrine of `pith and substance' for the purpose of determining whether it is legislation with respect to matters in one list or the other.
Precedent	Where the question for determination is whether a particular law relates to a particular subject mentioned in one l ist or the other, the courts look into the substance of the enactment.
Precedent	Thus, if the substance of the enactment falls within the Union List then the incidental encroachment by the enactment on the State List would not make it invalid.
Precedent	This principle came to be established by <ORG> when it determined appeals from Canada or Australia involving the question of legislative competence of the federation or the States in those countries.
Precedent	This doctrine came to be established in India and derives its genesis from the approach adopted by the courts including <ORG> in dealing with controversies arising in other federations.
Precedent	For applying the principle of `pith and substance' regard is to be had (i) to the enactment as a whole, (ii) to its main objects, and (iii) to the scope and effect of its provisions.

RatioOfTheDecision	60.A perusal of the Preamble, the Statement of Objects and Reasons and the Interpretation clauses of the <ORG> and the <ORG> would show that both the acts operate in different fields and the ambit and scope of each is distinct from the other.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as the <ORG> is concerned, it principally deals with prevention and control of criminal activity by organised crime syndicate or gang within India and its purpose is to curb a wide range of criminal activities indulged in by organised syndicate or gang.
RatioOfTheDecision	The aim of the <ORG>, on the other hand, is to deal with terrorist and certain unlawful activities, which are committe d with the intent to threaten the unity, integrity, security or sovereignty of India or with the intent to strike terror in the people or any section of the people in India or in any foreign country or relate to cessation or secession of the territory of India.
RatioOfTheDecision	61.Under the <ORG> the emphasis is on crime and pecuniary benefits arising therefrom.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the wisdom of the legislature these are activities which are committed with the objective of gaining pecuniary benefits or economic advantages and which over a period of time have extended to promoting insurgency.
RatioOfTheDecision	The concept of the offence of `terrorist act' under section 15 of the UAPA essentially postulates a threat or likely threat to unity, integrity, security and sovereignty of India or striking terror amongst people in India or in foreign country or to compel <ORG> or the Government of a foreign country or any other person to do or abstain from doing any act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The offence of terrorist act under Section 15 and the offence of Unlawful activ ity under Section 2 (1)  (o) of the <ORG> have some elements in commonality.
RatioOfTheDecision	The essential element in both is the challenge or threat or likely threat to the sovereignty, security, integrity and unity of India.
RatioOfTheDecision	While Section 15 requires some physical act like use of bombs and other weapons etc., Section 2 (1)(o) takes in its compass even a written or spoken words or any other visible representation intended or which supports a challenge to the unity, sovereignty, integrity and security of India.
RatioOfTheDecision	The said offences are related to the Defence of India and are covered by Entry 1 of the Union List.
RatioOfTheDecision	62.Moreover, the meaning of the term ` Unlawful Act ivity' in the <ORG> is altogether different from the meaning of the term ` Unlawful Act ivity' in the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is also pertinent to note that the <ORG> does not deal with the terrorist organisations which indulge in terrorist activities and similarly, the <ORG> does not deal with organised gangs or crime syndicate of the kind specifically targeted by the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, th e offence of organised crime under the <ORG> and the offence of terrorist act under the <ORG> operate in different fields and are of different kinds and their essential contents and ingredients are altogether different.
RatioOfTheDecision	63.The concept of insurgency under Section 2(1) (e) of the <ORG>, if seen and understood in the context of the Act, is a grave disturbance of the public order within the state.
RatioOfTheDecision	The disturbance of the public order, in each and every case, cannot be said to be identical or similar to the concepts of terrorist activity as contemplated respectively under Section 2(1)(o) and Section 15 of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Moreover, what is punishable under the <ORG> is promoting insurgency and not insurgency per se.
RatioOfTheDecision	64.The aforesaid analysis relating to the essential elements of offence of `promoting insurgency' under Section 2 (1) (e) of the <ORG> and the offence of terrorist act and unlawful activity under Section 15 and Section 2 (1)(o) of the <ORG> respectively, clearly establishes that the <ORG> occupies a field diffe rent than that occupied by the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is no clear and direct inconsistency or conflict between the said provisions of the two Acts.
RatioOfTheDecision	65.We therefore, for the reasons mentioned above, concur with the final decision reached by <ORG> in the impugned judgment and repel the challenge unhesitatingly.
RulingByPresentCourt	66.The appeals accordingly fail and are dismissed.
