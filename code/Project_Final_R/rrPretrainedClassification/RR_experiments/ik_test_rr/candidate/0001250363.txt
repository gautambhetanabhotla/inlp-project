Fact	JUDGMENT  1.
Fact	This appeal arises out of an application made under Order XXXIV, Rule 5, of the Code of Civil Procedure, for a final decree in a suit for sale upon a mortgage.
Fact	The preliminary decree under Order XXXIV, Rule 4, was made on the 27th of February 1909.
RulingByLowerCourt	That decree allowed a period of six months to judgment-debtor to pay the amount of the decree, and that period expired on the 26th of August 1909.
RulingByLowerCourt	Meanwhille the judgment-debtor appealed, with the result that the decree of the <ORG> of first instance was affirmed by the lower Appellate <ORG> on the 25th of January 1911, and on second appeal, by the High <ORG> on the 25th of January 1912.
Fact	Neither the first Appellate <ORG> nor this <ORG> extended time, for payment of the mortgage-money.
Fact	The present application was made on the 25th of April 1913.
Fact	It was contended on behalf of the judgment-debtors, that is the mortgagors, that the application was beyond time.
Fact	This contention was allowed by the <ORG> of first instance and the decision of that <ORG> was affi rmed by the lower <ORG>.
Argument	The decree-holders have preferred this appeal and it is urged on their behalf that limitation should be computed either from the date on which the decree of the <ORG> of first instance was affirmed by the lower <ORG> or when the decree of this <ORG> was made.
Argument	In order to consider whether the application is barred by limitation or not it is first, of all necessary to determine what Article of the first Schedule of the Limitation Act is applicable to the present case.
Argument	It is clear that Article 182 does not apply and there being no other Article which is applicable, the only Article which can be applied is Article 181.
Argument	Rule 5 of Order XXXIV provides that where payment is not made within the time fixed, the <ORG> shall on application made in that behalf by the plaintiff pass the final decree for the sale of the mortgaged property or a sufficient part thereof.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, it is necessary that an application should be made by the plaintiff in order to obtain a final decree  under that Order.
RatioOfTheDecision	The application is thus an application in the suit and since the passing of the present Code of Civil Procedure it can no longer be said to be an application in execution or for the execution of a decree.

Fact	The next question to be considered is, when did the right to apply accrue as provided in the 3rd column of that Article?
RatioOfTheDecision	There can be no doubt that after the expiry of the six months allowed by the decree of the <ORG> of first instance, the decree-holders-plaintiffs became entitled to apply for final decree.
RatioOfTheDecision	The mere fact that an appeal was preferred from the preliminary decree did not take away that right or postpone it.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is conceded by the learned <NAME> for the appellants, but he urges that he also acquired the right to apply when the decrees of <ORG>, namely, that of the first <ORG> of Appeal and of <ORG> were passed.
RatioOfTheDecision	It seems to us that limitation should be computed from the time when the right to apply first accrued, That right accrued, as we have said above, when the six months granted by the <ORG> of first instance to the judgment-debtors expired.
RatioOfTheDecision	The passing of the subsequent decrees by <ORG> only affirmed that right and did not give ri se to a fresh right, unless the decree of the <ORG> of first instance was in any respect varied by the Appellate <ORG>s.
Precedent	We think that the analogy of the decision of the majority of <ORG> in <CITATION_1114727> 28 Ind.

Precedent	That was a case in which the question was, whether the money sought to be recovered became due under Article 132 of the first Schedule when default was first made in the payment of instalments.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was held that the money became due when the first default was made.
RatioOfTheDecision	On the same principle limitation must be computed, in a case like the present, from the time when the plaintiffs' right to make an application for a final decree first accrued.
RatioOfTheDecision	Admittedly the right first accrued in this case on the 26th of August 1909, and more than three years having expired from that date when the present application was made, it is beyond time.
RulingByPresentCourt	We accordingly dismiss the appeal with costs, including fees on the higher scale.
