Fact	ORDER PATANJALI SASTRI, J.
Fact	- This is a petition under Section 66 (2) of the Indian Income-tax Act praying that this Court should direct <ORG> to refer the following questions to this Court for decision :-  "(1) Where it was found that the assessment year 1942-43 the business of <NAME>, was the sole business of <NAME>, had the Income-tax Officer jurisdiction to give an inconsistent and different finding on the same materials and evidence that for the succeeding assessment year 1943-44 the same business belonged to a firm of <NAME> and <NAME>.
Fact	(2) Whether the finding given by the Income-tax Officer regarding the ownership of the business of <NAME> & Co., in the assessment year 1942-43 was not final and conclusive so as to preclude the Income-tax Officer from coming to a different finding in the succeeding assessment year in the absence of fresh facts or materials, the previous finding stopping the Income-t ax Officer on grounds of natural justice and equity embodied in the principles of res judicata."
Argument	The <ORG> refused to refer these question on the ground that the only question that was raised and argued before it was the question of fact, namely, whether the business of <NAME>, also belonged to the appellant firm or it was independent concern belonging to one of the partners, <NAME>, and that the questions now sought to be raised before <ORG> were not raised before it at the hearing of the appeal.
Argument	The <ORG> therefore took the view that these question did not arise from its order and accordingly rejected the application for reference made under Section 66 (1) of the Act.
Argument	Thereupon the appellant preferred the present application under Section 66 (2) asking this Court to direct the Appellate <ORG> to state a case and to refer the questions aforesaid.
Argument	Section 66 (1) of the Act provides :-  "Within sixty days of the date upon which he is served with notice of an  order under sub-section (4) of Section 33 the assessee of the Commissioner may, by application in the prescribed form, accompanied where application is made by the assessee by a fee of one hundred rupees, require <ORG> to refer to <ORG> any question of law arising out of such order, and <ORG> shall with ninety days of the receipt of such application draw up a statement of the case and refer it to <ORG>."
Argument	It will be seen that the question of law which <ORG> can be required to refer to <ORG> under this provisions is a "question of law arising out of such order" i.e., order of <ORG> passed on appeal.
Argument	Mr. <NAME> for the petitioner contends that a question, though not raised before <ORG>, can well be said to "arise out of its order," if, on the facts of the case appearing from the order, the question fairly arises.
RatioOfTheDecision	I am unable to agree with that view.
RatioOfTheDecision	I am of opinion that a question of law can  be said to arise out of an order of <ORG> only if such order discloses that the question was raised before the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	This accords with the view taken by <ORG> of the provisions of Section 66 (2) which in this respect used similar language before the section was amended by the amending Act of 1939.
Precedent	See <NAME> v. Commissioner of Income-tax, Punjab.
Precedent	Mr. <NAME> referred us to <CITATION_1500215> , but I do not think that decision has any application here.
Precedent	The question considered by the learned Judges in that case relates to the formulation of questions of law or purposes of reference to <ORG> under Section 66 of the Act.
Precedent	It was held that under that section the assessee is not required to formulate precise question of law, but has merely to require the Commissioner to refer to <ORG> any question of law arising out of an order or a decision.
Precedent	Reference was also made to the <ORG> decision in <ORG> v. <NAME>, where their  Lordships allowed a question of law to be raised before them for the first time.
Precedent	The case furnishes no useful analogy as the scope of the remedy under Section 66 of the Indian Income-tax Act has to be determined with reference to the language of the statute.
Precedent	I am of opinion that <ORG> was right in refusing to refer the questions raised to this <ORG> and this petition must be dismissed with costs, Rs.
