Fact	<NAME>, J.
RulingByLowerCourt	1.
RulingByLowerCourt	The Commissioner of Income-tax noticed that the assessment of the assessee for the year 1968-69 "was erroneous and prejudicial to the interests of the revenue inasmuch as he did not disallow the interest attributable to that part of the capital borrowed by the firm, which was not utilised for the purposes of the firm's business and advanced indirectly to two partners, <NAME> and <NAME>, as investment in the properties".
Fact	The Commissioner of Income-tax issued a notice to the two partners.
Fact	A detailed written statement raising objections was filed before the Commissioner.
RulingByLowerCourt	Without dealing with the objections raised before him, the Commissioner held that the order of the Income-tax Officer was erroneous and prejudicial to the interests of the revenue and directed the Income-tax Officer to reframe the assessment after giving the assessee necessary opportunity to adduce evidence to prove the points raised by the assessee.
Fact	The assessee preferred an appeal to the  Income-tax Appellate <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG> took the view that the Commissioner had failed to indicate the material or the nature of the evidence before him on the basis of which he came to the conclusion that the order of the Income-tax Officer was erroneous and prejudicial to the interests of the revenues.
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG> also observed that the Commissioner had not given any reasons to justify his satisfaction that the order passed by the Income-tax Officer was prejudicial to the interests of the revenue.
RulingByLowerCourt	At the instance of the revenue, the following question has been referred to us for our opinion :  "Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the <ORG> was right in setting aside the order passed by the Commissioner of Income-tax under Section 263 of the Income-tax Act, 1961."
RulingByLowerCourt	2.
RulingByLowerCourt	A perusal of the order of the Commissioner of Income-tax clearly shows that the criticism of the <ORG> is well-founded.
RulingByLowerCourt	There is no indication in the order of the Commissioner as to the basis on which he came  to the prima facie conclusion that the capital borrowed by the firm was utilised for purposes other than that of the firm's business.
RulingByLowerCourt	When the assessee filed a detailed written statement before him, the Commissioner did not deal with any of the points raised in the statement.
RulingByLowerCourt	He thought that the best course in the circumstances was to remand the matter to the Income-tax Officer for consideration of the points raised in the assessee's written statement.
RulingByLowerCourt	That certainly was not the proper course to be adopted by him.
RulingByLowerCourt	It was necessary for the Commissioner to state in what manner he considered that the order of the Income-tax Officer was erroneous and prejudicial to the interests of the revenue and what the basis was for such a conclusion.
RulingByLowerCourt	After indicating his reasons for such a conclusion, it would certainly have been open to him to remand the matter to the Income-tax Officer for such other investigation or enquiry as might be necessary.
RulingByLowerCourt	But that was not the course which the Commissioner pursued.
Argument	The <ORG> wa s, therefore, justified in setting aside the order of the Income-tax Commissioner.
Argument	The learned counsl for the revenue urged that, while setting aside the order of the Commissioner, the <ORG> had purported to restore the order passed by the Income-tax Officer and this meant that the Commissioner was precluded from taking up the matter again.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not want to express any opinion on this question, since our jurisdiction is confined only to answering the question referred to us.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question referred to us is answered in the affirmative.
RulingByPresentCourt	There will be no order as to costs.
