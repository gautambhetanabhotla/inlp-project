RatioOfTheDecision	However, in our opinion, in the peculiar facts of this case, it cannot be said that the assessee had knowledge of its estimate of advance tax to be untrue or had reason to believe the same to be untrue.
RatioOfTheDecision	The assessee had, undoubtedly, claimed the waiving of that interest as a natural corollary of the Resolution dt.
RatioOfTheDecision	30.6.1978.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was also claimed that the assessee had commercial expediency for doing the same.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was tried to show that such commercial expediency arose by the subsequent agreement, whereby, the <ORG> had agreed to provide the security for the amount due from it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Assessee had, therefore, furnished its estimate for the advance tax as Nil, as it claimed that it had the income of only about Rs.800/-.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the subsequent year, the assessee claimed the loss of about Rs.17,000/-.
RatioOfTheDecision	Though the attempt on the part of the assessee was to give up the accrue d interest in the name of commercial expediency, there was no valid justification to relinquish the same, as has been found by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> has also specifically found that the only aim was to avoid payment of tax which had become due on the basis of the accrual of interest and commercial expediency was only a dignified guard in which the arrangement made to evade the tax was sought to be covered.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, it was shown to <ORG> that the penalties levied under Section 273(2)(a) of the Act were determined in case of two companies of the same Group, they being, <ORG> and <ORG>, wherein, it was held that the Resolution passed on 30.06.1978 for the foregoing interest had become applicable from 1.7.1978.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> took the view that the levy of interest under Section 215 of the Act and the levy of penalty under Section 273(2)(a) of the Act stand on different footings.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have no hesitation to accept this view of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Indeed, while the levy of inte rest under Section 215 of the Act is automatic, that is not the case with the penalty under Section 273(2)(a) of the Act, where the mensrea on the part of the assessee would have to be shown to the extent, it has been indicated in the language of the Section, where, therefore, there was some scope for the assessee to justify the estimate given by it and that the penalty could not be inflicted.
RulingByLowerCourt	Indeed, if the assessee in this case proceeded on the basis of Resolution dt.
RulingByLowerCourt	30.6.1978, it has to be held that the assessee had reasonably believed that the income of interest which was written off by the Resolution, could not be added to its income.
RatioOfTheDecision	If it genuinely proceeded under that bonafide impression, then in our opinion, <ORG> was right in writing off the penalty and upsetting the view of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	We accept the finding of <ORG>, which is in the following words:  "....no definite conclusion can be drawn that the assesse had reason to belive that the <NAME> estimate filed by it was untrue....."    26.
RatioOfTheDecision	Considering the overall facts in this case, we are of the clear opinion that <ORG> was right in setting aside the penalty of Rs.4 lakhs inflicted against the assessee under Section 273(2)(a) of the Act.
RulingByPresentCourt	We answer the issue accordingly.
RulingByLowerCourt	In the result, the appeals filed by the <ORG> fail and the judgment of <ORG> is confirmed without any costs.
