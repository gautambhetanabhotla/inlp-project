Precedent	8.
Precedent	The issue before us is whether the expenditure incurred by the assessee for modernisation and replacement came within the connotation of the words "current repairs" in Section 31(i) .
Precedent	9.
Precedent	Before analysing Section 31(i) of the Income-tax Act, we must look at the process of manufacture and the composition of a textile mill in the broad sense.
Precedent	Broadly, a textile mill manufactures different varieties of yarns, namely, Cotton Yarn, Melange Yarn, Colour Melange, Polyester Viscose Yarn and the process of manufacture goes through various Segments/Divisions.
Precedent	The first Segment is <ORG>.
Precedent	The function of <ORG> is to clean the raw cotton thoroughly before it is fed to <ORG>.
Precedent	The function of <ORG> is to remove the waste in the cotton received from <ORG>.
Precedent	In <ORG> there are individual carding machines.
Precedent	They are equipped with Autolevelers to produce silver.
Precedent	The carding machine removes neps formed in the blow room line during the process.
Precedent	The carding machine produces Silver for better quality of yarn.
Precedent	This Silver produced in <ORG> is carried to <ORG>, for manufacturing Combed Yarn.
Precedent	After the carding operation, the impurities present in the Silver will be removed in <ORG>.
Precedent	That silver which is produced in <ORG> will pass through the Draw Frames used in <ORG> in order to obtain parallel fibers.
Precedent	These parallel fibers go through Speed Frames in <ORG> in order to convert the Silver into thinner forms called as Roves which Roves are thereafter sent to <ORG>.
Precedent	In <ORG> we have what is called as the Ring Frames (machines) which are used to spin the Roves received from <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Ring Frames are machines, which are equipped with cleaners, removes the accumulated dirt.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Ring Frames play an important role in producing qu ality yarn.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thereafter, the yarn obtained from <ORG> goes to <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	In <ORG> we have Autoconers in order to produce fault-free yarn.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
RatioOfTheDecision	From the above facts, it is clear that <ORG>, Carding, Combing, Drawing, Roving, Spinning and Winding are different <ORG> in a textile mill.
RatioOfTheDecision	In each <ORG> there are several machines.
RatioOfTheDecision	Each of the above <ORG> perform different functions and the functioning of each <ORG> produces a different Output which is carried forward to the next <ORG> having different machines therein.
RatioOfTheDecision	For example, in the <ORG> there are different beaters (machines) which open the raw- cotton and remove the dirt therefrom.
RatioOfTheDecision	That cotton is forwarded to <ORG> in which there are Carding Machines equipped with Autolevelers which produces Silver which is then carried forward to <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is important to note that each <ORG> has different it ems of machines, for example, in the Blow Room we have machines called as Beaters.
RatioOfTheDecision	Similarly, in <ORG> we have Carding Machines with Autolevelers.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the Autoleveler fails, the Carding Machine becomes non-functional.
RatioOfTheDecision	If an Autoleveler is to be repaired then that repair would come within the connotation of the word "current repairs" because it is a part of the Carding Machine.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even if in a given case, replacement of an Autoleveler could come within the connotation of the word "current repairs" if the old part is not available in the market.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is a "current repair" because the Carding Machine remains as an asset without any change even after repair or replacement of the autoleveler.
RatioOfTheDecision	To give an example, a Compressor is an important part of an Air-condition Machine.
RatioOfTheDecision	Repair of the Compressor will come in the connotation of the word "current repairs" in Section 31(i) of the said Act because the assessee does not replace the Air-condition Machine.
RatioOfTheDecision	At the highest, he replaces a part of the Air-condi tion Machine.
RatioOfTheDecision	So is in the case of the picture tube in a Television Set, when the picture tube is replaced the Television Set is not replaced, therefore, such repairs alone can come within the connotation of the word "current repairs" in Section 31(i) of the said Act as it stood at the material time.
RatioOfTheDecision	They are effected to preserve and maintain the asset, viz, air- conditioner or carding machine.
RatioOfTheDecision	Lastly, it cannot be said that the textile mill constitutes a plant as it is one continuous process of manufacture beginning from Blow Room to the Winding Section.
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, different Outputs flow from different Segments of production like Blow Room, Carding, Combing, Roving, Winding etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the case of a textile mill there is no process whereby raw-material is fed on one end and the finished product comes out at the other end without intervention in-between.
RatioOfTheDecision	For example, in the case of continuous Casting Machine in the Steel Industry we have one continuous integrated process under which scrap (raw material) is  put in and what comes out is steel or iron or aluminium.
RatioOfTheDecision	Another example, in the case of <ORG> we have three chambers and Ducts.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the first milk is collected, in the second it is heated and in the third it is cooled.
RatioOfTheDecision	Duct carries hot and cold water.
Fact	The raw material is Raw Milk, the end product is the pasteurized milk.
Precedent	In the Heat chamber there is the heater.
Precedent	In the Cooling Chamber we have cooling plant which has a concept similar to air-condition plant.
Precedent	Such a process is one integrated process.
Precedent	Therefore, the <ORG> and <ORG> erred in holding that the manufacturing process in the textile mill is one continuous integrated process.
Precedent	11.
Precedent	An allowance is granted by clause (i) of Section 31 in respect of amount expended on current repairs to machinery, plant or furniture used for the purposes of business, irrespective of whether the assessee is the owner of the assets or has only used them.
Precedent	The expression "current repairs" denotes repairs which are attended to when the need for them  arises from the viewpoint of a businessman.
Precedent	The word "repair" involves renewal.
Precedent	However, the words used in Section 31(i) are "current repairs".
Precedent	The object behind Section 31(i) is to preserve and maintain the asset and not to bring in a new asset.
Precedent	In our view, Section 31(i) limits the scope of allowability of expenditure as deduction in respect of repairs made to machinery, plant or furniture by restricting it to the concept of "current repairs".
RatioOfTheDecision	All repairs are not current repairs.
Statute	Section 37(1) allows claims for expenditure which are not of capital nature.
Statute	However, even Section 37(1) excludes those items of expenditure which expressly falls in Sections 30 to 36 .
Statute	The effect is to delimit the scope of allowability of deductions for repairs to the extent provided for in Sections 30 to 36 .
Fact	To decide the applicability of Section 31(i) the test is not whether the expenditure is revenue or capital in nature, which test has been wrongly applied by <ORG>, but whether the expenditure is "current repairs".
RatioOfTheDecision	T he basic test to find out as to what would constitute current repairs is that the expenditure must have been incurred to "preserve and maintain" an already existing asset, and the object of the expenditure must not be to bring a new asset into existence or to obtain a new advantage.
RatioOfTheDecision	In fact, in the present case, in the balance sheet the assessee, viz, <ORG> <ORG> has indicated the above expense as an item incurred for purchase of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, <ORG> had erred in placing reliance on the report of <ORG> in coming to the conclusion that the textile mill is a plant under Section 31(i) .
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, each machine in a segment has an independent role to play in the mill and the output of each division is different from the other "Repair" implies the existence of a part of the machine which has malfunction.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the argument of the assessee herein before us is to be accepted it would result in absurdity and it would make the provisions of Section 31(i) completely redundant.
Argument	Acc ording to <NAME>, learned senior counsel for the assessee, the textile plant consists of about 25 machines.
Precedent	One of such machines is the Ring Frame.
Precedent	If the argument of the assessee is to be accepted, it would mean that periodically one machine out of 25 would be replaced, and on that basis, from time to time, each of these 25 machines in the textile plant would be entitled to claim allowance under Section 31(i) .
Precedent	In our view, the A.O.
Precedent	was right in holding that each machine including the Ring Frame was an independent and separate machine capable of independent and specific function and, therefore, the expenditure incurred for replacement of the new machine would not come within the meaning of the words "current repairs".
Precedent	In the present case, it is not the case of the assessee that a part of the machine (out of 25 machines) needed repairs.
Precedent	The entire machine had been replaced.
Precedent	Therefore, the expenditure incurred by the assessee did not fall within the meaning of "current repairs" in Section 31(i) .
Precedent	12.
Precedent	This Court in the case of <CITATION_429924>  approved the test formulated by <NAME> in the case of <CITATION_734835> (1956) 30 ITR 338 as to when the expenditure can be said to have been incurred on current repairs.
Precedent	In that case it was observed as follows:  "The simple test that must be constantly borne in mind is that as a result of the expenditure which is claimed as an expenditure for repairs what is really being done is to preserve and maintain an already existing asset.
RatioOfTheDecision	The object of the expenditure is not to bring a new asset into existence, nor is its object the obtaining of a new or fresh advantage.
RatioOfTheDecision	This can be the only definition of "repairs" because it is only by reason of this definition of repairs that the expenditure is a revenue expenditure.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the amount spent was for the purpose of bringing into existence a new asset or obtaining a new advantage, then obviously such an expenditure would not be an expenditure of a revenue nature but it would be a capital expenditure, and it is clear  that the deduction which the <ORG> has permitted under section 10(2)(v) is a deduction where the expenditure is a revenue expenditure and not a capital expenditure."
RatioOfTheDecision	In the said judgment, it has been further observed by <NAME> that the definition of the word "repair" does not create much difficulty, but the difficulty is created by the word "current" which qualifies the expression "repair".
RatioOfTheDecision	This adjective, namely, "current" is put in by the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It indicates that the <ORG> did not intend that the assessee should be permitted to claim allowance for all kinds of repairs, even though conceptually the expenditure may be revenue expenditure.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> intended to stress that under Section 31(i) the permissible deduction admissible is only for current repairs, therefore, the question as to whether the expenditure incurred by the assessee conceptually is revenue or capital in nature is not relevant for deciding the question as to whether such an expenditure comes within the etymologi cal meaning of the expression "current repairs".
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words, even if the expenditure is revenue, it may not fall in the connotation of "current repairs" in Section 31(i) .
RatioOfTheDecision	The test formulated above applies to cases where the assessee claims allowance under Section 31(i) .
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case, <ORG> has lost sight of the test to be applied for an expenditure to fall under Section 31(i) as "current repairs".
RatioOfTheDecision	It has embarked on the test which was not applicable, viz, whether the expenditure is revenue or capital in nature.
RatioOfTheDecision	The above test was not relevant during the assessment years in question as the explanation to Section 31(i) was inserted later on.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, applying the test laid down by <ORG> in the case of <ORG> (supra) the assessees were not entitled to claim allowance under Section 31(i) for current repairs.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, the Ring Frame by itself constituted an independent machine with an independent function, which was replaced by a new Rin g Frame giving enduring advantage to the assessee and, therefore, the expenditure incurred in that regard cannot come within the expression "current repairs".
Precedent	In our view, replacement of three Ring Frames constituted substitution of an old asset by a new asset and, therefore, the expenditure incurred did not constitute current repairs.

RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, the said judgment has no application with the facts of the present case.
RatioOfTheDecision	At the outset, we may state that replacement generally may not fall under the expression "current repairs" but, in certain cases, where the old parts were not available in the market or where the old parts had worked for 50 to 60 years, replacement can, in such cases of exception, fall within the expression "current repairs".
RatioOfTheDecision	In <ORG> case (supra) the finding recorded by the <ORG> and the High <ORG> was that old type of replacement parts were not available in the market and, therefore, the expenditure came within the expression "current repairs".
RatioOfTheDecision	That is not the case before us, hence, the said judgment has no application to the fa cts of the present case.
RatioOfTheDecision	Moreover, the judgment of this Court in <ORG> (supra) has not defined the word "asset" to mean the entire production system in the textile mill.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the said judgment, it is nowhere stated that the entire textile mill is one single asset and that it represents one single integrated process.
RatioOfTheDecision	14.
RatioOfTheDecision	Some of the decisions cited on behalf of the assessees are not being discussed by us as they deal with cases falling under Section 37 .
RatioOfTheDecision	That section is a residuary section.
RatioOfTheDecision	Under Section 37 , a particular item of expenditure may be deductible if the expenditure does not fall within Sections 30 to 36 ; that it should have been incurred in the accounting year; that it should be in respect of a business carried on by the assessee; that it should not be on personal account of the assessee; that it should not be in the nature of capital expenditure and that it should be spent wholly and exclusively for business.
RatioOfTheDecision	Whether expenditure is 'revenue' or 'capital in nature' would depend  upon several factors, namely, nature of the expenditure, nature of the business activity etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	For example, construction of the building for self-use may be capital in nature whereas in the hands of the builder a building constitutes his stock-in-trade and, therefore, on the sale of the building the expenditure has to be revenue.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the builder would be entitled to deduct such expenditure from the sale proceeds/gross income.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, whether an expenditure is revenue or capital in nature would depend on the facts of each case.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not wish to express any opinion on the applicability of Section 37(1) in the present case.
RatioOfTheDecision	There were certain civil appeals wrongly tagged with the present batch which will be decided separately by us as they concern with Section 37(1) .
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence we do not wish to express any opinion on applicability of Section 37(1) .
RatioOfTheDecision	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	Before concluding, one aspect needs to be discussed.
Argument	It was submitted on behalf of the assessees, in the present case, that although the assessees had c laimed deduction under Section 31(i) , they should be permitted to claim deduction under Section 37(1) as on facts it has been held by CIT(A), <ORG> and <ORG> that the expenditure was revenue in nature.
RatioOfTheDecision	We find no merit in this contention.
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, even if the expenditure incurred is revenue in nature, still it may not fall in the connotation of the words "current repairs" under Section 31(i) which test has not kept in mind.
RatioOfTheDecision	As held by <NAME> in the case of <ORG> (supra) all repairs do not attract Section 31(i) even though the expenditure is revenue in nature.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the basic test, which had not been applied, in the present case, by CIT(A), <ORG> and <ORG>, is whether the expenditure came within the expression "current repairs".
RatioOfTheDecision	Instead all the three authorities proceeded on the footing that since the expenditure was revenue it constituted "current repairs".
RatioOfTheDecision	It is for this reason that we have interfered with the concurrent finding s given by CIT(A), <ORG> and <ORG>.

RatioOfTheDecision	For the aforestated reasons, we find merit in the above batch of civil appeals filed by the <ORG>.
