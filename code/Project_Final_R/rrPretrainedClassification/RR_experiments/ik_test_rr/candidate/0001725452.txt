Fact	JUDGMENT <NAME>, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	Can a <NAME> maintain a petition for compensation under Section 110-A of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, (hereinafter referred to as the Act) in respect of the death of his son in an automobile accident, when the mother is alive?
Fact	That is the question that arises for determination in this appeal.
Fact	2.
Fact	One <NAME> died as a result of an automobile accident in Davangere City on 16-12-1965.
Fact	He was survived by his mother and his <NAME>.
Fact	On 10-2-1966 the <NAME> (appellant 1) made a petition under S, 110- A of the Act before <ORG>, Chitradurga, (hereinafter referred to as the <ORG>) claiming compensation.
Fact	More than a year later, i.e., on February, 1967, he made an application, I.A.IV, to implead the mother of the deceased as the second petitioner.
Fact	Respondents 1 to 3 herein, namely, the owner of the motor vehicle involved in that accident, <ORG> (wherein that vehicle was insured) and the driver of that vehicle (who were also  respondents 1 to 3 before the <ORG>), did not file any objections to I.A.IV.
Fact	The <ORG> allowed that application on 3-3-1967 and the mother was added as petitioner 2 in the petition.
Fact	3.
Fact	On 20-12-1967, petitioner 2 namely, the mother, made an application praying for condoning the delay in making her claim.
Fact	That application was opposed by the respondents.
Fact	The <ORG> dealt with that application along with the main petition.
Fact	4.
Fact	The <ORG> framed six issues.
Fact	The third issue is whether the death of <NAME> was on account of his own negligence; the first issue is regarding the right of the petitioners to claim compensation; the fifth issue is regarding the maintainability of the petition; and the second issue relates to the quantum of compensation.
Fact	5.
Fact	On the third issue, the <ORG> held that the accident was due to the rash and negligent act of the driver of the motor vehicle.
Argument	6.
Argument	The <ORG> considered issues Nos.
Argument	1 and 5 together and held that when the mother is alive, she alone is entitled to  claim compensation and that the <NAME> is not entitled to do so, that the mother must be regarded as having made the petition for compensation only on the day on which the application, I.A.IV, was filed for being added as a petitioner, that at that time the period of limitation for making such petition had expired, and that the delay in making such petition could not be condoned as she had not <NAME>wn that <NAME> was prevented by sufficient cause from making the petition in time.
Fact	In that view, the <ORG> dismissed the petition and did not consider it necessary to give a finding on the second issue which relates to the quantum of compensation.
Fact	7.
Fact	The petitioners have presented this appeal.
Argument	8.
Argument	Mr. <NAME>, learned counsel for the appellants contended that the view taken by the <ORG> that the <NAME> of the deceased could not maintain a petition for compensation under Section 110-A of the Act, was clearly unsustainable.
Argument	9.
Argument	In order to appreciate the above contention, it is necessary to set out c ertain statutory provisions.
Argument	10.
Statute	Sub-section (1) of Section 110-A of the Act reads:  "110-A (1).
Statute	An application for compensation arising out of an accident of the nature specified in Sub-section (1) of Section 110 may be made:--  (a) by the person who has sustained the injury; or  (b) where death has resulted from the accident, by the legal representatives of the deceased; or  (c) by any agent duly authorised by the person injured or the legal representatives of the deceased, as the case may be.
Statute	11.
Statute	In exercise of the powers conferred by Section 111-A of the Act, <ORG> made rules called 'the Mysore Motor Vehicles Rules, 1963'.
Statute	The title of Chapter VII of these Rules is "Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal Rules".
Statute	12.
Statute	Rule 342 of those Rules contains certain definitions,  13.
Statute	Sub-rule (2) of Rule 342 provides that the expression 'legal representative' shall have the meaning assigned to it under Sub-section (11) of Section 2 , C. P. C.  14.
Statute	The expression 'legal representative' had not been de fined in the Code of Civil Procedure, 1882.
Statute	The definition of that expression was embodied in Section 2(11) of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908.
Statute	The definition reads:  "2 (11).
Statute	'legal representative' means a person who in law represents the estate of a deceased person and includes any person who intermeddles with the estate of the deceased and where a party sues or is sued in a representative character the person on whom the estate devolves on the death of the party suing or sued."
Statute	15.
Statute	Section 8 of the Hindu Succession Act provides, inter alia, that the property of a male Hindu dying intestate shall devolve firstly upon the heirs specified in Class I of the Schedule to that Act; and that if there is no heir of Class I, then upon the heirs specified in Class IT of that Schedule.
Statute	Son, daughter, widow and mother are among the heirs specified in Class I of the Schedule, while <NAME> comes in Class II of that Schedule.
Statute	16.
Statute	The Fatal Accidents Act , 1855, was enacted to provide compensation to families for loss  occasioned by the death of a person caused by actionable wrong.
Statute	17.
Statute	Section 1-A of that Act reads:  "1-A.
Statute	Whenever the death of a person shall be caused by wrongful act, neglect, or default, and the act, neglect or default is such as would (if death had not ensued) have entitled the party injured to maintain an action and recover damages in respect thereof, the party who would have been liable to an action or suit if death had not ensued, shall be liable to an action or suit for damages, notwithstanding the death of the person injured, and although the death shall have been caused under such circumstances as amount in law to felony or other crime.
Statute	Every such action or suit shall be for the benefit of the wife, <NAME>, parent and child, if any, of the person whose death shall have been so caused, and shall be brought by and in the name of the executor administrator or representative of the person deceased;  and in every such action, the court may give such damages as it may think proportioned to the loss re sulting from such death to the parties respectively for whom and for whose benefit such action shall be brought, and the amounts so recovered, after deducting all costs and expenses, including the costs not recovered from the defendant, shall be divided amongst the before mentioned parties, or any of them, in such shares as the court by its judgment or decree shall direct."
Argument	18.
Argument	The <ORG> took the view that as the mother of the deceased was alive, the <NAME> could not be regarded as an heir of the deceased under Section 8 of the Hindu Succession Act, that the <NAME> could not be regarded as a legal representative of the deceased in view of the definition of the expression 'legal representative' as contained in Section 2(11) Civil Procedure Code, which definition has been adopted in Rule 342 of the Mysore Motor Vehicles Rules and that hence petitioner 1 (the <NAME>) had no locus standi to bring a petition under Section 110-A of the Act claiming compensation.
Argument	19.
Argument	In support of his contention.
Precedent	Mr. <NAME> <ORG> relied on the decision of this court in <CITATION_1543835> , 1969 Acc.
Precedent	C.J.
Argument	439 = (AIR 1970 Mys 67).
Argument	There, the question that arose was whether the adjudication of a <NAME>'s claim to compensation for the death of his son by an automobile accident, was taken away from the jurisdiction of <ORG> and placed within the exclusive jurisdiction of <ORG>.
Argument	<NAME>, who spoke for the <ORG>, referred to the provisions of the Fatal Accidents Act 1855, and said that Sections 110 to 110-F of the Act have not created any new right or any new remedy but have only changed the forum for enforcement of the remedy, and that the term 'legal representative' in Section 110-A included the person referred to as representative in Section 1-A of the Fatal Accidents Act, namely, the wife, or <NAME>, parent and child of the deceased.
Argument	His Lordship added that since the <NAME> comes within the meaning of 'parent' he can make his claim before <ORG> and that the jurisdi ction of <ORG> to entertain his suit for such claim, is clearly barred.
Argument	20.
Argument	Referring to Sub-rule (2) of Rule 342 (of the Mysore Motor Vehicles Rules) which adopts the definition of the term 'legal representative' in Section 2(11) Civil Procedure Code.
Precedent	His Lordship said that that term used in Section 110-A of the Act, cannot have the same meaning as that attributed to it in Section 2(11) Civil Procedure Code, and that the right of the representative of a person who died of an automobile accident, to put forward a claim under Section 110-A of the Act," cannot be confined to persons who come within the ambit of the term 'legal representative' as defined in Section 2(11) Civil Procedure Code.
Precedent	In that view.
Precedent	His Lordship held Sub-rule (2) of Rule 342 to be ultra vires of the Act and invalid.
Precedent	21.
Precedent	The observations of <ORG> in <CITATION_1040368> , as to the scope of the expression 'legal representative', do not appear to have been brought to the notice of the Bench of this court that decided t he case, 1969 Acc.
Precedent	CJ 439 = (AIR 1970 Mys 67).
Precedent	In <ORG> case, <ORG> quoted with approval the following observations of <NAME>, J., who spoke for the Bench in <NAME> v. <NAME>, (1904) 8 Cal WN 843.:  "From this review of the authorities it will appear that judicial decisions have extended the sense of the term 'legal representative' beyond that of its ordinary meaning of 'administrator, executor, and heir' and though such extension has been attended with doubt and has in some cases been the subject of conflicting decision it appears to me to be too late now to endeavour, however convenient it might be, to secure for the term that which is perhaps its strict and legitimate sense.
Precedent	I agree, therefore, in holding that the term is not limited to administrators, executors and heirs and am of opinion that it must now be held to include any person who in law represents the estate of a deceased judgment-debtor."
Precedent	22.
Argument	<ORG> added that the view expressed by <NAME> <NAME>, J., has been embodied in the present definition of 'legal representative' by Section 2(11) C. P. C.  23.
RulingByLowerCourt	The above observations of Woodro<NAME>, J., which have been approv-ed by <ORG>, would indicate that the term 'legal representative' as defined in Section 2(11) Civil Procedure Code, is not restricted in its meaning to heirs, executors and administrators of the deceased as the <ORG> which decided 1969 Acc.
RulingByLowerCourt	CJ 439 = (AIR 1970 Mys 67) seems to have thought.
RulingByLowerCourt	However, this does not a<NAME>ct the main conclusion of their Lordships that the term 'legal representative' in Section 110-A of the Act includes the persons referred to as 'representatives' in Section 1-A of the Fatal Accidents Act, namely, the wife or <NAME>, parent and child of the deceased,  24.
Precedent	Mr. <NAME> next relied on the decision of <ORG> and Haryana in <ORG> v. <NAME>, 1970 Acc.
Precedent	CJ 37 (Punj).
Precedent	There, the <NAME> of two boys who died of an automobile accident, had filed a claim a pplication under Section 110-A of the Act.
Precedent	That application was resisted on the ground that the claim should have been made both on behalf of the <NAME> and the mother of the deceased and that as the <NAME> alone had made the claim, no amount could be awarded in respect of the loss caused to the mo-ther.
Precedent	Repelling that contention.
Precedent	<NAME>, J., observed that under the Fatal Accidents Act , compensation is available to all those members of the family who suffer monetary loss as a result of the death of a member by accident, that the <NAME> is also entitled to compensation as a member of the family and that it is not necessary that all the members of the family should join as claimants.
Precedent	25.
Precedent	In the light of the above decisions, it is clear that under the Fatal Accidents Act , the <NAME> of the deceased, is one of the persons who is entitled to the benefit of the compensation and that he can bring a claim petition as a representative of the person deceased.
RatioOfTheDecision	The view taken by the <ORG> that the <NAME> of the dece ased is not a legal representative of the deceased, who can make a claim petition under Section 110-A of the Act, is clearly unsustainable.
RatioOfTheDecision	In that view, it is immaterial whether appellant-2, the mother of the deceased, did or did not join in that claim petition.
RatioOfTheDecision	It also becomes unnecessary to go into the question whether appellant-2 (the mother) was prevented by sufficient cause from making the claim for compensation within the time fixed in Section 110-A .
Precedent	26.
Precedent	In the result, this appeal is allowed; the order of the <ORG> dismissing the petition is set aside and the same is remanded to the <ORG> with a direction to dispose of the petition according to law and in the light of this judgment.
