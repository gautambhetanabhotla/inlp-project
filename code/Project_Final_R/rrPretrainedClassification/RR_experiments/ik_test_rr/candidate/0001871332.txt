Fact	ORDER <NAME>, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	This is a revision petition against the order of the Ex-Officio First Class Magistrate at Mannargudi in M. S. No.
Fact	1 of 1957 on his file.
Fact	The petitioners herein are A party, and the respondents B party.
Fact	A party, that is, the petitioners herein, asked for an order under Section 145, Criminal P. C., in respect of the properties which are subject-matter of dispute and which are mentioned in the schedule annexed to the petition.
Fact	In accordance with the procedure laid down, affidavits and also documents were filed by both parties.
Fact	As the lower court was unable to decide as to who was in possession of the properties, the matter was referred to <ORG>.
Fact	Tanjore, under Clause 1 of Section 146, Criminal Procedure Code .
Fact	<ORG>, after perusing the affidavits and the documents, gave a finding that B party was in possession of the lands and transmitted the findings together with records of proceedings to the Magistrate who made the reference.
Fact	The magistrate, thereup on, disposed of the petition in conformity with the decision given by <ORG> and found that B party was in possession of the lands.
Fact	Against that order <ORG> has filed this revision.
Fact	2.
Argument	Mr. <NAME>, appearing for B party raised a preliminary objection to the maintainability of this revision against the order passed by the Magistrate.
Argument	His contention is based upon Clause 1-D of Section 146 , which is as follows:  "No appeal shall lie from any finding of the civil court given on a reference under this section nor shall any review or revision of any such finding be allowed."
Argument	He contends that this revision does not lie in view of the above clause.
Argument	But Mr. <NAME>, appearing for <ORG>, points out that this clause is confined only to the appeals or reviews or revisions against the findings of the civil court.
Argument	I agree with his contention that the Sub-clause 1-D, refers only to an appeal, or review or revision against the finding of the civil court, that is, no appeal or review or revision lies  on the civil side against the finding of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	But certainly it is open to file a revision under Sections 435 and 439 against the order of the Executive First Class Magistrate at least to show that the order of the lower court is not in conformity with the decision of the civil Court and it cannot be said that no revision at all lies against the order of the Sub-Magistrate.
RatioOfTheDecision	I, therefore, overrule the preliminary objection and hold that revision lies against the order of the trial court under Sections 435 and 439 .
RatioOfTheDecision	3.
RatioOfTheDecision	The contention of Mr. <NAME> is that this court has jurisdiction to go into the correctness or legality or otherwise of the findings of the civil court.
RatioOfTheDecision	I do not think that what is prohibited in the section in one way can be gone into in another way.
Precedent	As pointed out by their Lordships at page 627 in <NAME> v. <NAME> and <ORG>, 1899 AC 626, "it is a very familiar principle that you cannot do that indirectly which you are prohibited from doing directly."
Precedent	The ab ove decision has been followed in <NAME> v. A. G. of Canada, AIR 1949 PC 190 and the observations are at paragraph 14 in page 193.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, for this court to go into the correctness of the findings of the civil court would be to do a thing indirectly what is directly prohibited.
RatioOfTheDecision	What utmost this court can do in revision is to ascertain whether the criminal court has implemented the decision of the civil Court.
RatioOfTheDecision	To this limited extent only a revision can lie.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is fairly conceded by Mr. <NAME> that the criminal court has followed the decision of the civil court.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, there is no scope for interference, as it is a statutory duly imposed upon the criminal courts to follow the findings of civil courts, and pass orders in accordance with that.
RatioOfTheDecision	This the trial court has done.
RulingByPresentCourt	Therefore, there are no grounds for interference in revision, and this revision petition is dismissed.
