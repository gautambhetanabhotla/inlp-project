RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, what we have to see in a case of this kind, when an interim injunction is sought during the pendency of the suit before the rights of the parties are finally decided, is first whether a 'prima facie' case has been made out by the plaintiff in respect of his claim, and secondly, whether the balance of convenience demands that the injunction should be issued.
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as the 'prima facie' case is concerned, without committing ourselves finally in the matter, we have been impressed by the fact that the 2 flas h lights in question, viz., that of the plff.
RatioOfTheDecision	& that of the defts are very much similar in appearance.
RatioOfTheDecision	Their colour design, chromium plating & the make up of the bottom end are similar.
RatioOfTheDecision	The only differences that one can see on a close examination of the two flash lights are, firstly, that while in the plaintiff's torch there is the word 'Eve-Ready'; in the defendants' torch there is the word 'LitReady'.
RatioOfTheDecision	The portion 'Ready' is impressed exactly in the same form and style in both the torches, that is to say, the difference is only in 'Eve' and 'Lit'.
RatioOfTheDecision	The indentations on the upper and the lower ends of the words 'Eveready' and 'Lit-ready' are almost the same.
RatioOfTheDecision	The words 'Eveready' and 'Litready' are in the same pyramidal design and of the same shape and form and measurements.
RatioOfTheDecision	The only other difference on the bottom cap of the torch is in respect of the words 'made in U. S.
RatioOfTheDecision	A.'
RatioOfTheDecision	which appear in the plaintiff's torch while we find the words 'made in India' in the defendants' torch.
RatioOfTheDecision	While a literate person may not b e misled because he would perhaps read whether the word was 'Eveready' or 'Litready', an illiterate person is likely to be confused and misled by the whole shape and design of the defendants' torch.
RatioOfTheDecision	Without going further into this matter, we think that this is enough so far as the making out of a 'prima facie' case is concerned and we are satisfied that the plaintiff has made out a 'prima facie' case.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	The balance of convenience, in our opinion, weighed heavily on the side of the plaintiff.
RatioOfTheDecision	The defendants are admittedly newcomers in the f.eld.
RatioOfTheDecision	The defendants, according to what the lower Court has found, have manufactured only about 2,000 torches of the style in dispute, of which they have sold 1,000 and 1,000 remain with them unsold.
RatioOfTheDecision	The defendants do not claim that they have already established a name for their 'Lit-ready' torches in the market.
RatioOfTheDecision	On the other hand, it has not been contested before us that the 'Eveready' torches of the plaintiff are selling throughout India and are sold in large numbers.
RatioOfTheDecision	Th e mere fact that the defendants have undertaken to keep an account would not be a safeguard against a possible injury to the plaintiff's i trade.
Precedent	In -- 'Read v. Richardson', (1881) 45 LT 54 (A), on the defendants undertaking to keep an account, <ORG>, M. R., refused an interlocutory injunction, it <ORG> granted it because, while be defendants had only recently commenced to use their mark, and their trade mark was admittedly not dependent on it, the plaintiffs had a large trade in connection with which they used a label alleged to be infringed, and their rights would have been irremediably and incalculably injured unless the injunction were granted, if their case should be established at the trial.

Precedent	To the same effect are the observations of <NAME>, in -- '<NAME> v. <NAME>', 3 Cal 417 at p. 424 (B):  "In this particular case I do not think that their keeping an account of sales would be a sufficient protection to the plaintiffs, because if  the defendants were allowed to use these marks in the market for several months in order to sell their cloth, they would become as well known in the market as importers and sellers of the cloth as the plaintiffs; and so the very mischief which the injunction is intended to guard against would have been effected."
RulingByPresentCourt	We, therefore, consider that this was a fit case in which a temporary injunction should have been issued.
RulingByPresentCourt	We, therefore, allow this appeal, set aside the order of the Court below and restrain the defendants, their servants and agents from selling the torch lights in dispute or manufacturing them during the pendency of the suit.
RulingByPresentCourt	We may make it clear that the observations that we have made in this case are not intended to influence the Court below in deciding the case on the merits at the final heaving of the case.
