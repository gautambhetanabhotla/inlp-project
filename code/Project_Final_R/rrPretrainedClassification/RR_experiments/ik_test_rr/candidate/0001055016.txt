RulingByLowerCourt	Under the revised notification dated December 17, 1994, three percent of the seats have been reserved for candidates belonging to hill areas and another three percent in favour of candidates belonging to Uttaranchal areas.
RulingByLowerCourt	These two reservations along with the reservations in favour of physically handicapped, children of deceased/disabled soldiers and dependents of freedom fighters are treated as horizontal reservations.
RulingByLowerCourt	In other words, the reservations in favour of hill areas and Uttaranchal areas are understood and treated as reservations relatable to Article 15(1) of the Constitution and not as reservations in favour of "socially and educationally backward classes of citizens or for the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes" within the meaning  of Article 15(4) of the Constitution.
RulingByLowerCourt	It has been held by this Court in <CITATION_1734464> (1975 (1) S.C.C.267) that the reservation of seats in favour of candidates belonging to hill areas and Uttarakhand areas are reservations within the meaning of Article 15(4) of the Constitution, i.e., they are reservations in favour of socially and educationally backward classes of citizens.
RulingByLowerCourt	This Court found that "the <ORG> has established that the people in hill and Uttarakhand areas are socially and educationally backward classes of citizens".
RulingByLowerCourt	It, therefore, follows that a separate horizontal reservation of six percent of the seats in favour of candidates from hill areas and Uttaranchal apart from and in addition to twenty seven percent reservation in favour of other backward class candidates is clearly illegal.
RulingByLowerCourt	Though this contention has not been specifically raised in these writ petitions we must yet take notice of this circumstance while making the appropriate directions in these matters.
RulingByLowerCourt	It isindeed surprising tha t the State of Uttar Pradesh which is a party to the above decision has failed to bear it in mind.
RulingByLowerCourt	The said decision has also been referred to approvingly in Indra Sawhney.
RulingByLowerCourt	The State of Uttar Pradesh shall keep this in mind for future selections as also in respect of those which may be now under way and make necessary corrections.

RatioOfTheDecision	On a careful consideration of the revised notification of December 17, 1994 and the aforementioned corrigendum issued by <ORG>, we are of the opinion that in view of the ambiguous language employed therein, it is not possible to give a definite answer to the question whether the horizontal reservations are overall reservations or compartmentalised reservations.
RatioOfTheDecision	We may explain these two expressions.
RatioOfTheDecision	Where the seats reserved for horizontal reservations are proportionately divided among the vertical (social) reservations and are not inter-transferable, it would be a case of compartmentalised reservations.
RatioOfTheDecision	We may illustrate what we say: Take this very case; out of the total 746 seats, 112 seats (representing fifteen percent) should be filled by special reservation candidates; at the same time, the social reservation in favour of Other Backward Classes is 27% which means 201 seats for O.B.Cs.
RatioOfTheDecision	; if the 112 special reservation seats are also divided proportionately as  between <ORG> and <ORG>, 30 seats would be allocated to the <ORG> category; in other words, thirty special category students can be accommodated in the <ORG> category; but say only ten special reservation candidates belonging to <ORG> are available, then these ten candidates will, of course, be allocated among <ORG> quota but the remaining twenty seats cannot be transferred to <ORG> category (they will be available for <ORG> candidates only) or for that matter, to any other category; this would be so whether requisite number of special reservation candidates (56 out of 373) are available in <ORG> category or not; the special reservation would be a water tight compartment in each of the vertical reservation classes (<ORG> and <ORG>).
RatioOfTheDecision	As against this, what happens in the over-all reservation is that while allocating the special reservation students to their respective social reservation category, the over-all reservation in favour of special reservation categories has yet to be honoured.
RatioOfTheDecision	This means that in the above illustration, the twenty remaining seats would be transferred to <ORG> category which means that the number of special reservation candidates in <ORG> category would be 56+20=76.
RatioOfTheDecision	Further, if no special reservation candidate belonging to <ORG> and <ORG> is available then the proportionate number of seats meant for special reservation candidates in <ORG> and <ORG> also get transferred to <ORG> category.
RatioOfTheDecision	The result would be that 102 special reservation candidates have to be accommodated in the <ORG> category to complete their quota of 112.
RatioOfTheDecision	The converse may also happen, which will prejudice the candidates in the reserved categories.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is, of course, obvious that the inter se quota between <ORG>, <ORG>, <ORG> and <ORG> will not be altered.
