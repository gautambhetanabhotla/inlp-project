RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	On a consideration of the merits of the case, it is necessary to notice at the outset that the conviction of the appellant is not based upon a mere  acceptance of the oath of <NAME> denying the alleged misappropriation by himself and the alleged authorisation by him to the appellant to raise money on his credit but on a consideration of the circumstances brought out in the evidence.
RatioOfTheDecision	Indeed all the three <ORG> had severe comments to make against the evidence of the Subordinate Judge.
RulingByLowerCourt	The trial Magistrate Characterised a material item of the evidence of <NAME> as "a piece of falsehood unworthy of a judicial officer of the standing of Mr. <NAME>".
RulingByLowerCourt	The Sessions Judge at the conclusion of the judgment stated as follows:  "Before concluding I would like to say that though I have exonerated Mr. <NAME> from all complicity in the matter of the misappropriation of Government money, I cannot exonerate him from all blame in connection with this affair.
RulingByLowerCourt	He has come in for a good deal of criticism which in my judgment is not altogether unmerited at the hands of the learned trial Magistrate both in regard to the truthfulness of the statement he made in  Court and the fact that he arrogated to himself the role of the complainant and the investigator in this case"  The learned Judge of <ORG> has also substantially agreed with this opinion.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question, threfore, that arises in this case is whether the circumstances brought out are or clear as to show that the defence is false or improbable.
RatioOfTheDecision	In a case like this depending on the conclusions drawn from circumstances, it is well settled that the cumulative effect of the circumstances must be such as to negative the innocence of the accused and to bring the offences home to him beyond any reasonable doubt.
RatioOfTheDecision	This Court has affirmed the proposition in - ' <CITATION_882637> ), in the following terms at pp.
RatioOfTheDecision	345-346.
RatioOfTheDecision	"It is well to remember that in cases where the evidence is of a circumstantial nature, the circumstances from which the conclusion of guilt is to be drawn should in the first, instance be fully established, and alt the facts so established should be consistent only with the hypothesis of the guil t of the accused.
RatioOfTheDecision	Again, the circumstances should be of a conclusive nature and tendency and they should be such as to exclude every hypothesis but the one proposed to be proved.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words, there must be a chain of evidence so far complete as not to leave any reasonable ground for a conclusion consistent with the innocence of the accused and it must be such as to show that within all human probability the act must have been done by the accused".
RatioOfTheDecision	We cannot help noticing that the Courts below do not appear to have kept this fundamental approach in view.
RatioOfTheDecision	The first question that arises in this case is whether from the circumstances it is so well-established beyond doubt that it is the appellant that has misappropriated the amount in question.
RatioOfTheDecision	There are at the outset two circumstances 'against' him, (1) that the amount in question was drawn by him from the <ORG>, and (2) that it remained unpaid to the parties concerned, viz., to <ORG> at <ORG> and <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	But he asserts that the money wa s taken from him by the Subordinate Judge himself temporarily for his own purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	Apart from the alleged receipt, Ex.
RatioOfTheDecision	D. A. which will be dealt with presently, the defence relies on the following circumstances.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is pointed out that the scope for misappropriation by one party or the other arose in this case by virtue of deviation from the normal practice of drawing the pay of outlying Tahsils by way of cash orders and that this deviation was at the instance of the Subordinate Judge himself and with his full knowledge, but that he now falsely denies all knowledge of the same.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is in evidence that the salary bill as originally presented to the <ORG> had a note at the top thereof that the amounts for the three outlying Tahsils were to be paid by cash-orders.
RulingByLowerCourt	The bill was returned on the 4th December for correction of a small mistake in the details thereof.
RulingByLowerCourt	The evidence shows that before re-presenting it to the <ORG>, the note above referred to was scored out under signatures of the Subordinate Judge a t the two ends of the note, thus converting the bill into one for payment of the entire amount thereof in cash to the appellant.
RulingByLowerCourt	A look at the original bill which has been exhibited also shows this.
RulingByLowerCourt	In addition to this authorisation to draw the entire amount in cash, in departure from the pre-existing practice, another circumstance has also been brought out in the evidence.
RulingByLowerCourt	As already stated the Subordinate Judge of Una sent a telegram to the District Judge of Hoshiarpur on the 14th December complaining that the pay of the Civil establishment was not disbursed till then.

Argument	P. W. 9/A, which is as follows:  "Generally salaries of officials in the <ORG> are received late.

RatioOfTheDecision	To our mind, the very fact that the defence was given out at such an early stage and that it has, to such a large extent, been corroborated is a strong reason for thinking that the defence was very likely to have been true.
