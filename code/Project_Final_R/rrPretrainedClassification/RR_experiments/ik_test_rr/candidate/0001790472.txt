RatioOfTheDecision	After giving the question our careful consideration we have come to the conclusion that the provisions of Madras Act, xv [15] of 1946 would not apply to the lease in question.
RatioOfTheDecision	In coming to this conclusion, we have be en impressed by the fact that the lease was entered into long after the Act had come into force and it must be presumed that the parties knew of its provisions.
RatioOfTheDecision	This Act was primarily intended to prevent unreasonable eviction of tenants and to regulate the letting of residential and non-residential buildings and to control the rents of such buildings.
RatioOfTheDecision	Though most of the provisions of the Act are obviously thus intended to safeguard the rights of tenants, there are provisions which, so far as they go, are in favour of the landlord also.
RatioOfTheDecision	Now Section 7 (2) (i) is one of such provisions which gives a right to the landlord to pray for the eviction of the tenant on default of due pay.
RatioOfTheDecision	ment of rent, Under that provision in a case where the rent is payable every month, even default in payment of the rent for one month entitled the landlord to apply for eviction of the tenant.
RatioOfTheDecision	This provision certainly is made for the benefit of the landlord.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is a well established principle of law that "every one has a right to waiv e and to agree to waive, the advantage of a law or rule made solely for the benefit and protection of the individual in his private capacity, which may be dispensed with without infringing any public right or public policy."

RatioOfTheDecision	There is no express prohibition against contracting out of it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Though we realise that the Act wag passed in accordance with a general policy, we cannot say that that policy would be violated if a contract is entered into between a landlord and tenant giving the tenant larger rights and greater privileges than those conferred on him by the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case though under Section 7 (2) (i) of the Act the landlord may be entitled to apply for eviction even when there was default in payment of a month's rent, the lease deed ex.
RatioOfTheDecision	pressly provides that it is only if the rent remains unpaid for two tenancy months after the same became payable that the landlord is entitled to re-enter upon the demised premises.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the absence of authority that it is not permissib le for the parties to contract themselves out of the provisions of this Act we hold that they can agree to be governed by terms and conditions which may be inconsistent with some of the provisions of the Act.

RatioOfTheDecision	ample, found in some enactments which declare that any action done in contravention of the enactment is void.

RatioOfTheDecision	Otherwise it comes to this, that an Act intended to prevent unreasonable eviction of tenants can be used for the perpetration of the very thing sought to be prevented.
