Fact	The only question that arises for consideration in this appeal is whether the intimation which the Income-tax Officer received from <ORG> would constitute 'information' within the meaning of Section 147(b) .
Statute	Section 147(b) provides:  notwithstanding that there ha s been no omission or failure as mentioned in Clause (a) on the part of the assessee, the Income-tax Officer has in consequence of information in his possession reason to believe that income chargeable to tax has escaped assessment for any assessment year, he may assess or reassess such income for the assessment year concerned.
Statute	5.
Statute	Sub-section (b) of Section 147 enables the Income-tax Officer to assess or reassess the income if in consequence of information in his possession he has reason to believe that income chargeable to tax has escaped assessment.
RatioOfTheDecision	Two conditions are necessary for invoking the Sub-section: (1) the officer should receive information after the original assessment; (2) in consequence of such information he should have reason to believe that income has escaped assessment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The 'information' may be of facts or of law.
RatioOfTheDecision	The 'information' of a fact may be from external source.
