Argument	SUBMISSIONS :  Mr. <NAME>, learned counsel appearing on behalf of the Appellants, would, inter alia, submit that the Reference Applications were barred under the principle of res judicata.

RatioOfTheDecision	In any event, Mr. <NAME> would contend that having regard to the fact that the Respondents herein are qualified Junior Engineers and have already crossed the age of forty, this Court may not, in its discretion, interfere with the impugned judgments.
RatioOfTheDecision	ANALYSIS :  The said writ petitions were filed by the Respondents on the following premise : (1) The Respondents had been appointed in the year 1993; (2) When the writ petitions were filed they were still in service; (3) They were appointed as <ORG> Graduate Engineers; (4) They were not being paid the regular scale of pay, although they had been doing the same work as was being done by the regular employees; (5) Having regard to the fact that they had rendered ab out four years of continuous service and being under threat of termination of service, they were entitled to be regularized in their service as also other benefits attached thereto, having completed 240 days of continuous service in a year.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have noticed hereinbefore that the factual premise that the Respondents had still been in service at the time of filing of the writ petitions had been found to be incorrect, as in fact their services had been terminated on or about 19.10.1996.
RulingByLowerCourt	It was further held that as they were appointed against a Scheme, the question of their continuance in service beyond the life thereof does not arise.
RulingByLowerCourt	<ORG> was furthermore of the opinion that their services cannot be directed to be regularized.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have noticed hereinbefore that although the principal plea of the Respondents in the said writ petition was for regularization of their services but they had also prayed for issuance of a writ of mandamus or for a direction not to discontinue their services as also payment of  arrears of salary from September 1996 onwards, difference or arrears of salary from the date of completion of 240 days and to grant all service benefits as applicable to regular employees like seniority, promotion, increments, allowance etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	The said prayer in the said writ applications had a direct nexus with the orders of termination of their services.
RatioOfTheDecision	A finding of fact having been arrived at that their services had been terminated and they were not entitled to continue in service, in our opinion, the legality or otherwise of the said purported orders of termination could not have been the subject-matter of proceedings under the Industrial Disputes Act ; for the reason that if the Respondents herein were not entitled to continue in their services by reason of the judgment of <ORG>, the question of their reinstatement with back-wages would not arise.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Respondents herein approached <ORG> with full knowledge that their services had been terminated.
RatioOfTheDecision	Their attempt to show that they were st ill in service had been disbelieved and it was found as of fact that their services had been terminated on 19.10.1996.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Respondents, therefore, while filing the writ application were bound to lay their whole claim having regard to the provisions contained in Order II Rule 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure or the principles analogous thereto.
RatioOfTheDecision	The very basis upon which the writ petitions were based was found to be incorrect.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was, thus, obligatory on the part of the Respondents herein to question their orders of termination upon placing correct facts before <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	They did not choose to do so.
RatioOfTheDecision	They did not pray for and obtain any leave of the court to raise the contention about the legality or otherwise of the orders of termination before an appropriate forum.
RatioOfTheDecision	Furthermore, their plea to the effect that they were entitled to continue in service was specifically rejected.
RatioOfTheDecision	In that view of the matter, the proceedings initiated before <ORG> questioning the orders of termination passed against  them by the Appellants praying for their reinstatement with full back- wages, in our opinion, was wholly misconceived.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such a plea was barred under the principle of Res Judicata.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is now well-settled that the general principle of Res Judicata applies to an industrial adjudication.
Precedent	In <NAME> (supra), this Court held :  "The principle of res judicata operates on the court.
Precedent	It is the courts which are prohibited from trying the issue which was directly and substantially in issue in the earlier proceedings between the same parties, provided the court trying the subsequent proceeding is satisfied that the earlier court was competent to dispose of the earlier proceedings and that the matter had been heard and finally decided by such court.
RatioOfTheDecision	Here the parties to the writ petition filed by the respondent in <ORG> and the industrial dispute were the same.
RatioOfTheDecision	The cause of action in both was the refusal of the appellant to allow the respondent to rejoin service.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> was competent  to decide the issue which it did with a reasoned order on merits and after a contested hearing.
RatioOfTheDecision	This was not a case where the earlier proceedings had been disposed of on any technical ground as was the case in <CITATION_1851717> [(1978) 3 SCC  119)] and <CITATION_1723525> [].
RatioOfTheDecision	The "lesser relief" of reinstatement which was the subject-matter of the industrial dispute had already been claimed by the respondent in the writ petition.
RatioOfTheDecision	This was refused by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The correctness of the decision in the writ proceedings has not been challenged by the respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	The decision was, therefore, final.
RatioOfTheDecision	Having got an adverse order in the writ petition, it was not open to the respondent to reagitate the issue before <ORG> and <ORG> was incompetent to entertain the dispute raised by the respondent and redecide the matter in the face of the earlier decision of <ORG> in the writ proceedings."
RatioOfTheDecision	It is no doubt true, as has been contended by Mr. <NAME>, that the burden of proof that the  Respondents were employed against a particular Scheme was on the Appellants but such a burden stood discharged and in any event the said question was no longer alive having regard to the decision of <ORG> in the earlier writ petitions.
RatioOfTheDecision	The plea raised before us by the Respondents to the effect that their termination of employment fell within sub-clause (bb) of clause (oo) of the Industrial Disputes Act , apart from having not been raised before <ORG> and <ORG>, in our opinion, is not available to them having regard to the decision of <ORG> in the writ petitions filed by the Respondents.
RatioOfTheDecision	The decision of a Division Bench of this Court in <CITATION_138212981> [], upon which reliance was placed by Mr. <NAME>, therefore, cannot be said to have any application whatsoever in the instant case.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may not be out of place to mention that completion of 240 days of continuous service in a year may not by itself be a ground for directing an order of regularization.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is also not the case of the Respondents that they were appointed in accordance with the extant rules.
RatioOfTheDecision	No direction for regularization of their services was, therefore, could be issued.
