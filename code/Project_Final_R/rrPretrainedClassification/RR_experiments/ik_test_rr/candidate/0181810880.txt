RulingByLowerCourt	Both these appeals have common factual matrix, and legal panorama and, therefore, are dealt with by this common judgment.
Fact	Factual backdrop in a nutshell is as follows:  <ORG> (hereinafter referred to as the 'Corporation') invited applications for recruitment to two posts of Manager (Finance and Accounts) by advertisement dated 18.7.1995.
Fact	The advertisement inviting applications for the two posts of Manager (Finance and Accounts), one post for general and one post of scheduled caste, prescribed the requisite educational qualification.
Fact	It was stipulated in the advertisement that the age and other qualifications were to be reckoned as of 31.7.1995.
Fact	It was also indicated that the applications in the prescribed format with complete info rmation should reach the prescribed authority before 29th July, 1995 and incomplete applications and applications without necessary enclosures were to be rejected.
Fact	Appellant and respondents 4 and 5 were applicants in response to the advertisement.
Fact	Though respondent No.4 was not qualified on the last date of submission of application, he was permitted to attend and appear for the written test.
Fact	However, on the date of interview he was eligible.
Fact	The written test was conducted on 1.10.1995 and the viva vice was conducted on 25.11.1995.
Fact	Similar was stated to be the position vis- à-vis respondent No.5.
Fact	When respondent No.4 was selected, appellant challenged his selection to be not in accordance with law.
Fact	It is to be noted that waiting list is prepared and respondent No.5 was placed in the waiting list.
Fact	A writ application was filed before <ORG> at Bangalore challenging the selection of respondent No.4 and placing respondent No.5 in the waiting list.
Fact	Though, learned Single Judge of <ORG>  held that respondent No.4 was ineligible as on the date of employment, he held that in public interest the selection was to be maintained.
Fact	A reference was made to the decision of this Court in <CITATION_980500> .
Fact	(1993 Supp (2) SCC 611) (described hereinafter as <NAME> – case No.I) where it was held that if the applicant had acquired qualification by the time of interview that is sufficient.
Fact	A writ appeal was filed before <ORG>.
Fact	The view of the learned Single Judge was affirmed by <ORG>.
Fact	A review application was filed inter alia taking the stand that the view in <NAME>'s case No.I has been later on over-ruled in <NAME> and <NAME>.
Fact	v <NAME> and Anr.
Fact	(1997 (4) SCC 18) (described hereinafter as <NAME> – case No.II).
Fact	Therefore, a review of the judgment of <ORG> was necessary.
Fact	<ORG> by the impugned judgment held that though admittedly on 18.7.1995 i.e.
Fact	on the date of advertisement the respondent No.4 was not qu alified to make an application, yet few dates and facts are relevant.
Fact	He had appeared for the M.B.A. examination in April 1995 and the results were declared on 4.9.1995.
Fact	The written examination was held on 1.10.1995 and viva voce was conducted on 25.11.1995.
Fact	At least by the time the written examination and the viva voce tests were held, he had acquired the requisite qualification.
Fact	Judgment in <NAME> case No.
Fact	I was delivered on 18.12.1992 and decision in the review petition in the said case was rendered on 10.3.1997.
Fact	The appointment of respondent No.4 was made when the earlier decision of <NAME> case No.I held the field.
Argument	It was, therefore, held that on the date of selection, the first judgment held the field; and, therefore, by applying logic of that decision the selection of respondent No.4 cannot be questioned.
Argument	Learned counsel for the appellant submitted that the approach of <ORG> is erroneous as the law declared by this <ORG> is presumed to be the law at all times.
Argument	<NAME> ally, the decision of this <ORG> enunciating a principle of law is applicable to all cases irrespective its stage of pendency because it is assumed that what is enunciated by the Supreme <ORG> is, in fact, the law from inception.
Fact	The doctrine of prospective over-ruling which is a feature of American jurisprudence is an exception to the normal principle of law, was imported and applied for the first time in <CITATION_21266288> .
Fact	(AIR 1967 SC 1643).
Fact	<CITATION_1246653> .
Fact	(1993 (4) SCC 727) the view was adopted.
Fact	Prospective over-ruling is a part of the principles of constitutional canon of interpretation and can be resorted to by this <ORG> while superseding law declared by it earlier.
Fact	It is a device innovated to avoid reopening of settled issues, to prevent multiplicity of proceedings, and to avoid uncertainty and avoidable litigation.
Fact	In other words, actions taken contrary to the law declared prior to the date of declaration are validated in larger public interest.
Fact	The law as declared applies to future cases.
Fact	(  <CITATION_897981> .
Fact	, <CITATION_199143> ).
Fact	It is for this Court to indicate as to whether the decision in question will operate prospectively.
Fact	In other words, there shall be no prospective over-ruling, unless it is so indicated in the particular decision.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not open to be held that the decision in a particular case will be prospective in its application by application of the doctrine of prospective over-ruling.
RatioOfTheDecision	The doctrine of binding precedent helps in promoting certainty and consistency in judicial decisions and enables an organic development of the law besides providing assurance to the individual as to the consequences of transactions forming part of the daily affairs.
RatioOfTheDecision	That being the position, <ORG> was in error by holding that the judgment which operated on the date of selection was operative and not the review judgment in <NAME> case No.II.
RatioOfTheDecision	All the more so when the subsequent judgment is by way of Review of the first judgment in which case there are no judgments at all and th e subsequent judgment rendered on review petitions is the one and only judgment rendered, effectively and for all purposes, the earlier decision having been erased by countenancing the review applications.
RatioOfTheDecision	The impugned judgments of <ORG> are, therefore, set aside.
RatioOfTheDecision	That brings us to the ticklish question as to how the reliefs can be moulded.
RulingByPresentCourt	It is not in dispute that subsequently the appellant has also been appointed on 9.11.2002.
RulingByPresentCourt	Though it was permissible for this case to set aside the appointments of respondent no.4 and respondent no.5, on the peculiar facts of this case, we consider it to be not called for and the rights of parties instead could be adjusted by working out equities, in the interests of substantial justice by adopting a different course.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appellant shall rank senior to respondent No.4 by treating his appointment to be with effect from the date of selection of respondent No.4.
RulingByPresentCourt	This shall be only for the purpose of fixing the seniority and continuity of service only not for entitle ment to any salary or other financial benefits.
RulingByPresentCourt	As respondent No.5 was only in the waiting list, and it is stated that he has been subsequently appointed, he will also rank below the appellant and respondent No.4.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeals are accordingly allowed.
RulingByPresentCourt	There shall be no order as to costs.
