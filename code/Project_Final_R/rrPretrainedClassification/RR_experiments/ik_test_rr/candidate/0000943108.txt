RatioOfTheDecision	PETITIONER: STATE OF KERALA & ORS.
RatioOfTheDecision	Vs. RESPONDENT: <NAME> & ANR.
RatioOfTheDecision	DATE OF JUDGMENT: 05/05/1997 BENCH: <NAME>, D.P.
Fact	WADHWA ACT: HEADNOTE: JUDGMENT:  O R D E R Leave granted.
Fact	This appeal by special leave arises from the judgment of <ORG> of <ORG>, made on July 24,1991 Writ Appeal No.86 of 1990.
Fact	The admitted position is that an extent of 1.94 acres of land was acquired way back in 1952 for construction of national highway.
Fact	The construction was completed in 1955. out of the extent of 1.94 acres, 80 cents of land were used and the balance land remained unused.
Fact	When respondent No.1 had applied for sale of the property by dated December 21,1979, the property was sought to be sold to him at the same rate at which compensation was awarded under Section 11, that was interdicted by way of writ petitions.
Fact	The sheet-anchor of the <ORG> to sustain the action is the executive order issued by the <ORG> for permission for alienation of the land.
Argument	<ORG> has dec lared the executive action as invalid in the light of the Kerala Land Assignment Act, 1960 (Act 30 of 1960) (for short, the `Act').
Argument	The high court has pointed out that the assignment is in contravention of the Act.
Fact	Thus, this appeal by special leave.
Fact	In view of admitted section that the land in question was acquired under the Land Acquisition Act , it stood vested in the <ORG> free from all encumbrances.
Fact	The question emerges: whether the Government can assign the land to the erstwhile owners?
RatioOfTheDecision	It is settled law that land is acquired for a public purpose was achieved, the rest of the land could be used for any other purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	In case there is no other public purpose for which the land is needed, then instead of disposal by way of sale to the erstwhile owner, the land should be put to public auction and the amount Fetched in the public auction can be better utilised for the public purpose envisaged in the Directive Principles of Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case, what we find is that the executive order is not i n consonance with the provision of the Act and is, therefore, invalid.
RatioOfTheDecision	under these circumstances, <ORG> is well justified in declaring the executive order as invalid.
RatioOfTheDecision	whatever assignment is made, should be for a public purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	otherwise, the land of the <ORG> should be sold only through the public actions so that the public also gets benefited by getting higher value.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeal is accordingly dismissed.
RulingByPresentCourt	No costs.
