RatioOfTheDecision	As the learned Judge has allowed the Writ Petition only on the ground that, the <ORG>'s Circular dated May 29, 1979 overrides the earlier circulars, we need examine only the scope and effect of this circular.
RatioOfTheDecision	On such scrutiny, we find the view taken by <ORG> is not correct and cannot, therefore, be sustained.
Precedent	The crucial words in the Circular are the following:  List...which is pending till now.
Precedent	The earlier instructions issued by the <ORG> imposed two conditions viz.
Precedent	(1) a waiting list would be effecti ve only for a period of one year and (2) the list should not contain more than 25 per cent surplus candidates against vacancies existing and anticipated.
Precedent	Therefore, the words "list which is pending" can only mean a list which had current force but which contained more names than 25 per cent surplus number of candidates against vacancies which existed or were likely to occur.
Precedent	It could never have been the intention of the <ORG> that lists prepared in the earlier years, irrespective of the interval of time, would be treated as pending lists and all the candidates included in those lists should be given employment before fresh lists of eligible candidates are prepared.
Precedent	Another significant feature to be noticed in this case is that after the list of the year 1976 was prepared, a fresh list of candidates had been prepared in the year 1978, and the selected candidates have been given appointments.
Precedent	In the face of this later list there is no scope for treating the 1976 list as a pending list.
Precedent	10.
