RatioOfTheDecision	Element of subjectivity:  2.
RulingByLowerCourt	In <NAME> (2) v. State of Karnataka, (2008) 13 SCC 767 this <ORG> noted in paragraph 44 of the Report that the expression “the rarest of rare cases” in <CITATION_307021> ,  indicates a relative category based on a comparison with other cases.
RulingByLowerCourt	In paragraph 45 of the Report, this <ORG> considered the expression as requiring a comparison between (i) cases of murder with other cases of murder of the same or of a similar kind or even of a graver nature and  (ii) the punishment awarded to the convicts in those cases.
RulingByLowerCourt	This <ORG> also expressed the view that there is hardly any field available for comparison.
RulingByLowerCourt	In other words, this <ORG> highlighted the difficul ty in the practical application of the “rarest of rare” principle since there is a lack of empirical data for making the two-fold comparison.
RulingByLowerCourt	3.
RulingByLowerCourt	The question therefore is: how does one determine that a case is rare as compared to another case?
RulingByLowerCourt	If such a comparison were possible, then on a relative basis could a particular case be described as rarer than an identified rare case?

RatioOfTheDecision	that for persons convicted of murder, life imprisonment is the rule and death sentence an exception.
