Fact	This is an appeal by one <NAME> against the order of the learned <ORG> Judge of Noakhali who has complained against him in that he brought a false charge of theft against one <NAME>.
Fact	The case of theft was instituted by <NAME> by giving information to the <ORG>.

Argument	The learned <ORG> Judge found the case false and complained against <NAME> under Section 476, Criminal P.C.
Argument	The first point urged by the learned vakil for the appellant is that the alleged offence was not committed in a proceeding or in relation to a proceeding before the <ORG> Judge.
Argument	The learned vakil contends that the offence, if any, was committed by the institution of the case before the <ORG> and further that Section 476 refers to offences which are committed after the proceedings have begun before the <ORG>.
Argument	2.
Fact	We think, however, that the words "in relation to" are sufficiently wide to cover a case where the offenc e was actually committed before the proceedings began in the complaining <ORG>.
Fact	In the present case the proceeding was the trial of <NAME> for theft by <ORG> and that <ORG> found that the charge was false and hence that the charge made to the <ORG> was false.
Fact	The false charge to the <ORG> was obviously a matter related to the proceedings in <ORG> for it was the basis of the proceedings.
Fact	Had there been no charge to the <ORG> there would have been no proceedings in <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	3.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as this <ORG> is concerned the question may be considered as one of first impression.
RatioOfTheDecision	Our attention has not been drawn to any decision of this <ORG> since Section 476 was amended.
Precedent	We are, however, fortified in the view that we take of the meaning of the expression 'in relation to' by a recent decision of the Patna High <ORG> in the case of <CITATION_1265595> A.I.R.
Precedent	1925 Pat.
Precedent	717, Mr. Justice <NAME> held that if two offences are even remotely connected by the relationship of cause and eff ect then the first may be said to be committed in relation to the second.
RatioOfTheDecision	If we consider the institution of the false base to the <ORG> as the cause of the proceedings before the Sessions Judge - and this we think no doubt it was - then it may beheld that the false case instituted before the <ORG> was in relation to the proceedings in <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	4.
RatioOfTheDecision	That being so the present case falls within Section 476 and the Sessions Judge had jurisdiction to make the complaint.
