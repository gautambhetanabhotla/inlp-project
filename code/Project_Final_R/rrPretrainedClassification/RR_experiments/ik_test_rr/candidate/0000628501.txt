RatioOfTheDecision	Any person aggrieved by the decision of the Collector is entitled to prefer an appeal to the Commissioner.
RatioOfTheDecision	Under section 12 of the Act every order made by the Collector or by the Commissioner, as the case may be is final save as otherwise expressly provided in the Act and such order cannot be called in question in any court by way of appeal or revision or in any original suit, application or execution proceedings.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 13 of the Act provides that no civil court shall have jurisdiction to entertain or adjudicate upon any question whether any property or any right to or any interest in any property is or is not shamlat deh vested or deemed to have been vested in a <ORG> under the Act or to questi on the legality of any action taken by the <ORG>er or the Collector or the <ORG> under the Act or in respect of any matter which the <ORG> or the Collector is empowered by or under the Act to determine.
RatioOfTheDecision	The contention of the <ORG> before the trial court was that the land in question was shamlat deh and it had been vested in it.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is no doubt true that the plaintiffs who claimed to be the owners along with some others of the suit land had avoided to seek a declaration that the suit land was not shamlat deh.
RatioOfTheDecision	They had, however, questioned the correctness of the entries in the revenue records which showed that the <ORG> was entitled to the suit land.
RatioOfTheDecision	The plaintiffs cannot by drawing their plaint cleverly by not claiming a declaration that the land in question was not shamlat deh confer jurisdiction on the civil court when by virtue of section 13 of the Act the jurisdiction of civil courts to try such suits had been taken away.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the instant case the suit had been filed against the Panchay at and the <ORG> had expressly claimed that the land in question belonged to it as <NAME>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It will not be possible in the circumstances for the civil court to make a declaration in favour of the plaintiffs without deciding the question whether the property in question was <NAME> or not and whether it belonged to the <ORG> or not.
Argument	Reliance was however placed by the learned counsel for the petitioners on a decision of <ORG> in <CITATION_1459649> .,  <ORG> Page 366 in which the suit had been held to be maintainable in a civil court even though the defendant had contended that the land involved in that suit was <NAME>.
Argument	<ORG> found that plaintiff in that case had only stated in the plaint that the land in question was 'Gali Sheh-re-aam' or a throughfare belonging to the Gram <ORG> which was being used by the plaintiff as an approach to his house for about 30 years and had prayed for an injunction restraining the defendant from interfering  with his right.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> in question had not been impleaded as a defendant.
RatioOfTheDecision	The plaintiff in that case had not claimed that the suit land belonged to him or that it did not belong to <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The crucial issue which had been framed in that case was whether the land in question over which the plaintiff had asserted his right was a street or not and whether the defendant had blocked the said street.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> held in the circumstances of that suit that the jurisdiction of the civil court had not been taken away by virtue of section 13 read with sections 13A and 13B of the Act which had been inserted by <ORG> into the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are of the view that the above decision is clearly distinguishable from the present case since in this case the <ORG> which had been impleaded as a defendant had raised the plea that the suit land was a part of <ORG> and that the plaintiffs had no right or title in it.
RatioOfTheDecision	This question has to be decided by the Collector only under section  11 of the Act and not by the Civil court.
RulingByPresentCourt	We do not, therefore, find any ground to interfere with the judgment of <ORG> of Punjab and Haryana against which this petition is filed.
RulingByPresentCourt	The petition is dismissed.
