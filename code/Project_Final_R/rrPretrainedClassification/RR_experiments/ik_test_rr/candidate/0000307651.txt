RatioOfTheDecision	Per contra, learned counsel for the respondent submitted that the direction of <ORG> is in order keeping in view the fact that the <ORG> at all points of time had taken the stand that it had a scheme for regularization.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is to be noted that the <ORG> as revealed from the affidavits filed before <ORG>, had stated that more than 3,000 workers were engaged from time to time on daily-wages basis, besides 970 workers working on regular basis.
RatioOfTheDecision	Whenever a post fell vacant or newly created with the approval of the competent authority and following due procedure the vacancies are filled up from amongst those who are on daily-wages according to their zone-wise seniority list separately maintained for Nagpur and Amaravati zones.
RulingByLowerCourt	This position is not dispute d by the respondents.
RulingByLowerCourt	That being so, the order of <ORG> is clearly untenable on more than one counts.
RulingByLowerCourt	Firstly, the order in writ petition no.
RulingByLowerCourt	143/83 and other connected cases dated 25th July, 1983 had become final and there was no challenge to it.
RulingByLowerCourt	Prayer in the subsequent writ petition to enforce <ORG> is clearly not maintainable.
RulingByLowerCourt	Merely because Section 50(B) of the Act was repealed that did not take away the effect of the order passed by <ORG> in the earlier cases.
RulingByLowerCourt	The prayer for enforcement of <ORG> order in its original form could not have been made, when the same had been modified by <ORG>'s order which had attained finality.
RulingByLowerCourt	Additionally, as observed by this <ORG> in <CITATION_893566> .
RulingByLowerCourt	(2001 (7) SCC 346) the status of permanency cannot be granted when there is no post.
Precedent	Again in <ORG> v. <NAME> (2001 (7) SCC 356), it was held that mere continuance every year of seasonal work obviously during  the period when the work was available does not constitute a permanent status unless there exists posts and regularization is done.
RulingByPresentCourt	Above being the position the impugned judgment of <ORG> cannot be maintained and is set aside.
RulingByPresentCourt	It is, however, not in dispute that except respondent no.8 who has died in the meantime the others have been at points of time regularized.
RulingByPresentCourt	The regularization shall take effect from the respective dates of order in that regard as passed by the authority and not from 7.11.1983 as directed by <ORG>.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeal is allowed to the aforesaid extent without any order as to costs.
