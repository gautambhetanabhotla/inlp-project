Fact	ORDER N. Santosh Hegde, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	Leave granted in all these special leave petitions.
Fact	2.
Fact	These appeals arise out of a common judgment dated 9.8.1996 delivered in RSA No.
Fact	1972/88 and other connected appeals, by <ORG> at Chandigarh confirming the judgment of the first appellate court and decreeing the suits of the plaintiffs.
Fact	3.
Fact	The plaintiffs, who are the respondents in these appeals, had filed suits for declaration and mandatory injunction against the appellant herein, namely, <ORG> (for short 'the <ORG>'), to allot them plots in lieu of lands acquired from them.
Fact	The trial court in some cases decreed the suit and in others, dismissed the suit.
Fact	In appeal, the first appellate court decreed all the suits and granted the relief prayed for.
Fact	The <ORG> preferred second appeals before <ORG> which confirmed the decree of the first appellate court, giving rise to these appeals.
Fact	The claim of the respondents in all these appeals arises on the  basis that they are all 'local displaced persons' who are entitled to a plot each in lieu of the lands acquired from them by the appellant-Trust, in view of the preference given to such local displaced persons under the allotment rules.
Argument	4.
Argument	Acquisitions involved in these appeals are made under Section 36 of the Punjab Town Improvement Act, 1922 (for short 'the Improvement Act ') which empowers the authorities concerned to prepare and publish improvement schemes within the local areas concerned.
Argument	Section 73 thereof empowers <ORG> to make rales consistent with the Act and applicable to all improvement trusts including the appellant herein.
Argument	5.
Argument	The State Government exercising the power vested in it under Section 73 of the Improvement Act on 5.1.1954 notified the Jullundur Improvement Land Disposal Rules, 1954.
Argument	Rule 2(b) of the said rales defines 'local displaced person' as a person whose property has been acquired by the Trust for the execution of a scheme under <ORG> eas Act, 1951 (for short 'the Damaged Areas Act).
Argument	Rule 5(ii) of the said Rules provides for certain preferential allotments in favour of local displaced persons, among others.
Argument	6.
Argument	On 18.9.1975, <ORG> promulgated a Rule titled as "Utilisation of Land and Allotment of Plots by Improvement <ORG> Rules, 1975".
Statute	Rule 2(a) thereof defines ""local displaced person" means a person who is the owner of a property acquired by the <ORG> for the execution of a Scheme and has been such owner for a continuous period of two years immediately before the first publication of the Scheme by the <ORG> under Section 36 of the Punjab Town Improvement Act, 1922.".
Statute	Sub-rule (ii) of Rule 7 of the said Rules makes reservation of plots and tenements in favour of local displaced persons.
Statute	On 22.12.1983, <ORG> promulgated another Rule titled "The Punjab Town Improvement (Utilisation of Land and Allotment of Plots) Rules, 1983.
Statute	Rule 2(d) of the said Rules defines `local displaced person' as follows:-  Local disp laced person" means a person who is the owner of any land acquired by the <ORG> for the execution of any scheme under the Act and who has been such owner for a continuous period of two years immediately before the first publication of such scheme by the <ORG> under Section 36 .
Argument	7.
Argument	Rule 4(2) of the said Rules provides for preferential allotment in favour of a local displaced person.
Argument	8.
Argument	A perusal of the above Rules shows that so far as the 1954 Rules are concerned, the definition of 'local displaced person' is confined to a person whose lands have been acquired for a scheme to be executed under the Damaged Areas Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not applicable to any other scheme.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as the 1975 and 1983 Rules are concerned, the definition of 'local displaced person' applies to a person whose property has been acquired and who has been owner of such property for a continuous period of two years immediately before the first publication of the scheme by the <ORG>, Thus, it is seen from all these three set of rules that in order t o have the benefit of a preferential allotment reserved for a local displaced person, there are certain qualifying standards.
RatioOfTheDecision	Unless and until a person who wants to take benefit of the preferential reservation qualifies to be a local displaced person, the said benefit is not available to him.
Argument	9.
Argument	In the suits filed by the respondents, the main claim of the plaintiffs was that they were 'local displaced persons' inasmuch as under the various schemes executed by the <ORG>, their lands had been acquired and, therefore, they became local displaced persons entitled for preferential allotment in their favour.
Argument	They also contended that the persons who are similarly situated as themselves, have been allotted plots on the basis of their being local displaced persons.
Argument	Therefore, they are also entitled to the same.
Argument	It was also contended on behalf of some of the respondents that the <ORG> had initially made allotments in their favour, pursuant to which they had even deposited certain sums of money, hence, it is not open t o the <ORG> to cancel the allotments.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
RatioOfTheDecision	As stated above, the trial court decreed some of the suits and dismissed the rest of them.
Fact	The first appellate court decreed all the suits and the same was confirmed by <ORG>.
Argument	It is contended on behalf of the appellant before us that none of the respondents-plaintiffs qualify to be 'local displaced person' so as to entitle him for a preferential allotment under the category of 'local displaced person'.
Argument	It was also contended that the courts below erroneously relied upon a decision of this Court in <CITATION_354806> .
Argument	.
Argument	On behalf of the respondents it was contended that all the respondents qualify to be beneficiaries under the reservation made for local displaced persons because their lands have been acquired.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even otherwise, in view of the fact that similarly situated persons were allotted lands on preferential basis, they too are entitled to the same.
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence, the impugned judgments do not call for any interference.
RatioOfTheDecision	11. learned Counsel for the appellant has f urnished us a Chart showing the dates of Section 36 Notification in each of these cases and the year of purchase by the respective respondents of the lands concerned.
RatioOfTheDecision	A copy of which was handed over to the learned Counsel for the respondents and the statement made in the said Chart has not been controverted before us.
RatioOfTheDecision	From the said Chart, it is seen that the Notification under Section 36 applicable to the acquisitions of the respondents in these appeals are dated 7.6.1963, 27.8.1964, 7.11.1966 and 3.9.1974, a date anterior to the coming into force of the 1975 and 1983 Rules.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not disputed in these cases, on the contrary it is admitted that the acquisitions made under the Notifications referred to above, are not made under the Damaged Areas Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	That being so, the definition as found in the 1954 Rules of 'local displaced person' does not apply to the acquisitions concerned in these appeals.
RatioOfTheDecision	If that be so, none of the respondents could claim a preferential allotment as contemplated under Rule 4(2) of the 1 954 Rules.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the very basis of the claim of preferential allotment is non est in these cases.
RatioOfTheDecision	Assuming for argument's sake that the 1975 or 1983 Rules are applicable to the acquisitions in question even then a bare perusal of the statements in the Chart referred to above, shows none of these respondents qualifies to be a local displaced person even under those Rules because they have not been owners of the lands acquired for a continuous period of two years immediately before' the first publication of the scheme by the <ORG> under Section 36 of the Improvement Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, none of these respondents qualifies to be a local displaced person for the purpose of preferential allotment of plots by the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Since it is the respondents who claim preferential allotment under the Rules, the onus is on them to establish the fact that they do fall within the definition of the 'local displaced persons' so as to entitle them to the benefit of preferential allotment.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, the respondents-plaintiffs hav e failed to discharge this burden and the courts below have erred in presuming that the respondents were entitled to preferential allotment reserved for the category of local displaced person.
RatioOfTheDecision	As a matter of fact, it seems that the courts below have proceeded on the basis that all persons whose lands have been acquired for the execution of any scheme by the <ORG>, automatically become local displaced persons.
RatioOfTheDecision	This view of the courts below is unsustainable.
RatioOfTheDecision	The benefit of reservation made for local displaced person will be available only to such of those persons who fulfil the requirements of the definition of 'local displaced person' under the relevant Rules applicable to the acquisition of their property.
RatioOfTheDecision	12.
RatioOfTheDecision	The lower appellate court and <ORG> relied upon a decision of this <ORG> in <ORG> case (supra) for decreeing the suits.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have carefully perused the above judgment.
Argument	In the said case, the appellant before this <ORG> challenged the validity of a notice issued under Section 36 of th e Improvement Act as also the Award dated 5.10.1972 made by the Collector, determining the compensation for the land acquired under the impugned Notification.
Argument	this <ORG> while refusing to quash the notice under Section 36 of the Act came to the conclusion that the amount awarded as compensation for the acquisition of the land was patently inequitable inasmuch as that while the appellant purchased the land from <ORG> at the price of Rs.
Argument	2,43,050/- on 25.6.1971, the Collector awarded the compensation only in amount of Rs.
Argument	1,10,561/-.
Argument	Because of this inequitable situation, this <ORG> enhanced the compensation to Rs.
Argument	2,43,050/- and in addition, directed payment of Rs.
Argument	50,000/- to the appellant for the improvements made in the acquired land.
Argument	this <ORG>, however, observed in para 9 of the judgment as thus: "Only one aspect remains to be dealt with Dr. <NAME> urged that whenever land of anyone was acquired for the purpose of scheme, each such owner, whose land was acquired, was allotted one plot ad measuring 1 Kanal at reserved price.
RatioOfTheDecision	We see no justification in treating the appellant differently."
RatioOfTheDecision	It is this observation that was relied upon by the courts below in support of its finding.
RatioOfTheDecision	With respect, we are unable to agree with the courts below on this aspect also.
RatioOfTheDecision	In <ORG> case (supra) this Court did not have an opportunity of analysing the definition of local displaced person nor did it have an occasion to consider the applicability of the reservations made under various allotment rules.
RatioOfTheDecision	The observations made in para 9 of the said judgment was based on a submission of the learned Counsel and not based on any principle of law.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, we are of the opinion that the said judgment does not lay down as a proposition of law that every person whose land is acquired for the purpose of execution of a scheme under the Improvement Act will be entitled to preferential allotment of a plot as a local displaced person'.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the said view of the matter, we are of the opinion that the reliance placed o n Northern Carriers' (supra) by the lower appellate court and <ORG> is erroneous.
RatioOfTheDecision	13.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> as well as the lower appellate court also relied upon the fact that the <ORG> had made similar preferential allotments as local displaced person in favour of other persons.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the courts below came to the conclusion that even the plaintiffs-respondents were entitled to such allotment.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, before coming to this conclusion the courts below should have first decided the question whether the allotment in favour of those persons was within the scope of the <ORG> applicable.
RatioOfTheDecision	If it was not within the scope of the <ORG> then even those allotments in favour of other persons will not create a right in the respondents to claim equality with them; may be, if the allotments were made wrongly in favour of those persons, the same may become liable for cancellation, if permissible in law, but that will not create an enforceable right on the respondents to claim similar wrongful allotments in thei r favour.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, even this ground relied upon by <ORG> as well as the lower appellate court is unsustainable.
RatioOfTheDecision	The courts below next relied upon the fact that in regard to some of the respondents, the <ORG> itself at a point of time made allotments and accepted initial deposits towards the consideration of the plot which was subsequently cancelled.
RatioOfTheDecision	Based on those facts, the courts below held that the <ORG> having once allotted the plots and having collected part of the consideration, it could not have cancelled the allotments, probably basing the respondents' case on the principle of promissory estoppel.
RatioOfTheDecision	Here the courts below have failed to notice the legal principle that there is no estoppel against law.
RatioOfTheDecision	The allotment of plots by the <ORG> is controlled by the statutory Rules.
RatioOfTheDecision	Any allotment contrary to those Rules will be against the law.
RatioOfTheDecision	Since the allotments made in favour of some of the respondents was based on wrong application of the reservation made for "local displaced person" those al lotments were contrary to law.
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence, the principle of promissory/equitable estoppel cannot be invoked to protect such illegal allotments.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the said view of the matter, we are unable to sustain the judgments and decrees impugned in these appeals.
RatioOfTheDecision	14.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, we were told at the <ORG> that the amount deposited by some of the respondents/allotters is still with the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	If so, they are entitled to get back those deposit without driving them to other legal proceedings.
RulingByPresentCourt	We direct the <ORG> to refund all such amounts deposited pursuant to the allotments in question made in favour of such of those respondents herein, with 12 per cent interest per annum from the date of deposit till the date of payment.
RulingByPresentCourt	15.
RulingByPresentCourt	For the reasons stated above, these appeals are hereby allowed, setting aside the judgments and decrees impugned in these appeals.
RulingByPresentCourt	No costs.
