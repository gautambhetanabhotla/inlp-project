RatioOfTheDecision	In their Lordships' opinion it is impossible to hold that this annual payment is " agricultural income " within the meaning of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not rent or revenue derived from land ; it is money payable under a contract imposing a personal liability on the covenantor, the discharge of which is secured by a charge on land.
RatioOfTheDecision	The covenantor is at liberty to make the payments out of any of her moneys, and is bound to make them whether the land is sufficiently productive or not.
RatioOfTheDecision	Their Lordships are in agreement with the opinion of the Judges, in India, which upon this point was unanimous.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	Upon the remaining questions there has been a division of opinion among those Judges.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Chief Justice and <NAME> were of opinion that the sum in question was not a capital sum, but was " income " within the meaning of that word as used in Section 12 (I) of the Act, and therefore taxable.
RatioOfTheDecision	On the other hand <NAME> <NAME> took the view that the transaction was a sale of the estate for a capital sum or price of an amount dependent on the duration of the appellant's life, and that each annual payment was an instalment of that capital sum and therefore not taxable as income.
RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RatioOfTheDecision	Their Lordships find themselves in agreement with the Chief Justice and <NAME> Indeed but for the elaborate argument addressed to the Board, they might well have contented themselves with adopting the careful judgment of the Chief Justice which covers the whole ground.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is impossible, without ignoring the plain language of the indenture of March 29, 1930, to treat the annual payments as instalments of a capital sum.
RatioOfTheDecision	To say that they are part of " the price " of the sale does not make them necessarily capital payments.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is only to say that they are part of the consideration for the transfer of the property, and that consideration may well take the form of annual sums which will be income in the hands of the payee.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this indenture the  intention of the appellant is made clear.
RatioOfTheDecision	He was absolute owner of the estate ; he could have given it away, What he does, and what he states in the document he wishes to do, is to part with the estate in order to get rid of his debts, and to obtain for himself an adequate income.
RatioOfTheDecision	He accordingly transfers the estate to the <NAME> and obtains from her in exchange (1) a covenant to pay the debts in the second schedule, (2) a sum of cash to meet the expenses of his daughter's marriage, and (3) a covenant to pay him a life annuity.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is clearly no ordinary bargain and sale by a vendor and purchaser at arm's length, for the money consideration bears no relation to the actual value of the property.
RatioOfTheDecision	The amount ultimately payable by the purchaser depends upon the life of the vendor.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is, their Lordships think, clearly a case where the owner of the estate has exchanged a capital asset for (inter alia) a life annuity which is income in his hands.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not a case in which he has exchanged his estate for a capital su m payable in instalments.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
RatioOfTheDecision	But it was argued that even though the life annuity be income, as distinct from capital, it still is not income taxable under the Act, because the words " income, profits and gains " in Section 12 (I) of the Act must be construed as including only such income as constitutes or provides a profit or gain to the recipient; i.e., that the word " income " is in some way limited by its association with the words " profits " and " gains."
RatioOfTheDecision	This being so, it is said that in view of (a) the true value of the estate (alleged to be about two crores of rupees) and (b) the age of the appellant (alleged to be about forty-seven at the date of the transaction), the annuity could never constitute or provide a "profit" or " gain " to him, and therefore cannot be " income " which is taxable under the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	11.
RatioOfTheDecision	Their Lordships agree with the opinion expressed by the Chief Justice upon this point.
RatioOfTheDecision	The word " income " is not limited by the words " profits " and " gains."
RatioOfTheDecision	Anything which can properly be  described as income, is taxable under the Act unless expressly exempted.
