RatioOfTheDecision	Apart from taking contentions on merits the appellants herein have also contended that there are no circumstances available to invoke extended period in these cases.
Fact	In the case of <ORG> pursuant to an inspection made by the officers in the unit of the appellant on 4.8.93 show cause notice was issued on 19.1.96.
Fact	The period covered is from December 1992 to March 1993.
Fact	In the case of <ORG> an inspection was made on 20/21.7.93.
Fact	Show cause notice was issued on 7.12.95 for a period from September 1992 to March 1993.
RulingByLowerCourt	3.
RulingByLowerCourt	The contentions on the issue of limitation was considered by the Commissioner under the terms  identical to the order dated 19.1.98 passed by him in <ORG>  4.
RulingByLowerCourt	Taking into consideration identical allegations raised by the <ORG> against <ORG>, the assessee's reply which is also identical as in the present case and where the view taken by the Commissioner on the issue of limitation was identical we held that the demand cannot be sustained on the ground of limitation.
RatioOfTheDecision	We find no reason to take a different view in the facts of these appeals.
RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the light of the above, we hold that the demand against the appellants are not sustainable invoking the larger period of limitation.
RatioOfTheDecision	Since the demand cannot be sustained, the penalties imposed on all the appellants also stand vacated.
RatioOfTheDecision	6.
RulingByPresentCourt	In the result, impugned orders are set aside and the appeals stand allowed.
