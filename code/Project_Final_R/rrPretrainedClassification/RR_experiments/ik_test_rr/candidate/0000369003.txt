Fact	JUDGMENT  1.
Fact	This appeal arises oat of proceedings in execution of a decree, It appears that the plaintiff-decree-holder brought a suit for recovery of a certain sum of money against the judgment debtor and sought to attach some of his properties before judgment.
Fact	Thereupon the parties came to terms and it was agreed that the amount claimed would be paid in certain instalments by the judgment-debtor and that on default the plaintiff would be entitled to realise the same from the moveable and immoveable properties of the judgment debtor, and from his person.
Fact	It was further agreed that the property which had been sought to be attached before judgment would remain charged for the decretal amount and would not he transferred by the judgment-debtor.
Fact	2.
Fact	Some of the properties sought to be attached before judgment were non-transferable occupancy holdings and some of the other properties were the dwelling houses of the judgment-debtor who is an agriculturist.
Fact	3.
Fact	A decree was passed on these terms by the Court.
Fact	The d ecree-holder then applied for execution of that decree by attachment and sale of the properties which had been sought to be attached before judgment.
Fact	Objection was raised on behalf of the judgment-debtor on the ground that the properties could not be attached or sold.
RulingByLowerCourt	The Courts below have disallowed the objection on the ground that as the decree-holder had been led to change his position by reason of the arrangement come to under the compromise, the judgment-debtor was equitably stopped from raising the objection that the properties were not saleable.
RulingByLowerCourt	4.
Fact	The judgment debtor has appealed to this Court.
RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	We think that the consent decree is certainly binding upon the judgment-debtor, and upon a proper construction of that decree we think that the properties, which the decree-holder sought to attach before judgment and which are now objected to as being not transferable, were under the terms of the decree liable for satisfaction of the amount of the decree.
RatioOfTheDecision	If that was not the object, we do not see the reaso n why the properties were to remain charged for the decretal amount.
RatioOfTheDecision	The decree, therefore, is binding upon the judgment-debtor.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is unnecessary to consider the question, whether it was necessary for the decree holder to bring a suit upon the charge, because in the present case the decree-holder has simply applied for attachment and sale of the properties as in execution of a decree for money.
RatioOfTheDecision	6.
RulingByPresentCourt	In these circumstances, the appeal must be dismissed with costs, one gold mohur.
