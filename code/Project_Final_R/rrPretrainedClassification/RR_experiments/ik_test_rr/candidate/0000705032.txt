Fact	We have considered the rival submissions of the parties.
Fact	It is an admitted position that the claimant was covered from 11.1.1993 to 10.1.1994 and the flood took place on 24.7.1993 and caused extensive damage to the building.
RulingByLowerCourt	It is submitted that as per the policy, fire policy is covered for flood, storm and tempest on payment of extra 20 per cent premium i.e.
RulingByLowerCourt	Rs.500/-.
RulingByLowerCourt	Therefore, there is no dispute that the incident has taken place during the coverage of the policy and the cause of the damage is flooding of water into the building.
RulingByLowerCourt	Th e basic submission which has been addressed by learned counsel for the appellant was that the company has not covered subsidence.
RulingByLowerCourt	Subsidence means " the gradual caving in or sinking of an area of land".
Fact	But on account of the water flooding into the premises of the claimant-respondent's factory from <ORG>, the land caved in as a result of which one column of the building collapsed.
Fact	The question is whether subsidence was covered in the policy or not.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this connection, a reference may be made to the terms of the policy.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause 8 of the policy deals with exclusions that if any loss is occasioned on account of these events then policy shall not cover.
RulingByLowerCourt	Clause 8 of the Exclusions in the Policy reads as under :  " 8.
RulingByLowerCourt	Any loss or damage occasioned by or through or in consequence directly or indirectly of any of the following occurrence namely,  (a) Earthquake, volcanic eruption, or other convulsion of nature.
Precedent	(b) Typhoon, storm, cyclone, tempest, Hurricane, Tornado, Flood and Inundation.  )
Precedent	War, in vasion, act of foreign enemy, hostilities or warlike operations (whether war be declared or not), Civil War.
Precedent	(d) Mutiny, civil commotion assuming the proportions of or amounting to a popular rising, military rising insurrection, rebellion, military or usurped power.
Precedent	(d) Burning, whether accidental or otherwise, forest bush and jungles and the clearing of lands by fire.
Precedent	In any action, suit or other proceeding where the Company alleges that the reason of the provisions of the above Exclusions any loss or damage is not covered by this Insurance, the burden of providing that such loss or damage is covered shall be upon the insured."
RatioOfTheDecision	A perusal of the aforesaid clause would clearly show that there is no exclusion clause for subsidence.
RatioOfTheDecision	Clause 8(b) only talks of typhoon, storm, cyclone, tempest, hurricane, tornado, flood and inundation.
RatioOfTheDecision	None of the events mentioned above includes subsidence.
RatioOfTheDecision	We fail to understand from where the surveyor has brought the expression "subsidence" although clause 8 which specifically  talks about exclusions, does not mention anything like subsidence.
RatioOfTheDecision	The policy is covered for flood and inundation for which the claimant is covered by paying extra premium, therefore, now to say that the policy has not covered subsidence, which is not a clause in the present policy cannot be sustained.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, on the basis of this ground, repudiation of the claim of the claimant by the appellant does not appear to be justified.
RatioOfTheDecision	Had this been the clause, that if damage is caused on account of sinking and caving of the building i.e.
RatioOfTheDecision	subsidence then perhaps this would have come to the rescue of the company but since in the exclusion clause there is no mention of subsidence, therefore, this ground taken by the appellant-company and by the surveyor to defeat the claim, is absolutely unwarranted.
RatioOfTheDecision	Now, coming to the next question of collapse of the building on account of poor construction of column no.3 of the building, there also the submission appears to be not justified.
RatioOfTheDecision	In fact, the <ORG> has certified th at this building has a first class construction.
RatioOfTheDecision	Normally when the company insures any factory, then their Officers and the Engineers used to inspect the building to find out whether there is any defect in the construction or the construction is of poor quality.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case, the company certified that it is a first class construction, then for some defect which has not been noticed by the company, no benefit could be given to the company for such defect.
RatioOfTheDecision	More so, in the present case, as pointed out that because of defective structure i.e.
Precedent	column No.3, the building has collapsed but the question is what aggravated or accentuated this, factory is in place for more than 12 years & it is on account of flood water entering in factory that has caused this damage.
Precedent	So called defect was aggravated on account of flooding of the water in the premises of the factory, if the flood water had not entered into the factory, perhaps the construction which stood good for 12 years, would have lasted long.
Precedent	The cause of th e damage to the column No.3 of the building was flood water.
Precedent	Therefore, the company cannot escape the liability to compensate the claimant for collapse of the building on account of floods.
RulingByPresentCourt	As a result of above discussion, we are of opinion that the view taken by <ORG> is correct and is fully justified and there is no ground to interfere with the order.
RulingByPresentCourt	As such, the appeal is dismissed.
