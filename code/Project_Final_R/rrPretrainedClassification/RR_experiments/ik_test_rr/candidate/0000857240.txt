RatioOfTheDecision	We have considered the respective arguments/submissions.
RatioOfTheDecision	Article 245 of the Constitution is the source of legislative power of <ORG> and <ORG> legislatures.
Statute	It provides that subject to the provisions of t he Constitution, <ORG> may make laws for the whole or any part of the territory of India, and the legislature of a <ORG> may make laws for the whole or any part of the <ORG>.
Statute	The legislative field of the <ORG> and <ORG> legislatures has been specified in Article 246.
Statute	In terms of Clause (1) of Article 246 , <ORG> has exclusive power to make laws with respect to any of the matters enumerated in List I in the Seventh Schedule.
Statute	Under Clause (2) the <ORG> and subject to Clause (1), the legislature of any <ORG> also have power to make laws with respect to any of the matters enumerated in List III in the Seventh Schedule.
Statute	Subject to Clauses (1) and (2), the legislature of <ORG> has exclusive power to make laws for such <ORG> or any part thereof with respect to any of the matters enumerated in List II in the Seventh Schedule.
Statute	It is thus evident that <ORG> has exclusive power to legislate with respect to any of the matters enumerated in List I and <ORG> legislatures enjoys similar power with r espect to any of the matters enumerated in List II.
Statute	The combined effect of the different clauses of Article 246 is that in respect of any matter falling within List I, <ORG> has exclusive power of legislation, whereas the <ORG> legislature has exclusive power to make laws for such <ORG> or any part thereof with respect to any of the matters enumerated in List II in the Seventh Schedule and with respect to the matters enumerated in List III, both the <ORG> and <ORG> legislature have power to make laws.
Statute	Article 254 which contains mechanism for resolution of conflict between Central and <ORG> legislations enacted with respect to any matter enumerated in List III of the Seventh Schedule reads as under:  "254.
Statute	Inconsistency between laws made by <ORG> and laws made by the Legislatures of <ORG>s.-- (1) If any provision of a law made by the Legislature of a <ORG> is repugnant to any provision of a law made by <ORG> which <ORG> is competent to enact, or to any provision of an existing law with  respect to one of the matters enumerated in the Concurrent List, then, subject to the provisions of clause (2), the law made by <ORG>, whether passed before or after the law made by the <ORG> of such State, or, as the case may be, the existing law, shall prevail and the law made by the <ORG> of the State shall, to the extent of the repugnancy, be void.
Statute	(2) Where a law made by the <ORG> of a State with respect to one of the matters enumerated in the Concurrent List contains any provision repugnant to the provisions of an earlier law made by <ORG> or an existing law with respect to that matter, then, the law so made by the <ORG> of such State shall, if it has been reserved for the consideration of the President and has received his assent, prevail in that State:  Provided that nothing in this clause shall prevent <ORG> from enacting at any time any law with respect to the same matter including a law adding to, amending, varying or repealing the law so made by the <ORG> ture of the <ORG>."
