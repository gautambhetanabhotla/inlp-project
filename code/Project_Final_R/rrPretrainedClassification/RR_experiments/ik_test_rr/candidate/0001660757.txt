RatioOfTheDecision	We have already decided that the office in question is an hereditary one.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question now is whether the transfer of it by the last  holder to the plaintiff's <NAME> was a valid one.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to general principles, a religious office cannot, prima facie, lie made the subject of alienation.
RatioOfTheDecision	The succession to such an office is governed, in the first instance, by the will of the founder, and, in the absence of direct evidence on that point, by usage of the particular institution from which the founder's will may be inferred.
RatioOfTheDecision	A religious office appears to us to stand with reference to alienability on a different footing from private property.
Precedent	It was argued at the last hearing on the authority of the case of <NAME> v. <ORG> 6 Bom., 298 that the holder of a religious office may transfer it to one who is in the line of descent, whether he be the next heir or a possible future heir, and that the plaintiff's <NAME> was, in the present instance, the next heir.
Precedent	The finding, however, returned by the Subordinate Judge shows that he was not the sole next heir, because he had three brothers.
Precedent	In <NAME> v. <ORG> 6 Mad., 76 it is  observed by the learned Judge, with reference to a contention that the alienee was of the same caste and sect as the alienor: "To hold so would tend to public mischief in inducing needy incumbents of hereditary religious offices, who desired to sell them to give a dishonest recognition, to qualifications which, in fact, were not the qualifications demanded by the nature of the office."
RatioOfTheDecision	Unless the alienee is the sole heir, the alienor might be under the temptation to make the office the subject of bargain and thereby defeat the intention of the founder.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was in this view that we called for a finding at the former hearing.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are not prepared to dissent from the dictum above quoted, and to hold that in the absence of special usage an alienation would be valid if made in favour of any person other than the sole immediate heir.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was then argued that in the case before us the brothers of the plaintiff's <NAME> consented to the alienation in his favour, and that there is evidence to that effect on the reco rd.
RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RatioOfTheDecision	On looking at the evidence of <NAME>, one of the brothers, we find no distinct admission regarding the office.
RatioOfTheDecision	Moreover this point was not taken at the last hearing, nor were we asked to call for a finding as to the alleged consent.
RatioOfTheDecision	We cannot at this stage allow this point to be raised and order a new trial regarding it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Of course it is not intended that those who may have a claim by hereditary right as the legal heir should be in any wise prejudiced by this judgment.
RulingByPresentCourt	We must reverse the decree of the Courts below and dismiss the suit.
