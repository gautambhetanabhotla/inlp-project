Fact	Before dealing with the case law on the p oint, we shall examine the relevant provisions of the Indian Evidence Act , relating to the admissibility of recitals as to boundaries in documents not inter partes.
Fact	The relevant sections are Sections 11 , 13 , 32 , 155 and 157 .
Fact	We shall first take up Section 11 , which deals with facts relevant when a right or custom is in question, one such fact under S. 13(a) being any transaction by which the right or custom in question was created, claimed, modified, recognised, asserted or denied.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even giving the widest interpretation to the word 'transaction' we consider it difficult to bring the recitals as to boundaries in documents not inter partes under any of the verbs "created" "modified" "recognised".
RatioOfTheDecision	"asserted" or "denied".
RatioOfTheDecision	We shall next take up Section 32 which deals with statements written or verbal of facts made by a person who is dead.
RatioOfTheDecision	In order to render a person's statement admissible under Section 32(3) , such a statement must be against his pecuniary or proprietary interest.
RatioOfTheDecision	Further what is admissible u nder Section 32(3) is only the statement and not the document containing the statement.
RatioOfTheDecision	We therefore, consider that the documents in question cannot be brought under Section 32(3) .
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 32(7) deals with the statements contained in any deal, will or other document which relates to any such transaction as is mentioned in Section 13(a) .
RatioOfTheDecision	In the view that we have taken that such documents are not admissible under Section 13(a) , they are not equally admissible under section 32(7) .
RatioOfTheDecision	Regarding Section 11 of the Act which also deals with relevancy of facts, there is a distinction between the existence of a fact and a statement as to its existence.
RatioOfTheDecision	<CITATION_1003128> , AIR 1934 All 406 (FB).
RatioOfTheDecision	The Section makes the existence of facts admissible, and not statement cannot fall under Section 32(3) , it cannot equally fall under Section 11 .
