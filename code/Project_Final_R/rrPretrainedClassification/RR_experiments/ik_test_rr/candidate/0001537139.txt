RatioOfTheDecision	If a temple gets into ruins and a person, as a matter of mere benefaction, erects fresh buildings and dedicates them to the same sort of worship as had been carried on in the old temple, such person may properly be treated as the founder of the new temple, even though, in constructing it, he used materials of the former temple or other property belonging thereto.

RatioOfTheDecision	No doubt power to alienate one's property is not exactly of the same nature as the power to appoint a successor to the office, for the former is a component part or incident of ownership.
RatioOfTheDecision	But where in such an estate as that of a trustee the devolution is by appointment, the power to appoint a successor is as much an incident of the trusteeship as the right to alienate is an incident of ownership.
