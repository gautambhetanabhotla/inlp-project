Fact	8) POINT: The first argument of the appellant is concerned, it is true that as per Ex.B.2the driver possessed learners licence to driver non- transport auto rickshaw by the date of accident.
Fact	The crime vehicle was a transport auto rickshaw.
RulingByLowerCourt	Hence, he was not having effective driving licence.
RulingByLowerCourt	Having regard to it and relying upon the decision of <ORG> in Swaran Singhs case (2 supra), the <ORG> held that it is not a case of driver not having licence at all and there was no gross violation of the terms of the policy and further the violation was not a material violation of the terms of the policy.
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG> thus directed <ORG> to pay and recover the compensation.
RulingByLowerCourt	The above finding of the <ORG> is now challenged in this appeal on the ground that pay and recovery can be ordere d by <ORG> under Article 142 of the Constitution of India and the same cannot be ordered by the Tribunal and further even assuming that such power is vested with <ORG>, the same is a discretion but not mandatory in every case as per the dictum laid down in <NAME> case (2 supra).
RatioOfTheDecision	I am afraid, this argument is not correct.
Fact	In <NAME> case (2 supra), <ORG> was dealing with wide spectrum of defence pleas of Insurance Companies basing on the deficiencies in driving licences.
Fact	Such deficiencies are:  a) Fake driving licenses of the driver.
Fact	b) Driver not having licence whatsoever.
Fact	c) No renewal of driving licence as on the date of accident.
Fact	d) License granted for one class or description of vehicle but vehicle  involved in accident was of different class or description.
Fact	e) Driver holding only a learners licence.
Fact	a) <ORG> after discussing various issues involved in this  regard, held that an Insurance Company in order to succeed in its defence pleas touching the driving  licence issues must:  (i) Firstly establish that the insured was guilty of negligence and failed to exercise reasonable care in the matter of fulfilling the condition of the policy regarding use of vehicle by a duly licensed driver or one who was not disqualified to drive at the relevant time.
Fact	(ii) Secondly, the breach which was committed by the insured was so fundamental as is found to have contributed to the cause of the accident.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even upon establishing the above conditions by <ORG>, the <ORG> can direct that the insurer is liable to be reimbursed by the insured for the compensation and other amounts which it has been compelled to pay to the third party under the award of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	b) In the light of above decision, when the facts and evidence in the instant case are perused, as rightly observed by the <ORG>, it is not a case of driver not holding licence at all.
RatioOfTheDecision	It cannot be said that his possessing a different licence was the fundamental cause for the accident.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the  <ORG> rightly directed <ORG> to pay and recover the compensation.
Precedent	<NAME> the argument of appellant that such power was vested only with <ORG> under Article 142 of the Constitution of India is concerned, in Swaran Singhs case (2 supra), <ORG> held that the <ORG> has necessary power and jurisdiction to decide disputes inter se between insurer and insured and further held as follows: Where on adjudication of the claim under the Act the tribunal arrives at a conclusion that the insurer has satisfactorily proved its defence in accordance with the provisions of Section 149(2) read with Sub-section (7), as interpreted by this Court above, the <ORG> can direct that the insurer is liable to be reimbursed by the insured for the compensation and other amounts which it has been compelled to pay to the third party under the award of the tribunal.
RatioOfTheDecision	c) In view of the above clarification, the contrary argument of the appellant cannot be appreciated.
RatioOfTheDecision	Hence the cited decisions wil l not improve his case.
RatioOfTheDecision	9) <NAME> quantum of compensation is concerned, the <ORG> having regard to the recitals in Ex.A.1FIR to the effect that the deceased was running a rice mill on lease, fixed the notional income of the deceased as Rs.3,000/- p.m and accordingly, computed compensation.
RatioOfTheDecision	I see no irregularity in it as contended by the appellant.
RatioOfTheDecision	So at the outset, I find no merits in the appeal.
