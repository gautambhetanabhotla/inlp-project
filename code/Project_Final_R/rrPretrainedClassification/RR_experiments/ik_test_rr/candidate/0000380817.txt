RatioOfTheDecision	7.
RulingByPresentCourt	We shall take up each of the three amounts covered by the questions separately.
RulingByPresentCourt	We shall first consider the rent received by the assessee on the lease of machinery and examine whether it is income attributable to the priority industry, coming within the scope of s. 80-I of the Act.
Statute	This section was deleted with effect from 1st April, 1973.
Statute	At the relevant time, when the section was in operation, it ran as follows :  "Deduction in respect of profits and gains from priority industries in the case of certain companies.
Statute	-(1) In the case of a company to which this section applies, where the gross total income includes any profits and gains attributable to any priority industry, there shall be allowed, in accordance with and subject to the provisions of this sect ion, a deduction from such profits and gains of an amount equal to eight per cent.
Statute	thereof, in computing the total income of the company...."  8.
Statute	There are two other sub-sections to s. 80-I of the Act, and it is not necessary to extract them here.
Statute	The section would apply where the gross total income included any profits and gains attributable to any priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	When once the operation of this section is attracted, then there has to be an allowance by way of deduction from the profits attributable to the priority industry, an amount equal to eight per cent.
RatioOfTheDecision	of such profits in computing the total income.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, the point that requires scrutiny is, whether the rent received from the lease of the machinery used in the priority industry is a profit attributable to the priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not in dispute that the product manufactured by the assessee comes within the scope of the 6th-Schedule to the Act as it was in force at the relevant time.
RatioOfTheDecision	The term "priority industry" has been defined in s. 80 B(7) of the Act as meaning, among others, manufacture or production of any one or more of the articles or things specified in the list in the 6th Schedule.
RatioOfTheDecision	The said Schedule thus describes such of those articles or things the manufacture of which would constitute a priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	Item (20) describes automobile ancillaries as one of the articles and things, the manufacture of which would constitute a priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not in dispute that the assessee was manufacturing automobile ancillaries before the factory was leased out and even after leasing out, the factory was producing the same articles.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question is, whether the rent received would, on these facts, be profits attributable to the priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
RatioOfTheDecision	The expression "attributable to" occurring in a similar provision in the same Act came to be considered by <ORG> in <CITATION_81055>  113 ITR 84.
RatioOfTheDecision	In that case, the assessee was running a priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	It had certain machinery which was sold.
RatioOfTheDecision	The balancing charge as result  of the sale of the machinery was brought to tax under s. 41(2) of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The question was, whether the amount so brought to tax could be treated to be profits attributable to the priority industry so as to be eligible for exemption or relief contemplated by s. 80E which corresponds to s. 80-I of the Act, now under consideration.
RatioOfTheDecision	In construing the expression attributable to", <ORG> pointed out at a page 93 :  "As regards the aspect emerging from the expression 'attributable to' occurring in the phrase 'profits and gains attributable to the business of' the specified industry (here generation and distribution of electricity) on which the learned Solicitor-General relied, it will be pertinent to observe that the legislature has deliberately used the expression 'attributable to' and not expression 'derived from'.
RatioOfTheDecision	It cannot be disputed that the expression 'attributable to' is certainly wider in import than the expression 'derived from'.
RatioOfTheDecision	Had the expression 'derived from' been used, it could have with  some force been contended that a balancing charge arising from the sale of old machinery and buildings cannot be regarded as profits and gains derived from the conduct of the business of generation and distribution of electricity.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this connection, it may be pointed out that whenever the <ORG> wanted to give a restricted meaning in the manner suggested by the learned Solicitor-General, it has used the expression 'derived from', as, for instance, in section 80J .
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, since the expression of wider import, namely 'attributable to', has been used, the <ORG> intended to cover receipts from sources other than the actually conduct of the business of generation and distribution of electricity."
RatioOfTheDecision	11.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is clear from the passage given above that the term "attributable to" is wider that the term "derived from".
RatioOfTheDecision	We have thus to find out whether the profit is ascribable to the running of the priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is nothing in the provision which requires that the assessee alone should run the p riority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may be that on account of business exigencies the <ORG>, instead of directly running the factory, my lease it to another and purchase the end product.
RatioOfTheDecision	So long as the end product is manufactured in the factory, the manner in which the business is carried on is not really material.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> may run the industry himself or may have it run by another.
RatioOfTheDecision	He may appoint a manager, for instance, and run the business.
Statute	He may lease it out.
Statute	So long as the factory manufactures the products coming within the list of articles and things in the Sixth Schedule, the <ORG> would be eligible for the relief under s. 80-I of the Act, as the profits derived from the factory either by lease or otherwise would be attributable to the priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> must have something to do with the factory and the income earned must have nexus with the priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	If these attributes are satisfied, then the <ORG> would be eligible for the concession under s. 80-I of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	12.
RatioOfTheDecision	The last sent ence in the passage from <ORG> judgment extracted above cannot be understood to mean that any income received from sources other than the actual conduct of the business of the priority industry would qualify for the relief, however remote the connection of such income may be.
RatioOfTheDecision	This passage has to be understood in the sense that it would envisage the relief being granted in all cases where there is some direct nexus between the income and the priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	The nature of the source would be immaterial.
RatioOfTheDecision	For instance, in the present case, even if the income from the lease of the factory had been assessed under the head "Other sources", the above passage would support the view that it is eligible for the concession.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is in that sense that the last sentence of <ORG> judgment has to be understood.
RatioOfTheDecision	13.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned counsel for the Commissioner contended that the source or receipt by the assessee was only the lease amount and that it cannot have anything to do with the priority indust ry contemplated by s. 80-I of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The source cannot be divorced from its origin, namely, the factory in the present case.
RatioOfTheDecision	As the factory is producing the automobile ancillaries, the income either by way of rent or by direct manufacture would qualify for the relief under s. 80-I of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The result is that the rental income would have to be taken as eligible for the relief under s. 80-I of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	14.
RatioOfTheDecision	We would now take up for consideration the amount derived by the assessee as and by way of export incentives.
RatioOfTheDecision	From the statement of facts given above, it would be clear that this incentive has been given to the assessee in order to make good the loss that arose to it by reason of the export fetching a lower price than what the assessee would have obtained if the products had been sold in India.
RatioOfTheDecision	The export incentive is thus bound up with the manufacture and sale of automobile ancillaries and is, therefore, attributable to the business of priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	The amount of incentives would, therefore, quali fy for the relief under s. 80-I of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	The last item to be considered is the interest earned by the assessee from the fixed deposit with <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It had also derived interest on deposits from other banks, etc., as well as from the <ORG> Bonds.
RatioOfTheDecision	With references to the rest of the items, the denial of the relief under s. 80-I of the Act is not in dispute.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is only with reference to the interest received from <ORG> on the fixed deposit that the assessee claims that such interest should be attributed to the priority industry and should qualify for the relief envisaged by s. 80-I of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The connection between the fixed deposit and the priority industry is stated to be that the amount was found to be surplus in the assessee's hands and it was, therefore, invested in the bank and that thereafter when the assessee wanted money from the bank, on the security of the said fixed deposit, amounts were lent by the bank.
Fact	The total amount in the fixed deposit was  Rs.
Fact	11,00,000 and the assessee had borrowed as on the closing date of each of these two years Rs.
Fact	4,50,000 and Rs.
Precedent	5,81,255.62 respectively.
Precedent	16.
Precedent	<ORG>, in dealing with this claim of the assessee, has observed as follows :  "Such borrowing was obviously for business purposes and the interest paid by the assessee on such borrowing had been included in the expenditure.
Fact	The interest referred to above was interest received by the assessee from the said bank on such fixed deposit.
Fact	(The above details are apparent from the balance-sheet of the assessee as on March 31, 1970, and March 31, 1971).
RatioOfTheDecision	In these circumstances, the making of the deposit and the borrowing of the moneys on the same fixed deposit can be treated as part of the assessee's business.
RatioOfTheDecision	The result is, the income received from the bank by way of interest on such fixed deposit can be said to be attributable to the profits of the priority industry."
RatioOfTheDecision	17.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> seems to have missed the real point that arises in this case.
RatioOfTheDecision	The real question is, whether the interest derived from the deposit is attributable to the priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	The surplus funds, whenever deposited, in the bank were clearly not required for the priority industry and that is how it came to be kept in a fixed deposit for a term.
Argument	If nothing more had happened, the fixed deposits could only be taken as originating from the funds not required by the priority industry.
Argument	Its connection with the priority industry would only be remote.
Argument	18.
Argument	As and when the assessee required moneys, it approached the bank for loans.
Argument	It was at that stage that the bank took this fixed deposit as security for the loans.
Argument	We are, however, concerned with the deposit having nexus with the priority industry at the stage at which the amounts were deposited in the bank and started earning interest.
Argument	Else, it would be enough for an assessee to offer as security all his properties and claim the entire amount of income earned from all of them as eligible for the relief under s. 80-I .
Argument	Such a result  could not have been intended.
Argument	Just as any person can make a fixed deposit in the bank, the assessee also did.
RatioOfTheDecision	For instance, in the present case itself, there were deposits in <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The assessee did not get relief in respect of the interest income from the said deposits as attributable to the priority industry, and in our opinion rightly, as there was no connection between the fixed deposit and the priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have to consider the question of the interest income at the stage at which the deposit was made and the use of the deposit receipt as security does not make the income that of the priority industry.
Precedent	19.
Precedent	In fact, in a case which arose in this court in Addl.
Precedent	<CITATION_1324979> .
Precedent	119 ITR 523, the question of the character of the interest or its relation to the priority industry came to be examined.
Argument	That was a case of a company engaged in the distribution of electricity.
Argument	It had to create a "contingencies reserve".
Argument	The sums appropriated to that reserve had to be invested in approved  securities contemplated by the Indian Trusts Act , 1882.
Argument	The assessee received interest on such investments and claimed the relief as a priority industry in respect of such interest.
Argument	It was contended by the assessee that since it was a statutory obligation to invest the amount available in the contingencies reserve in an approved security and since it could not carry on the business without complying with statutory obligation, the interest arising from such investment should be considered as attributable to the profits and gains of the priority industry within the meaning of s. 80-I of the Act.
Argument	This contention was negatived by this court, At page 526, it was pointed out that deduction is possible only in respect of profits and gains arising from the specific activities or business of generation or distribution of electricity or any other form of power and that the immediate source of income in that case was the investment in <ORG>.
Argument	securities.
Argument	It was added (p. 526) :  "In order to attract the provisions of s.  80-I the income should be in the nature of profits or gains arising out of the business of a priority industry.
Argument	Neither the statutory reserve not the securities in which the amounts have been invested can be considered to be a trading asset of the assessee.
Argument	Any investment of the contingency reserve, therefore, could not be taken as having a direct relationship or nexus with the business of generating and supplying energy.
Precedent	The interest received on such investment, therefore, could not be considered to be profit or gain attributable to the business of the assessee which alone can be taken into account for the purpose of s. 80-I of the Act."
Precedent	20.
Precedent	During the course of the argument there was some debate as to whether this particular passage would have validity after the judgment of <ORG> in [1978] 113 ITR 84 ( <CITATION_81055> ).
RatioOfTheDecision	In the context of the pronouncement of <ORG>, the view taken by this court that the profits and gains must arise from the specific activities or business of genera tion of electricity may have to be reconsidered.
RatioOfTheDecision	As "attributable to" is wider than "derived from", the relief is not confined only to the profits of the priority industry strictly so called.
RatioOfTheDecision	It would have a wider ambit.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, the conclusion arrived at in that case is unexceptionable because the character of the interest is different from the character of the income attributable to the priority industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	The result is that in so for as the interest income was concerned, the <ORG> acted erroneously in granting the relief.
