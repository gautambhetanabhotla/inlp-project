Fact	ORDER  1.
Fact	Mr. <NAME>, learned Counsel for the State of Jammu & Kashmir has placed on record a copy of the teleprinter message from the concerned authority dated August 30, 1984 informing him that Mr. <NAME> the detenu, whose detention has been challenged in this Writ Petition under Article 32 of the Constitution has been released on August 24, 1984.
Fact	This teleprinter message is followed by a letter confirming the same.
Fact	Mr. <NAME>, learned Counsel has authenticated the letter as well as the teleprinter message, and placed them on record.
Argument	2.
Argument	Mrs. <NAME>, learned Counsel for the petitioner and a member of the working committee of <ORG> has filed this writ petition for writ of Habeas Corpus questioning the validity of the detention of <NAME>, who is a sitting Member of <ORG> of Jammu & Kashmir State.
Argument	When the letter and teleprinter message were shown to Mrs. <NAME>, she said that Mr. <NAME> has not been released because she had contacted all the p ossible sources enquiring about the release of the petitioner, but he could not be contacted and therefore there is reason to believe that the statement made by Mr. <NAME> is not correct.
Argument	We are not persuaded to accept this submission because it is difficult to believe that the <ORG> would make through its learned Counsel a wholly false statement in respect of a detenu.
Argument	3.
Argument	The petitioner is at liberty to take other steps if the petitioner is not released as stated by the learned Counsel.
Argument	4.
RulingByPresentCourt	This petition has become infructuous and stands disposed of accordingly.
