RatioOfTheDecision	No doubt the appeal in this case is one which is given by Section 4 76B of the Criminal P.C., but the very words used in Section 476B of the Criminal P.C.
RatioOfTheDecision	indicate with sufficient clearness that the <ORG> to which the appeal lies is one to which the <ORG> making or filing the complaint is subordinate; in other words, if it is a civil <ORG> which has made an order under Section 476 of the Criminal P.C., the appeal against such an order must lie to and be heard by the authority or tribunal to which such civil <ORG> is subordinate.
RatioOfTheDecision	It follows, therefore, that the original order, having been made by a <ORG> in a civil suit, the appeal against an order by the <ORG> under Section 476 of the Criminal P.C.
RatioOfTheDecision	lay to the District Judge to whom the <ORG> was subordinate.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, it would follow that, although the appeal itself is one which is allowed by the Code of Criminal Procedure, the appeal must be heard by the District Judge to whom the <ORG> is subordinate, i.e., by an appellate <ORG> exercising civil appellate jurisdiction.
