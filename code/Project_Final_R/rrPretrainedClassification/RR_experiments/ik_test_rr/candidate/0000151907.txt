Fact	In <ORG> v. <ORG> [1992 Suppl.
Argument	(3) S.C.C.215], this Court had, while declaring that Article 16(4) does not contemplate or permit reservation in the matter of promotions, declared that for the several reasons stated therein, the reservations already made shall continue for a period of five years from the date of the said judgment.
Argument	In Para 829 [at Page 747] of the majority judgment, it was directed that "our decision on this question shall operate only prospectively and shall not affect promotions already made, whether on temporary, officiating or regular/permanent basis.
Argument	It is further directed that wherever reservations are already provided in the matter of promotion-be it <ORG> or <ORG>, or for that matter servi ces under any corporation, authority or body falling under the definition of '<ORG>' in Article 12- such reservations shall continue in operation for a period of five years from this day".
Argument	Then, in the next para, Para 831, the majority judgment made the following observations:  "We must also make it clear that it would not be impermissible for the <ORG> to extend concessions and relaxations to members of reserved categories in the matter of promotion without compromising the efficiency of the administration.
Argument	The relaxation concerned in <CITATION_1130169> , and the concessions namely carrying forward of vacancies and provisions for in-service coaching/training in <CITATION_1111529> , , are instances of such concessions and relaxations.
Argument	However, it would not be permissible to prescribe lower qualifying marks or a lesser level of evaluation for the members of reserved categories since that would compromise the efficiency of administration.
Argument	We reiterate that while it may be permissible to prescribe a reasonably  lesser qualifying marks or evaluation for the OBCs, SCs and STs - consistent with the efficiency of administration and the nature of duties attaching to the office concerned - in the matter of direct recruitment, such a course would not be permissible in the matter of promotions for the reasons recorded hereinabove."
Argument	<NAME>, J. expressed himself on this aspect in Para 549, which reads:  "There is no doubt that the meaning of the various expressions used in Article 16 , viz., 'matters relating to employment or appointment to any office', and 'appointments or posts' cannot be whittled down to mean only initial recruitment and hence the normal rule of the service jurisprudence of the loss of the birth marks cannot be applied to the appointments made under the article.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, as pointed out earlier, the exclusive quota is not the only form of reservation and where the resort to it such as in the promotions, results in the inefficiency of the administration, it is illegal.
RatioOfTheDecision	But that is not the end of the road no r is a backward class employee helpless on account of its absence.
RatioOfTheDecision	Once he gets an equal opportunity to show his talent by coming into the mainstream, all he needs is the facility to achieve equal results.
RatioOfTheDecision	The facility can be and must be given to him in the form of concessions, exemptions etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	such as relaxation of age, extra attempts for passing the examinations, extra training period etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	along with the machinery for impartial assessment as stated above.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such facilities when given are also a part of the reservation programme and do not fall foul of the requirement of the efficiency of the administration.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such facilities, however, are imperative if, not only the equality of opportunity but also the equality of results is to be achieved which is the true meaning of the right to equality."
RatioOfTheDecision	The question before <ORG> was whether the saving of reservations provided in Para 829 takes within its purview the provisions providing for lesser qualifying marks in the qualifying exami nation for promotion.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> held that inasmuch as the expression "reservation" provided in Article 16(4) takes within its fold concessions and facilities including provision for lesser qualifying marks in the qualifying examination for promotion, such a provision is also saved by virtue of the declaration contained in Para 829.
RatioOfTheDecision	The declaration made by the <ORG> reads thus:  "14.
RatioOfTheDecision	We therefore hold that the status quo in the matter of reservations in promotion required to be maintained by <ORG> for five years, would also include status quo being maintained in the matter of prescribing lesser qualifying marks in the qualifying examination for promotion, within which period the authorities could take the steps indicated in the judgment.
RatioOfTheDecision	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	In view of what is stated above, we hold that the impugned Memorandum cannot be assailed and are legally sustainable."
RatioOfTheDecision	The memorandum referred to in Para 15 aforesaid is the Memorandum dated January 21, 1977 which provided that where the promotions are  made on the basis of seniority subject to fitness and where a qualifying examination is held to determine the fitness of the candidates for such promotions, suitable relaxation in the qualifying standard in such examination should be made for Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes to the extent of the relaxation to be decided on each occasion, whenever such examination was held, taking into account all relevant factors including the number of vacancies reserved, performance of Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe candidates as well as the general candidates in that examination, the minimum standard of fitness for appointment to the post and the overall strength of the cadre and that of the Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe in that cadre [Purport of the Office Memorandum taken from Para-2 of the <ORG>'s judgment].
RatioOfTheDecision	Pursuant to the said Office Memorandum, <ORG> has been issuing orders from time to time providing lesser qualifying marks for passing the qualifying examination prescribed for promotion , in the case of Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe.
RatioOfTheDecision	The precise question raised before the <ORG> was whether the said provision is saved by the declaration contained in Para 829 of this Court's judgment.
RatioOfTheDecision	Having heard the counsel for the parties and considered the various opinions in <NAME>, we are of the opinion that the very posing of the question as well as the answer given by the <ORG> are erroneous and unsustainable.
RatioOfTheDecision	According to Para 831, extracted hereinabove, while it is "permissible to prescribe a reasonably lesser qualifying marks or evaluation for the OBCs, SCs and STs - consistent with the efficiency of administration and the nature of duties attaching to the office concerned - in the matter of direct requirement, such a course would not be permissible in the matter of promotion for the reasons recorded hereinabove".
RatioOfTheDecision	At the same time, it is held that "it would not be impermissible for the <ORG> to extend concessions and relaxations to members of reserved categories of the administrati on.
RatioOfTheDecision	The relaxation concerned in <NAME> and the concessions namely carrying forward of vacancies and provisions for in-service coaching/training in Karamchari Sangh are instances of such concessions and relaxations.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, it would not be permissible to prescribe lower qualifying marks or a lesser level of evaluation for the members of reserved categories since that would compromise the efficiency of administration."
RatioOfTheDecision	The relaxation concerned in <ORG> v N.M. <NAME> [1976 (2) S.C.C.310] is also set out in Para 713 of the majority judgment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The concession was providing "temporary exemption to members already in service belonging to any of the Scheduled Castes or Scheduled Tribes from passing all tests (unified, special or departmental test) for a period of two years.....
RatioOfTheDecision	They were required to pass the tests within the period of exemption."
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as the concessions in <CITATION_1111529> [1981 (1) S.C.C.246] are concerned, they are specified in Para 831 itself as referring to carrying forward vacancies  and provisions for in-service coaching/training.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is thus clear from a reading of Para 831 that so far as promotions are concerned, it is not permissible to provide lesser qualifying marks of evaluation in favour of OBCs/SCs/STs since that would compromise the efficiency of administration, while the same can be provided in the matter of direct recruitment.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as promotions are concerned the only provision permitted other than the provision for reservation is providing the concessions and reservations like the ones provided in <ORG>, which do not take in provision for lower qualifying marks or a lesser level of evaluation.
RatioOfTheDecision	To the same effect are the observations of <NAME>,J.
RatioOfTheDecision	in Para 549, which we have extracted hereinabove.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned Judge also speaks of "concessions/exemptions etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	such as relaxation of age, extra attempts for passing the examination, extra training period etc."
RatioOfTheDecision	The other learned Judges in their separate opinions have merely held that reservation in the matter  of promotions is not permissible under Article 16(4).
RatioOfTheDecision	They have not separately dealt with the concessions and facilities which can be extended to these reserved categories.
RatioOfTheDecision	[Of course, one of the learned Judges who constituted the majority, <NAME> (as the learned Chief Justice then was) was of the opinion that it was not necessary to consider in that case the question whether Article 16(4) permits reservation in the matter of promotions.]
RatioOfTheDecision	In the light of the fact that <NAME> and <NAME>,JJ.
RatioOfTheDecision	have agreed with the conclusions arrived at in the majority judgment and in the absence of any contrary proposition in the opinion of any other learned Judge, it must be held that the law on this question is the one declared in Para 831.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are, therefore, of the opinion that so far as the provision for lower qualifying marks or lesser level of evaluation in the matter of promotion is concerned, it is not permissible under Article 16(4) in view of the command contained in Article 335 of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	In other words,  even if it is assumed for the sake of argument that reservation is permitted by Article 16(4) in the matter of promotions, a provision for lower qualifying marks or lesser level of evaluation is not permissible in the matter of promotions, by virtue of Article 335.
RatioOfTheDecision	If so, there can be no question of such a provision or "concession", as it is called by the <ORG>, being saved by the declaration in Para 829 of the said judgment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned counsel for the parties referred to certain decisions of this <ORG> but, in our opinion, it is wholly unnecessary to refer to them since none of them deal with the question at issue.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeal is accordingly allowed with the above clarifications.
