Precedent	But if the court enters upon a finding that any of the remaining persons who participated in the crime had shareo common intention with the main perpetrators of the crime, the court is not helpless in seeking the aid of Section 34 () to enter a conviction against such persons arraigned as accused.

RatioOfTheDecision	In such a situation, the absence of a charge under one or other of the various heads of criminal liability for the offence cannot be said to be fatal by itself, and before a conviction for the substantive offence, without a charge, can be set aside, prejudice will have to be made put.

Precedent	Though the Code does not make any distinction between an appeal from acquittal and an appeal from conviction so far as powers of the appellate court are concerned, certain unwritten rules of adjudication have consistently been following by Judges while dealing with appeals against acquittal.
Precedent	No doubt, <ORG> has full power to review the evidence and to arrive at its own independent conclusion whether the appeal is against conviction or acquittal.
Precedent	But while dealing with an appeal against acquittal the appellate court has to bear in mind: first, that there is a general presumption in favour of the ignorance of the person accused in criminal cases that presumption is only strengthened by the acquittal.
Precedent	The second is, every accused is entitled to the benefit of reasonable doubt regarding his guilt and when the trial court acquitted him.
Precedent	He would retain that benefit in the appellate co urt also.
Precedent	Thus, appellate court in appeals against acquittals has to proceed more cautiously and only if there is absolute assurance of the guilt of the accused, upon the evidence on record, that the order of acquittal is liable to the interfered with or disturbed.
