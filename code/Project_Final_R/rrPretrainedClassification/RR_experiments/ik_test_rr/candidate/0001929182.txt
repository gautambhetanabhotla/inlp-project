RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	As already stated, both the <ORG> and the <ORG> have found that the change in the method of valuation of the stock from "total cost" to "direct cost" is bona fide, a that it is intended to be followed cons istently from year to year and that the change is not restricted for a particular period.
RatioOfTheDecision	Before us the said finding has not been challenged.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, we have to proceed on the basis that the assessee is entitled to change the method of valuation of the stock from "total cost" to "direct cost".
RatioOfTheDecision	The difference between the view taken by the <ORG> and the <ORG> is that while the <ORG> felt that the new method of valuation of stock should be adopted both for closing and opening stock, the <ORG> has held that being the first year when the change is contemplated on a permanent basis, the closing stock alone can be valued on the new basis leaving the value of the opening stock arrived at as per the total cost method intact.
RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RatioOfTheDecision	The main contention of the <ORG> in this case is that the change in the method of valuation stock should be applied both to the opening stock as well as closing stock, which alone will indicate the true or real profits as has been held in <CITATION_670728> .
RatioOfTheDecision	4 ITC 245 (PC), and that it  is not open to the assessee to apply the new method to the closing stock alone without reference to the opening stock.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, according to the learned counsel for the <ORG>, whatever be the method of valuation adopted by the assessee, it should be applied to both the opening and closing stock, as otherwise it will give a distorted picture of the assessee's income.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG>'s reasoning for rejecting this contention of the <ORG> is that so far as the first year when the change is introduced is concerned, there is bound to be a slight distortion in the profits but that will get adjusted in course of time as the new method of valuation of stock is going to be applied on a permanent basis year after year.
RatioOfTheDecision	10.
RatioOfTheDecision	On a due consideration of the matter, we are inclined to agree with the view of the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the assessee is called upon to apply the new method of valuation to the opening stock of the accounting year as well, then, in consequence, the value of the closing stock of the year previous to the accou nting year will also get altered and that will result in the modification of the assessment for the previous year.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is for this reason the <ORG> has stated that thought by adoption of the new method of valuation for the closing stock alone the <ORG> may appear to get some unintended benefit, in course of time it will get adjusted and the <ORG> will not be a loser.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even apart from this reason, if the <ORG>'s contention that the new method should be adopted both to opening stock and closing even in the first year of the introduction of the new method is accepted, then it will lead to the position that the <ORG> cannot at all change the method or the <ORG> has to revalue the closing stock of the previous year which will be the opening stock of this year, and such a revaluation on the new basis as per the <ORG> is not ordinarily possible.
RatioOfTheDecision	That when a new method of valuation of stock is adopted in any particular year, the <ORG> can on that basis leave intact the valuation of the opening sto ck on the old method has been laid down in a series of cases.

RatioOfTheDecision	16.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is well-established, as pointed out by <ORG> in <CITATION_1330871> , that whichever method of book-keeping is adopted, in the case of a trading venture, for computing the true profits of the year stock-in-trade must be taken into account and must be taken into acco unt and if the value in the of the stock-in-trade is not taken into account in the ultimate result the profit or loss resulting from trading is bound to get absorbed or reflected in the stock-in-trade unless the value of the stock-in-trade remains unchanged, at the commencement of the year and at the end of the year.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is, therefore, necessary to value the stock-in-trade at the end of the year by adopting a sound commercial practice of valuing the stock either at cost or market price, whichever is lower.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this case the <ORG> has been adopting total cost method of valuing the closing stock till the beginning of the accounting year.
RatioOfTheDecision	During the accounting year the <ORG> has changed the method of valuation of stock from "total cost" to "direct cost" methods is that in a total cost method, certain overheads are added while in the direct cost method the same overheads are excluded.
RatioOfTheDecision	17.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not the contention of the <ORG> that the adoption of the direct cost method in the valuation of the closing is no t a sound commercial practice.
RatioOfTheDecision	As a matter of fact, <ORG> in <NAME> v. <ORG> [1961] 2 All ER 167, had recognised the "direct cost" method as one of the recognised methods in commercial practice.
Fact	In that case in the computation of profits, for purposes of income-tax and the profits tax, of a company producing motor vehicle bodies to order, work-in-progress had been consistently valued by the company by the "direct cost" method since 1924 and that method had been accepted by <ORG>.
Fact	By "direct cost" method, the value of the work-in-progress comprised the direct cost of the materials and labour used in the work.
Fact	In the assessment year 1951-52, <ORG> claimed that the "on cost" method of assessing the work-in-progress should be employed.
Fact	Under the "on cost" method, the value included a proportion of the indirect expenditure such as business overheads.
Fact	In the year of assessment 1951-52, the application of the "direct cost" method would result in the valu e of the work-in-progress being Pounds 2,000 less at the end of the year than at the beginning.
Fact	The employment of the "on cost" method would result in the valuing beginning Pounds 14,000 more at the end of the year than at the beginning.
Fact	The increase of Pounds 14,000 was due to the fact that a higher proportion of the overheads were attributed to work-in-progress at the end of the year than at the beginning owing to a slackening of business during the year and consequential increased costs of production.
Fact	There had been no other change of circumstances.
Fact	The court found that different accountants were applying either one or the other of the two methods producing a true figure of profit.
RulingByLowerCourt	On the question as to which of the method of valuation should be applied for valuing the work-in-progress in the case, the court held in determining what, in the circumstances of a particular business, was the figure which fairly represented the costs of the work-in-progress as per the method employed consistently since 1924, th e <ORG> has not discharged the burden of showing good reason that the method should be changed to the "on cost" method and, therefore, the assessee is entitled to continue to adopt the "direct cost" method as before.
RulingByLowerCourt	18.
RulingByLowerCourt	Thus, the adoption of "direct cost" method by the assessee cannot be questioned by the <ORG> as it has been found by the <ORG> that the adoption of this method is bona fide and is a permanent arrangement.
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG>'s only contention is that it has shown to be prejudicial to the <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	As pointed out in <CITATION_127231> and <CITATION_1819721>  44 ITR 22 (Mad), merely because the new method adopted by the assessee was detrimental to the <ORG>, that alone can never be the basis for denying the right to change the method.
RulingByLowerCourt	Further, even though the change of the method has resulted in a detriment to the <ORG> in the year in question, since the method is to be followed consistently year after year in future, this apparent detriment to the <ORG> will get adjusted and disappe ar.
RulingByLowerCourt	Therefore, in view of the findings of the <ORG> that the change of the method is bona fide and is intended to be followed in future, year after year, the change has to be accepted by the <ORG>, notwithstanding the fact that during assessment year which is the first year when change of method is brought about it has resulted in a prejudice or detriment to the <ORG>.
RulingByLowerCourt	So long as the method of valuation adopted by the assessee gets recognition from the practising accountants and the commercial world for valuation of stock-in-trade, the adoption of that method cannot be questioned by the <ORG> unless the adoption of that method is found to be not bona fide or restricted for a particular year.
RulingByLowerCourt	19.
RulingByLowerCourt	In view of the above discussion, we answer the question referred to us in the affirmative and against the <ORG>.
