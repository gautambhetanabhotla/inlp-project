RatioOfTheDecision	We may now examine the evidence adduced by the prosecution.
RatioOfTheDecision	At the outset, we may say that the Magistrate has improperly admitted in evidence certain incriminating statements said to have been made by the appellant to Messrs. <NAME> and <NAME>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It has been contended on behalf of the appellants that the <ORG> Officers, who have large powers of search, arrest and detention, are to be regarded as Police Officers within the meaning of Section 25 of the Evidence Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	We need not  decide this question, as it is the case for the prosecution that two <ORG> Sergeants accompanied the Excise Officers, assisted in the arrest of the appellant, and were in the house as part of the force in charge of the prisoners at the time when the statements are said to have been made.
RatioOfTheDecision	Though these <ORG> Officers were not in the room with the appellant, <NAME>, <NAME> and the excise peons, yet in our opinion when, making a statement in that room, the appellant is to be regarded as in <ORG> custody for the purposes of Section 26 of the Evidence Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The statements are, therefore, inadmissible.
