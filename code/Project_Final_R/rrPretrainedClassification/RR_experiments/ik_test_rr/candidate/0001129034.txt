Fact	JUDGMENT <NAME>, C.J.
Fact	1.
Fact	In this case a Rule has been issued on the opposite party to show cause why the order of the <ORG> at Tangail, <ORG>, dated the 20th November 1909, should not be set aside or why such other order should not be passed as to this Court might seem fit and proper.
Fact	The occasion of this rule was that a decree was passed in <ORG> in March 1909 for a sum in excess of a thousand rupees.
Fact	There was an application for transfer of the execution proceedings which resulted in their being transferred to the <ORG> at Tangail, <ORG>.
Fact	Certain orders were passed by that <ORG>, and on the 20th of November, he made an order of release, and it is this order that is named in the Rule.
Argument	Subsequently an exception being taken to the proceedings, the <ORG>, on the 8th of January, made an order to the effect that the proceedings before him were without jurisdiction.
Argument	Though the Rule only mentions the order of the 20th of November 1909, I notice that the applica tion on which the Rule was granted referred to the orders generally, and I think we can fairly treat this application as though the order of the 8th of January-was called in question, so that what we have to decide is whether or not the <ORG> had jurisdiction to entertain these execution proceedings.
Argument	Now, his jurisdiction did not extend to suits in excess of a thousand rupees, so that if the matter had to be determined by the terms of the Code, then under the present Code as under the Code of 1882, the <ORG> bad not jurisdiction for the purposes of these execution proceedings.
RatioOfTheDecision	Nor do I think that jurisdiction was created by Clause (b) of Section 31 of the Presidency Small Cause Courts Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	This Act is Act XV of 1882; and was passed on the same day as Act XIV of 1882 which was the Code of Civil Procedure of that year and, I think it would be reading into the words of Section 31 , Clause (b), a meaning of which they are not reasonably susceptible, when regard is had to the circumstances, if we were to hold th at <ORG>, which was not competent for the purposes of the Code, would be competent by reason of the provisions in this section.
RatioOfTheDecision	I think the fair meaning of Section 31 , Clause (b), is that <ORG> to which a decree may be sent for execution is <ORG> competent to deal with it under the provisions of Act XIV of 1882 and so the <ORG> which under the provisions of the present Code is also competent to deal with it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Therefore, I think the <NAME> rightly held that he had no jurisdiction, and that being so not only will the order of the 10th of November 1909 be of no avail but the other orders must share the same fate, the order of the 8th of January 1910 being correct.
RatioOfTheDecision	2.
RatioOfTheDecision	But then we have been asked to transfer this proceeding from the <ORG> of the <NAME> at Tangail to the <ORG> of a Judge having jurisdiction in the matter.
RatioOfTheDecision	No rule has been granted which would invite our making any such order, so that in any case I should have felt slow to accede to this application.
RatioOfTheDecision	But apart from that,  proceedings that were without jurisdiction were not proceedings that could be transferred under the provisions of the old Code and I think they are equally incapable of transfer under the new Code.
RatioOfTheDecision	The broad result of this is that the applicant has failed, and I think it would be meaningless for us to say that we make the Rule absolute so far as it relates to the order of the 20th November 1909, for that has already come to nothing.
RulingByLowerCourt	I think the proper order for us to make is to discharge the Rule with costs, hearing fee, three gold mohurs.
