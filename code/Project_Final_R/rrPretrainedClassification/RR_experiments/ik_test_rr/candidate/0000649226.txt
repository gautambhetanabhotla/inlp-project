Fact	In this appeal by the first defendant the main and the most important question that was raised on his behalf  by his learned advocate relates to the share decreed in favour of the plaintiff by the learned Subordinate Judge in items 1, 2, 17 and 18 of Schedule II to the plaint.

Fact	Under the Act , as it would be seen from the language of Section 3(2) the right is created in favour of the widow of a deceased coparcener in the property governed by the Act, her interest being the same as that of her <NAME>.
Fact	As it has now been finally settled by the Federal Court that the Act does not apply to agricultural land in the Governors' P rovinces, it must be taken that when <NAME> died in June, 1942, his interest in the agricultural land survived to the other coparcener, namely, the first defendant and that thereafter he became the sole and exclusive owner of the agricultural lands.
Fact	The produce of the lands thereafter belongs exclusively to the first defendant.
Fact	If on the date of the death of her <NAME> the plaintiff had acquired no interest in the agricultural lands by virtue of the provisions of the Act, it is difficult to see how she could claim a share in the produce of the lands which upon the death of her <NAME> became the sole and exclusive property of the first defendant.
Fact	The right of succession or of survivorship must be determined as on the date of the death of the <NAME> of the plaintiff and not as on the date of the institution of this suit for partition.
Fact	If no interest in the agricultural lands devolved on the widow, i.e., the plaintiff, on the death of <NAME> Naicker in the income that subsequently accrued from suc h property the plaintiff gets no share or interest.
RatioOfTheDecision	The reasoning therefore of the learned Judge on the basis of which he granted the decree for partition of these items of the second schedule to the plaint seems to lis to be entirely wrong.
