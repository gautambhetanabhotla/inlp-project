RulingByLowerCourt	Learned counsel for the <ORG> contends that the expression "iron and steel" means iron and steel in the original condition and not iron and steel in the shape of bars, flats and plates.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our view, this contention is not sound.
RatioOfTheDecision	A comparison of the said two Notifications brings out the distinction between raw-materials of iron and steel and the goods prepared from iron and steel : while the former is exempted from tax, the latter is taxed.
RatioOfTheDecision	Ther efore, iron and steel used as raw-material for manufacturing other goods are exempted from taxation.
RatioOfTheDecision	So long as iron and steel continue to be raw-materials, they enjoy the exemption.
RatioOfTheDecision	Scrap iron purchased by the respondent was merely re-rolled into bars, flats and plates.
RatioOfTheDecision	They were processed for convenience of sale.
RatioOfTheDecision	The raw-materials were only re-rolled to give them attractive and acceptable forms.
RatioOfTheDecision	They did not in the process lose their character as iron and steel.
RatioOfTheDecision	The dealer sold "iron and steel" in the shape of bars, flats and plates and the customer purchased "iron and steel' in that shape.
RatioOfTheDecision	We, therefore, hold that the bars, flats and plates sold by the assessee are iron and steel exempted under the Notification.
RatioOfTheDecision	The conclusion arrived at by <ORG> is correct.
