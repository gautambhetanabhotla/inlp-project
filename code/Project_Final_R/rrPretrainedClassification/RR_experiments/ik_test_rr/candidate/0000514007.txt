RatioOfTheDecision	Allowing the appeal to this <ORG>, 160 ^ HELD: In an action where a party has prayed for a larger relief it is always open to the <ORG> to grant him any smaller relief that he may be found to be entitled to in law and thereby render substantial justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> can take note of changed circumstances and suitably  mould the relief to be granted to the party concerned in order to mete out justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	As far as possible the anxiety and endeavour of the <ORG> should be to remedy an in justice when it is brought to its notice rather than deny relief to an aggrieved party on purely technical and narrow procedural grounds.
RatioOfTheDecision	[162 G-163 A] In the instant case the writ petition contained the prayer for the quashing of the gradation list in so far as it related to the inter-se ranking of the appellant vis-a- vis respondents nos.

RatioOfTheDecision	3, 4, 5, 7 and 8 and placed them below the appellant thereby redressing the grievance of the appellant in so far as it pertained to the ranking of the said respondents.
RatioOfTheDecision	It, therefore, became unnecessary for the appel lant to pursue his claim for relief with respect to the ranks assigned to those five respondents.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was under those circumstances that the appellant submitted before the single Judge at the time of final hearing of the writ petition that he was pressing the writ petition only in so far as it related to his claim for seniority over the 6th respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	This will not operate to preclude him from seeking a lesser relief namely the quashing of the list only in so far as it pertains to the fixation of the inter-se seniority between himself and the 6th respondent.

RatioOfTheDecision	The Judgment of the Court was delivered by <NAME> This appeal preferred by special leave is against the  judgment of the Division Bench of <ORG> setting aside the decision of a learned single judge of that Court and dismissing a writ petition filed by the present appellant.
RatioOfTheDecision	The appellant, who was working as an officer of <ORG> in the State of Andhra Pradesh, approached <ORG> challenging the provisional integrated gradation list of Forest officers of the former Andhra and Hyderabad States published under the provisions of the States Reorganization Act, as annexure to a State Government order dated January 27, 1962.
RatioOfTheDecision	The contentions raised by the petitioner in the writ petition were mainly two-fold.
RatioOfTheDecision	Firstly, it was urged that the inter-se seniority between the appellant and the 6th respondent, both of whom originally belonged to <ORG>, had been wrongly fixed in the provisional gradation list by showing the 6th respondent as senior to the appellant, whereas the appellant was legally entitled to seniority over the 6th respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	Secondly, it was contended  that respondents Nos.
RatioOfTheDecision	3, 4, 5, 7 and 8 who were officers allotted to the State of Andhra Pradesh from the Telengana region of the former Hyderabad State, had been erroneously assigned ranks above the appellant in the integrated gradation list in violation of the principles laid down by <ORG> for equation of posts and the fixation of inter-se seniority between the persons drawn from the two sources.
RatioOfTheDecision	By the time the writ petition came up for hearing before the learned single judge, <ORG> had already set right the appellant's grievance concerning his ranking and the seniority in relation to respondents 3, 4, 5, 7 and 8.
RatioOfTheDecision	It therefore became unnecessary for him to pursue the second contention aforementioned and hence he pressed before the learned single judge only the plea concerning his claim for seniority over the 6th respondent was well founded.
RatioOfTheDecision	Accordingly, the learned single judge found that the contention put forward by the appellant that he was entitled to seniority over  the 6th respondent was well founded.
RatioOfTheDecision	Accordingly, the learned judge allowed the writ petition and issued a writ of mandamus directing <ORG> and <ORG> to modify the gradation list by showing the appellant as senior to the 6th respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	The 6th respondent carried the matter in appeal before <ORG> of <ORG> by filing Appeal No.
RatioOfTheDecision	691 of 1978.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> took the view that since the prayer contained in the writ petition was for the issue of a writ of mandamus directing respondents No.
RatioOfTheDecision	1 and 2 to forbear from implementing the provisional gradation list published along with the <ORG> order dated January 27, 1962, and inasmuch as the petitioner had not pressed the said prayer for quashing of the list in so for as it related to the officers of Telengana region (respondents 3, 4, 5, 7 and 8), the writ petition should have been dismissed on that short ground and the question relating to inter-se seniority between the petitioner and the 6th respondent oug ht not to have been decided by the learned single judge.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this view, <ORG> allowed the writ appeal, set aside the order passed by the learned single judge and dismissed the writ petition.
RatioOfTheDecision	The appellant has come up to this Court questioning the legality and correctness of the aforesaid reasoning and conclusion of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true that the writ petition contained a prayer for the quashing of the gradation list in so far as it related to the inter-se ranking of the petitioner vis-a-vis respondents Nos.
RatioOfTheDecision	3 to 8 and the petitioner (appellant) had also sought the issuance of a writ of mandamus directing respondents Nos.
RatioOfTheDecision	1 and 2 to forbear from implementing or acting upon the said gradation list.
RatioOfTheDecision	But subsequent to the institution of the writ petition, <ORG> has refixed the ranks of respondents Nos.
RatioOfTheDecision	3, 4, 5, 7 and 8 (Telengana officers) and placed them below the appellant thereby redressing the grievance of the appellant in so far as it pertained to the ranking of the afo resaid respondents.
RatioOfTheDecision	It therefore became unnecessary for the appellant to pursue his claim for relief with respect to the ranks assigned to those five respondents.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was under those circumstances that the appellant submitted before the learned single judge of <ORG>, at the time of final hearing of the writ petition, that he was pressing the writ petition only in so far as it related to his claim for seniority over the 6th respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	We fail to see how the fact that the appellant had sought in the writ petition the issuance of a writ of mandamus directing respondents 1 and 2 to forbear from implementing or acting upon the provisional gradation list will operate to preclude him from seeking a lesser relief, namely, the quashing of the list only so far as it pertains to the fixation of the inter-se seniority between himself and the 6th respondent.
RatioOfTheDecision	The material facts and circumstances had undergone a substantial change subsequent to the filing of the original petition and it was in consequence thereof t hat it had become unnecessary for the petitioner to pursue his original prayer for the grant of a larger relief.
RatioOfTheDecision	Besides ignoring this crucial aspect, <ORG> of <ORG> has also lost sight of the well established principle that in an action where a party has prayed for a larger relief it is always open to the court to grant him any smaller relief that he may be found to be entitled in law and thereby render substantial justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> can undoubtedly take note of changed circumstances and suitably mould the relief to be granted to the party concerned in order to mete out justice in the case.
RatioOfTheDecision	As far as possible the anxiety and endeavour of the <ORG> should be to remedy an injustice when it is brought to its notice rather than deny relief to an aggrieved party on purely technical and narrow procedural grounds.
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not, therefore, find it possible to uphold the view expressed by <ORG> of <ORG> that since the writ petition was not pressed in so far as it related to th e officers belonging to the Telengana region the question of inter-se seniority between the writ petitioner and the 6th respondent should not have been considered by the single judge and the writ petition should have been dismissed.
RulingByPresentCourt	Accordingly, we set aside the judgment of <ORG> and remand the writ appeal to <ORG> for fresh disposal in accordance with law.
