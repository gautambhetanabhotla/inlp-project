RatioOfTheDecision	16.
RatioOfTheDecision	After hearing Dr. <NAME> for the appellants and Mr. <NAME> for the State Government, we have reached the conclusion that in view of the sudden firing that took place at that hour of the night it was not possible for <NAME> to clearly identify the persons who had fired the shots at him, that in all likelihood the names he mentioned at the time were the result of the reaction on his mind on the occasion.

RatioOfTheDecision	53 of its judgment seem to be based more on conjectures than on admissible material.

It seems to us that in this case <ORG> failed to appreciate the true effect of the decision of <ORG> in--' <CITATION_256823> ', AIR 1945 PC 118 (A), and its judgment in regard to the applicability of Section 34 , I. P. C. has to be reversed.
