Precedent	That is how the matter had come before this <ORG>.
Precedent	The main point in controversy before us turns on the question whether the provisions of Art.
Precedent	311(2) of the Constitution are attracted to the case of a public servant who was still a probationer and had not been confirmed in a substantive post.
Precedent	The question whether there had or had not been a violation of the principles of natural justice, which was the basis of the decision in the High <ORG> as indicated above, was not raised before us.
RatioOfTheDecision	The judgment of the High <ORG> could be allowed to rest on that basis alone, but as it appears that this <ORG> granted special leave to determine the co ntroversy based upon the interpretation of Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	311(2) of the Constitution, we think it necessary to express our opinion on that part of the case.
RatioOfTheDecision	The decision of this <ORG> in <CITATION_1270113> 1958 (1) LLJ 544 ] had not been given till then.
RatioOfTheDecision	If that decision were in existence then, perhaps the special leave would not have been granted.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, the controversy raised in this case is completely covered by the decision of <ORG> of this <ORG> in <NAME> case (supra.)
RulingByLowerCourt	The main question for decision in that case was whether the appellant <NAME> had been reduced in rank by way of punishment as a result of the order of the General Manager of the Railway.
RulingByLowerCourt	Though, in that case, this <ORG> decided that the order impugned had not that effect, this <ORG> went elaborately into all the implications of the service conditions, with particular reference to the Railway Service Rules and the constitutional provisions contained in S.240 of the Government of India Act, 1935, and Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	311 of the Constit ution.
RatioOfTheDecision	The elaborate discussion in that judgment has reference to all stages of employment in the public services including temporary posts, probationers, as also confirmed officers.
RatioOfTheDecision	In so far as those observations have a bearing on the termination of service or discharge of a probationary public servant, they may be summarized as follows :1.
RatioOfTheDecision	Appointment to a post on probation gives to the person so appointed no right to the post and his service may be terminated, without taking recourse to the proceedings laid down in the relevant rules for dismissing a public servant, or removing him from service.
RatioOfTheDecision	2.
RatioOfTheDecision	The termination of employment of a person holding a post on probation without any enquiry whatsoever cannot be said to deprive him of any right to a post and is, therefore, no punishment.
RatioOfTheDecision	3.
RatioOfTheDecision	But, if instead of terminating such a person's service without any enquiry, the employer chooses to hold an enquiry into his alleged misconduct or inefficiency of for some similar reason, the termination of service is by  way of punishment, because it puts a stigma on his competence and thus affects his future career.
RatioOfTheDecision	In such a case, he is entitled to the protection of Art.
Precedent	311(2) of the Constitution.
Precedent	4.
Precedent	In the last-mentioned case, if the probationer is discharged on any one of those grounds without a proper enquiry and without his getting a reasonable opportunity of showing cause against his discharge, it will amount to a removal from service within the meaning of Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	311(2) of the Constitution and will, therefore, be liable to be struck down.
RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	But, if the employer simply terminates the services of a probationer without holding an enquiry and without giving him a reasonable chance of showing cause against his removal from service, probationary civil servant can have no cause of action, even though the real motive behind the removal from service may have been that his employer thought him to be unsuitable for the post he was temporarily holding, on account of his misconduct or inefficiency or some such cause.
RatioOfTheDecision	It would th us appear that, in the instant case, thought the respondent was only a probationer he was discharged from service really because the Government had on enquiry come to the conclusion, rightly or wrongly, that he was unsuitable for the post he held on probation.
RatioOfTheDecision	This was clearly by way of punishment and, therefore, he was entitled to the protection of Art.
Precedent	311(2) of the Constitution.
Precedent	It was argued on behalf of the appellant that the respondent, being a mere probationer, could be discharged without any enquiry into his conduct being made and his discharge could not mean any punishment to him, because he had no right to a post.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is true, if the Government came to the conclusion that the respondent was not a fit and proper person to hold a post in the public service of the <ORG>, it could discharge him without holding any enquiry into his alleged misconduct.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the Government proceeded against him in that direct way, without casting any aspersion on his honesty or competance, his discharge would not, in law, hav e the effect of a removal from service by way of punishment and he would, therefore, have no grievance to ventilate in any Court.
RatioOfTheDecision	Instead of taking that easy course, the Government chose the more difficult one of starting proceedings against him and of branding him as a dishonest and an incompetent officer.
RatioOfTheDecision	He had the right, in those circumstances, to insist upon the protection of Art.
Precedent	311 (2) of the Constitution.
Precedent	That protection not having been given to him, he had the right to seek his redress in Court.
Precedent	It must, therefore, be held that the respondent had been wrongly deprived of the protection afforded by Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	311(2) of the Constitution.
RulingByPresentCourt	His removal from the service, therefore, was not in accordance with the requirements of the Constitution.The appeal is accordingly dismissed with costs.
