Fact	The short question that falls for consideration in this appeal is whether it is permissible to club vacancies of a number of years while preparing the select list for promotion to <ORG> (for short 'Service') from <ORG>.
Fact	By the impugned judgment dated November 30, 1993 <ORG> Ahmedabad Bench (hereinafter referred to as 'the <ORG>') has held that such clubbing of vacancies is not p ermissible and that separate select lists should be prepared by <ORG> for each year.
Fact	The Indian Administrative Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1954 make provision for appointment to the Service by promotion from amongst the substantive members of <ORG> [Rules 4(1) (b) and 8(1)].
Fact	Such promotion to the Service from <ORG> is governed by the Indian Administrative Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulation, 1955 (hereinafter referred to as 'the Regulations') Regulation 3 makes provision for constitution of a <ORG> to make the selection.
Fact	Regulation 5 prescribes the procedure for making the selection by the <ORG> and the preparation of a list.
Fact	The said list prepared by the <ORG> in accordance with Regulation 5 is required to be forwarded by <ORG> to <ORG> [Regulation 6] and the list as approved by <ORG> forms the select list for purpose of promotion of the members of <ORG> ivil Service [Regulation 7].
Fact	Appointments to the Service are made by <ORG> on the recommendation of <ORG> from the said Select List [Regulation 9].
Fact	The respondent was a member of <ORG>, having been recruited to the said service Class I, having been recruited to the said service in the year 1967.
Fact	The select list for promotion to the Service from <ORG> was prepared by <ORG> under the Regulations in July 1979.
Fact	Even though respondent had put in the requisite 8 years' continuous service in <ORG> in July 1979, he was not considered by <ORG> because he fell outside the zone of consideration.
Fact	Thereafter no select list was prepared for promotion of <ORG> officers in Gujarat till December 1986/January 1987.
Fact	The respondent was not selected in the said selection.
Fact	He filed a petition (O.A.
Fact	NO.
Fact	646 OF 1988) before the <ORG> wherein he assailed the select list prepare d by <ORG> in December 1986/January 1987 on the ground that it was not permissible to club together the vacancies of the years 1980 to 1986 for the purpose of making selection under Regulation 5 of the Regulations and that <ORG> should have met every year to prepare a select list for the vacancies of each year.
Fact	The said application submitted by the respondent has been allowed by the <ORG> by the impugned judgment.
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG> has held that the action of the appellants in clubbing the vacancies and thereby enlarging the zone of consideration could have prejudiced the respondent and, therefore, it was illegal.
RulingByLowerCourt	The <ORG> has directed the appellant to prepare select list from year to year from 1980 to 1986 and thereafter on the basis of vacancies from year to year without clubbing the vacancies in any particular year and as the part of the exercise to consider the case of the respondent for promotion to the <ORG> and should has name figure in the select list and, should th e vacancies permit, to appoint him to the <ORG> and to give him all consequential benefits on the basis of such appointment from the date of the appointment.

Statute	A perusal of Regulation 5 shows that clause (1) required that <ORG> shall ordinarily meet at intervals not exceeding one year and prepare a list of such members of <ORG> as are held by them to be suitable for promotion to the <ORG>.
Statute	The said clause also required that the number of the members of <ORG> included in the list shall not be more than twice the number of substantive vacancies anticipated in the course of the period of twelve months commencing from the date of preparation of the list.
Statute	Under clause (2) <ORG> was required to consider the cases of members of State Civil <ORG> in the order of a senior ity in that service of a number which was equal to five times (subsequently reduced to three times) the number referred in clause (1).
Statute	Under the third proviso to clause (2) it was prescribed that <ORG> shall no consider the case of member of <ORG> unless on the first day of January of the year in which it meets his is substantive in State Civil Service and has completed not less than eight years of continuous service (whether officiating substantive) in the post of Deputy Collector or in other post or posts declared equivalent thereto by <ORG>.
Statute	In respect of released Emergency Commissioned or short service Commissioned officers appointed to <ORG> the period of continuous service was four years under the fourth proviso to clause (2).
Statute	In view of clause (3) cases of members of <ORG> who had attained the age of 52 years (subsequently raised to 54 years ) on the first day of January of the year in which <ORG> m eets were not to be considered by the <ORG>.
Statute	Under clause (6) the list prepared by the Selection <ORG> was required to be reviewed and revised every year.
Statute	If clause (1) is read with the other provisions in Regulation 5 referred to above the inference is inevitable that the requirement in clause (1) of Regulation 5 that the Selection <ORG> shall meet at intervals not exceeding one year and prepare a list of members of <ORG> who are suitable for promotion in the Service was intended to be mandatory in nature because the eligibility of the persons to be considered both in the matter of length of service and are under clauses (2) and (3) is with reference to the first date of January of the year in which the Selection <ORG> meets and the number of members of <ORG> to be considered for selection is also linked with the number of substantive vacancies anticipated in the course of the period of twelve months commencing from the date of preparation of the list.
RatioOfTheDecision	We  are, therefore of the view that the requirement prescribed in sub-regulation (1) of Regulation 5 regarding the <ORG> writing at intervals not exceeding one year and preparing a list of such members of <ORG> who are suitable for promotion to the <ORG> was a mandatory requirement which had to be followed.
RatioOfTheDecision	The earlier decisions of this Court also lend support to this view.
RatioOfTheDecision	<CITATION_714743> .,1974 (1) SCR 797, this Court was construing Regulations 4 and 5 of the Indian Administrative Service/Indian Police Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, as they stood at that time.
RatioOfTheDecision	The provisions in those regulations were similar to those contained in Regulation 5 referred to above.
Statute	In Regulation 4 (1) there was a requirement that the <ORG> shall meet at intervals not exceeding one year and consider the cases of all substantive members of <ORG> who on the first day of January of the year had completed not less than eight years of continuous service.
Statute	Un der Regulation 4(2) it was prescribed that the <ORG> shall not consider the case of members of <ORG> who had attained the age of 52 years on the first day of the January of the year in which the meeting of the <ORG> is held.
Statute	Regulation 5(4) prescribed that the list so prepared shall be reviewed and revised every year.
Statute	<NAME> in his concurring judgment, has said :-  "The purpose of an annual revision or revision or review is to make an assessment of the merit and suitability of all the then eligible candidates and make a fresh list of the required number of the most suitable candidates from among them.
Statute	In other words, the purpose of the annual review or revision of the select list is to prepare a list and to include therein the required number of the most suitable persons from among all the then eligible candidates- [P. 802] "When Regulation 5(4) says that the list prepared in accordance with Regulation 5(1) shall be reviewed or revised every year, it really means that there mu st be an assessment of the merit and suitability of all the eligible members every year.
Statute	The paramount duty cast upon the <ORG> to draw up a list under Regulation 5(1) of such members of <ORG> as satisfy the condition under Regulation 4 and as are held by the <ORG> to be suitable for promotion to the service would be discharged only if the <ORG> makes the selection from all the eligible candidates every year."
Statute	[p. 802] <NAME>, as the learned Chief Justice then was, he said:-  "The required number has thus to be selected by a comparison of merits of all the eligible candidates of each year."
RatioOfTheDecision	[p.818] Clause (1) of Regulation 5 of the Regulations differs from clause (1) of Regulation 4 which was considered by this <ORG> in Mohan Lal Capoor (supra) in the sense that the word "ordinarily" found in clause (1) of Regulation 5 was not contained in clause (1) of Regulation 5 was not contained in clause (1) of Regulation 4.
RatioOfTheDecision	The insertion of the word "ordinarily" does not, in our  opinion, alter the intendment underlying the provision.
RatioOfTheDecision	It only means that unless there are good reasons for not doing so, <ORG> shall meet every year for making the selection.
Precedent	<CITATION_1810596> ., 1993 Supp.
Argument	(3) SCC 575, this Court was constructing the provisions of Regulation 5 of the Indian Police Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1995 which is in pari material with clause (1) of Regulation 5 and contained the word "ordinarily", It was observed :-  "......since the preparation of the select list is the foundation for promotion and its omission impinges upon the legitimate expectation of promotee officers for consideration of their claim for promotion as <ORG> officers, the preparation of the select-list must be constructed to be mandatory.
Fact	The <ORG> should, therefore, meet every year and prepare the select-list and be reviewed and revised from time to time as exigencies demand."

Fact	The failure on the part of <ORG> to meet during a particular year would not dispense with the requirement of preparing the Select List for that year.
RatioOfTheDecision	If for any reason <ORG> when it meets next, should, while making the selection, prepare a separate list for each year keeping in view the number of vacancies in that year after considering the <ORG> officers who were eligible and fall within the zone of consideration for selection in that year.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the present case, <ORG> did not meet during the years 1980 to 1985 and it met in December 1986/January 1987 and a Consolidated Select List was prepared for the vacancies of the years 1980 to 1986.
RatioOfTheDecision	There was thus a failure to comply with the mandatory requirement of Regulation 5 of the Regulations.
RatioOfTheDecision	In <NAME> (supra) select lists had not been prepared for the years 1971, 1975, 1976, 1979 and  1980.
RatioOfTheDecision	During the pendency of the appeal in this Court <ORG> was directed to prepare the select list on national basis for the said years and select lists were then prepared.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the instant case, <ORG> officers who were selected in the select list prepared in December 1986/January 1987 have not been impleaded as parties and, therefore, their appointment to the <ORG> cannot be upset.
RatioOfTheDecision	In his application before the <ORG> the respondent sought a direction for consideration of his case afresh for the purpose of inclusion in the select list.
RatioOfTheDecision	The respondent can seek such consideration only in a way that it does not disturb the appointment of other <ORG> officers who have been appointed to the <ORG> on the basis of the Select List of December 1986/January 1987.
RatioOfTheDecision	For that purpose out of the said officers whose appointment is not to be disturbed those who were senior to the respondent in the <ORG> will have to be adjusted against the vacancies for the years  1980-1986.
RatioOfTheDecision	If, as a result of such adjustment the vacancies of a particular year/years are completely filled, then no further action is to be taken in respect of the vacancies for that/those year/years.
RatioOfTheDecision	If after such adjustment the vacancies of a particular year/years are not completely filled, steps will have to be taken to prepare notional Select List/Lists for the vacancies of that/these year//years separately from amongst <ORG> officers who are eligible and fall within the zone of consideration for selection in respect of the vacancies of the particular year.
RulingByPresentCourt	If the name of the respondent is included in the notional Select List/Lists so prepared or any particular year/years during the period 1980 to 1986 and is places in the order of merit so as to have been entitled to be appointed against a vacancy of that particular year, he can justifiably claim to be appointed to the <ORG> against that vacancy of that year.
RulingByPresentCourt	But that appointment of other <ORG> officers, through junior to  the respondent, made on the basis of the Select List of December 1986/January 1987 and the vacancy against which the appointment of the respondent would be made will have to be adjusted the subsequent vacancies falling within the promotion quota prescribed for <ORG> Civil <ORG> officers.
RulingByPresentCourt	Therefore, while upholding the judgement of the <ORG> that the respondent is entitled to seek fresh consideration on the basis that the selection should be made for vacancies occurring in each year separately, but in substitution of the directions given by the <ORG> in the regard, the following directions are given :-  (1) The number of vacancies falling in the quota prescribed for promotion of <ORG> Civil <ORG> officers to the <ORG> shall be determined separately for each year in respect of the period from 1980 to 1986.
RulingByPresentCourt	(2) The <ORG> Civil <ORG> officers who have been appointed to the <ORG> on the basis of the impugned Select List of December 1986/January 1987 and were senior to the respondent in the <ORG>  Civil <ORG> shall be adjusted against the vacancies so determined on year wise basis.
RulingByPresentCourt	(3) After such adjustment if all the vacancies in a particular year or years are filled by the officers referred to in paragraph (2), no further action need be taken in respect of those vacancies for the said year/years.
RulingByPresentCourt	(4) But, if after such adjustment vacancy/vacancies remain in a particular year/years during the period from 1980 to 1986, notional Select List/Lists shall be prepared separately for that year/years on a consideration of all eligible officers falling within the zone of consideration determined on the basis of the vacancies of the particular year.
RulingByPresentCourt	(5) If the name of the respondent is included in the notional Select List/Lists prepared for any particular year/years during the period 1980 to 1986 and if he is so placed in the order of merit so as to have been entitled to be appointed against a vacancy of that particular year, he be appointed to the <ORG> against that vacancy of that year with all conseque ntial benefits.
RulingByPresentCourt	(6) The vacancy against which the respondent is so appointed would be adjusted against the subsequent vacancies falling in the promotion quota prescribed for the <ORG> officers.
RulingByPresentCourt	(7) Such appointment of the respondent would not affect the appointments that have already been made on the basis of the impugned Select List of December 1986/January 1987.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appeal is disposed of accordingly.
