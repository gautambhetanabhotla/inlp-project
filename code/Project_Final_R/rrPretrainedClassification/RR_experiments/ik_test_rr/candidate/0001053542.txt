Argument	The learned counsel seeks to adopt the aforesaid observations of the High <ORG> as his submissions and contends that i n view of the aforesaid reasoning which are cogent and sound it cannot be said that the impugned amendment is unconstitutional.

Argument	In the earlier case between the parties to which reference has already been made, <ORG> issued writs directing <ORG> to fore-bear from collecting from the appellant any amount representing the sales tax on the following, namely, excise duty, health cess and education cess imposed on <ORG> or special liquor and to refund to the appellant what might have been collected from them by way of sales tax on items of excise, health cess and education cess on <ORG> or special liquor.
Argument	<ORG> had passed the aforesaid order issuing appropriate writ in view of <ORG>'s finding that sales tax is not payable on excise duty, health cess and education cess.
Argument	In view of the aforesaid judgment and order passed by <ORG> amounts collected by the States by way  of Sales Tax on items of excise, health cess and education cess on Arrack or special liquor from the appellant became refundable to the appellant.
Argument	The impugned amendment has been passed, as the <ORG>ment of objects which we have earlier set out clearly indicates to over-ride the judgment of <ORG> and to enable the <ORG> to hold on to the amount collected as sales tax on excise duty, health cess and education cess, if any, on Arrack or special liquor.
Argument	It has to be noted that the said judgment of <ORG> in the earlier case had become final and conclusive inasmuch as the special leave petition filed against the judgment by the <ORG> was withdrawn.
Argument	The <ORG> instead of seeking to test the correctness and effect of the judgment and order of <ORG> thought it fit to have the judgment and order nullified by introducing the impugned amendment.
Argument	The amendment does not proceed to cure the defect or the lacuna by bringing in an amendment providing for exigibility of sales tax on excise duty, heal th cess and education cess.
RatioOfTheDecision	The impugned Amending Act may not, therefore, be considered to be a Validating Act .
RatioOfTheDecision	A Validating Act seeks to validate the earlier Acts declared illegal and unconstitutional by <ORG>s by removing the defect or lacuna which led to invalidation of the law.
RatioOfTheDecision	With the removal of the defect or lacuna resulting in the validation of any Act held invalid by a competent <ORG>, the Act may become valid, if the Validating Act is lawfully enacted.
RatioOfTheDecision	But the question may still arise as to what will be the fate of acts done before the Validating Act curing the defect has been passed.
RatioOfTheDecision	To meet such a situation and to provide that no liability may be imposed on the <ORG> in respect of such acts done before the passing of the Validating Act making such act valid, a Validating Act is usually passed with retrospective effect.
RatioOfTheDecision	The retrospective operation relieves the <ORG> of the consequences of acts done prior to the passing of the Validating Act .
RatioOfTheDecision	The retrospective operation of a Validating Act properl y passed curing the defects and lacuna which might have led to the invalidity of any act done may be upheld, if considered reasonable and legitimate.
Argument	841  In the instant case, the <ORG> instead of remedying the defect or removing the lacuna has by the impugned amendment sought to raise the rate of tax from 6.1/2% to 45% with retrospective effect from the Ist April 1966 to avoid the liability of refunding the excess amount collected and has further purported to nullify the judgment and order passed by <ORG> directing the refund of the excess amount illegally collected by providing that the levy at the Higher rate of 45% will have retrospective effect from Ist of April, 1966.
Argument	The judgment of <ORG> declaring the levy of sales tax on excise duty, education cess and health cess to be bad has become conclusive and is binding on the parties.
Argument	It may or may not have been competent for the <ORG> Legislature to validly remove the lacuna and remedy the defect in the earlier levy by seeking to impose sa les tax through any amendment on excise duty, education cess and health cess; but in any event, <ORG> has not purported to do so through the Amending Act .
Argument	As a result of the judgment of <ORG> declaring such levy illegal, the <ORG> became obliged to refund the excess amount wrongfully and illegally collected by virtue of the specific direction to that effect in the earlier judgment.
Argument	It appears that the only object of enacting the amended provision is to nullify the effect of the judgment which became conclusive and binding on the parties to enable <ORG> to retain the amount wrongfully and illegally collected as sales tax and this object has been sought to be achieved by the impugned amendment which does not even purport or seek to remedy or remove the defect and lacuna but merely raises the rate of duty from 6.1/2% to 45% and further proceeds to nullify the judgment and order of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, the enhancement of the rate of duty from 6.1/2% to 45% with r etrospective effect is in the facts and circumstances of the case clearly arbitrary and unreasonable.
RatioOfTheDecision	The defect or lacuna is not even sought to be remedied and the only justification for the steep rise in the rate of duty by the amended provision is to nullify the effect of the binding judgment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The vice of illegal collection in the absence of the removal of the illegality which led to the invalidation of the earlier assessments on the basis of illegal levy, continues to taint the earlier levy.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, this is not a proper ground for imposing the levy at the higher rate with retrospective effect.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may be open to the <ORG> to impose the levy at the higher rate with prospective operation but levy of taxation at higher rate which really amounts to imposition of tax with retrospective operation has to be justified on proper and cogent grounds.
RatioOfTheDecision	This aspect of the matter does not appear to have been properly considered by <ORG> and <ORG> in our view was not right in holding that b y the enactment of S. 2 of the impugned Act the very basis of the complaint made by the petitioner before this <ORG> in the earlier writ petition as also the basis of the decision of this <ORG> in <NAME>'s case that the <ORG> is collecting amounts by way of tax in excess of what was authorised under the Act has been removed".
