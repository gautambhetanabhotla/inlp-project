Fact	The learned Trial Judge in this case, which is a claim by a grandson against his grandmother, was asked to take into consideration a document which purports to be a deed of compromise dated 28th December 1918.

RatioOfTheDecision	The learned Vakils have argued this case with great preciseness and^ Mr. <NAME> for the appellant does not ask us to say that there is any magic about this document because it is in the nature of a nominal settlement which would excuse it from registration.
RatioOfTheDecision	It has been held by the learned Trial Judge on the argument of the respondent that it does require registration and to test that, I must look a t the section of the Registration Act which is material, and' that is Section 17 .
Fact	By Section 17(1)(b) among documents which require registration are nontestamentaly instruments which purport or operate to create, declare, assign, limit or extinguish, whether in present or in future, any right, title or interest, whether vested or contingent of the value of Rs.
Fact	100 and upwards, to or in immoveable property.
RatioOfTheDecision	Now it is said that this part of the document is a declaration of right in immoveable property and that, therefore" by virtue of Section 49 , the document would create no rights in the property in question.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is said, on the other side, that this is not a document which purports to create or modify in any way anybody's title to immoveable property.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is simply a statement of fact, as I said in the course of the argument, that <NAME>'s house is <NAME>'s house.

RatioOfTheDecision	There 'declare' is placed along with 'create', 'assign', limit', or 'extinguish', a 'right', 'title' or 'interest' and these words imply a definite change of legal relation to the property by expression of <NAME> embodied in the document referred to.
RatioOfTheDecision	I think this is equally the case with the word 'declare'.
RatioOfTheDecision	It implies a declaration of <NAME>, not a mere statement of fact, and thus a deed of partition, which causes a change of legal relation to the property divided amongst all the parties to it, is a declaration in the intended sense, but a letter containing an admission, direct or inferential, that a partition once took place, does not declare a right within the meaning of the section.
RatioOfTheDecision	It does in one sense 'declare' a right, that is the existence of the right is directly or indirectly stated by the writing, but it is not  the expression or declaration of Will by which the right is constituted."
