Argument	JUDGMENT  1.
Argument	It is contended that, as an appeal lies from the order made by the District Judge, the appellant's petition under Section 622 was not maintainable, and that, therefore, it was properly rejected.
Argument	It must be conceded that the order is appealable under Section 244 of the Code of Civil Procedure--Vallabhan v. Panqtmni I.L.R., 12 Mad., 454 and Muttia v. Appasami I.L.R., 13 Mad., 504.
Argument	2.
Argument	It is urged on behalf of respondent that as he is not an assignee of the decree, but one who attached it under Section 273, the above rulings are not applicable.
Argument	This, however, makes no difference in principle, as one who attaches a decree is the decree-holder's representative within the meaning of Section 244, as was also held by <ORG> in <CITATION_1752424> ., 15 Cal., 371.
Argument	3.
Argument	It is further contended that the objection that the District Judge's order is appealable was not urged before the learned Judge or before us when we made our former order.
RatioOfTheDecision	This is true; but the objection is one that goes to t he jurisdiction of the <ORG> to interfere at all under Section 622.
RatioOfTheDecision	We must, therefore, entertain the objection.
RatioOfTheDecision	4.
RatioOfTheDecision	The learned Judge's order dismissing the petition was, therefore, correct; but it should have proceeded on the ground that the application under Section 622 could not be entertained, the order objected to being appealable.
RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RulingByPresentCourt	We dismiss this appeal, but, under the circumstances, without costs.
