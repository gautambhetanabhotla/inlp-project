RatioOfTheDecision	To allege such misconduct against him and then to stigmatise the respondent as "not fit" for working in the higher post could appear, on the facts and circumstances of the particular case, to be more vindictive than just and fair.
RatioOfTheDecision	It may mar or delay his chances of promotion in future.
RatioOfTheDecision	We, however, refrain from commenting further on what may or may not have been the real cause of the respondent's reversion.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the respondent is really unfit or inefficient, as comp ared with his juniors, there is no reason why, on a comparative assessment of merits, at a time when such assessment may be called for under the rules (there should be rules on the subject if there are none so far), his juniors in service should not be preferred over him.
RatioOfTheDecision	A decision given after fair comparisons with records of others officiating in the same cadre would have ensured that no violation of Article 16 took place.
RatioOfTheDecision	The sudden reversion of the petitioner, for the reason given in tile reversion order, could be held to amount to an unjustified stigma which could not be said to be "devoid of an element of punishment".
RatioOfTheDecision	As we have indicated, there is no magic formula or uniform set of facts which could convert even an apparently colourless or innocuous order into punitive or unjustifiably discriminatory action.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is, however, well established that even an apparently inoffensive order may fail to pass tested imposed by Articles 16 and 311 of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	Dealings of superior officers with their subo rdinates in Government service in a Welfare State must be shown to be based on fair-play and reason when facts are actually proved which indicate that these requirements may be lacking.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even if the case before us could be one in which <ORG> could have refrained from interfering, we do not consider it to be a fit case for invoking our jurisdiction under Article 136 of the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> has only quashed an order of reversion which was detrimental to the respondent and was passed in violation of rules of natural justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	It did not give the respondent any other or consequential relief.
RatioOfTheDecision	And, as we have already indicated, it is still open for the authorities to proceed in a just and legal way against the respondent if there is really a substantial case against him deserving punitive action.
RatioOfTheDecision	As we are leaving the authorities free to take action, in accordance with either applicable rules for a comparative assessment of merits of the respondent and others who may be eligible to officiate in  the post of a Senior Station Incharge, or, to take disciplinary proceedings, it considered necessary no observation made that in this judgment or by <ORG> will operate as a finding on any question except that the quashed reversion order was punitive and passed contrary to rules of natural justice embodied in Article 311(2).
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not necessary to invoke the aid of Article 16 of the Constitution at all on such a finding.
