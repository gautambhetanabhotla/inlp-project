RatioOfTheDecision	8.
Precedent	<CITATION_6544121> (2006 (3) SCC 1) it was noted as follows:  "The decisions cited have uniformly held that res judicata does not apply in matters pertaining to tax for different assessment years because res judicata applies to debar courts from entertaining issues on the same cause of action whereas the cause of action for each assessment year is distinct.
Precedent	The courts will generally adopt an earlier pronouncement of the law or a conclusion of fact unless there is a new ground urged or a material change in the factual position.
Precedent	The reason why the courts have held parties to the opinion expressed in a decision in one assessment year to the same opinion in a subsequent year is not because of any principle of res judicata but because of the theory of precedent or the precedential value of the earlier pronouncement.
RatioOfTheDecision	Where facts and law in a subsequent assessment year are the same, no authority whether quasi-judicial or judicial can generally be permitted to take a different view.
RatioOfTheDecision	This mandate is subje ct only to the usual gateways of distinguishing the earlier decision or where the earlier decision is per incuriam.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, these are fetters only on a coordinate <ORG> which, failing the possibility of availing of either of these gateways, may yet differ with the view expressed and refer the matter to a <ORG> of superior strength or in some cases to a <ORG> of superior jurisdiction.
RatioOfTheDecision	A decision can be set aside in the same lis on a prayer for review or an application for recall or under Article 32 in the peculiar circumstances mentioned in <CITATION_123456797> (2002 (4) SCC 388).
RatioOfTheDecision	As we have said, overruling of a decision takes place in a subsequent lis where the precedential value of the decision is called in question.
RatioOfTheDecision	No one can dispute that in our judicial system it is open to a court of superior jurisdiction or strength before which a decision of a <ORG> of lower strength is cited as an authority, to overrule it.
RatioOfTheDecision	This overruling would not operate to upset the binding nature of the decision on the parties  to an earlier lis in that lis, for whom the principle of res judicata would continue to operate.
RatioOfTheDecision	But in tax cases relating to a subsequent year involving the same issue as an earlier year, the court can differ from the view expressed if the case is distinguishable or per incuriam.
RatioOfTheDecision	The decision in <CITATION_90703> (2003(3) SCC 239) related to the year 1988.
RatioOfTheDecision	Admittedly, the present dispute relates to a subsequent period.
RatioOfTheDecision	Here a coordinate <ORG> has referred the matter to a larger <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	This <ORG> being of superior strength, we can, if we so find, declare that the earlier decision does not represent the law.
RatioOfTheDecision	None of the decisions cited by the State of U.P.
RatioOfTheDecision	are authorities for the proposition that we cannot, in the circumstances of this case, do so.
Argument	This preliminary objection of the State of U.P.
Argument	is therefore rejected."
