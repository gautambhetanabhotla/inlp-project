Statute	Rule 52 inter alia provides that -any person aggrieved by an order of <ORG> refusing to grant a mining lease may within two months of the date of such order apply to <ORG> for reviewing the same.
Statute	Rule 53 prescribes a f ee.

RatioOfTheDecision	This Court had occasion to consider the nature of the two kinds of acts, namely, judicial which includes quasi- judicial and administrative, a number of times.
RatioOfTheDecision	<CITATION_1954356> (1), it adopted the celebrated definition of a quasi-judicial body given by <NAME> in <CITATION_1752215> (2), which is as follows:-  " Whenever any body of persons having legal authority to determine questions affecting rights of subjects, and having the duty to  act judicially act in excess of their legal authority they are subject to the controlling jurisdiction of <ORG> exercised in these writs. "
RatioOfTheDecision	This definition insists on three requisites each of which must be fulfilled in order that the act of the body may be a quasi-judicial act, namely, that the body of persons (1) must have legal authority, (2) to determine questions affecting the rights of subjects, and (3) must have the duty to act judicially.
Precedent	After analysing the various cases, <NAME> (as he then was) laid down the following principles as deducible therefrom in <NAME> case (1)at p. 725 :-  " (i) That, if a statute empowers an authority, not being a <ORG> in the ordinary sense, to decide (1) [1950] S.C.R.

"
RatioOfTheDecision	It is on these principles which are now well-settled that we have to see whether <ORG> when acting under r. 54 is acting in a quasi-judicial capacity or otherwise.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not necessary for present purposes to decide -whether <ORG> when it grants a lease is acting merely administratively.
RatioOfTheDecision	We shall assume that the order of the <ORG> granting a lease under the Rules is an administrative order.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have, h owever, to see what the position is after <ORG> has granted a lease to one of the applicants before it and has refused the lease to others.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mr. <NAME> contends that even in such a situation there is no right in favour of the person to whom the lease has been granted by <ORG> till <ORG> has passed an order on a review application if any.
RatioOfTheDecision	Rule 55, however, makes clear that the order of <ORG> is final subject to any order by <ORG> under r. 54.
RatioOfTheDecision	Now when a lease is granted by <ORG>, it is quite possible that there may be no application for review by those whose applications have been refused.
RatioOfTheDecision	In such a case the order of <ORG> would be final.
RatioOfTheDecision	It would not therefore be in our opinion right to say that no right of any kind is created in favour of a person to whom the lease is granted by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	The matter would be different if the order of <ORG> were not to be effective until conf irmation by <ORG>; for in that case no right would arise until the confirmation was received from <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	But r. 54 does not provide for confirmation by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It gives power to <ORG> to act only when there is an application for review before it under r. 54.
RatioOfTheDecision	That is why we have not accepted Mr. <NAME>'s argument that in substance <ORG> order becomes effective only after it is confirmed; r. 54 does not support this.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have not found any provision in the Rules or in the Act which gives any power to <ORG> to review suo motu the order of <ORG> granting a lease.
RatioOfTheDecision	That some kind of right is created on the passing of an order granting a lease is clear from the facts of this case also.
RatioOfTheDecision	The order granting the lease was made in December 1952.
RatioOfTheDecision	In April 1953 the appellant was put in possession of the areas granted to him and actually worked them thereafter.
RatioOfTheDecision	At any rate, when the statutory rule grants a righ t to any party aggrieved to make a review application to <ORG> it certainly follows that the person in whose favour the order is made has also a right to represent his case before the authority to whom the review application is made.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is in the circumstances apparent that as soon as r. 52 gives a right to an aggrieved party to apply for review a lis is created between him and the party in whose favour the grant has been made.
RatioOfTheDecision	Unless therefore there is anything in the statute to the contrary it will be the duty of the authority to act judicially and its decision would be a quasi-judicial act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The next question is whether there is anything in the <ORG> which negatives the duty to act judicially by the reviewing authority.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mr. <NAME> urges that r. 54 gives full power to <ORG> to act as it may deem I just and proper' and that it is not bound even to call for the relevant records and other information from,, <ORG> before deciding an application for review.
RatioOfTheDecision	That is  undoubtedly so.
RatioOfTheDecision	But that in our opinion does not show that the statutory Rules negative the duty to act judicially.
RatioOfTheDecision	What the Rules require is that <ORG> should act justly and properly; and that is what an authority which is required to act judicially must do.
RatioOfTheDecision	The fact that <ORG> is not bound even to call for records again does not negative the duty cast upon it to act judicially, for even courts have the power to dismiss appeals without calling for records.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus r. 54, lays down nothing to the contrary.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are therefore of opinion that there is prima facie a lis in this case as between the person to whom the lease has been granted and the person who is aggrieved by the refusal and therefore Prima facie it is the duty of the authority which has to review the matter to act judicially and there is nothing in r. 54 to the contrary.
RatioOfTheDecision	It must therefore be held that on the Rules and the Act, as they stood at the relevant time, <ORG> was acting in a quasi-judicial ca pacity while deciding an application under r. 54.
RatioOfTheDecision	As such it was incumbent upon it before coming to a decision to give a reasonable opportunity to the appellant, who was the other party in the review application whose rights were being affected, to represent his case.
RulingByPresentCourt	In as much as this was not done, the appellant is entitled to ask us to issue a writ in the nature of certiorari quashing the order of January 28,1954, passed by <ORG>.
RulingByPresentCourt	We therefore allow the appeal and setting aside the order of <ORG> quash the order of <ORG> passed on January 28, 1954.
RulingByPresentCourt	It will, however, be open to <ORG> to proceed to decide the review application afresh after giving a reasonable opportunity to the appellant to represent his case.
RulingByPresentCourt	The appellant will get his costs throughout from the third respondent, who is the principal contesting party.
