Statute	Section 59 (1) specifies 'operating maintenance and management expenses', 'taxes (if any) on income and profits', 'depreciation and interest payable on all debenture, bonds and loans', as in cluded in 'expenses properly chargeable to revenues'.
Statute	Section 59 (2) stipulates that in specifying the surplus, the <ORG> shall have due regard to the availability of amoun ts accrued by way of depreciation and the liability for loan amortization.
Statute	It also stipulates that a reasonable sum to contribute towards the cost of capital works and a reasonable sum by way of return on the capital provided by <ORG> should be left in the surplus.
Statute	This sub-section, therefore, makes it clear that the <ORG> is to provide for (1) loan amortization; (2) contribution towards the cost of capital works; and (3) return on the capital.
Statute	Section 67 prescribes the priority to be observed by the <ORG> in the matter of discharging the liabilities enumerated therein out of its revenues.
Statute	First the operating maintenance and management expenses have to be met, next provision has to be made for payment of taxes on Income and Profits and thereafter various items of expenditure are mentioned in order of priority.
Statute	If any amount is left after the discharge of the liabilities enumerated in s. 67 , the balance shall be utilised for the other purposes specified in s. 59 in such manner as the <ORG> may  decide.

RatioOfTheDecision	The very provision for repayment of capital necessarily implies payment of all interest accrued upto the date of repayment of the capital.
RatioOfTheDecision	If arrears of interest cannot be paid from revenue receipts, such arrears cannot be paid from the capital receipts.
Statute	What may be paid out of capital receipts and th e circumstances under which the payment may be made are expressly provided in s. 67 (2) which says that if for any reasons beyond the control of the <ORG> the revenue receipts in any year are not adequate to meet the operating, maintenance and management expenses, taxes on income and profits, and the liabilities referred to in clauses (i) and (ii) of s. 67 (1), then the shortfall shall be paid out of its capital receipts with the sanction of <ORG>.
Statute	There is no doubt that arrears of interest are, under the scheme of accounting contemplated by the Act, required to be paid out of revenue receipts of the <ORG> and are expenses properly chargeable to revenues within the meaning of that expression in s. 59 of the Act.

Statute	The <ORG> has clarified the aforesaid position by the Amending Act 16 of 1983 which came into force from April 1, 1985.
