Argument	3.
Argument	<ORG> has pointed out that under the scheme of the Act for the purpose of imposition of property tax under Sub-section (1) of Section 127A, each tenement has to be separately assessed and no tax can be levied for a building with annual letting value up to rupees eighteen hundred.
RatioOfTheDecision	The aggregation of annual letting value of all buildings owned by a single individual could be applied only for exemption and not for taxation.
RatioOfTheDecision	The unit of tax is a building (property) and not a person.
RatioOfTheDecision	If a person owns more than one building within the urban area to which the Act is applicable, the aggregate annual letting value  of all the buildings cannot be taken into consideration for assessment of tax.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the quarters are let out to different persons, each quarter has to be valued as a separate unit.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the annual letting value of each quarter does not exceed the limit prescribed by the Act, it will be exempt from assessment <ORG> relied upon the previous decisions construing the corresponding provisions in the M.P.

Argument	4.
RatioOfTheDecision	It seems to us that <ORG> had a mechanical approach to construction.
RatioOfTheDecision	The mechanical approach to construction is altogether out of step with the modern positive approach.
RatioOfTheDecision	The modern positive approach is to have a purposeful construction that is to effectuate the object and purpose of the Act.
RulingByPresentCourt	Section 127A must, therefore, receive a purposeful construction.
Statute	Sub-section  (1) contains a table for taxation.
Statute	There is no provision for taxation in respect of a building having annual letting value less than rupees eighteen hundred.
Statute	Clause (b) of Sub-section (2) expressly exempts buildings and lands, the annual letting value of which does not exceed eighteen hundred rupees.
Statute	The proviso permits adding up of annual letting value of all such buildings or lands owned by a single individual in the Municipality.
RatioOfTheDecision	The proviso no doubt states that the annual letting value aggregated shall be deemed to be "for the purpose of this clause" meaning thereby for the purpose of Clause (b), that is for exemption.
RatioOfTheDecision	But the purpose of the proviso is to deny exemption to buildings or lands owned by the same person and of which the total annual letting value exceeds rupees eighteen hundred.
RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is quite true that each building is a unit for the purpose of taxation and there is no provision for taxation of building and land of which the annual letting value is up to rupees eighteen hundred.
RatioOfTheDecision	But when a ggregation of annual letting value of all buildings or lands is permitted, then, all such buildings or lands have to be taken as one unit for the purpose of taxation.
RatioOfTheDecision	Any other construction would render the proviso nugatory and defeat the object of the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> could not have intended that all buildings or lands owned by a single individual should get exemption from taxation even if their total letting value exceeds rupees eighteen hundred.
RatioOfTheDecision	6.
RatioOfTheDecision	The decisions of <ORG> taking contrary view cannot be said to have laid down the law correctly.
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
