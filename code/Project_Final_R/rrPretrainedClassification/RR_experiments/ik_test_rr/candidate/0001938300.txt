Fact	JUDGMENT <NAME>, J.
Fact	1.
Fact	This is an appeal by the insurance company against the award of <ORG>, Gurgaon, dated 22nd of April, 1971, whereby a sum of Rs.
Fact	8,000 has been awarded to each of the three claimants against <NAME>, driver, <NAME>, owner of the truck and the appellant-insurance company.
Fact	Out of the amount awarded Rs.
Fact	20,000 was to be paid to the claimants by the insurance company and the remaining by the owner and the driver of the truck jointly and severally.
Fact	2.
Fact	<NAME> was conductor on truck No.
Fact	HRG-1170, which belonged to <NAME>, respondent, and used to be driven by <NAME>, respondent.
Fact	The truck was insured with <ORG>, New Delhi, and under the policy there was a special clause that the insurance company would be liable to compensate not only under the Workmen's Compensation Act , 1923, but also under the Fatal Accidents Act or at common law in respect of any personal injury to any paid driver (or  cleaner or conductor or a person employed in loading and/or unloading).
Fact	On 12th of March, 1969, while <NAME>, conductor, was checking the wheels of the truck, the driver started it with the result that <NAME> was crushed under the wheel of the truck as a result of which he died at the spot.
Fact	The widow, a minor son and a minor daughter of the deceased-conductor filed a claim petition before <ORG>, Gurgaon, for awarding Rs.
Fact	50,000 as compensation.
Fact	3.
Fact	The application was contested by all the respondents except <NAME>, driver, who did not appear and was proceeded ex parte.
Fact	The insurance company took the plea that by virtue of Section 95 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, <ORG> has no jurisdiction and it was further pleaded that the liability, if any, with regard to a conductor, which was required to be covered under the provisions of the Motor Vehicles Act , was limited up to the liability as fixed under the Workmen's Compensation Act .
Fact	It was al so stated that the maximum liability of the insurance company was limited to Rs.
Fact	20,000 only, besides taking other pleas that there was no rash and negligent driving by the driver of the truck and for that reason no compensation was liable to be paid.
Fact	On the contest of the parties, the following issues were framed :  1.
Fact	Whether the <ORG> has no jurisdiction to try this petition ?
Fact	2.
Fact	Whether the award cannot be passed against the insurance company ?
Fact	3.
Fact	Whether the deceased <NAME> was a conductor on truck No.
Fact	1170-HRG?
Precedent	4.
Precedent	Whether the deceased was killed as alleged by the applicants and to what compensation are the applicants entitled ?
Precedent	5.
Precedent	Whether the applicants are the heirs of the deceased ?
Precedent	6.
Precedent	Relief.
Precedent	4.
Precedent	The <ORG> held under issue No.
Precedent	1, on the basis of certain decisions which have been noticed, that it had the jurisdiction and this point has not been raised before me in appeal.
Precedent	As regards issues Nos.
Precedent	2, 3 and 4 which were dealt with together, it was held that the case was not covered by <ORG> n 95 of the Motor Vehicles Act with the result that the Workmen's Compensation Act was not applicable and, therefore, the liability of the insurance company was not limited to the amount which could be awarded under the Workmen's Compensation Act .
Precedent	The driver of the truck was held guilty of rash and negligent driving and these issues were decided in favour of the claimants.
Fact	Under issue No.
Fact	5, it was held that all the claimants are heirs of the deceased.
Argument	5.
Argument	Before me, Mr. <NAME>, wanted to raise his first submission which was also raised before the Tribunal that since the deceased was a conductor on the truck, the case fell under Section 95(2)(b) of the Motor Vehicles Act and the compensation which had to be awarded to the claimants had to be calculated in the manner given in the Workmen's Compensation Act .
Argument	Before this point could proceed further for discussion, Mr. <NAME>, appearing for the claimants, pointed out from the insurance policy, which is on the record, that there was a special clause i n the insurance policy for which the insurance company took higher premium and according to that clause this point need not be gone into although he is on firm footing on this point also, in view of the <ORG> decision in <CITATION_945489>  ACJ 422.
Argument	6.
Argument	I think Mr. <NAME>, counsel for the claimants, is right in saying that the aforesaid point raised by Mr. <NAME> need not be gone into in view of the special provision in the policy on the basis of which the claimants would be entitled to claim compensation at common law.
Precedent	In support of this point, Mr. <NAME>, apart from relying on the special clause, has cited before me a judgment of <ORG> in <CITATION_433240> , AIR 1975 Mad 198, and two <ORG> judgments of <ORG> in Punjabhai Prabhudas & Co. v. <NAME>  ACJ 44; AIR 1977 Guj 179 and <CITATION_663776>  ACJ 186; AIR 1979 Guj 26.
Precedent	For facility of reference, the quotation from <ORG> may be reproduced (at p. 33 of AIR 1979 Gu j 26):  "'...the endorsement quoted above shows that the legal liability of the insurance company was not limited only to one arising under the Workmen's Compensation Act , 1923, but also extended to the liability arising under the Fatal Accidents Act , 1855, or at common law.
Precedent	The expression at common law used in Endorsement No.
Precedent	16 knocks the bottom of the insurance company's defence down.... ' "  7.
Precedent	Mr. <NAME> had no reply to the aforesaid argument except that he wanted to rely on <NAME> v. <NAME> [1966] ACJ 284, a decision of <ORG>, where a car passenger was not allowed any compensation against the insurance company.
RatioOfTheDecision	That case has nothing to do with the present case as there is a specific provision in the policy for grant of compensation to the conductor and other employees of the insured truck under the common law.
RatioOfTheDecision	As such, this case does not militate against the view taken by the Madras and Gujarat High Courts with which I fully agree.
RatioOfTheDecision	Accordingly, I hold that in view of t he special provisions the claimants were entitled to the compensation under common law and they could be awarded compensation beyond the amount fixed in the Schedule to the Workmen's Compensation Act .
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	No meaningful argument was raised before me to show that the driver of the truck was not guilty of rash and negligent driving when he started the truck without ensuring whether the conductor has come out from under the truck after checking the wheels.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is a clear case of rash and negligent driving and the insurance company is liable to pay compensation to the heirs of the deceased.
RatioOfTheDecision	However, Mr. <NAME> was fair enough to concede that in view of Section 96(2) of the Motor Vehicles Act and the decisions in <CITATION_135147>  29 Comp Cas (Ins) 60 ; AIR 1959 SC 1331 and <CITATION_791759>  36 Comp Cas 340 ;  ACJ 203, the insurance company cannot raise any argument regarding the quantum of compensation.
RatioOfTheDecision	9.
RatioOfTheDecision	For the reasons recorded above, I find no merit in the appeal filed by the insurance company and dismiss the  same.
RulingByPresentCourt	However, there will be no order as to costs.
