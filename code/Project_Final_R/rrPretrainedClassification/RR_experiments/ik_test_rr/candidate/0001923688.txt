RulingByLowerCourt	We have heard learned counsel for the parties at length and have perused the record.
Argument	Dr. <NAME>, Learned Senior Advocate appearing on behalf of the appellants contended that the direction issued by <ORG> vide letters dated 18.7.1988 and 5.5.1989 under Section 78-A of the Act was binding on the Board.
Argument	It was contended that the directions given in the aforesaid letters was on questions of policy and <ORG> was fully authorised to give such directions to the Board in exercise of its power conferred under Section 78-A of the Act.
Argument	It was further submitted that the appellants were selected for the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers after passing the written and oral examination and were kept in the merit/panel list of 1984.
Argument	It was conte nded that merely because the appellants have an undertaking while being appointed as Operators that they would not lay any claim on the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers, cannot deprive them from being appointed on future vacancies of Junior Electrical Engineers.
Argument	The undertaking was unconscionable and violative of Article 16 of the Constitution.
Argument	It was submitted that some of the appellants have become over age and it would not be possible for the appellants to compete with the fresh incumbents in case the posts are filled by direct recruitment.
Argument	It was contended that all the appellants are Diploma holders and have additional advantage of experience on the post of Operators and in these circumstances the appellants should be absorbed against the vacant posts of Junior Electrical Engineers without competing with the direct recruits.
Argument	On the other hand, Learned Counsel appearing for the Board supported the judgment of <ORG> and contended that the panel of 1984 exhausted after one year and thereafter t he appellants had no right or claim whatsoever on the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers to be filled now through advertisement issued on 29.7.1989.
Argument	It was further submitted that the <ORG> had shown a gesture of goodwill by giving appointment to the appellants on the post of Operators and now the appellants cannot put forth a legal right for being appointed as Junior Electrical Engineers after a period of more than 4 years on the basis of inclusion in the panel of 1984.
Argument	They have to compete with other fresh competitors who have become eligible for such posts to be filled by direct recruitment according to the Rules.
Argument	It has been submitted that Section 78-A of the Act empowers <ORG> to issue directions of policy and no direction under such power can be given to the <ORG> to make appointment of the appellants dehors the Rules.
Statute	Section 78-A of the Act reads as under :-  "Directions by <ORG>.
Statute	- (1) In the discharge of its functions, the <ORG> shall be guided by such directions on  questions of policy as may be given to it by <ORG>.
Argument	(2) If any dispute arises between the <ORG> and <ORG> as to whether a question is or is not a question of policy, it shall be referred to the <ORG> whose decision thereon shall be final."
Argument	The above provision clearly lays down that the <ORG> shall be guided by such directions on questions of policy as may be given to it by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the circumstances of the case before us the directions given under letters dated 18.7.1988 and 5.5.1989 cannot be considered as directions on any question of policy.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as the appointment of staff is concerned, Section 15 empowers the <ORG> to appoint such officers and employees as may be required to enable the <ORG> to carry out its functions under the Act.
Statute	Section 15 of the Act reads as under :-  "Appointment of staff.
Statute	- The <ORG> may appoint a Secretary and such other officers and employees as may be required to enable to <ORG> to carry out its functions under this Act :  P rovided that the appointment of the Secretary shall be subject to the approval of <ORG>."
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, under the proviso to Section 15 , it is only the appointment of the Secretary which is subject to the approval of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as other staff is concerned, it lies with the <ORG> to make appointment of all officers and employees as may be required to enable the <ORG> to carry out its functions under the Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, we agree with the view taken by <ORG> in this regard that the direction given by <ORG> to appoint the appellants as Junior Engineers by the <ORG> does not involve any matter of policy and it would be an encroachment on the powers of the <ORG> given under Section 15 of the Act.
Argument	It is not in dispute that the life of the panel was one year which came to an end on 13.9.1985 and that being so no right can be claimed by the appellants after 13.9.1985 on the basis of inclusion of their names in the panel list of 1984 for the posts of <ORG> eers.
Argument	So far as giving of any undertaking is concerned, we are in agreement with the contention of the Learned Counsel for the appellants that such undertaking cannot estop the appellants from being considered for the future vacancies of Junior Electrical Engineers and the appellants cannot be deprived for all times to come.
Argument	The post of Junior Electrical Engineer is now governed by the Bihar State Electricity Junior Electrical Engineer (General) Cadre Rules, 1982 and specially Rule 7 read with modification made by Notification dated 14.10.1988 issued under Section 79 (c) of the Act which reads as follows :-  "Appointments to the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers from amongst the employees of the Board having Diploma in Electrical Engineering will be made on the basis of the recommendations of <ORG> which will be constituted by the Chairman for the purpose.
RatioOfTheDecision	<ORG> will examine the records and interview the candidates who obtain diploma in Electrical Engineering while in the  service of the <ORG> along with those employees who had obtained diploma in Electrical Engineering before joining the service of the <ORG>."
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, by a combined reading of Rule 7, posts of Junior Electrical Engineers (General) Cadre shall be filled by direct recruitment from Diploma holders in Electrical Engineering and by absorption of the board's employees serving on lower posts.
RatioOfTheDecision	Appointment by direct recruitment and by absorption shall be made against vacancies in a calendar year in the ratio of 75-25.
RatioOfTheDecision	Now in case of appointment to the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers if made by absorption from amongst the employees of the <ORG> serving on lower posts that has to be made through a process of screening by <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	So far as appointment to be made by direct recruitment, the candidates have to be selected on the basis of merit after going through the process of written and oral examination and the appellants shall have to compete with all the Diploma holders who would compete for such posts  of Junior Electrical Engineers to be filled by direct recruitment.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> in this regard has clearly stated in the counter affidavit filed before this Court that the appellants who apply for the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers as and when advertisement is issued and appear at the competitive examination to be held again by the <ORG> for appointment on the posts of Junior Engineers and in case they Compete, they would be appointed against the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG>, of course, cannot deny the right of the appellants for appointment on the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers now sought to be filled on the ground that the appellants had given any undertaking at the time of their appointment as Operators.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> has to make appointments for the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers both by way of direct recruitment from Diploma holders in Electrical Engineering and by absorption of the <ORG>'s employees serving on lower posts in the ratio of 75- 25 as contemplated in Rule 7 an d the appellants would also have a right to be considered for such appointments.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have been informed during the course of argument on behalf of the <ORG> that no written or oral examination has been conducted to fill the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers in pursuance to the advertisement issued on 29.7.1989.
RulingByPresentCourt	Thus, taking in view the entire facts and circumstances of the case and in order to do full justice to all the persons concerned, we direct the <ORG> to issue a fresh advertisement for filling of the posts of Junior Electrical Engineers having fallen due upto 31st March, 1992 and to make appointments in the ratio of 75 per cent by direct recruitment and 25 percent by absorption as contemplated under Rule 7.
