Fact	JUDGMENT  1.
Fact	This is a defendant's appeal arising out of a suit brought by rival pre-emptors for a division of property with the contesting defendant.
Fact	On the 9th August 1923, certain vendors sold the property to certain vendees.
Fact	<NAME>, the contesting defendant brought a suit for pre-emption against the vendees and obtained a decree on the 14th March 1924.
Fact	He obtained possession under the said decree.
Fact	The present plaintiffs, who are eight in number, brought the present suit on the 19th July 1924, for a share in the property so pre-empted.
Fact	The defendant contested that, inasmuch as he was a co-sharer and had acquired the property prior to the present suit, Section 20 was a bar to the plaintiffs' claim.
Fact	<ORG> instance acceded to this contention and dismissed the suit, but the lower appellate Court has come to a contrary conclusion.
RulingByLowerCourt	2.
RulingByLowerCourt	It cannot be doubted that before the passing of the Agra Pre-emption Act the mere fact that a rival pre-emptor had obtained a decree for pre-emption did not preve nt other rival pre-emptors from claiming a share in the property as against him, provided their suit was instituted within one year from the sale.
RulingByLowerCourt	In the case of <CITATION_1299758>  32 All.
Argument	340, it was pointed out that a right of pre-emption was not a right of repurchase but a right of substitution for the original vendee, and that under the decrees in favour of the rival pre-emptors there has not been any fresh transfer in their favour, but they have been put in the places of the original vendees.
Argument	3.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion that law has in no way been altered by the new Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 4, Sub-clause (9) embodies the principle that the right of pre-emption is not a right of transfer but a right of substitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	Under Section 13, when two or more persons claiming pre-emption are equally entitled, the property shall be equally divided between them, each paying an equal share of the consideration for the transfer.
RatioOfTheDecision	It matters little who comes to Court first, provided that they all come within the period of limitation.
RatioOfTheDecision	Secti on 20 of the Act cannot apply to the case where one rival pre-emptor has obtained a decree for pre-emption first.
RatioOfTheDecision	The expression where the purchaser has transferred the property in dispute to a person having a right of pre-emption, etc.
RatioOfTheDecision	cannot cover the case of a pre-emptor obtaining a decree for pre-emption for, as pointed out by us, that is not a case of a transfer by the vendee to the pre-emptor, In our opinion, therefore, the view taken by the learned District Judge was correct and the present plaintiffs are entitled to their proportionate share in the preempted property.
RatioOfTheDecision	4.
Fact	<NAME> was one of the claimants.
Fact	The present plaintiffs, who are eight in number are also claimants.
Fact	These latter are, therefore, entitled to eight-ninths of the property sold under the deed of 9th August 1923, on payment of eight-ninths of the sale consideration.
Fact	The appeal is accordingly dismissed with costs including in this Court fees on the higher scale
