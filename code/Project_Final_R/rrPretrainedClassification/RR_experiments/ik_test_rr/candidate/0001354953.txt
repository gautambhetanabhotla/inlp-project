RatioOfTheDecision	Does the rule of natural justice has no exception ?
RatioOfTheDecision	Is denial of opportunity of hearing, in every circumstance, arbitrary?
RatioOfTheDecision	The State of Karnataka and <ORG>, through t hese appeals, seek answer to these questions.
RatioOfTheDecision	They are aggrieved by directions, issued by <ORG>, to get the answer books of candidates evaluated who in the competitive examinations conducted by the commission for <ORG> for categories `A' and `B' post, were guilty of writing their roll numbers not only on the front page of the answer books, in the space provided for it, but even at other places in disregard of instructions issued by the <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Basis for the direction was failure of the <ORG> to afford any opportunity to the candidates to explain their bonafide and innocence therefore it was arbitrary and it entailed grave consequences for those who were aspirants for entering into public service.
RatioOfTheDecision	Power and authority of the <ORG> to hold examinations, regulate its working and functioning take action against erring candidates guilty of misconduct are all provided for by the rules and instructions issued in exercise of power conferred by the Statut es.
RatioOfTheDecision	The claim of the candidates that they did not vest any right in the <ORG> to take such action was negatived by the tribunal.
RatioOfTheDecision	But it faulted in inferring that no penalty was provided for breach of instructions requiring a candidate not to write his roll number inside the answer book.
RatioOfTheDecision	Relevant clause (1) of the Instructions to Candidates is extracted below:  "Before commencing your answers please write your register number and other particulars in the space provided above.
RatioOfTheDecision	Do not write your name or register number or sign any where in the answer book or on any loose sheets, such as precis sheets,maps, graph papers, etc.'
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not disputed and it was found, even by the tribunal that it was printed on the first page of every, answer book.
RatioOfTheDecision	Its observance was mandatory and its disregard was punishable is clear from instruction (xii) and (xiii) of General Instructions to the candidates which are extracted below:  "(xii) The candidates must abide by such instructions as may be specified on the cover of the  answer book or any further instructions which may be given by the Supervisor/Invigilator of the Examination.
RatioOfTheDecision	(xiii) If the candidates fail to do so or indulge in disorderly or improper conduct, they will render themselves liable to expulsion from examination and or such other punishment as the <ORG> may deem fit to impose."
RatioOfTheDecision	Is the expression, 'such other punishment as the commission may deem fit to impose' vague and thus arbitrary?
RatioOfTheDecision	We do not think so.Read with clause (xii) it presents no difficulty.
RatioOfTheDecision	It provides action for breach of that which is, clearly, specified.
RatioOfTheDecision	It cannot be characterised as vague.
RatioOfTheDecision	And then any capricious exercise of power can always be assailed.
RatioOfTheDecision	More important than this is that provisions attempting to infuse discipline in competitive to be conducted by the <ORG> cannot be construed with same yardstick as a provision in penal statutes.
RatioOfTheDecision	Moreover the <ORG> did not impose any penalty on the candidates.
RatioOfTheDecision	Their examination was not cancelled nor they were debarred from taking  any examination conducted by the <ORG> for that year or any year, in future.
RatioOfTheDecision	Their marks in papers, other than those in which they were found to have acted in disregard of instructions were declared.
RatioOfTheDecision	The only action taken was that those answer books in which roll numbers had been written inside were not subjected to evaluation.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion there was nothing, basically,wrong in it.
RatioOfTheDecision	The <ORG> did not treat it as misconduct.
RatioOfTheDecision	The action could not be termed as arbitrary.
RatioOfTheDecision	Nor it was abuse of power which could be corrected by judicial review.
RatioOfTheDecision	Such instructions are issued to ensure fairness in the examination.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the fast deteriorating standards of honesty and morality in the society the insistence by the <ORG> that no attempt should be made of identification of the candidate by writing his roll number anywhere is in the larger public interest.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is well known that the first page of the answer book on which roll number is written is removed and a fictitious code number is provided to rule out any  effort of any approach to the examiner.
RatioOfTheDecision	Not that a candidate who has written his roll number would have approached the examiner.
RatioOfTheDecision	He may have committed a bonafide mistake.
RatioOfTheDecision	But that is not material.
RatioOfTheDecision	What was attempted to be achieved by the instruction was to minimise any possibility or chance of any abuse.
RatioOfTheDecision	Larger public interest demands of observance of instruction rather than its breach.
RatioOfTheDecision	Was natural justice violated ?
RatioOfTheDecision	Natural justice is a concept which has succeeded in keeping the arbitrary action within limits and preserving the rule of law.
RatioOfTheDecision	But with all the religious rigidity with which it should be observed, since it is ultimately weighed in balance of fairness, the courts have been circumspect in extending it to situations where it would cause more injustice than justice.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even though the procedure of affording hearing is as important as decision on merits yet urgency of the matter, or public interest at times require flexibility in application of the rule as the circumstances of the case and the nature of  the matter required to be dealt may serve interest of justice better by denying opportunity of hearing and permitting the person concerned to challenge the order itself on merits not for lack of hearing to establish bonafide or innocence but for being otherwise arbitrary or against rules.
RatioOfTheDecision	Present is a case which, in our opinion, can safely be placed in a category where natural justice before taking any action stood excluded as it did not involve any misconduct or punishment.
RatioOfTheDecision	Competitive examinations are required to be conducted by the <ORG> for public service in strict secrecy to get the best brain.
RatioOfTheDecision	Public interest requires no compromise on it.
RatioOfTheDecision	Any violation of it should be visited strictly.
RatioOfTheDecision	Absence of any expectation of hearing in matters which do not affect any interest and call for immediate action, such as the present one, where it would have delayed declaration of list of other candidates which would have been more unfair and unjust are rare but well recognised exceptions to the rule of natural ju stice.
RatioOfTheDecision	It cannot be equated with where a student is found copying in the examination or an inference arises against him for copying due to similarity in answers of number of other candidates or he is charged with misconduct or misbehavior.
RatioOfTheDecision	Direction not to write roll number was clear and explicit.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was printed on the first page of every answer book.
RatioOfTheDecision	Once it was violated the issue of bonafide and honest mistake did not arise.
RatioOfTheDecision	Its consequences, even, if not provided did not make any difference in law.
RatioOfTheDecision	The action could not be characterised as arbitrary.
RatioOfTheDecision	It was not denial of equal opportunity.
RatioOfTheDecision	The reverse may be true.
RatioOfTheDecision	The tribunal appears to have been swayed by principles applied by this <ORG> where an examinee is found copying or using unfair means in the examination.
RatioOfTheDecision	But in doing so the tribunal ignored a vital distinction that there may be cases where the right of hearing may be excluded by the very nature of the power or absence of any expectation that the hearing shall be afforded.
RatioOfTheDecision	Rule of hearing has been  construed strictly in academic disciplines.
RatioOfTheDecision	It should be construed more strictly in such cases where an examinee is competing for <ORG> post.
RatioOfTheDecision	The very nature of the competition requires that it should be fair, above board and must infuse confidence.
RatioOfTheDecision	If this is ignored then, as stated earlier, it is not only against public interest but it also erodes the social sense of equality.
RatioOfTheDecision	The tribunal in issuing directions approached the matter technically and has attempted to make out much where it would have been better part of discretion to refuse to interfere.
RatioOfTheDecision	The tribunal completely misdirected itself in this regard.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion its order cannot be maintained.
