RatioOfTheDecision	There may be many good reasons for the authors of the Constitution giving separate treatment to gas and gas- works.
RatioOfTheDecision	If one can surmise, it may be that, as the industry of gas an d gas-works was confined to one or two States and was not of all India importance, it was carved out of entry 24 and given a separate entry, as otherwise if a declaration by law was made by <ORG> within the meaning of entry 7 or entry 52 of List I, it would be taken out of the legislative power of States.
RatioOfTheDecision	Be it as it may, the express intention of the Constitution is to treat it, in normal times, as a state subject and it is not in the province of this Court to ascertain and scrutinize the reasons for doing so.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is suggested that this interpretation would prevent <ORG> to make law in respect of gas and gas-works during war or other national emergency.
RatioOfTheDecision	Apart from the relevancy of such a consideration, the apprehension has no justification, for under Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	249 <ORG> is enabled to take up for legislation any matter which is specifically enumerated in List II whenever <ORG> resolves by two-thirds majority that such a legislation is necessary or expedient in the national interest.
RatioOfTheDecision	So  too, under Art.
RatioOfTheDecision	250 <ORG> can make laws with respect to any of the matters enumerated in the <ORG> List, if a Proclamation of Emergency is in operation.
RatioOfTheDecision	Article 252 authorizes the <ORG> to legislate for two or more <ORG>s, if <ORG> of those :<ORG>s give their consent to the said course.
RatioOfTheDecision	Subject to such emergency or extra- ordinary powers, the entire industry of gas and gas-works is within exclusive legislative competence of a <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is, therefore, clear that the scheme of harmonious construction suggested on behalf of the <ORG> gives' full and effective scope of operation for both the entries in their respective fields, while that suggested by learned counsel for the appellant deprives entry 25 of all its content and even makes it redundant.
RatioOfTheDecision	The former interpretation must, therefore, be accepted in preference to the latter.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this view,' gas and-gas works are within the exclusive field allotted to the <ORG>s.
RatioOfTheDecision	On this interpretation the argument of the learned Attorney-  General that, under Art.

RatioOfTheDecision	We, therefore, hold that the impugned Act was within the legislative competence of the West Bengal <ORG> and was, therefore, validly made.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this view the alternative argument advanced on behalf of the <ORG>, namely, that the impugned Act was made by virtue of entry 33 and entry 42 of List III need not be considered.
RatioOfTheDecision	We should not be understood to have expressed our view one way or other on this aspect of the case.
RatioOfTheDecision	Nor is the contention of learned Attorney General that s. 20 of the Central Act Would still be valid vis-a-vis gas industry has any force.
RatioOfTheDecision	Under s. 20 of the Central Act, "After the commencement of this Act, it shall not be competent for any <ORG> Governmentor a local authority to take over the management or control of  any industrial undertaking under any law for the time being in force which authorizes any such Government or local authority so to do."
RatioOfTheDecision	We have expressed the view that the <ORG> of a State has the exclusive power to make law in respect of gas industry by virtue of entry 25 of List II, and that entry 24 does not comprehend gas industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	As we have indicated earlier, the expression "industry" in entry 52 of List I bears the same meaning as that in entry 24 of List II, with the result that the said expression in entry 52 of List I also does not take in a gas industry.
RatioOfTheDecision	If so, it follows that <ORG> , in so far as it purported to deal with the gas industry, is beyond the legislative competence of <ORG>.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 20 is an integral part of <ORG> , and if it is taken out of the Act, it can only operate in vacuum.
RatioOfTheDecision	The said section was introduced for the effective implementation of the provisions of <ORG> .
RatioOfTheDecision	It was also enacted by virtue of entry 52 of List I of the Seventh Schedu le to the Constitution.
RatioOfTheDecision	If the Act was constitutionally void in so far as it purported to effect the gas industry, for the aforesaid reasons, s. 20 would equally- be void to the same extent for the same reasons.
RatioOfTheDecision	In this context two decisions of this <ORG>, namely <CITATION_567012> (1), and <CITATION_754207> (  2) may usefully be consulted, for in the said decisions this court held that ancillary provisions enacted for carrying out the objects of a main Act would fall with the main Act on the ground that they were enacted only to subserve the purpose of the main Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	Section 20 , therefore, will not avail the appellant to question the validity of the <ORG> action.
RatioOfTheDecision	In the result, we agree with the High <ORG> that the impugned Act was within the legislative competence of the West Bengal <ORG> Legislature and was validly made.
