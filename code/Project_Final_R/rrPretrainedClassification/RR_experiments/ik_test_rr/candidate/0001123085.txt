Fact	ORDER  1.
Fact	The petitioner has filed this writ petition seeking a direction to quash the show cause notice dated 27/29.6.1989 issued by the third respondent, Assistant Collector (Exports), New Delhi.
Fact	It was alleged that the petitioner imported certain quantity of photographic films (colour) `Konica' which were lying in <ORG>, C.W.C., Okhla, and that these goods had been imported from Japan under Open General license (OGL) for home consumption and could not be re- exported to any country and that the petitioner attempted to export the same to Singapore which was in violation of the Export (Control) Order, 1977, as amended.
Fact	2.
Fact	During the pendency of this petition, an adjudication order was made by the Additional Collector of Customs which is dated 19.7.1989.
Fact	By this order the goods were ordered to be confiscated under S. 113(d) of the Customs Act, 1962 (for short `the Act') but the petitioner exporter was allowed to clear the goods for export on payment of redemption fine amounting to Rs.
Fact	4,50 ,000.00 and on payment of appropriate customs duty for ex-bond clearance.
Fact	Personal penalty for Rs.
Fact	50,000/- in respect of each of the shipping bills totaling Rs.
Fact	1,50,000.00 was also imposed under S. 114 of the Act.
Fact	There are three shipping bills.
Fact	3.
Fact	Thereafter, the petitioner sought to amend his petition to challenge this order which is, of course, appealable under the Act.
Argument	Mr.<NAME> submitted that this order was principally based on a letter dated 25.5.1989 received from the Chief Controller of Imports & Exports (<ORG>) wherein he said that goods imported under OGL were for home consumption and should not be allowed to be re-exported.
Argument	Mr.<NAME> said this clarification given by the <ORG> was law and as such the goods in question would become prohibited goods export of which could not be allowed.
Argument	It was brought to our notice by Mr.<NAME> that similar goods had been allowed to be re-exported by <ORG> without payment of duty and in support of this contention he filed various shipping bills  showing "Foreign Produce to be Re- exported".
Fact	Last such shipping bill is of September 1989.
Fact	We gave various opportunities to Mr.<NAME> to find out as to how <ORG> were allowing re- export of the goods in question without payment of duty.
Argument	He expressed his inability to throw any light on this as he said that he was unable to get any information or instructions from the <ORG>.
Argument	4.
Statute	S. 3(33) of the Act defines prohibited goods which mean "any goods the import or export of which is subject to any prohibition under this Act or any other law for the time being in force but does not include any such goods in respect of which the conditions subject to which the goods are permitted to be imported or exported have been complied with".
Statute	The goods in question were stored on import in a warehouse pending clearance.
Statute	Under S. 46 of the Act, the importer of any goods, other than goods intended for transit or transhipment, shall make entry thereof by presenting to the proper officer a bill of entry for home  consumption or warehousing in the prescribed form.
Fact	It is not disputed that in the present case, a bill of entry for warehousing in the form prescribed was filed (Ss.
Fact	46 and 49).
RatioOfTheDecision	Then, under S. 51 of the Act, where the proper officer is satisfied that any goods entered for export are not prohibited goods and the exporter has paid the duty, if any, assessed thereon and any charges payable under the Act in respect of the same, the proper officer may make an order permitting clearance and loading of the goods for exportation.
RatioOfTheDecision	Under sub-s.(1) of S. 69 , any warehoused goods may be exported to a place outside India without payment of import duty if -  "(a) a shipping bill or a bill of export has been presented in respect of such goods in the prescribed form;  (b) the export duty,penalties,rent,interest and other charges payable in respect of such goods have been paid; and  (c) an order for clearance of such goods for exportation has been made by the proper officer."
RatioOfTheDecision	5.
RatioOfTheDecision	The goods in question, being the photographic  films (colour), fall under App.6,List 8,Part II,Serial No.41 of the Import and Export Policy, 1988-91, and their import is allowed by all persons for actual use/stock and sale.
RatioOfTheDecision	The contention of Mr.<NAME> is that since the goods were imported for stock and sale, these could not be re- exported.
RatioOfTheDecision	We are unable to agree with the condition of Mr.<NAME> or with the view taken by the respondents.
RatioOfTheDecision	Again, to us, the goods do not appear to be prohibited goods.
Statute	We may usefully refer to Para 4 of Section I, dealing with Export Control, in Import and Export Policy, 1988-91, Vol.II,in respect of Export Control and Procedures, which is as follows :-  "Only item included in Schedule I to the Exports (Control) Order, 1988 are under control.
RatioOfTheDecision	No such item can be exported unless it is covered by a valid license issued by a licensing authority competent to grant an export license for that item.
RatioOfTheDecision	Goods which are not included in this Schedule can be shipped without any export license unless their export is controlled under any  other law for the time being in force."
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, the Export (Control) Order, 1988 is not applicable to photographic film (colour).
RatioOfTheDecision	6.
RatioOfTheDecision	If reference is made to S. 74 of the Act, it appears that when any goods capable of being easily identified which have been imported into India upon which any duty has been paid on importation, are to be re- exported and the goods are not prohibited goods, then clearance for exportation can be given by the proper officer (S. 51) and on such exportation 98% of the duty paid on importation is to be re-paid as drawback.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have not been shown which are those goods which can thus be re-exported and where import duty already paid is to be claimed as drawback.
RatioOfTheDecision	We have also not been shown any provision of law stating that the goods which have been imported could be sold only in the country itself.
RatioOfTheDecision	The clarification given by the <ORG> & E does not appear to be appropriate.
RatioOfTheDecision	We may also note that under S. 18 of the Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 and various other provisions thereof,  there are sufficient safeguards to see that proper sale price on export of goods is realized.
RatioOfTheDecision	It is not the case of the respondents that there is dearth of photographic film (colour) in the country, and export of the goods in question would certainly result in earning of some foreign exchange for the country.
RatioOfTheDecision	7.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mr.<NAME> said that we should safeguard the respondents in case it is ordered that the goods be allowed to be re-exported.
Argument	Mr.<NAME> has filed affidavits of Mr.<NAME> and <NAME> respectively Managing Director and Director of the petitioner, to the effect that they are owners of property bearing No.13-A/25, WEA, Karol Bagh, New Delhi, and that they would not dispose of,alienate or otherwise create any charge on this property without the leave of the court and during the pendency of this writ petition and further that this property shall remain subject to charge of this court and in case the petitioner ultimately loses, this property could be used to secure the interests of the re spondents.
Argument	Mr.<NAME> has stated that both Mr.<NAME> and Mrs.<NAME> give undertaking to this property could be taken as security for payment of any amount of duty or other charges which may be found due to the respondents in the present writ petition.
RatioOfTheDecision	We accept this undertaking.
RatioOfTheDecision	Both Mr.<NAME> and Mrs.<NAME> shall remain bound by what they have stated in their respective affidavits.
RatioOfTheDecision	Mr.<NAME> did say that a bank guarantee should be asked for, but in the circumstances of the present case we do not think that to be a proper course.
RatioOfTheDecision	Accordingly, we direct that the petitioner will be allowed to re-export the goods in question without payment of any duty and, of course, subject to the provisions of sub-s.(1) of S. 69 of the Act which have been noted above.
RatioOfTheDecision	8.
RatioOfTheDecision	We would like to add that the view which we have taken above is only a prima facie view and is subject to final determination in the petition.
RulingByPresentCourt	All the CMs stand disposed of.
