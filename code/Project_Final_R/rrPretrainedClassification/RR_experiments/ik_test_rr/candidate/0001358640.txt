RatioOfTheDecision	From a plan reading of the extracted provision, it is clear that a tax leviable under Clause (b) is, in terms, a tax on "persons".
RatioOfTheDecision	The expression "persons" undoubtedly includes natural persons.
RatioOfTheDecision	The class of such taxable persons has been indicated by the <ORG> with reference to their o ccupational activity.
Statute	Thus, in order to be authorised a tax under Clause (b) of Section 61(1) must satisfy two conditions : First, it must be a tax on persons.
Statute	Second, such persons must be practising any profession or act or carrying on any trade or calling in the municipality.
RatioOfTheDecision	15.
RatioOfTheDecision	There can be dispute that the appellants are "persons" and as such, satisfy the first condition.
RatioOfTheDecision	Even the learned Counsel for the appellants has candidly conceded that the individual partners are also ''persons" within the meaning of the said Clause (b).
RatioOfTheDecision	Controversy thus becomes narrowed down into the issue : Whether persons collectively doing business in partnership, in the municipality, fulfil the second condition ?
RatioOfTheDecision	That is to say, such persons "carry on any trade or ceiling in the municipality" within the contemplation of Clause (b) ?
RatioOfTheDecision	16.
RatioOfTheDecision	In our opinion, for reasons that follow, the answer to this question must be in the affirmative.
RatioOfTheDecision	17.
RatioOfTheDecision	"Partnership" as defined in Section 4 of the Indian Partnership Act, 1932 is the relation  between persons who have agreed to share the profits of a business carried on by all or any of them for the benefit of all.
RatioOfTheDecision	The Section further makes it clear that a firm or partnership is not a legal entity separate and distinct from the partners.
RatioOfTheDecision	Firm is only a compendious description of the individuals who compose the firm.
RatioOfTheDecision	The crucial words in the definition of 'partnership' are these that have been underlined.
RatioOfTheDecision	They hold the key to the question posed above.
RatioOfTheDecision	They show that the business is carried on by all or any of the partners In the instant case, admittedly, all the plaintiff appellants are carrying on the business in partnership.
RatioOfTheDecision	All the six partners are sharing the profits and losses.
RatioOfTheDecision	All the six partners are jointly and severally responsible for the liabilities incurred or obligations incurred in the course of the business Each partner is considered an agent of the other.
RatioOfTheDecision	This being the position, it; Is not possible to hold that each of the six partners is not carrying on a trade or calling within t he purview of Clause (b) of Section 61(1) of the Municipal Act.
RatioOfTheDecision	At the most it can be said that each of these six persons in severally as well as collectively carrying on a trade in the Municipality.
RatioOfTheDecision	There is nothing in the language of Section 61 or the scheme of the Municipal Act which warrants the construction that persons who are carrying on a trade in association or partner ship with each other cannot be individually taxed under Clause (b) of Section 61(1) .
RatioOfTheDecision	On the contrary, definite indication is available in the language and the scheme of this statute that such partners can be taxed as persons in their individual capacity.
RatioOfTheDecision	As noticed already, Clause (b) makes it clear in no uncertain terms that this is a tax on 'persons'.
RatioOfTheDecision	Its incidence falls on individuals, who belong to a clase practising any profession or art; on carrying on a trade or calling in the municipality.
RatioOfTheDecision	To hold that persons who are collectively carrying on a trade in the municipality cannot be taxed individually, could be to read into the s tatute words which are not there.
RatioOfTheDecision	There are no words in Clause (b) or elsewhere in the statute which expressly or by necessary implication, exclude or exempt persons carrying on a trade collectively in the municipality from being taxed as individuals.
RatioOfTheDecision	To attract liability to a tax under this clause, it is sufficient that the person concerned is carrying on a trade in the municipality, irrespective of whether such trade is being carried on by him individually or in partnership with others.
RatioOfTheDecision	Thus, both the conditions necessary for levying a tax under Clause (b) of Sub-section (1) of Section 61 of the Municipal Act existed in this case.
RatioOfTheDecision	The appellants are 'persons' and they are carrying on a trade in Chheharta Municipality.
