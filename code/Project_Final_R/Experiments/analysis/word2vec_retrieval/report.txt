========================================================================
  ANALYSIS 13 — DATA-TO-RESULTS NARRATIVE
========================================================================

  OBS: W2V MAP tops out at 0.2531 vs TF-IDF 0.5964
  MECH: W2V represents documents as points in a continuous semantic space. Legal documents share broad overlapping vocabulary across all topics, causing embeddings to cluster densely. TF-IDF's exact term matching via n-grams is a better discriminator for this domain.
  RESULT: W2V underperforms TF-IDF by 57.6% MAP. Semantic generalization hurts more than it helps in high-homogeneity legal text.

  OBS: Skip-gram MAP 0.2280 consistently > CBOW 0.2202
  MECH: Skip-gram predicts surrounding context from a center word, forcing each word to develop a precise representation. CBOW averages context to predict the center word — effectively blurring representations. For rare legal citation and statute terms, skip-gram learns sharper embeddings.
  RESULT: Skip-gram is the clear winner; all top-5 configs use skip-gram.

  OBS: Bigram tokenisation drops MAP by ≈0.0605
  MECH: A bigram vocabulary on a corpus of ~2000 documents is dramatically underdetermined: most bigram pairs appear only once or twice, below the min_count threshold, resulting in massive OOV rates and degenerate zero-vectors for short documents.
  RESULT: Bigram W2V should not be used unless the corpus has 100k+ documents.

  OBS: TF-IDF pooling marginal gain: Δ=0.0062 per (arch,dim,window,ng) pair
  MECH: TF-IDF weighting suppresses 'legal boilerplate' tokens (e.g. 'section', 'pursuant', 'court') that are near-omnipresent in the corpus. These high-DF tokens pull mean vectors toward a common centroid, erasing discriminative signal.
  RESULT: Always use TF-IDF pooling for legal/corpus-specific W2V; mean pooling is suboptimal.

  OBS: Signal gap (rel minus irrel cosine) = 0.1183
  MECH: The cosine similarity of relevant pairs is only marginally higher than irrelevant pairs. The entire legal corpus is in-domain, so W2V embeddings are compressed into a narrow semantic cone. This causes many 'close but wrong' candidates to appear before truly relevant ones.
  RESULT: The narrow signal gap explains why MAP plateaus ~0.25 regardless of dimension or window tuning — it is a fundamental limitation of the representation.

  OBS: Dim=200/300 outperforms dim=100 by ≈0.0245 MAP
  MECH: Higher dimensions allow the model to encode multiple orthogonal legal concepts simultaneously. 100-d compresses too aggressively. However, marginal gains between 200 and 300 shrink, suggesting diminishing returns on this corpus size.
  RESULT: dim=200 is the sweet spot: large enough for expressiveness, small enough to avoid overfitting the sparse co-occurrence statistics of this corpus.
