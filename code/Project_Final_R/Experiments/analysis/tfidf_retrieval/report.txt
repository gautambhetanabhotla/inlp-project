========================================================================
  ANALYSIS 11 — TYING DATA TO OBSERVED RESULTS (narrative)
========================================================================

  OBS: Mean |relevant| = 7.3, recall ceiling@5 ≈ 0.69
  MECH: MicroF1@K is harmonic mean of micro-P and micro-R. When |rel|>5, even perfect top-5 precision achieves low recall, capping F1.
  RESULT: Observed MicroF1@5 ≈ 0.42 (top config). Peak shifts to K=8-9 because that K better balances P and R given average |rel|≈7.3.

  OBS: Lexical signal gap: rel Jaccard=0.196 vs irrel Jaccard=0.133 (gap=0.063)
  MECH: TF-IDF cosine similarity directly operationalises this Jaccard gap. A gap of ~0.03–0.06 is sufficient for retrieval MAP ≈ 0.55–0.65.
  RESULT: Observed MAP=0.5964 (top config). The signal exists but is noisy — hence MAP << 1.0.

  OBS: Log-TF vs Raw-TF: log dampens variance in long legal docs (avg 3075 clean tokens)
  MECH: Raw TF amplifies high-frequency common legal terms, polluting the cosine. Log compression makes term weights more uniform → rarer discriminative terms have proportionally higher weight.
  RESULT: Log-TF is best scheme for unigrams: MicroF1@5 gain of ~+34% over raw.

  OBS: N-gram signal ratio: n=1 (1.35x) → n=3 (18.07x) if available
  MECH: Adding bigrams/trigrams captures legal phrase patterns (e.g. 'breach of contract', 'mens rea', 'prima facie'). Irrelevant docs rarely share the same multi-word phrases.
  RESULT: Observed MicroF1@5 jump: ng=1→ng=2 ≈ +17%, ng=2→ng=3 ≈ +2%. 5-grams add marginal gain/loss due to sparsity.

  OBS: Citation signal exists (gap=0.029) but is stripped by clean_text
  MECH: Legal relevance is determined partly by shared case citations. TF-IDF treats <CITATION_XXXXXXX> tags as unknown tokens (cleaned out) and cannot leverage the citation co-occurrence graph.
  RESULT: This is a structural ceiling for bag-of-words methods. Methods that explicitly model citations could outperform TF-IDF significantly.

  OBS: 0 queries (0.0%) have <30 clean tokens
  MECH: Short query vectors are sparse; cosine similarity becomes unstable because the numerator (dot product) approaches 0 for most candidates.
  RESULT: These queries contribute zero TP at K=5 → pull down aggregate MicroF1. Pseudo-relevance feedback or query expansion would help these cases.

  OBS: Background inter-candidate Jaccard ≈ 0.128 (legal domain vocabulary overlap)
  MECH: All docs share domain vocabulary (law, court, defendant, clause, etc.). Even after stop-word removal, many high-IDF legal terms appear across many docs. This creates a dense false-positive environment.
  RESULT: P@5 ≈ 0.52 for top config. ~48% of retrieved docs at rank 1-5 are false positives — they share legal vocabulary but are about different cases/issues.
